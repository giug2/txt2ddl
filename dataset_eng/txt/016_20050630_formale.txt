The data model describes the management of plays, theaters and related programming. The `Author` entity, identified by `author_id`, contains personal data such as `Name`, `Surname`, `Date of birth` and `Date of death`. Each author can be associated with one or more `Play` entities, establishing a one-to-many relationship. The `Play` entity, identified by `theatre_work_id`, includes attributes such as `Title` and `creation year`. The `Teatro` entity, identified by `teatro_id`, stores facility-specific information such as `name`, `address` and `phone`. Programming is managed through the `Work in billboard` entity, identified by `id_opera_in_billboard` and characterized by `Start date` and `Final date`. This entity acts as a link, as each instance of `Public Play` is associated with a single `Play` and a single `Theatre`, implementing two one-to-many relationships. The pricing structure is defined by the `Price` entity, identified by `price_id`. A price is uniquely determined by the combination of three entities referenced via one-to-many relationships: a `Theater`, a `Show Category` (identified by `show_category_id` and a `Description`) and a `Seat Type` (identified by `seat_type_id` and a `Description`). Finally, the system manages tariff concessions through the `Reduction Type` entity, identified by `reduction_type_id` and equipped with a `description`. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Theatre` and `Reduction Type`, indicating that a theater can offer multiple types of reduction and the same reduction can be valid in multiple theatres.
