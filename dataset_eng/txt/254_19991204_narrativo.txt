This system was designed to tell the story of the academic path within a university, starting from its organizational structure up to the single exam taken by a student. The beating heart of the institution is represented by its Campuses, each identified by a unique code and a specific name, which constitute the physical locations of university life. Within this context, the academic organization is divided into Departments, each with its own code and name, which represent the fundamental units of teaching and research. A single Department can extend its presence across multiple campuses, creating an interconnected knowledge network.

Each Department offers different Degree Courses, which are the training path chosen by students. A degree program is uniquely defined by the combination of its name and the department it belongs to, and is further characterized by the type of degree it confers, such as bachelor's or master's degree. The study plan of each degree course is made up of a series of specific teachings. Each course is identified by a unique code within its degree course and has a value in terms of University Educational Credits (CFU).

To allow student evaluation, each course includes several exam sessions during the academic year. Each session is an event in itself, marked by its own unique ID. The protagonist of this entire ecosystem is the Student, registered in the system with a Student Number that identifies him unequivocally, together with his name and surname. The crucial moment that connects the student to his study path is registration for the exam. When a student decides to take an exam, a record is created that links him to a specific Exam Session on a specific Registration Date. In this way, each student can have numerous registrations throughout his career, and each exam session collects the registrations of all the students who intend to participate, thus completing the cycle of educational management.
