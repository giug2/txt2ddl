Welcome to the heart of our academic management system, a platform designed to precisely map the vibrant network of relationships that animate our university. At the center of our organization are the fundamental structures: the different **Campus**, which represent our physical offices spread across the territory, and the **Departments**, the nerve centers where knowledge is cultivated and research is carried out. These entities form the foundation on which the entire academic ecosystem is built. The protagonists of this scenario are the **Professors**, whose role is dynamic and multifaceted. We understand that a teacher can work in different contexts; therefore, the system is designed to handle the complexity that a single professor can be affiliated with multiple departments and can even teach on different campuses. Each of his assignments is unique and is identified precisely by the specific combination of professor, department and campus, reflecting the fluid nature of academic collaborations. Alongside teachers, the new generation of researchers is growing: **PhD students**. Their training path is intrinsically linked to a guiding figure. The system formalizes this fundamental link by establishing that each PhD student is followed by a single supervising professor, guaranteeing a clear and direct mentorship relationship. Furthermore, this supervision is strictly contextualized: each doctoral student is not only linked to the professor as an individual, but to his specific academic role within a specific campus, thus ensuring that the research path is always anchored to a well-defined structure and working environment. In this way, the system does not simply catalog information, but tells the story of the human and professional connections that define our university community.
