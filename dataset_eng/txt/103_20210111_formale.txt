The data model defines six main entities: Store, Type, Product, Employee, City, and Manufacturer. The `Shop` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has the `Name` and `Address` attributes. The `Type` entity is defined by an `Acronym` (PK) and a `Name`. The `Product` entity is characterized by a primary key composed of `Code` and `fk_producer` and includes the `Name` attribute. The `Employee` entity is identified by the `Serial Number` (PK) and contains `First Name` and `Surname`. The `City` and `Manufacturer` entities are both identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. The relationships between these entities are as follows: There are two many-to-many (N:N) relationships, one between `Store` and `Type` and another between `Store` and `Employee`. The `Product` entity is linked by a one-to-many (1:N) relationship with `Type`, indicating that each product belongs to only one type, and by another one-to-many (1:N) relationship with `Manufacturer`, specifying that each product is made by a single manufacturer. Finally, the `Employee` entity is connected to `City` via two distinct many-to-one (N:1) relationships: the first, identified by the `Birth` attribute, establishes the employee's city of birth, while the second, `Resident`, defines the employee's city of residence.
