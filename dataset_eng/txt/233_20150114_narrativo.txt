This system was designed to be the beating heart of logistics management, offering precise and dynamic mapping of everything that happens within our storage spaces. At the center of everything we find the concept of **Warehouse**, each physical structure identified by a unique code and located by a specific address. Inside each warehouse, the organization is widespread: the space is divided into numerous **Shelves**, each of which has its own identification code to allow immediate and error-free localization. This hierarchical structure ensures that each shelf unambiguously belongs to only one warehouse, whereas a warehouse naturally hosts a multitude of shelves. The system also manages a general catalog of each **Product** we deal with, identified by its SKU code, and keeps track of the personnel working in the field through the figure of the **Operator**, recognized by his personal identification number. The fundamental control activity is represented by **Inventory**, an event that takes place on a specific date and during which the number of counted items is recorded. The most important relationship manifests itself during this operation: a single inventory event can involve the control of numerous different shelves, and, at the same time, a single shelf will be the subject of multiple inventories over time, creating a detailed and verifiable history of stocks and checks carried out.
