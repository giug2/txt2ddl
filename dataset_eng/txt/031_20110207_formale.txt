The data model describes the structure for managing products, orders and related master data. The `Product` entity is identified by a unique `Code` (PK) and includes the `Name`, `Available Quantity` and `Price` attributes. The `Category` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Description`. The `Supplier` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. The `Order` entity is tracked via a unique `Number` (PK) and contains `Date` and `Status` information. Finally, the `Customer` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and includes `Name` and `Address`. The relationships are defined as follows: There is a many-to-one relationship between `Product` and `Category`, and between `Product` and `Supplier`, indicating that each product belongs to only one category and is supplied by only one supplier. A many-to-one relationship also links `Order` and `Customer`, establishing that each order is placed by a single customer. A many-to-many relationship exists between `Product` and `Order`, meaning that an order can contain multiple products and a product can be included in multiple orders.
