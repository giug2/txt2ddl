The data model describes a university academic structure. The `Department` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, groups together multiple `Matter`. Each `Subject`, defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, belongs to a single `Department` (relation N:1). The subjects are associated with the degree courses through an N:N relationship. The `CorsoDiLaurea` entity is described by `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Description`. Multiple degree courses can refer to a single `Chair` (relation N:1), which is identified by the `Name` (PK) and has the attributes `Office` and `Full Professor`. For exam management, each `Degree Course` is associated with multiple `Exam Sessions` (1:N relationship); a session is identified by a primary key composed of `Date` and `fk_course_di_graduation` and specifies a `Time`. Multiple exam sessions can take place in a single `Room` (N:1 relationship), an entity defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. The management of the teaching staff is entrusted to the `Teacher` entity, with `Number` (PK), `Surname` and `Name`. The assignment of teachers to chairs takes place via the associative entity `IncaricoDidattico`, whose primary key is made up of `fk_cattedra` and `fk_docente`. This entity establishes a 1:N relationship as both `Chair` and `Teacher`, allowing each of them to have multiple assignments. Each teaching assignment is in turn associated with a single `Academic Role` (N:1 relationship), an entity defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`.
