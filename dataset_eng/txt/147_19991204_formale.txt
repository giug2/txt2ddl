The data model defines the structure for managing a fleet of vehicles, its owners and technical characteristics. The `Car` entity represents a single vehicle, uniquely identified by the `Plate` (PK) and characterized by the `Year` attribute. The `Person` entity represents an individual, whose primary key is the `Fiscal Code`, and includes personal details such as `Surname` and `First Name`. The technical description of the vehicle is hierarchical: the `Nation` entity, identified by `Nation Sign` (PK), is associated with the manufacturing `Home` entity, which has the `Code` as its primary key. Each `House` produces one or more `Model`s; the `Model` entity is identified by a primary key composed of the `Name` of the model and a foreign key `fk_casa` which establishes its membership. Models are classified using the `Category` entity, identified by `CategoryCode` (PK). In turn, each model is divided into one or more `Versions`, the entity of which is identified by a key composed of `Version Code` and `fk_model` and contains the `Displacement` attribute. The relationships structure the model as follows: a `Nation` can have multiple producing `Houses` (1:N); a `House` produces multiple `Model`i (1:N); a `Model` belongs to a `Category` (1:N) and can have multiple `Version`s (1:N). Each individual `Car` is an instance of a specific `Version` (1:N relationship between `Version` and `Car`). Finally, ownership is defined by a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between `Person` and `Car`, allowing a person to own multiple vehicles and a vehicle to have multiple owners.
