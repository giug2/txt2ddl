The data model describes the structure for managing missions, funding lines, projects and ministries. The main entities defined are Mission, Line, Recommendation, Source, Project and Ministry. The Mission entity is identified by a `Number` attribute that acts as the primary key and has a `Name`. The Line entity is in an identifying relationship with Mission; its primary key is composite, made up of its `Number` and the foreign key `fk_mission`, which refers to the Mission it belongs to. The other Line attributes are `Name` and `Budget`. The registry entities Recommendation, Source, Project and Ministry are all identified by a unique `Code` as the primary key and described by a `Name`.

The interconnections between entities are defined by different relationships. There is a one-to-many relationship between Mission and Route, where each Mission can contain multiple Routes. Mission and Recommendation are linked by a many-to-many relationship. Line and Source are also connected by a many-to-many relationship, which is characterized by an `Amount` attribute that specifies the value of the loan. Each Line can finance multiple Projects, establishing a one-to-many relationship. Finally, the relationship between Ministry and Project is twofold: a one-to-many relationship defines a Ministry as coordinator of many Projects, specifying the role via the `Coordinator` attribute; in parallel, a many-to-many relationship allows multiple Ministries to be associated with a Project as participants, with details provided by the `Participants` attribute.
