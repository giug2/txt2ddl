The data model defines the hierarchical structure of an academic organization. The root entity is `Faculty`, uniquely identified by a primary key `faculty_id`. The `Department` entity is connected to it via a many-to-one relationship, where each department belongs to a single faculty; its primary key is composite, made up of the `department_id` and `fk_faculty` attributes. Continuing in the hierarchy, the `Course of Study` entity is associated with `Department` with a many-to-one relationship. The identification of the course of study is guaranteed by a composite primary key that includes `id_corso_di_studio` and the foreign key `fk_department`. Finally, the `Teacher` entity is linked to a specific `Course of Study` through a many-to-one relationship, and is identified by a composite primary key made up of `teacher_id` and the foreign key `fk_corso`. Each dependent entity then includes the parent entity's identifier in its primary key, establishing a set of identifying relationships.
