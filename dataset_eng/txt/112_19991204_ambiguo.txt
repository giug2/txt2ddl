So, we have some stuff in this system. There is a list of people, each with their name, surname and that tax code that makes them unique. Then, of course, there are the cars, and each car is identified by its license plate and has a year of production. It works like this: a person can own multiple cars, but each car is linked to only one person, in short to its owner. Each car, then, is of a certain model. The models are a bit particular: to know which one it is, the name is not enough, you also need to know who made it, the manufacturer. Plus, each model has some kind of category. Of course, for a single model type, there may be a lot of identical cars around, but a specific car belongs only to that model. Going further upstream, there are the car manufacturers, the brands. Each has its own code to recognize it and a name. A house can make a lot of different models, but a model is always and only of that house. Finally, each manufacturer belongs to a nation. Nations are registered with an acronym and their full name. In a country there may be several car manufacturers, but each company has its headquarters in only one country.
