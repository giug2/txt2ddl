The data model defines the organizational structure of the personnel and their physical locations. The `LOCATION` entity represents company locations, each uniquely identified by `location_id` and described by `address` and `city`. The `OFFICE` entity is connected to it, which identifies a specific work space within a location. The primary key of `OFFICE` is composed of `office_id` and `fk_location`, establishing a one-to-many identification relationship in which each office necessarily belongs to only one location. The entities `MANAGER` and `ASSISTANT` represent the staff, identified by `manager_id` and `assistant_id` respectively, and both contain personal details such as `name` and `surname`. Finally, a many-to-many-to-many ternary relationship links the entities `MANAGER`, `ASSISTANT` and `OFFICE`, modeling the complex working association whereby a manager can operate with multiple assistants in different offices, and vice versa.
