Basically, we have a few things mixed up here. There are offices, such as branches of a company, and each has its own code to recognize it. Inside each headquarters, then, there are various offices; each office necessarily belongs to one and only one location, it cannot exist alone. Indeed, to identify an office you must know both its code and that of the location where it is located. Assistants work in these offices. Even they are strictly tied to one office, it's not like an assistant can work in multiple places at the same time, and obviously an office can have a lot of assistants. Finally there are the managers, who have their own personal code. Their situation is more flexible: one manager can be in charge of multiple offices, and at the same time an office can have more than one manager supervising it, so their responsibilities can overlap quite a bit.
