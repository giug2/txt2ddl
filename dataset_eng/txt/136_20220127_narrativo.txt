This system is designed to map and manage a company's intricate organizational network, offering a clear view of how people, teams and locations interact with each other to achieve common goals. At the heart of our model are the physical foundations of the organization, the **LOCATIONS**, each identified by a unique `location_id` code and located by a specific `location_address`. Within these offices the operational units, the **DEPARTMENTS**, come to life, each distinguished by its own `department_id` identifier and a `department_name` that describes its function. The workforce that animates these spaces is represented by two key figures: the **MANAGERS**, registered with a personal `manager_id` and their `manager_name`, and the **ASSISTANTS**, also equipped with a unique `assistant_id` and an `assistant_name`. The physical and organizational structure is defined by a clear and hierarchical relationship: each **DEPARTMENT** is unequivocally assigned to one and only one **LOCATION**, although a single location can naturally host numerous different departments. However, the true beating heart of company operations emerges from the complex and dynamic interaction between staff. The system models flexible, multidimensional collaboration, recognizing that a **MANAGER** is not tied to a single team, but can supervise activities in multiple **DEPARTMENTS** and, in each of them, collaborate with different **ASSISTANTS**. Conversely, an **ASSISTANT** can support multiple managers in various departmental contexts, and a **DEPARTMENT** thus becomes a dynamic hub where different combinations of managers and assistants intertwine. This network structure allows you to precisely map who works with whom and in what context, reflecting the fluid and collaborative nature of the modern organization.
