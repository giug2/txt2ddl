Welcome to the beating heart of our academic management system, a digital world designed to map and organize the university's entire educational offering in a clear and interconnected way. At the top of our structure we find the Departments, the large academic families that form the foundations of the university. Each Department is uniquely identified by its Name and has a physical reference location. From each of these Departments multiple Degree Courses arise and are managed, creating an indissoluble bond: a Degree Course always belongs only to one Department, while a Department can be the cradle of numerous training proposals.

Each Degree Course, in turn, is defined not only by its Name, which is unique within its own department, but also by its Year of Activation, which tells us its story. To provide order and scientific classification, each Degree Course is associated with a specific Disciplinary Area. These areas, identified by an SSD (Scientific-Disciplinary Sector) Code and a clear description, group courses with thematic affinities, allowing you to understand the field of study at a glance. Multiple courses can converge in the same disciplinary area, but each individual course has one and only one classification.

Going into the detail of the training path, each Degree Course is divided into a series of Teachings, the subjects that students will face. A course is defined by its Name, which makes it unique within its course, and enriched by crucial information such as the number of Educational Credits it confers, the hours of lessons expected and the Course Year in which it is placed. Naturally, a degree course is made up of many courses, but each course is an integral part of one single degree course.

Finally, it is the teachers who bring every lesson to life. Each professor in our system is a unique individual, recognized by his or her Matriculation Number. We know his academic range, years of experience and the office where he receives students. The link between teachers and subjects is managed with precision: a single teaching is entrusted to the expertise of a single teacher, but a teacher, with his experience, can be the holder of multiple courses, even in different degree courses, thus weaving the web of knowledge throughout the entire academic organisation.
