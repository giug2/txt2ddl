Welcome to the beating heart of sports competition, a system designed to capture every highlight of a championship. At the center of everything lies the concept of **Tournament**, each edition of which is identified by a unique code and recognizable through its specific name. A tournament, however, is nothing without its protagonists: the **Teams**. Each team, defined by its unique name, participates in a specific tournament, and for each competition it takes part in, the system meticulously tracks its performance, recording its position in the standings and the points accumulated over the course of the season.

The real action takes place on the pitch, during every single **Match**. A match is a unique event, unequivocally defined by the combination of the two contending teams, one playing at home and the other away, and by the exact date on which it is played. Of course, the stadium hosting the event is also recorded. For each match, the system keeps track of the final result, noting the number of goals scored by each of the two teams, a fundamental element for determining the ranking.

But a team is much more than just a name; it is a collection of individual talents. The system manages the details of each **Player**, who belongs to a specific team and is uniquely identified by his shirt number within the team. We know the full name and date of birth of each athlete. Finally, to have a complete picture of each clash, the system links players to the matches in which they took part. Since a player can participate in many matches and a match involves many players, this relationship allows us to know exactly who took the field and in what specific role they contributed to the performance of their team on that particular day.
