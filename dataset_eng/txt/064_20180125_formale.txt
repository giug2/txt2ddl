The data model describes the structure for managing academic events, organized in series of conferences. The `Series` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Title`, represents the main container. Each series is associated with multiple `Meeting` entities in a one-to-many relationship. A `Conference` is uniquely identified by a composite primary key made up of `fk_serie`, `Date` and `year`, establishing a direct link with the series it belongs to. Each conference takes place in a specific `City`, defining a many-to-one relationship, and is divided into multiple `Session` entities through a one-to-many relationship. The `Session` is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has the `Title` and `Time` attributes. The `Person`, `Institution` and `City` entities are defined by a `Code` (PK) and, where applicable, a `Name`. Participation in sessions is managed through the `Person with affiliation` entity, identified by its own `Code` (PK). This entity acts as a bridge: it establishes a many-to-one relationship with `Institution` and a one-to-many relationship with `Person`, allowing you to associate a person with a specific institution. The relationship between `Session` and `Person with affiliation` is many-to-many, indicating that a session can have multiple affiliated participants and a person with his affiliation can participate in multiple sessions. Finally, the model establishes that a `City` can host multiple `Institutions`, formalizing a one-to-many relationship.
