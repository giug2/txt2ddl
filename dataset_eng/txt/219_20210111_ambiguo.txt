So, basically there's a whole system for keeping track of universities. Each university has its own acronym, which is the thing that immediately identifies it, and then obviously it also has its full name. Inside every university there are a lot of departments; each department is connected to only one university, and to recognize it a specific code is used for that department within that university. Of course, a department also has a name and a location somewhere.

These departments then deal with the various degree courses. This is a bit intertwined, because a department can deal with several courses, and at the same time a degree program can be managed by several departments together. Each course, to be clear, has its own code and name. In turn, the degree courses are headed by a faculty, which also has a code and a name, and a single faculty can group together quite a few different courses.

Then there are the teachers, who are the people who teach. Each teacher has his own number, name and surname. Professors are linked to departments, and here too things are flexible: a professor can work for multiple departments and a department obviously has many professors. When a teacher is associated with a department, his role is also specified, such as his classification. Finally, some personal information of the teachers is also kept track of, linking them to the municipalities, which are identified by an ISTAT code and a name. In particular, there is a link that tells us which municipality a teacher is born in and another link, again with a municipality, which tells us where he or she resides.
