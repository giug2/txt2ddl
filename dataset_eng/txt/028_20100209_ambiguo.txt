So basically we have this checking account stuff. Each account has its own identification number, which cannot be the same as another, and then there is the usual information, such as who the owner is, where he lives, and the money that was there at the beginning and end of a certain period. Then, obviously, each current account is connected to a lot of movements, which would be operations such as income and expenditure. Each movement is a thing in itself, with its own unique code, the date on which it occurred, a small description to understand what it is about and, of course, the amount. This is how it works: an account has many movements, but a specific movement belongs only to that account. Finally, there is also a kind of label for movements, called 'Movement Type', which serves to classify them. Each type has its own ID and description, such as 'transfer', 'withdrawal', 'deposit' and so on. Here too, a type of movement, such as 'withdrawal', can be associated with an infinite number of different operations, but each single operation is of only one type, it cannot be a withdrawal and a transfer at the same time.
