The data model describes a system for managing sports information, structured around five main entities: `Club`, `City`, `Country`, `Player` and `Role`. The `Company` entity is identified by a `Code` attribute that acts as a primary key (PK) and has the `Name`, `Color` and `Foundation` attributes. The `City` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, as is the `Country` entity, characterized by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. The `Player` entity is uniquely identified by a `Code` (PK) and includes personal and physical data such as `Surname`, `First Name`, `Height` and `Weight`. Finally, the `Role` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a descriptive `Name`. The relationships between entities are defined as follows: a relationship of type N:1 links `Company` to `City`, indicating that each company is based in a single city, which can host multiple companies. In turn, an N:1 relationship links `City` to `Nation`, establishing that each city belongs to a single nation. There is a 1:N relationship between `Club` and `Player`, whereby each player belongs to only one club, which can have many players. Each player is also associated with their city of birth via an N:1 relationship with the `City` entity and with a specific position on the pitch via an N:1 relationship with the `Position` entity.
