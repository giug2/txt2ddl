Welcome to a meticulously organized automotive universe, a system designed to catalog and understand every detail of the world of four wheels. At the center of this system we find the large car manufacturers, each uniquely identified by its name and characterized by a physical address. Each house is the root from which an entire family of vehicles sprouts.

In fact, each house creates a line of models. A model is uniquely defined by the combination of its name and the company that produces it, and is further specified by the year of production. This bond is indissoluble: each model belongs to one and only one car manufacturer, which can obviously boast numerous models in its portfolio. To help navigate this vast offering, each model is classified within a specific Market Segment. A segment, identified by a code and description, represents a category such as "SUV", "city car" or "sedan". While a model can belong to only one segment, a segment can naturally group together many different models, even from competing manufacturers.

The exploration doesn't end here. A single model rarely exists in a single form; rather, it is divided into different versions, each designed for a specific need. Each version is closely linked to its original model and is distinguished by its own name, a specific price, a certain number of ports and a maximum achievable speed. Thus, a model can have multiple versions, but each version descends from a single and specific model. The beating heart of each version is its Engine. Each engine is an entity in its own right, cataloged with a unique code and described by its fundamental technical data: displacement, number of cylinders and power output. The relationship here is very flexible: each version is equipped with a specific type of engine, but the same engine can be the winning choice to power many different versions, even belonging to different models, creating an interconnected system that reflects the complexity and engineering of the automotive world.
