Basically, we've got a little bit of stuff here that's about art and the places it's found. Let's start with the nations, each has its own acronym to immediately recognize it and then the full name. Of course, every country is full of cities, and a city is only in one country, it cannot be in two places. Cities also have their own name and identification code so as not to confuse them. In these cities, then, there are museums; a city can have several, but a museum is only in one city. Each museum has its own name and ID to keep track of it.

Inside a museum, which is made up of many rooms, each room clearly belongs to that single museum. A room has its own name and we know how big it is, but to be sure which room we are talking about, we must always also know which museum it is in, so these two pieces of information together make it unique. Works of art are exhibited in the rooms. A room can contain a lot of works, but each work stays in a specific room. Each work of art is registered with its own code, has a title and we know the year it was created.

Finally, there are the artists, those who made the works. Each artist has his own identification number, name and surname. An artist may have created quite a few works, but each work is linked to a single artist. And, to close the circle, each artist is connected to their country of origin, so we know what country they come from.
