So, in practice there is this stuff that is the Festival Circuits, each with its own name and a code to avoid confusion. Each circuit, then, has a lot of editions that take place over time; to understand which one we are talking about, you look at which circuit it belongs to, the start date and the year. A bit of a mess of codes, but it works. Clearly, each edition of the festival takes place in some city, which has its own identifier to be recognized.

During one of these editions, there are a lot of performances, concerts in short. Each concert has its own code, and we know more or less on which stage and at what time it is held. And here comes the fun part: who plays? Well, a concert can have multiple artists performing, and the same artist can do multiple concerts during the festival.

To manage who plays, they did something a bit particular. There is a list of 'artists on the bill', which are basically the 'positions' in the lineup, each with its own code. Then there are the actual artists, the individual ones with their stage name, and an artist can be on the bill several times. But it's not over. Many of these 'bill artists' may also be linked to a band. So basically, a place in the lineup can be for a guy alone or for someone who is part of a group. Finally, so as not to miss anything, the bands are linked to their city of origin, so a lot of different groups can emerge from one city.
