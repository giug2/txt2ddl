So, here we talk about how the university is organized, more or less. There is a list of all the study courses, you know, like Engineering, Literature, and so on, each one has its own code so as not to confuse them. Each course of study is made up of quite a few individual courses, which you then have to follow and take the exam. This course is kind of the central piece of the puzzle; to understand what course we are talking about, you need to look at two things: which course of study it is in and what subject it is. Basically, a course is the combination of a subject and a course of study. Each course then has its own reference texts, books to buy, and can have more than one. Then, on the other hand, we have the list of subjects, which are disciplines in themselves, such as "Physics 1" or "Greek Literature", each with its own number. And finally, there are the professors, also with an identification code. A teacher can teach different subjects, so it is not strange to find the same teacher for multiple exams.
