Welcome to the beating heart of a system designed to orchestrate and celebrate the world of classical music. This digital universe has been meticulously designed to capture every nuance of a musical performance, from the genius of the composer to the illuminated stage. At the center of everything we find the concept of `Concert`, understood not as a single event, but as a complete artistic program, identified by a unique code, a title and a description that tells its soul. Each concert program is performed by a specific `Orchestra`, recognized by its name, which has a venue and a conductor.

Of course, a program can come to life on multiple occasions. For this reason, each `Concert` can be associated with multiple `Event`(s), each uniquely defined by its `Date` and enriched by the start time. Each single `Event` takes place in a specific `Theatre`, which is in turn identified by a code and a name, thus providing the physical context in which the magic happens. The music itself is represented by the `Piece` entity, each song with its `Code` and `Title`. The authorship of each piece is clearly attributed to a single `Composer`, identified by a code and his name, although a composer can be the author of countless pieces.

The link between pieces and concerts is dynamic: a `Concert` is composed of a sequence of several pieces, and the same `Piece` can be included in the program of several concerts. To maintain artistic order, the `POSITION` of each piece within each concert program is specified. Behind every performance is the collective talent of the orchestra. The internal structure of an `Orchestra` is defined through the `Composition` entity, which represents each individual post or role within it. An orchestra is made up of many of these positions, and each position is occupied by a `Musician`, identified by his `Number`, first name and last name. Finally, to complete the picture, each position (`Composition`) is linked to a single `Instrument`, described by a code and a name, thus specifying which instrument the musician plays in that specific role within the orchestra.
