The data model describes the management of condominium entities. The `Condominium` entity is uniquely identified by the `Name` attribute, which acts as the primary key, and has an `Address`. Each `Condominium` is associated with one or more `Scala` entities through a one-to-many relationship. The `Scala` entity is identified by a primary key composed of its `Code` and the foreign key of the `Condominium` to which it belongs. Each `Staircase`, in turn, is connected to multiple `Apartment` entities via a one-to-many relationship. The `Apartment` entity is identified by a primary key composed of the `Internal` number and the foreign key of the reference `Scala`; also includes the `Thousandths` attribute. The `Person` entity is defined by the `Tax Code` as the primary key and by the `Surname` and `First Name` attributes. There are two distinct one-to-many relationships between `Apartment` and `Person`: one to associate people as `owner` and a second to associate them as `tenant`.
