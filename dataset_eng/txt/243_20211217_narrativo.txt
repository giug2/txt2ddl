Welcome to the digital heart of our university, a system designed to map and manage the entire academic offering in a clear and interconnected way. At the center of everything we find the different Faculties, each identified by a unique code and known by its specific name, such as "Engineering" or "Letters and Philosophy". Each Faculty is home to numerous degree courses; in fact, a degree course, with its specific ID, name and annual cost, cannot exist except within the faculty that provides it, creating an indissoluble bond between the two. The Faculties, in turn, do not operate in isolation, but must comply with specific Ministerial Directives, each defined by a code and a title. This relationship is flexible: a single faculty can be subject to multiple directives and, vice versa, the same directive can be applied to different faculties, ensuring compliance with national standards. To support students, Degree Courses can offer access to various Scholarships, each with its own code and name. A course can make multiple scholarships available and a single scholarship can be allocated to students of different courses; when a course and a scholarship meet, the exact value of the financial contribution offered is specified. Going into the detail of the training path, each Degree Course is made up of a series of Teachings, i.e. individual subjects of study, each identified by a code and a name. A course belongs to a single degree course, defining its study plan. The academic management of these courses is entrusted to the Departments, which are also recognized by a code and a name. The relationship between departments and courses is twofold and reflects the complexity of academic collaboration: on the one hand, each course has a main reference department, which appoints its head and takes care of its organisation. On the other hand, from an interdisciplinarity perspective, a single teaching can make use of the expertise of multiple departments, and a department can provide its collaborators for different teachings, creating a network of knowledge that enriches the entire educational offering.
