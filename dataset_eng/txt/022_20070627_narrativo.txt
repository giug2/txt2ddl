Welcome to the heart of our academic archive system, a digital universe designed to map and enhance the world of knowledge and research. Here, each piece of information intertwines to tell the story of intellectual progress, starting from the foundations, the great **Institutions** of knowledge. Each of them, whether it is a university or a research centre, is uniquely registered through an identifying **Code** and described by its **Name**, **Address** and the **Country** to which it belongs.

These institutions are the academic home of the protagonists of our history: the **Authors**. Each author is a unique figure in our system, identified by a personal **Code** and characterized by their **Surname**, **Name** and **E-mail address**. The link between an author and his institution is fundamental; while one institution may be the point of reference for numerous researchers, each author is affiliated with only one specific institution, creating a clear bond of belonging.

The fruit of the work of these authors is the **Article**, the central nucleus of the knowledge produced. Each article is an entity in itself, with its own unique **Code**, a **Title** that summarizes its essence, a possible **Subtitle** to delve deeper into the context and, naturally, the complete **Text** of the scientific contribution. The nature of research is often collaborative, and our system reflects this perfectly: a single article can be the result of the joint work of multiple authors, and, at the same time, a prolific author can contribute to the writing of countless articles over the course of his career.

Once completed, an article must be shared with the world, and this is done through a **Publication**. Let's imagine a publication as a single issue or issue of a scientific journal. Each article finds its place in one and only one publication, and to facilitate consultation, we precisely record its physical location, indicating the **Start Page** and the **End Page**.

The publications, in turn, are not isolated elements but are part of a larger and more structured collection. Each publication is identified by a progressive **Number** and its **Date** of release, but its identity is complete only in relation to the **Volume** to which it belongs. In fact, multiple publications, such as the different issues of a magazine released during the year, are grouped into a single **Volume**. Each volume is uniquely defined by the **Year** of reference and has a general **Title** that describes the series. In this way, the system organizes the continuous flow of knowledge in a hierarchical, clear and navigable structure, which takes us from the individual author to the complete year of his discoveries.
