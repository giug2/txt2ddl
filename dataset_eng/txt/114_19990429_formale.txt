The data model describes a structure for managing academic entities, divided into six main entities. The `Department` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, is connected to the `Professor` entity via an N:1 relationship, establishing that multiple professors belong to a single department. The `Professor` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, is in turn associated with the `Sector` entity with an N:1 relationship, implying that a professor belongs to a single sector. The `Sector` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, groups together multiple instances of the `Discipline` entity through a 1:N relationship. As a result, the `Discipline` entity has a composite primary key of `Code` and `fk_sector`, as well as a `Name` attribute, and binds to the `Sector` with a cardinality of N:1. The `Matter` entity is defined by a composite primary key that includes `Suffix` and `fk_discipline`; it has a 1:N relationship with `Discipline`, where a single subject can be associated with multiple disciplines. A 1:N recursive relationship is also defined on the `Matter` entity. The `Course` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and equipped with the `CS` and `Semester` attributes, is connected to `Subject` by an N:1 relationship, so each course corresponds to a single subject. Finally, an N:1 relationship links `Course` to `Professor`, indicating that multiple courses can be taught by a single professor.
