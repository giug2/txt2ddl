So, here we have some kind of system for running a university or something. At the center of everything are the Departments; each has its own identification code, a name, an address and a contact. Almost all things start from these departments. For example, each department manages study courses, such as three-year or master's degrees. Each course has its own code, a full name and we also know when it was founded. At the head of these courses there is always a Coordinator, a person with his own ID, name and surname, of whom we keep track of when he started and when his assignment will end. A coordinator can be responsible for multiple courses, but a course always has only one coordinator. Then, within a course of study of a certain department, there are the Teachings Provided, which are practically the subjects that are offered in a given academic year. To identify them, you need to know both which course they belong to and which department they are part of. The teachers hold these lessons, each with their own number, name and surname. This is flexible: a professor can teach multiple courses, and at the same time a subject can be taught by multiple professors. Let's move on to the money. There are Contribution Quotas, that is, how much you pay. The amount depends on a couple of things. It is linked to a specific course provided, but also to the student's Income Range, which is nothing more than a category with a code and an explanation. In practice, for each subject, there are different prices depending on income. Each specific amount is therefore a combination of a subject and an income range. Finally, there are the Types of Exemption, which are the various concessions or discounts on taxes. These also have a code and a description. A department can provide different types of exemption, and the same type of exemption can be valid in multiple departments, there is no exclusivity.
