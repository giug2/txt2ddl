The data model defines the structure for managing information about music festivals and the artists participating in them. The system is composed of four main entities. The `Festival` entity represents an event, uniquely identified by `Festival_ID` (PK) and described by the `Name` and `EditionYear` attributes. The `Location` entity defines the geographic location, identified by `ID_Location` (PK) and specified by `CityName` and `Country`. The `Artist` entity catalogs performers, each with an `Artist_ID` (PK) and attributes such as `ArtName`, `RealName` and `CareerStartYear`. Finally, the `Musical Genre` entity classifies the music through `Genre_ID` (PK) and `Name`. The interconnections between these entities are modeled by three one-to-many (1:N) relationships. A relationship between `Location` and `Festival` establishes that a single location can host multiple festivals, while each festival takes place in only one location. A second relationship links `Festival` and `Artist`, indicating that a festival may feature multiple artists, but each artist is associated with a single festival. Finally, a relationship between `Musical Genre` and `Artist` defines that a genre can be attributed to multiple artists, while each artist belongs to a single musical genre.
