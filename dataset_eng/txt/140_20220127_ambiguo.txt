So basically we have a few things to keep an eye on here. There are the managers, each with their own code to recognize him, and then there are the assistants, also with their own identification number. In addition to people, we also manage physical places, i.e. locations, which are basically a list of places, each with its own ID. Inside each headquarters, then, you will find a lot of offices. The somewhat particular thing is that an office is identified not only by its number but also by the office in which it is located, so office 'A' in the Rome office is different from office 'A' in Milan. It is obvious that a branch has many offices, but an office is only in one branch. The real tangle, however, lies in the way people work. In practice, a manager, an assistant and an office are all connected to each other in a kind of large network. This means that a manager can work with multiple assistants in different offices, and an assistant can lend a hand to multiple managers, perhaps even moving between different offices. As a result, in a single office you can find a mix of managers and assistants all collaborating together. It's a very flexible system where everything is connected to everything.
