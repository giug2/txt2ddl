This system was designed to map and manage the intricate world of academia, offering a complete and interconnected view of university institutions, their internal organization and teaching staff. Let's imagine it as a digital atlas of higher education that starts from the territorial context to arrive at the individual teaching. Our journey starts from the broadest geographical level: the REGION. Each Italian region is uniquely identified by an ABBREVIATION and has a complete NAME. Within each region several CITIES flourish, each with its own ID and NAME, and each city belongs unambiguously to only one region. Cities are the beating heart of academic life, as each CITY can host multiple UNIVERSITIES. Each university, in turn, is identified by a unique ID and has a distinctive NAME, but is physically located in one and only one city, creating an indissoluble bond with its territory. The structure of each UNIVERSITY is divided into a multiplicity of DEPARTMENTS. A department is uniquely defined not only by its NAME, but by the combination of this with the university it belongs to; this means that departments with the same name can exist in different universities. For each department, we also keep track of the NUMBER_OFFICES available to it. The training offer comes to life through the COURSES. Each course is distinguished by a unique CODE, has a specific COURSE_NAME and refers to a specific ACADEMIC_YEAR. The relationship between courses and departments is very flexible: a department can provide numerous courses, and, at the same time, a single course can be of an interdisciplinary nature, being offered jointly by multiple departments. Finally, at the center of this ecosystem we find the PROFESSOR. Each teacher is registered with a unique SERIAL NUMBER, together with his NAME and SURNAME. The system links professors to the broader context, associating each of them with a specific REGION of reference. On a teaching level, a professor can hold more than one COURSE, but each single course, in a given year, is entrusted to the responsibility of a single teacher, guaranteeing a clear attribution of teaching.
