The data model defines a hierarchical structure centered on the `Company` entity, uniquely identified by a `company_id` primary key. The `Division` entity is linked to it through a one-to-many relationship, where each division belongs to a single company. The identification of the `Division` entity is guaranteed by a composite primary key, formed by its identifier `division_id` and the foreign key `fk_company`, establishing an identification dependency. In turn, the entities `Employee` and `Project` are linked to `Division` by one-to-many relationships. Both an employee and a project are uniquely defined in the context of their division, as indicated by their composite primary keys: `employee_id` with `fk_division` for the `Employee` entity, and `project_id` with `fk_division` for the `Project` entity.
