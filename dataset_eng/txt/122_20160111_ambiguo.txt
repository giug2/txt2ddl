So, in practice we have a list of all the various versions of a product, and each one is recognizable by its own unique identification code. Then, separately, there is a catalog of all the accessories that can be added; these also have their own code to avoid confusion, as well as a name that makes us understand what it is. The crucial point is how these two things go together: any one version can mount a lot of different accessories, and, vice versa, the same accessory can be compatible with many versions. The interesting thing is that the price of an accessory is not fixed, but depends precisely on this combination: the cost of a certain optional only comes out when you associate it with a specific version, so it is a fact that links the two things.
