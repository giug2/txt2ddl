This system was designed to map and manage the complex web of responsibilities and collaborations within a large organization, precisely delineating who works where and for whom. At the center of everything we find the fundamental entities that make up our corporate structure: the different OFFICES, i.e. the branches or physical locations in which the company operates, and the OFFICES, which represent the individual departments or functional units present in each location.

The beating heart of the organization is represented by the MANAGERS, whose responsibilities are modeled in a very flexible way. A manager is not simply a person, but a specific role linked to a context. In fact, his position is uniquely defined not only by his personal identifier, but also by the office and location in which he operates. This allows us to manage complex scenarios: a single manager can have responsibilities in multiple offices, and, at the same time, an office can be managed by a team of multiple managers. The system meticulously tracks every single assignment, creating an unbreakable bond between the person, their department and their physical location.

To support this managerial structure, we find the ASSISTANTS. Their role is closely linked to that of the managers they assist. Each assistant is assigned to a specific manager in a specific location, creating a clear line of hierarchical support. This means that multiple assistants can collaborate to support a single manager, but each assistant knows exactly which specific task of his manager to refer to. In this way, the system does not simply list the personnel, but dynamically describes the operational relationships, ensuring that each team member has a clear and well-defined role within the specific location and office in which they are inserted.
