The data model defines the structure for managing music festivals. The central entity is `CircuitoFestival`, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, which has a one-to-many relationship with `EdizioneFestival`. Each `FestivalEdition` is uniquely determined by a composite primary key that includes `fk_circuit`, `StartDate` and `Year`. Each edition takes place in a specific `City`, identified by a `Code` (PK), establishing a many-to-one relationship between `Festival Edition` and `City`. An edition includes multiple performances, modeled by the `Performance` entity (with `Code` as PK, `StageName` and `Time`), in a one-to-many relationship. Participation in exhibitions is managed through the associative entity `ArtistaInCartellone`, identified by a `Code` (PK). There is a many-to-many relationship between `Performance` and `ArtistaInBillboard`, indicating that a performance can include multiple artists and an artist can perform multiple times. Artists are represented by two entities: `Artist` (PK `Code`, `ArtName`) and `Band` (PK `Code`, `Name`). A single `Artist` can have multiple appearances on the bill (1:N relationship with `ArtistaInBillboard`), and multiple artists on the bill can belong to a single `Band` (N:1 relationship). Finally, the `Band` entity is associated with a source `City` via a many-to-one relationship.
