So, basically there is this thing about museums, each with its own unique code and name. Inside each museum it is possible to create routes, itineraries, which have their own acronym and a specific name, but obviously each itinerary is linked to a single museum, it cannot exist on its own. These itineraries are composed of a series of works of art; each work is a thing in itself, identified by a code, and can be included in several different itineraries, just as an itinerary is made up of many works. The particular thing is that when a work is part of a certain path, it is assigned an order number, just to understand the sequence. Furthermore, guided tours can be organized for each itinerary. Each visit has its own code, name, time and duration, and is always connected to a specific itinerary, even if an itinerary can have multiple associated visits. Finally, these visits do not happen when they happen: there is a connection with the days of the week. The same guided tour may be available on multiple days, and on a given day there may be several guided tours. When you combine a visit and a specific day, you also keep track of the number of attendees, probably to know how many people are there.
