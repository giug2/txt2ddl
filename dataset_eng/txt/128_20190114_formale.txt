The data model describes the structure of an academic information system. The `University` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and characterized by `Name` and `Foundation`, is located in a specific `City`, establishing an N:1 cardinality relationship. The `City` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Country`. The `Professor` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Last Name` and `First Name`, is associated with a single `University` via a direct N:1 relationship. To historicize professional relationships, the associative entity `Afferenza` is used, which connects a professor to a university with a certain qualification in a time interval. The `Afference` entity has a primary key composed of `Start Date` and `fk_professor` and includes the `End Date` attribute. 1:N relations from the entities `Professor`, `University` and `Qualification` converge on this entity. The latter, which defines the academic roles, is identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. Consequently, a professor can have multiple affiliations over time, each university can host several affiliations and a qualification can be assigned in multiple contexts of affiliation.
