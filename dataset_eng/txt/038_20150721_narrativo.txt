This system was designed to completely and efficiently manage the supply of a service, placing the relationship with the customer and the territorial organization at the center of attention. The heart of our information universe is represented by the Person, the end customer, who is uniquely identified through his Fiscal Code and for whom we store essential personal data such as surname, first name and date of birth. Each person can be the owner of one or more Users, which represent the service provided at a specific point. Each user is distinguished by a unique number and is linked to a supply address. This link allows us to know that a single customer can have multiple active contracts, for example for the main house and a second home, while each single user can always be traced back to a single person. The life of each user is marked by a precise economic cycle: for each active service, the system periodically generates numerous bills, each identified by a unique code, with a specific issue date and an amount to be paid. At the same time, for the same user, all Payments made over time are recorded; also in this case, each transaction is an event in itself, with its own identification code, a date and an amount paid. To ensure optimal management in the area, the users are organized according to a hierarchical geographical structure. Each user belongs to a District, defined by a unique Prefix and a descriptive Name. In turn, the districts are not isolated but are grouped into larger areas, the Provinces, each identified by an acronym and a name. In this way, a province can contain several districts, but each district belongs to a single province, creating a clear and structured map of the distribution of services across the national territory.
