This system was designed to precisely orchestrate and monitor a complex shipping and logistics network, offering a complete view ranging from strategic planning to tracking individual deliveries. Its beating heart is the organization of the territory, divided into Regions, each identified by a unique code, which in turn contain several Provinces, recognizable by their acronym. It is within these provinces that our Warehouses are located, the real operational nodes of the network, each with a specific code and physical address.

Transport operations are entrusted to different couriers, each registered with its own company name. Each courier is responsible for managing one or more Routes, i.e. the predefined routes that connect a departure warehouse to an arrival one. Each route is not just a simple connection, but is also characterized by the type of vehicle best suited to travel it, be it an agile van or a large truck.

Planning is fundamental: for each route a Planned Service is defined, which represents the weekly operating calendar. This service establishes which days of the week, such as Monday or Tuesday, the route is active, specifying the expected departure and arrival time. This is a real roadmap that defines the regular rhythm of our operations.

When planning translates into reality, we enter the domain of Actual Shipping. Each time a scheduled service runs on a specific date, it is recorded as a one-time shipment. For this shipment, we not only associate the exact vehicle that carried out the transport, identified by its license plate, but we also record the actual departure and arrival times. This allows us to compare planning with execution, monitor performance and guarantee the efficiency and punctuality of the entire logistics system, from the vehicle's departure from the warehouse to its arrival at its destination.
