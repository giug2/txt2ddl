So, basically we have a system to track customers and what they buy. Each customer is registered with his own personal code that distinguishes him from all the others, and then obviously we have his name and surname. When a customer makes a purchase, an order is created, which has its own unique identification number and the date on which it was made. Clearly, a customer can place multiple orders over time, but each order belongs only to that customer.

Inside each order, then, there are the individual things purchased, the so-called order lines. An order can be made up of lots of different lines. Each row is identified by its progressive number within the same order, so to know which row we are talking about you always have to look at both the order number and the row number. For each product in a row, we write down a description of what it is, the price, the expected delivery date and also which supplier the goods come from.
