The data model describes the management of sports teams, cities and players. The `NATION` entity is identified by `country_id` (PK) and has a `name` attribute. Each nation has multiple `CITY` entities associated with it through a one-to-many relationship; each `CITY`, identified by `city_id` (PK) and a `name`, belongs to only one nation. The `TEAM` entity, identified by `team_ID` (PK) and described by `NAME` and `FOUNDATION_DATE`, is based in a single `CITY`, establishing a one-to-many relationship between the two. The `PLAYER` entity, with primary key `player_id` and `name` and `surname` attributes, is associated with a `YOUTH_SOCIATION` of origin, identified by `youth_society_id` (PK) and a `name`; the relationship is one-to-many, indicating that a club trains multiple players. To link teams to players in a given period, the `ROSA_SEASON` entity is defined by a primary key composed of `SEASON` and `fk_team`, which implements the one-to-many relationship with the `TEAM` entity. Finally, a many-to-many relationship exists between `SEASON_SQUAD` and `PLAYER`, meaning that a seasonal squad is made up of multiple players and a player can play in multiple seasonal squads over time.
