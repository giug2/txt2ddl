The data model is composed of four main entities: MANAGER, OFFICE, ASSISTANT and HEADQUARTERS. The SEDE entity is identified by a primary key (PK) called `sede_id`. The OFFICE entity is defined by a composite primary key, formed by the `office_id` and `fk_sede` attributes, where `fk_sede` also acts as a foreign key (FK) towards the HEADQUARTERS entity. This structure establishes a many-to-one (N:1) identifying relationship between OFFICE and HEADQUARTERS, implying that an office is also uniquely determined by the headquarters it belongs to. The MANAGER and ASSISTANT entities are identified by the primary keys `manager_id` and `assistant_id` respectively. The model also includes a ternary relationship with many-to-many-to-many (N:N:N) cardinality that associates the MANAGER, ASSISTANT, and OFFICE entities, indicating that a manager can collaborate with multiple assistants in multiple offices and vice versa.
