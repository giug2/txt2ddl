The data model describes an academic information structure, divided into five main entities: Department, City, Nation, Student and Degree Course. The Department entity, uniquely identified by a Code (PK), is characterized by attributes such as Name, Logo Color and Foundation Year. The geographical entities City and Country are defined respectively by a Code (PK) and a Name. The Student entity is identified by the Student Number (PK) and includes personal information such as Surname, Name, BirthDate and an academic data such as Grade Point Average. The CorsoDiLaurea entity is defined by a Code (PK) and a Name. The relationships between the entities are defined as follows: each Department is located in a specific City (relation N:1), and each City belongs to only one Nation (relation N:1). A Department is associated with multiple Students (1:N relationship). Each Student, in turn, is associated with a City and enrolled in a single Degree Course, establishing in both cases N:1 type relationships.
