The data model describes a healthcare management system. The `Patient` entity is identified by a primary key `patient_id` and contains the personal data attributes `name` and `surname`. The `UnitaOperativa` entity is defined by a primary key `operational_unit_id` and the `unit_name` attribute. The `Doctor` entity is identified by a primary key composed of `doctor_id` and `fk_operating_unit`; this last attribute also acts as a foreign key towards `UnitaOperativa`, establishing a many-to-one (N:1) type relationship in which each doctor is associated with a single operational unit. The `Treatment` entity is identified by a primary key composed of `treatment_id` and `patient_fk`, with `patient_fk` representing a foreign key to the `Patient` entity, thus implementing a one-to-many (1:N) relationship whereby a patient can receive multiple treatments. The model also predicts a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between `Doctor` and `Patient`, indicating that a doctor can follow multiple patients and a patient can be followed by multiple doctors. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship is defined between `Doctor` and `Treatment`, meaning that a doctor can be responsible for multiple treatments and a treatment can be supervised by multiple doctors.
