So, in this system we have a few things going on. There are the managers, each with their own identification code, and then there are their assistants, who also have a code to recognize them. All this staff works within departments, which are like offices, and obviously each department also has its own number. These departments, in turn, are not scattered randomly, but are located within a headquarters, a building in short, which has its own ID to avoid confusion. The rule is that a location can host a lot of different departments, but a department is fixed in one location, it cannot be in two places. Inside each department, then, you will find quite a few people: there are several managers who report to that single department and the same thing goes for assistants, there can be many of them. Things become more flexible when we look at how they collaborate with each other: a manager can have the help of multiple assistants, and an assistant can easily lend a hand to multiple different managers. In short, there isn't an exclusive one-to-one bond between them, it's a bit of an intertwining.
