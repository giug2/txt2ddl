Basically, we have a system that tracks a little bit of everything. There are the students, each with their own personal code to avoid confusion and, obviously, their full name. Same thing for teachers, they also have a unique identifier and name. Then there are the courses; to understand which course it is, its code is not enough, you also need to know who the professor is who teaches it, because that pair of information makes it unique. In short, each course is linked to a single teacher, but a professor can easily deal with multiple subjects. This gets a little complicated because, as you might imagine, a lot of students can take the same course, and a student can enroll in as many courses as they want. Finally, there are the classrooms, each with its own number to recognize it. The lessons of a course are not always held in the same place, they can move to various rooms, and a single room is used for a lot of different courses.
