This system was designed to map and manage the complex operational life of an organization, putting its two most precious resources at the center: the people and the initiatives they carry out. At the heart of the system we find the entity of **Employees**, who represent the company's human capital. Each employee is uniquely registered through his **Serial number**, a personal identifier that distinguishes him from all the others. In addition to this, for each person we know the **Surname**, the **Name**, the **Employment Date**, which tells us their history within the company, and their **Main Role**, i.e. their official and general qualification. Alongside people, there are **Projects**, which constitute strategic and operational activities. Each project is unequivocally identified by a **Project Code**, ensuring that each initiative is traceable and well defined.

The true essence of this model emerges in the way it connects these two realities. The relationship between employees and projects is dynamic and flexible: a single project can require the skills of many employees to complete, and, at the same time, a single employee can lend his or her talents to multiple projects at the same time. To manage this complexity, the system doesn't just say who works where, it also specifies *how* they contribute. In fact, when an employee is assigned to a project, his **RoleInProject** is defined, which describes his specific function in that context. This means that an employee with a Primary Role of "Senior Developer" could, for example, act as "Lead Developer" in one project and as "Mentor" in another, providing a detailed and precise view of the actual organization of work and responsibilities within each team.
