So, in practice the system manages a few branches scattered around, each identified by its own code and located in a certain city. In each branch, obviously, there is a fleet of cars; each vehicle is assigned to one and only one branch and has a whole series of associated information, such as the model, license plate, segment to which it belongs, number of seats, year and engine type.

Cars, then, can have different types of setup, you know, those special configurations, and obviously the same setup can be available on multiple vehicles. The same logic applies to promotions: a car can be included in several different offers, and a single promotion can be applied to a lot of vehicles.

The most complicated part is the pricing. There is something called the Rental Fee, which is essentially the daily amount you pay. This rate is not fixed, but is closely linked to the type of vehicle you choose. But not only that, the price also depends on the time of year, such as whether it is high season or any weekday. And finally, even additional services, those extras that you can ask for, such as navigator, have their own cost which influences the final rate. In short, each rate is a kind of combination of these three things: car, period and extra services.
