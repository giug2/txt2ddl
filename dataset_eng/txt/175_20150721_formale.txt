The data model defines the structure for managing culinary information, divided into five main entities: Chef, Dish, Menu, Dish Type and Recipe. The `Chef` entity is uniquely identified by `chef_id`. The `Menu` entity is identified by `menu_id`, while `Dish Type` and `Recipe` are identified by `dish_type_id` and `recipe_id` respectively. The `Plate` entity is characterized by a primary key composed of the `fk_menu` and `fk_tipologia_piatto` attributes, which also act as foreign keys. The defined relationships are as follows: A one-to-many relationship exists between `Chef` and `Plate`, associating each dish with an individual chef. There is a one-to-many relationship between `Menu` and `Dish`, indicating that a menu contains multiple dishes. Similarly, a one-to-many relationship links `DishType` to `Dish`, classifying each dish under a specific type. Finally, the `DishType` entity has a one-to-many relationship with `Recipe`, associating each recipe with a specific type.
