The data model defines the organizational and teaching structure of an academic institution. The `Department` entity, uniquely identified by `Name` (PK) and managed by a `Director`, offers multiple degree programs. The `CorsoDiLaurea` entity is identified by a composite primary key made up of `Code` and `Name` and includes a `Description`. Each degree course belongs to a single department and takes place in a specific `Room`, which is identified by a `Code` (PK) and characterized by a `Name` and a `Seat Capacity`. The system also manages the `Teacher` entity, identified by the `Serial number` (PK) and described by `Name` and `Surname`. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Department` and `Teacher`, qualified by the `Subject` attribute to specify the discipline taught by a teacher in a given department. The degree courses are made up of teaching modules. The `ModuloDidattico` entity, identified by the `Code` (PK) and with a `Title`, is associated with a single `Author`, in turn defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. The relationship between `ModuloDidattico` and `CorsoDiLaurea` is many-to-many, indicating that a module can belong to multiple courses and a course is composed of multiple modules. Finally, the `ExamSession` entity is defined by a composite primary key that includes the `Date` and a foreign key `fk_course`, establishing a one-to-many relationship in which each degree course can have multiple exam sessions.
