This system was designed to tell the complete story of a patient's journey within a healthcare network, precisely managing every administrative and financial aspect. At the center of everything is the **Patient**, uniquely identified by his **Fiscal Code** and described by his personal data as **Surname**, **Name** and **BirthDate**. Every time a patient requires hospital care, a **Admission** is recorded, a unique event marked by its own **Admission_ID** and associated with a specific **Department** of hospitalization. The system ensures that each individual hospitalization is always linked to a single patient, allowing their medical history to be easily reconstructed over time, since a patient can have multiple hospitalizations during their lifetime.

Each **Admission** takes place within a well-defined structure, the **Hospital**, which is identified by a **Hospital Code** and known by its **Structure Name**. This connection ensures that we always know where a particular hospitalization took place, as a hospital can naturally host countless hospitalizations. At a higher organizational level, each **Hospital** belongs to a specific **ASL** (Local Health Authority), which has its own **Name** and a unique **ASL Code**. This hierarchical structure allows for an overall vision of local healthcare, since a single ASL is responsible for the supervision of multiple hospitals.

The management of a **Hospitalization** inevitably generates economic aspects, which are traced through the issuing of one or more **Invoices**. Each invoice is a separate document, with its own **InvoiceNumber**, an **Amount** to be paid and a **IssueDate**. The system is flexible: a single hospitalization can give rise to multiple invoices, perhaps for different services or issued at different times. Finally, to close the financial cycle, each **Invoice** is linked to the **Payment** made to settle it. Each payment transaction is recorded with a unique **Transaction_ID**, reporting the **AmountPaid** and the **PaymentDate**. This allows you to easily manage even installment or partial payments, since a single invoice can be paid off through multiple payments, ensuring impeccable financial traceability.
