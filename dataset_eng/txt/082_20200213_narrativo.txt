This system is designed to map the entire academic architecture of a university institution, creating a clear, interconnected picture that extends from the broad general divisions down to the individual teaching staff. At the top of this structure we find the **Faculties**, the large thematic areas that group together similar disciplines, each identified by a unique code that represents its essence. Each Faculty, in turn, is divided into a series of specialized **Departments**. A Department cannot exist in isolation, but is always and uniquely associated with a specific Faculty to which it belongs, thus establishing a direct hierarchical link. This means that, while a Faculty can host numerous Departments, each Department responds to only one of them. The very identity of a Department is defined not only by its specific code, but also by the Faculty that hosts it, ensuring that there is no ambiguity within the organization. Going into further detail, we discover that each Department is the beating heart of the training offer, as it manages and organizes one or more **Study Courses**. Similarly to the previous report, a Study Course is conceived and administered by a single Department, which defines its path and objectives. A Department can therefore be responsible for various degree, three-year or master's courses, but each of these training courses has a single departmental "home". The identifier of a course is, in fact, closely linked to that of its Department, underlining this dependence. Finally, the soul of each Study Course is represented by the **Teachers**. In this model, each teacher is primarily affiliated with a single Course of Study. This association defines the main academic context in which the professor works, teaches and carries out research. Although a Course of Study is enriched by the contribution of many teachers, each teacher has a single reference affiliation, creating a strong and clear link with a specific training path. The system uniquely tracks each professor's assignment to his or her course, thus completing a detailed and coherent map that connects each professor to his or her area of ​​expertise, to his or her department and, ultimately, to his or her faculty.
