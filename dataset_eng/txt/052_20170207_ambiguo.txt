So basically we're keeping track of a few things here. There is a list of Customers, each with their own identification code and name, just to know who they are. Then we have the Employees, who work the same: a number to recognize them and their name. In addition to people, we also manage the Projects, which also have a code and a name to avoid confusion, and finally there are the company Departments, such as administration or whatever, also identified by a code and a name. Now, how do these things tie together? Well, an Employee necessarily works in only one Department, he cannot be in two places at the same time. Obviously, a Department is full of people, so it has a lot of Employees within it. Regarding Projects, it's a bit the same story: a Project has its own team, so a group of Employees works on it, but each Employee is dedicated to only one Project at a time. Finally, Clients are the ones who give us the work: a single Client may have commissioned multiple Projects from us over time, but each Project we do is for a single, specific Client. You don't mix things up, every project has its client and that's it.
