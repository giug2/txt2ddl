The data model describes a bibliographic cataloging system structured around six main entities. The `Writer` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK), records the authors' personal information with `Surname` and `Name` attributes. The `Book` entity lists the works, each with a unique `Code` (PK) and a `Title`. Thematic classification is managed by the `Genre` entity, defined by `Genre Abbreviation` (PK) and `Name`. Publishing houses are modeled in the `Publisher` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Name` and `City`, while publishing series are defined in `Series` (`Series Initials` PK and `Name`). The `Edition` entity represents the specific publication and acts as a connection point, characterized by a `Year` and a composite primary key made up of `fk_book` and `fk_publisher`. The relationships define the structure: a many-to-many (N:N) cardinality relationship connects `Writer` and `Book`, indicating that a work can have multiple authors and an author multiple works. A book belongs to only one `Genre` (1:N relationship). A `Book` can have multiple `Editions` (1:N), each `Edition` is published by only one `Publisher` (1:N) and can belong to only one `Series` (1:N).
