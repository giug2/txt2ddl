The data model describes the structure of a university academic system. The `Campus` entity is identified by a `CampusCode` (PK) and has a `CampusName`. The `Department` entity, identified by a `Department Code` (PK) and a `Department Name`, is linked to `Campus` by a one-to-many relationship. Each `Department` manages multiple `CorsoDiLaurea` entities, establishing a one-to-many relationship. A `Degree Course` is identified by a primary key composed of `CourseName` and `fk_department` and includes the `DegreeType` attribute. In turn, a `CorsoDiLaurea` includes multiple `Teaching` entities, each identified by a primary key composed of `CodiceInsegnamento` and `fk_corso_laurea`, and characterized by a number of `CFU`. The `ExamSession` entity, with primary key `SessionID` and `AcademicYear` attribute, is associated with `Teaching` in a relationship such that an exam session can be linked to multiple courses. The `Student` entity is defined by its `Number` (PK) and the `Surname` and `First Name` attributes. Finally, the associative entity `IscriveEsame` connects students and exam sessions; it is identified by a primary key composed of `Registration Data` and `fk_session_exam`. This structure means that a `Student` can make multiple registrations and that multiple registrations can refer to the same `Exam Session`.
