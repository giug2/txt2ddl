The data model describes the structure for managing musical and concert events. The `Piece` entity represents a musical composition, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Title`. Each piece is associated with a `Musician`, defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`, via a many-to-one relationship. The `Concert` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Title` and `Description`, is composed of multiple pieces, establishing a many-to-many relationship with the `Piece` entity. Each concert is performed by a single `Orchestra`, in a many-to-one relationship. The `Orchestra` entity is identified by its `Name` (PK) and has a `Conductor`. The composition of an orchestra is defined by a many-to-many relationship with the `Orchestral` entity, which represents a single musician identified by `Serial Number` (PK), `First Name`, `Last Name` and `Instrument`. A concert can have multiple performances, modeled by the `Replica` entity, in a one-to-many relationship. The `Replica` is identified by a primary key composed of `Data` and `fk_concert`, which refers to the corresponding concert. Finally, each replication takes place in a specific `Room`, identified by `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Capacity`, according to a many-to-one relationship.
