Our system is designed to manage the entire universe of air travel, putting the passenger experience at the center and orchestrating every detail of their trip, from the moment of booking to arrival at their destination. It all starts with our traveller, the **Passenger**, a unique person in our database, identified by a personal code and characterized by his name, surname and telephone number. When a passenger decides to travel, they create a **Booking**, a digital container that represents their entire itinerary and which is sealed with a unique booking code. This link is flexible: a single booking can include multiple passengers, such as a family on vacation, and at the same time, a single passenger can accumulate several bookings over time for his various trips.

Each booking, however, is not a monolithic block, but is made up of one or more **Booking Segments**. A direct journey from one city to another will be represented by a single segment, while a more complex itinerary with one or more stopovers will be divided into multiple segments, each numbered progressively but all belonging exclusively to the same booking. Each segment is full of crucial details: specify the **Class** of travel chosen, which can be for example Economy or Business, described by a code and a clear description. But above all, each segment is inextricably linked to a **Specific Flight**, i.e. the exact plane that the passenger will take on a specific date and at a specific time.

To fully understand flight logistics, we distinguish between the concept of specific flight and that of **Abstract flight**. The abstract flight is the air route in its essence, such as "flight AZ175 from Rome to New York", identified by a flight number and managed by a specific **airline**, in turn defined by a code and a name. This abstract flight defines the route by connecting a departure **airport** to an arrival one, and potentially also intermediate stopovers, each identified by a code, a name and the city it belongs to. Furthermore, each abstract route is associated with a particular type of **Aircraft**, i.e. the aircraft model, with its code and name, designated to serve that route. From this "model" of abstract flight then arise its innumerable concrete manifestations: specific flights. A single abstract flight, in fact, generates multiple specific flights, one for each day and time in which that route is actually operated. In this way, our system masterfully intertwines passenger records with their travel choices, linking them to airline operational planning and airport and aircraft logistics, ensuring that every single seat on every flight is tracked and managed with the utmost precision.
