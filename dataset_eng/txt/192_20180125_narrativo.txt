Imagine building a vast and perfectly organized digital catalogue, where each item has its own precise identity and location. At the center of this universe lies the single **Product**, each unique and unmistakable example thanks to its identification code, the **SKU**, and its **Name** which describes it to the world. But a product does not exist in a vacuum; it needs a context that makes it easy to find and understand.

For this, we have created a clear hierarchical structure. It all starts from a large general **Category**, such as "Electronics" or "Clothing", identified by its own **Code** and **name**. Each category, in turn, is divided into more specific groups, the **Subcategories**. A subcategory, such as "Smartphones" or "Shirts", is defined by its **Code** and **name**, but always carries with it an unbreakable bond with its parent category, ensuring that each subcategory belongs to one and only one main category. It is precisely within these subcategories that our products find their place: a single subcategory can accommodate numerous products, but each product belongs exclusively to one of them, creating an impeccable order.

In parallel with this product classification, we tell the story of the origin of each item. Behind every product there is a **Manufacturer**, the company that created it, identified by a **Code** and a **name**. Often, a manufacturer manages multiple brands, so in our system a single manufacturer can be associated with multiple **Brands**. Each **Brand**, in turn recognized by a **Code** and a **name**, represents the identity with which the product is presented to the public. Finally, the circle closes: each **Product** is linked to a specific **Brand**, allowing us to easily group all the items of a specific brand and know that each brand can offer a vast assortment of products. In this way, the system not only catalogs items, but tells the story of each product, from its corporate origin to its precise position in the market.
