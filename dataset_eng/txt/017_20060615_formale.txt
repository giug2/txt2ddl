The data model describes the management of sports competitions. The `Tournament` entity is identified by a unique `Code` which acts as the primary key and has a `Name`. The `Team` entity is uniquely identified by its `Name`, which constitutes its primary key. The `Player` entity is defined by a composite primary key that includes a foreign key to the team it belongs to (`FK_TEAM`) and the `Shirt` number; it also includes the `Name` and `Date of Birth` attributes. The `Match` entity represents a single match and is identified by a composite primary key made up of the foreign keys of the home and away teams (`FK_HOME_TEAM`, `FK_AWAY_TEAM`) and the `Date` of the event; the `Stadium` attribute completes the entity. The relationships are defined as follows: a one-to-many (1:N) relationship connects `Tournament` and `Team`, indicating that a tournament is composed of multiple teams and each team participates in only one tournament; the association tracks the `POSITION` and `POINTS` of each team. A one-to-many (1:N) relationship exists between `Team` and `Player`, whereby each player belongs to only one team. Each `Match` is linked to two `Team` entities through the home and away team roles; a team's participation in a match is characterized by the `GOALS` attribute, which specifies its score. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship exists between `Game` and `Player` to represent the formation of a game, specifying the `ROLE` of each participating player.
