The data model describes a book cataloging system. The `BOOK` entity, identified by a unique `BOOK_ID`, represents the intellectual work with attributes such as `ORIGINAL_TITLE`, `PUBLICATION_YEAR` and `AUTHOR`. Each book is associated with a `PUBLISHING HOUSE` (identified by `PUBLISHER_ID` and `PUBLISHER_NAME`) and a `LITERARY_GENRE` (identified by `GENRE_ID` and `GENRE_NAME`), establishing one-to-many relationships from these two entities towards `BOOK`. A work can have multiple `EDITIONS`, each uniquely identified by its `ISBN` and described by `TITLE`, `NUMBER_PAGES` and `PRICE`. Each edition is tied to a specific `BOOK` and `FORMAT` (defined by `FORMAT_ID` and `DESCRIPTION`), setting up one-to-many relationships from `BOOK` to `EDITION` and from `FORMAT` to `EDITION`. Finally, each edition can have multiple physical instances, represented by the `COPY_BOOK` entity. Each copy is identified by a `COPY_ID` and has its own attributes such as `CONSERVATION_STATUS` and `PURCHASE_DATE`, maintaining a one-to-many relationship with the specific `EDITION` it belongs to.
