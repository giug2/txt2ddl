The data model describes a corporate organizational structure centered on the Company, Division, Employee, and Project entities. The `Company` entity is identified by a primary key `company_id` and has a `company_name` attribute. The `Division` entity is a weak entity, dependent on `Company`, whose primary key is composed of `division_id` and `fk_company_id`; this foreign key implements an N:1 cardinality relationship, associating each division with a single company. Similarly, the entities `Employee` and `Project` are defined depending on `Department`. The `Employee` entity is identified by a primary key composed of `employee_id` and `fk_division_id`, establishing an N:1 relationship with `Division`, and includes the `firstname` and `lastname` attributes. The `Project` entity is identified by a primary key composed of `project_id` and `fk_division_id`, implementing an N:1 relationship where each project belongs to only one division. Finally, an N:N cardinality relationship is defined between `Employee` and `Project`, meaning that an employee can participate in multiple projects and a project can have multiple employees assigned.
