The data model defines a hierarchical organizational structure composed of four main entities: Company, Division, Team and Employee. The `Company` entity serves as the root of the hierarchy and is uniquely identified by a `company_id` primary key. Each `Division` is associated with a single `Company` in an N:1 cardinality relationship and is identified by a composite primary key, consisting of `division_id` and `fk_company`, where the latter attribute establishes the link with the parent entity. Similarly, the `Team` entity depends on a `Division` via an N:1 relationship, and its primary key is a composition of `team_id` and `fk_division`. At the lowest level of the hierarchy, the `Employee` entity is assigned to a specific `Team`, following an N:1 relationship, and is identified by a composite primary key consisting of `employee_id` and `fk_team`. The key structure highlights a series of identifying relationships in which the existence of an entity depends hierarchically on the superior one.
