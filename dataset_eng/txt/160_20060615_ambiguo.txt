So, basically all the stuff related to music festivals is handled here. There is a list of festivals, each with its own identification code and name, just to clarify. Then, of course, there are the bands, and to recognize them we simply use their name. This is how it works: a lot of bands are connected to each festival and, for each of these, we keep aside the total score it has accumulated and its final position in the rankings.

Every band is made up of people, the musicians. For each musician we know which band he plays in, we have a sort of serial number so as not to confuse him with others, his name and his date of birth. The centerpiece of everything are the performances, which seem to be some kind of challenges that take place on a certain date. For each performance, you always know who the "main" band was and who the "challenging" band was, and also which stage they performed on. A single band can participate in a lot of these performances, either as a protagonist or as a challenger, and for each time they play we keep track of the votes they received.

Finally, there is also a connection between the musicians and the individual evenings. During a specific performance, we know which musicians were present and for each of them we note what role they had on stage at that precise moment. Clearly, a musician can play in a lot of different performances over time.
