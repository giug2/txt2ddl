So, basically here we have a few things that revolve around the world of cooking. There is a list of chefs, each recognizable by their own personal code. Then, obviously, there are the menus, and these too have their own number to avoid confusion. Alongside this, we keep track of the various types of dishes, you know, like starters, first courses, second courses... each category has its own identifier. The highlight is the dish itself, which is a bit of a meeting point for everything else. To exist, a dish must necessarily be part of a certain menu and belong to one of those categories. It's as if each menu could have many dishes, and each category could contain lots of different dishes. Furthermore, it seems that each chef is responsible for preparing multiple dishes, so a chef is connected to quite a few things to eat. Finally, we also keep the recipes somewhere. Each recipe, which has its own unique code, is linked to a specific type of dish. This means that for a category, let's say, 'desserts', there can be a lot of different recipes associated with it.
