Basically, we handle some healthcare-related stuff here. There is a list of Regions, each with its own name and unique identification code. Each region, then, has a certain number of ASLs under it. We are interested in knowing the name, address of these ASLs and, obviously, they have their own unique code so as not to confuse them. Each ASL has a lot of patients. Of these patients, the key thing we need is the social security number, which is their main identifier, but we also record the name. And then, the key part: the swabs. Each patient can take more than one swab over time. For each swab we note the date, the result (whether it went well or badly, in short) and a certain CT code. To be sure of identifying a swab without the possibility of error, its CT code is not enough, but we must also match it to the patient who took it. Therefore, the pair "swab code" and "patient" is the one that gives us certainty.
