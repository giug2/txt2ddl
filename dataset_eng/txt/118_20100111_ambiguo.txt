So, basically the whole world of concerts is managed here. There's something called a Season, defined by a code and a year, which holds a bunch of Concerts together. Each Concert has its identification code, a title and a description, just to understand what it is about. Obviously a concert is held in a hall, which has its own code, a name and a capacity to know how many people are there. An Orchestra is playing, identified by its name, and the same concert can be performed several times, and these Repeats are distinguished by the date on which they take place, but are always linked to the same concert.

Inside a concert, there is a kind of program, a list of performances; each has its own position in the set list and is clearly linked to a specific concert. Each performance, then, refers to a specific piece of music. The Pieces have their own code and title, and are always linked to the Musician who composed them, who is registered with his own code and name.

Finally there are the Artists, those who play, with a serial number, name and surname. The matter becomes a bit particular here: it seems that there is an almost one-to-one link between an Orchestra and an Artist to say which Instrument he plays. But more flexibly, a lot of artists can conduct multiple orchestras and an orchestra can be conducted by multiple people, so it's a relationship that goes both ways.
