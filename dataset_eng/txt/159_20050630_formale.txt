The data model describes a management system for e-learning courses. The `Teacher` entity, identified by `teacher_id` (PK), contains the trainer's personal information, including `Name`, `Surname`, `Date of birth` and `Collection date`. Each teacher can teach multiple courses, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `Course` entity. The `Course` entity, with primary key `course_id`, includes the `Title` and `publication_year` attributes. A course can be delivered in multiple temporal instances, represented by the `Course Session` entity, which is identified by `course_session_id` (PK) and defined by a `Start Date` and an `End Date`. The relationship between `Course` and `Course Session` is one-to-many. The sessions are hosted on an `E-learning Platform`, identified by `platform_id` (PK) and described by `name`, `site_url` and `support_contact`; the relationship between platform and session is also one-to-many. The model also includes the management of tariffs and discounts. The `Fee` entity, with key `fee_id`, is linked in a one-to-many relationship with `E-learning Platform`, `Access Level` (defined by `access_level_id` and `Description`), and `Enrollment Type` (defined by `enrollment_type_id` and `Description`), indicating that a fee is a specific combination of these three elements. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship exists between `E-learning Platform` and the `Discount Type` entity (identified by `discount_type_id` and `description`), allowing a platform to offer various discounts and a discount to be available across multiple platforms.
