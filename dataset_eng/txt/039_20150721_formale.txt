The data model describes the structure of an academic management system based on five entities: `Professor`, `Course`, `Subject`, `Text` and `Course`. The `Professor` entity is uniquely identified by `professor_id` (PK), the `Study Course` entity by `study_course_id` (PK), the `Materia` entity by `matter_id` (PK) and the `Text` entity by `testo_id` (PK). The `Course` entity acts as a link and represents the specific instance of a course; it is identified by a composite primary key, formed by the attributes `fk_corso_studio` and `fk_materia`. The relationships are defined as follows: There is a one-to-many relationship between `Course of Study` and `Course`, indicating that each study path includes multiple courses. Similarly, a one-to-many relationship links `Subject` to `Course`, since a subject can be delivered in distinct courses. A `Professor` can own multiple courses, establishing a one-to-many relationship, while each course is assigned to a single professor. Finally, a `Matter` can have multiple educational `Texts` associated with it, defining a one-to-many relationship between the two entities.
