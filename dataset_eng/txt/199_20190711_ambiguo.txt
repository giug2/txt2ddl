Basically, we have a little bit of stuff going around flights. There is a list of pilots, each with their own code, and then a list of the various aircraft models, also identified by a small number. The central point is a kind of register that puts these two things together, saying which pilot is capable of flying which type of plane; in practice, a pilot can be rated for a lot of different planes, and one model of plane can obviously be flown by many people. Every day, flights are kept track of, each with its own date and ID. A daily flight is linked to a group of these ratings, and the same pilot-aircraft combination can appear on many different flights. To obtain these ratings, you need flight licenses. These licenses are issued by flight schools, and each school can produce several. The somewhat peculiar thing is that every single qualification of a pilot on a certain plane seems to be linked to more than one licence, as if different pieces of paper were needed for each skill.
