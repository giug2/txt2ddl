The data model is structured around four main entities: MANAGER, DEPARTMENT, ASSISTANT and HEADQUARTERS. The HEADQUARTERS entity is identified by a primary key `head_office_id`, while the MANAGER entity is identified by the `id_manager` primary key. The DEPARTMENT entity is characterized by a composite primary key, made up of the `department_id` and `fk_sede` attributes; the latter acts as a foreign key, establishing a many-to-one (N:1) relationship with the LOCATION entity, in which each department is associated with only one location. Similarly, the ASSISTANT entity has a primary key composed of `assistant_id` and `fk_department`, where `fk_department` implements a many-to-one (N:1) relationship with the DEPARTMENT entity, tying each assistant to a single department. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship is defined between the DEPARTMENT and MANAGER entities, which implies that a manager can supervise multiple departments and a department can be managed by multiple managers.
