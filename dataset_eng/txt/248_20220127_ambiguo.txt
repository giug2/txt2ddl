In practice, we have a system that revolves around campuses, each with its own code to recognize it. Inside each campus there are various departments; the interesting thing is that a department does not exist alone, it is always linked to a specific campus, so much so that to identify it you need both its code and that of the campus to which it belongs. Then there are the students, they are also closely linked to a department, and again a student is defined by his number and the department he is in. Clearly, a department may be full of students, but one student is only in one department. Finally, there are the professors, each with their own identifier. They are a little more flexible: a professor can be associated with several different departments and, vice versa, a department can have a lot of professors within it. It's kind of intertwined.
