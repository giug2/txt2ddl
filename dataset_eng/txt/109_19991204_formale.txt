The data model defines the structure for managing bibliographic information, divided into three main entities: `Writer`, `Book` and `Publisher`. The `Writer` entity is uniquely identified by a `Code` (PK) attribute and also includes the `Last Name` and `First Name` attributes. The `Book` entity is characterized by a `Code` as primary key (PK) and by the `Title` and `genre` attributes. The `Publisher` entity is identified by its `Name` (PK) and has the `City` attribute. The entities are interconnected by two relationships. A many-to-many (N:N) relationship connects `Writer` and `Book`, indicating that a writer can be the author of multiple books and, conversely, a book can be written by multiple authors. Additionally, a one-to-many (1:N) relationship exists between `Publisher` and `Book`, establishing that one publisher publishes multiple books, while each book is published by a single publisher. This publication relationship is qualified by the `series` and `year` attributes, which specify its details.
