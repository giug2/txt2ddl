The data model describes a system for managing utilities, billing and territorial records. The `Person` entity is identified by the Fiscal Code (CF), which acts as a primary key (PK), and contains the personal data attributes Surname, name and date of birth. The `User` entity represents a provision service, identified by a `Number` (PK) and localized by an `Address`. The `Bill` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK), an `Issuance Date` and an `Amount`. The `Payment` is tracked via a `Code` (PK), a `Date` and an `Amount`. The territorial structure is defined by the `Province` entity, with `Acronym` primary key and `Name` attribute, and by the `District` entity, with `Prefix` (PK) key and `Name` attribute. The logical interconnections are established by the following relationships: a one-to-many relationship links `Person` to `User`, so each person can be the owner of multiple users. In turn, each `User` is associated with a single `District`, which belongs to a single `Province`, establishing two one-to-many hierarchical relationships. The billing process is managed by a one-to-many relationship between `User` and `Bill`, where each user can have multiple bills associated with it. Finally, a one-to-many relationship between `Bill` and `Payment` models the possibility that a single bill can be paid via multiple payments.
