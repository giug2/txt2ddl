The data model defines the structure for managing events, topics and related sessions. The `Event` entity represents a main event, uniquely identified by `event_id` (PK). The `Topic` entity catalogs the topics covered, with `topic_id` as the primary key. The `Speaker` entity manages speakers, identified by `speaker_id` (PK), while the `Material` entity represents supporting resources, identified by `material_id` (PK). The core of the model is the `Session` entity, which represents a single intervention and is identified by a composite primary key made up of `Room`, `fk_topic` and `fk_event`. There is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between `Event` and `Session` and between `Topic` and `Session`, indicating that each session belongs to only one event and deals with only one topic. There is a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between `Session` and `Material`, so multiple sessions can share the same material. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship connects `Speaker` and `Session`, allowing a speaker to participate in multiple sessions and a session to have multiple speakers.
