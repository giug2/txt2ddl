This system was designed to manage with maximum precision and safety the beating heart of any aviation operation: the assignment of pilots to flights, ensuring that each person at the controls is perfectly qualified for the aircraft they are piloting. At the center of this universe we find the fundamental figures of the PILOT and the AIRCRAFT MODEL, which respectively represent the flight professionals and the machines they operate. However, the mere existence of a pilot and a plane is not enough; it is essential to formally certify the link between the two. This is where the crucial concept of PILOT_AIRCRAFT_RANKING comes into play, a specific record that certifies the exact proficiency of a given pilot on a particular aircraft model. This means that each pilot can hold multiple ratings, one for each type of aircraft they are authorized to command, and, similarly, each aircraft model can be flown by numerous different pilots, each with their own certification.

The validity of these qualifications is supported by a solid training and legal path. Each specific qualification of a pilot on an aircraft is in fact supported by one or more FLIGHT_LICENSES, official documents that certify their qualification. In turn, these licenses do not arise out of nowhere, but are issued by a recognized FLIGHT_SCHOOL, which acts as a guarantor of the training received. In this way, the system draws a clear line of responsibility: each school can issue numerous licenses, but each individual license comes from a single school, creating a verifiable supply chain of trust.

Finally, this entire structure of skills and certifications converges in daily operations, represented by the FLIGHT_DEL_DAY. Each flight, uniquely identified on a specific date, represents a concrete event in which safety is a priority. The system connects each flight to one or more qualified pilots, allowing a complete crew to be formed. At the same time, each pilot, with his specific qualifications, will participate in countless flights during his career. This flexible architecture ensures that for each individual takeoff, the system can verify not only who is on board, but also that each crew member has the correct certification, with valid licenses issued by accredited schools, for the specific aircraft model used on that mission.
