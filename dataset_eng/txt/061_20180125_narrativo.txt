This system was designed to organize and make the world of academic and professional events explorable, providing a clear map of both knowledge and the places where it is shared. At the heart of this structure we find the single Congress, each event is unique, identified by a specific code and characterized by a title that summarizes its central theme. To orient yourself in this universe of knowledge, the topics are organized in a hierarchical and intuitive way. There are large general thematic areas, each defined by its own code and name, which represent the large fields of knowledge. Each Area, in turn, is divided into numerous more specific Subjects, each of which is closely linked to the area to which it belongs. This structure guarantees that each Subject belongs to one and only one Area, creating an orderly and unambiguous classification system. Each Congress is dedicated to a single and precise Subject, thus allowing users to easily identify the events most relevant to their field of specialization. In parallel with the thematic classification, the system also organizes the geographical dimension. The world is divided into Nations, each identified by a unique code, and within each Nation there are multiple Cities. Each City, with its code and name, belongs exclusively to a single Nation, establishing a clear territorial hierarchy. Finally, knowledge and place meet: each Congress takes place in a specific city, which can obviously host numerous events over time. In this way, the entire system allows you to navigate between events starting from a broad country to discover the conferences of a city, or starting from a vast area of ​​interest to delve deeper into a specific subject, always finding the perfect event in the right place.
