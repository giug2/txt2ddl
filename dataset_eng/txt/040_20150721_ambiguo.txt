So, basically here we have some stuff that revolves around the organization of university courses. There is a sort of list of Professors, each with their own personal code so as not to confuse them. Then we have study courses, such as three-year or master's degrees, and these also have their own identifying number. Same thing for the Subjects, such as Analysis or Physics, and for the Texts, which would be the books: each of these has its own unique code. The central point of everything is the actual Courses table. A Course, however, is not a simple thing with its ID and that's it; to understand exactly which one we are talking about, you need to put together three pieces of information: which study course it belongs to, which subject it is and which channel it is held in. In short, it is a mix of these three things that makes it unique. As for connections, it works like this: a Professor can teach a lot of different Courses, but each single Course is assigned to only one professor. Similarly, a course of study is obviously made up of many courses, but a specific course is part of a single course of study. Even for the subjects the situation does not change: a subject can be divided into several courses, perhaps for different channels or addresses, but a course always and only concerns one subject. Finally, there is a link between Subjects and Texts: for a given subject there may be multiple recommended textbooks, but a textbook is always associated with one and only one subject.
