The data model describes an academic management system. The `Student` entity is identified by a `Number` (PK) and contains personal data such as `Name`, `Surname` and `Telephone`. Each student can be associated with multiple study plans and, vice versa, a plan can be followed by multiple students, establishing an N:N relationship between `Student` and `Study Plan`. The `Study Plan` entity is uniquely identified by a `Plan_ID`. A single curriculum is composed of multiple courses, as defined by the `Course in Plan` entity in a 1:N relationship. `Course in the Plan` is a specific row of the plan, identified by a primary key composed of `Row_ID` and `fk_piano_di_studi`. Each `Course in the Plan` is associated with a `Typology` (e.g. mandatory, optional) in an N:1 relationship, where `Typology` is identified by a `Code` (PK). Furthermore, each `Course in the Plan` corresponds to a specific `Course Session` (N:1), which represents the actual lesson time.

The `Teaching` entity represents the abstract academic course and is identified by a key composed of `Teaching_Code` and `fk_department`. Each `Teaching` belongs to a single `Department` (relation N:1), which is identified by a `Code` (PK). A `Teaching` is associated with a `Room` and a `Venue` (both N:1 relationships), where `Room` and `Venue` are identified respectively by their `Code` (PK). The `Course Session` entity defines a specific lesson instance of a `Teaching` (1:N relationship) and is identified by a primary key composed of `Time`, `Week_Day` and `fk_teaching`.
