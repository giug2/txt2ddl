The data model describes the management of musical events and the composition of orchestras. The `Composer` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and characterized by `Surname` and `First Name`, has a one-to-many relationship with the `Piece` entity. Each `Piece`, identified by a `Code` (PK) and with a `Title`, is therefore the work of a single composer. Pieces are performed during a `Concert`, which is defined by a `Code` (PK), `Title` and `Description`. The relationship between `Piece` and `Concerto` is many-to-many, indicating that a piece can be performed in multiple concerts and a concert includes multiple pieces; this association is qualified by the `Position` attribute, which specifies its execution order in the program. Each `Concert` is associated with a single `Orchestra`, a single `Theatre` and can be linked to multiple `Events`. The `Orchestra` entity is identified by the `Name` (PK) and has a `Venue`. The `Theatre` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has a `Name`. An `Event` is uniquely defined by a `Date` (PK) and a `Time`. The internal structure of an orchestra is modeled through the `Composition` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK). This entity acts as a link, associating a `Musician`, an `Instrument` and an `Orchestra` in a one-to-many relationship from each of these three entities to `Composition`. The `Musician` is identified by a `Serial Number` (PK) and has a `Surname` and `Name`, and can be associated with multiple orchestras. The `Tool` is identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Name` and `Type`.
