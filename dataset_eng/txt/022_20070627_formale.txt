The data model describes the structure of a database for managing academic publications. The `Institution` entity represents an organization, uniquely identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Name`, `Address` and `Country`. The `Author` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK), contains the `Surname`, `First Name` and `E-mail` attributes. The `Article` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and includes `Title`, `Subtitle` and `Text`. The `Volume` entity represents an annual collection, identified by the `Year` (PK) and given a `Title`. The `Publication` entity represents a specific issue and is identified by a composite primary key made up of `Number` and `fk_volume`, as well as having the `Data` attribute. The relationships between entities are defined as follows: a many-to-one (N:1) relationship links `Author` to `Institution`, indicating that each author belongs to only one institution. A many-to-many (N:N) relationship exists between `Article` and `Author`, allowing an article to have multiple authors and an author to write multiple articles. A many-to-one (N:1) relationship links `Article` to `Publication`, where each article belongs to only one publication; this association includes the `Page Start` and `Page End` attributes. Finally, a many-to-one (N:1) identifying relationship links each `Publication` to a unique `Volume`.
