So, here we have a system that tracks a few things, mainly conferences. Each `Conference` has its own `Code` which makes it unique and, obviously, a `Title` to understand what it is about. These conferences don't appear out of nowhere, they are always connected to a specific 'City'; it seems that a city can host a lot of conferences, but a conference is held in only one place.

Speaking of `Cities`, each one has its own `name`, but a code is not enough to identify it. No, here to be sure which city we are talking about, we use a combination of its `Code` and a reference to the country, a kind of `fk_nation`. This is because, I imagine, cities with the same name can exist in different places. And in fact, there is the `Nation`, which is simpler: a `Code` to recognize it and a `name`. Logically, a `Nation` contains many `Cities`, but a `City` is only in one country.

Returning to conferences, each one has a main topic, a `Matter`. Each `Conference` is linked to a single `Subject`, but the same `Subject` can be the topic of a lot of different conferences. The `Matter` also has its identifying `Code` and a `name`. Finally, these subjects are not placed randomly, but are grouped into larger categories, the `Areas`. Each `Area` has its own `Code` and `name` and can contain quite a few subjects, while a `Subject` only ever belongs to a specific area. It's a way to bring some order, in short.
