Welcome to the beating heart of a system designed to manage and celebrate the world of classical music, a universe where every detail, from the individual musician to the large orchestra, finds its place. At the center of it all is the `Concert`, a unique event identified by a `Code` and a `Title` and enriched by a `Description` that captures its essence. Each concert program, however, can come to life several times; for this reason, each `Replica` of a concert is recorded with its specific `Data`, inextricably linked to the original program to which it belongs. The magic of a concert takes place in a physical place, the `Hall`, characterized by an identifying `Code`, a `Name` and a maximum `Capacity` of spectators. The same hall can host countless different concerts over time, but each concert is assigned to a specific hall.

Giving voice to the music is the `Orchestra`, recognized by its `Name` and led by a `Conductor`. Each concert is entrusted to the performance of a single orchestra, which can boast a repertoire of numerous concerts performed. An orchestra is a living entity, composed of its `Orchestralists`. Each orchestra member is an individual with a `Name`, `Surname` and a unique `Serial number`. The link between musicians and orchestras is dynamic: a musician can collaborate with multiple orchestras and an orchestra is made up of many musicians. To manage this complexity, the system records the `Instrument` played by a specific orchestra within a specific orchestra.

Finally, the soul of each concert is the repertoire, made up of musical `Pieces`, each with its own `Code` and `Title`. A concert is a symphony of several pieces, and a single piece can be performed in several concerts. Behind every piece there is the genius of a `Musician`, identified by a `Code` and a `Name`. The system recognizes that a musician can compose a vast number of pieces, but each piece of music is attributed to a single, great author, thus completing a detailed and interconnected picture of musical life.
