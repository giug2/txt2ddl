This system was designed to organize and manage the entire educational offering of an academic institution, creating a clear and interconnected information ecosystem for students and administrators. At the center of everything we find the concept of "Course of study", which represents the main degree path, such as Computer Engineering or Classics. Each study course acts as a large container for the different academic disciplines. Within these paths, teaching is divided into "Subjects", which are individual disciplines such as "Mathematical Analysis" or "Latin Literature". To support the learning of each subject, one or more reference "Texts" are associated, ensuring that different textbooks can be recommended for a single discipline. However, the real delivery of teaching occurs through the "Course", which is the specific instance of a subject taught in a given context. A Course is uniquely defined not only by the Subject it deals with and the Study Course to which it belongs, but also by a specific "Channel", allowing you to manage multiple parallel classes for the same teaching. Finally, the "Professor" is there to guide the students on this path. The system establishes that each individual Course, with its channel and its membership, is entrusted to the responsibility of a single Professor, who, in turn, can be responsible for teaching numerous different Courses within the academic offer.
