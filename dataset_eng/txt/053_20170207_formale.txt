The data model defines the structure for managing departments, employees, customers and projects. The `Department` entity represents an organizational unit, uniquely identified by `department_id` (Primary Key) and described by `department_name`. The `Employee` entity is identified by a composite primary key made up of `employee_id` and `fk_department`; the `fk_department` attribute establishes a many-to-one relationship with `Department`, associating each employee with a single department. The `Customer` entity, identified by the primary key `customer_id`, contains the customer's demographic information. The `Project` entity is defined by a composite primary key that includes `project_id` and `customer_fk`, establishing a one-to-many relationship where each project is associated with a single customer. The model also provides two many-to-many relationships: one between `Employee` and `Project`, to track the assignment of staff to various projects, and one between `Employee` and `Customer`, to manage associations between employees and customers.
