The data model describes the structure for managing film productions and screenings. The central entity is `Film`, identified by a unique `Code` (PK) and characterized by `Title` and `Plot`. Each film is produced by a single `Production House` in an N:1 relationship; the `CasaProduzione` entity is identified by the `Name` (PK) and includes the `Headquarters` and `Director` attributes. A movie is composed of multiple `Scene` entities, in an N:N relationship that includes an `ORDER` attribute to specify the sequence. The `Scene` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Title`, and is associated with a single `Screenwriter` (N:1 relationship), in turn identified by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. Distribution is managed via the `Projection` entity, identified by the `Date` (PK) and with a `Time` attribute, which refers to a single film (1:N relationship) and takes place in a single `Cinema` (N:1 relationship). The `Cinema` entity is defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. The involvement of the actors is modeled by the `Participation` entity, with its own `Code` (PK), which connects an `Actor` (identified by `Serial number` PK, `Surname` and `Name`), a `Production House` and a `Role` (identified by `Code` PK and `Name`). An actor and a production company can be involved in multiple participations (1:N cardinality from `Actor` and `Production House` towards `Participation`), while each participation refers to a single role (N:1 cardinality).
