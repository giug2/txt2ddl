So, in practice we have a kind of registry where we keep track of people, each identified by their tax code, and then there is the usual data such as name, surname and when they were born. Each person can have a lot of utilities in their name, such as electricity or gas contracts, but each utility is linked to only one person. These users have their own specific number that distinguishes them and an address. For each user, then, there are all the bills that arrive over time, each with its own code, date and amount. In the same way, again for a single user, we record all the payments that are made, also with their own code, a date and the amount paid. There is also a division by zone: the users are grouped into districts, which have a prefix that identifies them and a name. In turn, these districts are contained within provinces, which are defined by an acronym and their full name. So, to make a long story short, a province contains a few districts, and each district has quite a few users within it.
