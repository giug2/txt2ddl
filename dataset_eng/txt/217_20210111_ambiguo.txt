So, basically we have a bit of Department related stuff; each of these has its own code to recognize it, a name and is located in a certain location. These departments are connected in some way to the Degree Courses, in the sense that a department can have a lot of courses and, at the same time, a course can be one thing that belongs to multiple departments. Each Degree Course, in turn, has its own code and name, nothing strange. Going into more detail, each course is made up of many Teachings, which are the actual subjects; to identify a subject we need both its specific code and the code of the course to which it is linked, and then obviously it has a title. Then there are the Professors, each with their own number, name and surname. They are also linked to departments a bit like courses, i.e. a professor can teach for multiple departments and a department has quite a few associate professors. Finally there is the whole geographical part: we have the Regions, with their code and name, and each region contains a bunch of Cities, each with its ISTAT code and its name. The interesting thing is that cities are connected to professors in two different ways: we know the city of birth of a professor and we also know the city where he resides, so a city can be the place of birth for many professors and, separately, the place of residence for many others.
