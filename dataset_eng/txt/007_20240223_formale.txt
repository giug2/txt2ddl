The data model describes a system for managing press reviews. The REVIEW entity is uniquely identified by the DATE attribute and contains information on TIME and EDITOR. Each review is composed of multiple articles, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the ARTICLE entity, which is defined by the pk_article, title and author attributes. An article can be published in multiple newspapers, formalizing a many-to-many relationship with the NEWSPAPER entity, which is identified by a CODE and has a name. This N:N relationship is enriched by the Pages attribute, which specifies the article's location within a given newspaper. The management of authors and their affiliations is modeled through the entities AUTHOR, ORGANIZATION and the associative entity AUTHOR WITH RELATIONSHIP AND ROLE. The AUTHOR entity is identified by a CODE and a NAME, just as the ORGANIZATION entity is defined by CODE and NAME. The AUTHOR WITH RELATIONSHIP AND ROLE entity connects an author to an entity with a specific ROLE, using a composite primary key made up of fk_ENTERTAINMENT, fk_AUTOR and ROLE. This associative entity has a one-to-many relationship with both the AUTHOR and the ORGANIZATION. Finally, each ARTICLE is connected to one or more instances of AUTHOR WITH RELATIONSHIP AND ROLE, allowing its authors to be specified for each article, each with their own affiliation and contextual role.
