The data model describes an academic management system based on five main entities. The `Faculty` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by a `Name`. The `CorsoDiLaurea` entity is identified by a primary key composed of `Sigla` ​​and `fk_idfacolta`, and has a `Name` attribute; the relationship with `Faculty` is one-to-many (1:N), indicating that each degree course belongs to only one faculty. The `Subject` entity is defined solely by its `Code` (PK) and is linked to `CorsoDiLaurea` by a many-to-many relationship (N:N), which includes the `CourseYear` attribute to specify the year in which a subject is delivered in a given course. The `Lesson` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Title`, `Time` and `Duration`; it has a one-to-many (1:N) relationship with `CorsoDiLaurea`, meaning that each lesson belongs to a single course. Finally, the `DayOfTheWeek` entity, defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`, is connected to `Lesson` via a many-to-many relationship (N:N), enriched by the `ClassroomCapacity` attribute, to specify on which day and with what capacity a certain lesson is held.
