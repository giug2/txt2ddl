Imagine navigating the educational offering of a university, a rich and interconnected ecosystem of knowledge, people and resources. This system is designed to map precisely this academic universe in a clear and flexible way. At the center of everything we find the concept of "Course", which is not simply a subject, but its specific and unique incarnation in a given context. Each Course is in fact uniquely defined by the combination of the Subject it deals with, the Study Course to which it belongs and a specific Channel, which could represent a section or a group of students.

Each major area of ​​knowledge, such as "Mathematics" or "Literature", is represented by a "Subject". This, in turn, can take place in multiple distinct courses, allowing the university to offer the same teaching to different groups or in different paths. The "Course of Study" intervenes to give order to this offer, such as a "Bachelor's Degree in Engineering" or a "Master's Degree in Philosophy", which acts as a large thematic container grouping within it all the Courses relevant to that training path.

Naturally there are the "Professors" to guide the students on this journey. The system recognizes the collaborative and dynamic nature of teaching: a single Course can be entrusted to a team of multiple teachers, and at the same time a Professor can lend his expertise to different Courses, even of different subjects or study paths, creating a network of professional exchange. Finally, to support learning, each Course makes use of teaching materials, represented here by the "Text". From a perspective of efficiency and standardization, a single textbook of particular value can be adopted as a reference by numerous courses, becoming a pillar for training in a specific discipline through different channels and paths. In this way, the system does not simply list data, but tells the story of how knowledge, teachers and resources intertwine to create each student's educational experience.
