So, in practice there is this general structure, a kind of School, which has its own unique identification code. Under each School, there are quite a few different Departments; each department has its own recognition number, but to really understand which one it is, you always need to know which school it belongs to. Inside each Department we find all the people and activities: on one side there are the Professors, on the other the various Study Courses. A professor is identified by his personal code, but this code only makes sense within his department, so he also carries the department ID with him. Exact same logic for a Study Course: it has its own specific code, but is always linked to a single department. The interesting thing is that, even if each professor and each course belongs to only one department, they mix a lot with each other. A professor can be involved in a lot of different courses, and obviously a single course needs many professors to function, so there is a lot of back and forth connections between who is teaching and what is being taught.
