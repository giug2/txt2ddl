The data model defines the structure for managing shipments. The central entity is **Shipping**, uniquely identified by an `ID` attribute. Each shipment is necessarily associated with a **Type** (identified by the primary key `CT`), a **Sender** (identified by `CM`) and a **Destination** (identified by `CDes`), establishing three one-to-many relationships towards the Shipment entity. The **Sender** entity, which has the `CM` and `Name` attributes, is in turn classified by a single **Category** (identified by `CCM`), in a one-to-many relationship. A shipment is made up of one or more packages, described by the **Package** entity. The latter is a weak entity, whose composite primary key is formed by the `NP` attribute and the `fk_shipment` foreign key, which inextricably links it to the relevant shipment. Finally, each parcel is characterized by a specific **Dimension** (identified by `CDim`), according to a one-to-many relationship. The **Type**, **Category**, **Target**, and **Size** entities act as encoding tables, each having a primary key and a `Description` attribute.
