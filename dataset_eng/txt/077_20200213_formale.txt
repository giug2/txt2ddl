The data model defines the structure of an academic system divided into four main entities: School, Department, Professor and Course of Study. The School entity is identified by a primary key `school_id` and contains the `school_name` attribute. Similarly, the Department entity is defined by a primary key `department_id` and the `department_name` attribute. The Study Course entity has a composite primary key, made up of `study_course_id` and `fk_scuola`, and has the `course_name` attribute; the presence of `fk_school` in the key establishes an N:1 relationship with the School entity, associating each course with a single school. The Professor entity also has a composite primary key, consisting of `professor_id`, `fk_school` and `fk_department`, in addition to the `firstname` and `lastname` attributes. The foreign keys `fk_school` and `fk_department` implement N:1 relationships, tying each professor to a specific school and a single department. There is also an N:N relationship between the entities Professor and Course of Study, which indicates that a professor can teach multiple courses and, vice versa, a course can be taught by multiple professors.
