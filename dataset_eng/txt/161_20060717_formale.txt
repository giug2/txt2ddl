The data model defines the structure for a product and logistics management system. The `Courier` entity, identified by `CourierID` (PK), represents shipping carriers and includes the `CompanyName` attribute. The `GeographicArea` entity is defined by `AreaID` (PK) and `AreaName`. The `Warehouse` entity, with primary key `WarehouseID`, has a `LocationName`. The `Supplier` entity is tracked via `SupplierID` (PK) and contains `BusinessName` and `AverageSupplyCost`. The `Product` entity, uniquely identified by `SKU`, includes `ProductName`, `Description`, `SupportContact`, and `Availability`. Linked to it are the `VarianteProdotto` entity, with `CodiceVariante` key and `PrezzoVendita` attribute, and the `Promozione` entity, identified by `IDPromozione` and `NomePromozione`.

The relationships are structured as follows: a many-to-many (N:N) relationship connects `Carrier` and `GeographicArea`, indicating that a carrier can serve multiple areas and an area can be served by multiple carriers. There is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between `Carrier` and `Warehouse`, where each warehouse is managed by only one carrier. Similarly, one-to-many relationships tie `Warehouse` to both `Supplier` and `Product`, implying that each supplier and each product is associated with a single warehouse. A product can have multiple variants, as defined by the one-to-many (1:N) relationship between `Product` and `ProductVariant`. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between `Product` and `Promotion` allows one product to be subject to multiple promotions and one promotion to apply to multiple products.
