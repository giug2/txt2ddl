The data model defines a system for managing products, brands and customers. The **Brand** entity is identified by a `BrandID` (PK) attribute and contains the `BrandName`. The **ProductCategory** entity is identified by a `CategoryCode` (PK) and described by a `CategoryName`. The **Product** entity is defined by a primary key composed of `ProductName` and `fk_brand`, and includes a `Type` attribute. The **Item** entity, representing the physical instance of a product, is uniquely identified by its `Serial` (PK) and has a `Production Data`. The **Customer** entity is identified by `CustomerID` (PK) and contains the personal data attributes `Surname` and `Name`. The structural relationships of the model are as follows: a one-to-many relationship (1:N) connects **Product Category** to **Brand**; a one-to-many (1:N) relationship links **Brand** to **Product**, implemented through the `fk_brand` foreign key in the Product entity; a one-to-many (1:N) relationship exists between **Product** and **Item**; finally, a one-to-many relationship (1:N) associates a **Customer** with multiple **Items**.
