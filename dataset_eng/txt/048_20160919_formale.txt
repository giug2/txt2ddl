The data model describes a hierarchy of information for managing cars. At the highest level are the entities `BRAND` and `MARKET SEGMENT`, both identified by a `CODE` attribute as the primary key (PK) and containing a descriptive attribute, `NAME` and `DESCRIPTION` respectively. The `MODEL` entity is identified by a `CODE` (PK) and has the attributes `NAME`, `LENGTH` and `PLACES`; each model is associated with a single `BRAND` and a single `MARKET SEGMENT` through one-to-many relationships. At a more specific level, the `VERSION` entity is defined by a `CODE` (PK) and the `NAME`, `DISPENSE` and `PRICE` attributes. Each version belongs to a single `MODEL` and has only one `POWER TYPE`, the latter defined in the entity of the same name via `CODE` (PK) and `DESCRIPTION`. Finally, the `CAR` entity represents the physical instance of a vehicle, uniquely identified by the `CHASSIS` (PK) and characterized by `COLOR` and `DATE`. Each car corresponds to one and only one `VERSION`, establishing a one-to-many relationship between `VERSION` and `CAR`.
