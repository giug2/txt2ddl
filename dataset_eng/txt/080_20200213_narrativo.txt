This system was designed to map the organizational architecture of an academic institution, offering a clear and structured view of its fundamental components. At the top of our structure we find the Faculty, the main entity that brings together broad and homogeneous disciplinary areas. Each Faculty is unique, unequivocally identified by its own code, the faculty_id. Going down one level, each Faculty is divided into one or more Departments, which represent the nerve centers of research and teaching. The relationship between these two entities is very close: a Department cannot exist except as part of a specific Faculty. In fact, its identity is defined by a composite key that includes not only its unique code, department_id, but also the identifier of the Faculty to which it belongs, fk_facolta, ensuring that each Department is always and only associated with a single Faculty. The training offer comes to life within each Department. We therefore find the Study Courses, each of which is managed and organized by a single reference Department. Also in this case, the link is indissoluble: a Study Course is identified by a combination of its code, id_corso_studio, and the code of the Department that provides it, fk_department. At the same time, the beating heart of each Department is its academic body, represented by the Professors. Each Teacher is affiliated with only one Department, and his identification in the system depends precisely on this affiliation, being defined by a key that links his personal identifier, teacher_id, to that of his Department, fk_department. In summary, the model describes a clear hierarchy in which a Faculty can contain many Departments, and each Department, in turn, can host numerous Professors and manage various Study Courses, thus ensuring a logical and coherent organization of the entire university.
