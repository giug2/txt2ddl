This system was designed to offer a clear and complete vision of the structure and management of a large strategic plan, divided into different areas of intervention. At the heart of it all we find the concept of **Mission**, a high-level objective identified by a number and a specific name, which represents the overall strategic vision. To translate this vision into concrete actions, each Mission is broken down into one or more **Lines** of intervention. Each Line, characterized by its own number, name and dedicated Budget, belongs exclusively to a single Mission, thus guaranteeing a clear hierarchy between the strategic objectives and their practical implementation.

The operational foundations of this plan are **Projects**, the tangible initiatives that bring the objectives to fruition. Each Project, identified by a unique code and a name, is directly linked to one and only one Line of intervention, allowing you to precisely trace how each individual action contributes to the overall picture. The governance of these projects is entrusted to the **Ministries**. For each Project, a specific Ministry takes on the role of main **Coordinator**, ensuring guidance and responsibility for the initiative. However, collaboration is fundamental, and for this reason a Project can see the participation of multiple other Ministries, whose roles are defined as **Participants**, creating a network of shared skills and responsibilities.

To ensure financial transparency, the system meticulously tracks the origins of funds. Each Line of intervention can be financed by different **Sources**, and the system records the exact **Amount** that each Source allocates to a specific Line, offering a detailed picture of the financial flows. Finally, to contextualize the plan within a broader framework of directives or guidelines, each Mission can be associated with one or more strategic **Recommendations**. Similarly, a single Recommendation can influence several Missions, showing how the plan's objectives respond to broader needs and directions. In this way, the system not only organizes the activities, but tells their story, from the strategic why to who does what and with what resources.
