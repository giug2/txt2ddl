Welcome to a perfectly organized literary universe, a system designed to explore the intricate connections between stories, their authors, and the editions that bring them into our hands. At the heart of this digital world are the **Writers**, central figures identified by a unique **Code**, as well as by their **Name** and **Surname**. They are the ones who give life to **Books**, intellectual works defined by a **Title** and also recognized by their own identifying **Code**. The relationship between creator and creation is dynamic and reflects the collaborative reality of writing: a single book can be born from the pen of multiple writers, just as an author can enrich the world with a vast bibliography of works.

To guide the reader in this vast panorama, each **Book** finds its place within a single and specific literary **Genre**, which is defined by a **Genre Initials** and an explanatory **Name**. This classification allows you to easily navigate between different categories, from fantasy to non-fiction, ensuring that each work has its clearly defined place.

However, a book is not just an abstract idea, but a concrete object that reaches the public. Here the concept of **Edition** comes into play, which represents the specific publication of a book at a given time. A literary work can in fact have multiple editions over the years, each edited by a **Publisher**, a publishing house identified by a **Code**, a **Name** and the **City** in which it is based. The link is clear and direct: a specific edition, characterized by its **Year** of publication, is always the result of the work of a single publisher. The very identity of an edition is uniquely defined by the combination of the book to which it refers and the publisher who produced it. To further enrich the organization, an edition can be included in a prestigious editorial **Collection**, a series recognizable by a **Collection Initials** and a **Name**. While a series can accommodate numerous editions within it, each individual edition can belong to only one series, thus creating an orderly and coherent structure. In this way, the system weaves a complete network of relationships that tells the entire journey of a book, from the writer's initial inspiration to the physical copy we find in the bookstore.
