The data model defines the structure for managing academic courses. The `Professor` entity is uniquely identified by `professor_id`. The entities `Materia`, `Corso di Studio` and `Testo` are identified respectively by the primary keys `id_materia`, `id_corso_di_studio` and `id_testo`. The `Corso` entity acts as a central connecting element, with a composite primary key made up of the `course_id`, `fk_materia`, `fk_professore` and `fk_corsodistudio` attributes. The relationships implemented are the following: a `Professor` can be associated with many `Courses` (1:N) and a `Subject` can be delivered in multiple `Courses` (1:N). Several `Courses` belong to a single `Course of Study` in a many-to-one relationship (N:1). Finally, a many-to-one relationship is defined between `Subject` and `Text`, so multiple subjects can adopt the same text.
