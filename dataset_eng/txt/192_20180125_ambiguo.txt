So, in this system we have a few things. There is the 'Product', which is the actual item, and to recognize it a special code is used, the SKU, which cannot be duplicated; then it also has a name, obviously. Each product must necessarily be within a 'Subcategory' and belong to a 'Brand', only one per type. So, a subcategory or a brand can have a lot of products under them, but not the other way around. The subcategories are a bit strange: to identify them, their code is not enough, you always have to also know which main 'Category' they are connected to, in short, the combination of the two codes is what counts. And as you can imagine, a large category is divided into many subcategories, but one of these is always and only under a single category. The main categories, however, are simple: a code to find them and a name. Then there are the brands, also with an identification code and a name. The thing to know is that each brand is produced by a 'Manufacturer'. A manufacturer may have several brands in its belly, but a brand belongs to only one manufacturer. And in the end, even the producers have their own code and name to know who they are.
