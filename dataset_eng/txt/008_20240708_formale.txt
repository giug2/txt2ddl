The data model describes the management of musical events. The `Piece` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Title`, is associated with a single `Composer` (N:1 relationship), which is defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. A `Piece` can be performed in multiple concerts and a `Concert` can include multiple pieces, establishing an N:N relationship. The `Concert` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has the `Title` and `Description` attributes. Each concert is performed by a single `Orchestra` (relation N:1). The `Orchestra` entity is identified by the `Name` (PK) and contains `Venue` and `Conductor` information. A `Concert` can have multiple performances, represented by the `Event` entity. An `Event` is uniquely defined by a composite primary key made up of `Date` and `fk_concert` and includes the `Time` attribute. Each `Event` takes place in a single `Theatre` (N:1 relationship), an entity defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. The composition of an orchestra is managed through the `Composition` associative entity, which links a `Musician` to an `Orchestra`. The `Musician` entity is identified by `Serial Number` (PK) and has `Last Name` and `First Name`. `Composition` has a composite primary key (`fk_orchestra`, `fk_musician`) and associates each musician within an orchestra with a single `Instrument` (N:1 relationship). The `Tool` entity is defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`.
