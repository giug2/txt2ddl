Basically, we have a few things that revolve around professors and courses. There is the `PROFESSOR`, who has his own identification code, `id_profESSOR`, which distinguishes him from all the others, and then there are his `personal data`, in short his personal information. Each professor may be tied to a lot of different courses, but each single `COURSE` is taught by only one teacher. This `COURSE` is sort of the center of everything, but to identify it you have to put three pieces together: the `channel`, a link to the `COURSE OF STUDY` and another to the `SUBJECT` it concerns. Only with this combination do you know what specific course you are talking about. Then, for convenience, it also has its own `course_name`. Speaking of the `COURSE OF STUDY`, this is easier: it has its own code, `id_corso_di_studio`, to be recognized, and a name, `name_course_of_study`. Of course, a course of study includes quite a few courses. Similarly, there is `MATTER`, which has its own code, `matter_id`, and a name, `matter_name`. A subject, such as 'Mathematical Analysis', can be divided into many different courses. Finally, each subject is connected to some `TEXT`. A `TEXT` is simply a book, with its own code, `text_id`, and a `title`. For a subject there may be many textbooks, but each book is associated with only one subject.
