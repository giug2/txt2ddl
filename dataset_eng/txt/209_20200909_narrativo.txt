This system was designed to map and manage the entire academic and administrative architecture of an educational institution, telling the story of how knowledge is organized, delivered and economically supported. At the heart of everything we find the Departments, real pillars of the organization, each identified by a unique code, a name, a location and a reference contact. Each Department is the cradle from which the various Study Courses are born, complete training courses such as a three-year or master's degree, each with its own identification code, an official name and the year of foundation. To ensure firm guidance, each Course of Study is entrusted to the supervision of a Coordinator, a key figure identified by an ID, name and surname, whose role is defined by a start and end date. A single coordinator may have the responsibility of leading multiple courses of study.

Going into the detail of the teaching offer, the system tracks every single Teaching Provided, which represents the concrete activation of a subject in a specific Academic Year. Each of these courses belongs exclusively to a single Study Course and is managed by a single relevant Department. The teachers who give life to these courses are registered in the system with their student number, name and surname. The relationship between who teaches and what is taught is extremely flexible: a teacher can hold multiple courses, and, vice versa, a single course can be entrusted to the joint expertise of multiple teachers.

In parallel with the teaching structure, the system also precisely manages the financial aspects. The Contribution Fee determines the exact amount that a student must pay for a given course. This amount is not the same for everyone, but varies based on the economic situation, which is categorized into a specific Income Band, defined by a code and a description. Therefore, for the same course provided, there will be multiple contribution quotas, each linked to a different income bracket. Finally, to support the right to education, the system also provides for the management of Exemption Types, i.e. the various forms of economic relief. Each type of exemption has its own code and description. Their application is managed at a departmental level, with the possibility for a department to offer multiple types of exemption and for the same type to be available in different departments, thus creating a complete and interconnected academic ecosystem.
