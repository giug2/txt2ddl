The data model describes the organizational and teaching structure of an academic institution, defining the entities Faculty, Department, Teacher and Course of Study. The `Faculty` entity is uniquely identified by the `faculty_id` (PK) attribute and has a `faculty_name`. Each `Department` refers to a single faculty, establishing an N:1 relationship; it is identified by a primary key composed of `department_id` and `fk_faculty_id`, where the latter acts as a foreign key towards `Faculty`. The `Teacher` entity is associated with a single department through an N:1 relationship, and is identified by a primary key composed of `teacher_id` and `fk_id_department`, which references the `Department` entity. Similarly, the `Course of Study` entity is managed by a single department, with an N:1 relationship implemented via a primary key composed of `studio_course_id` and `fk_department_id`. Finally, the model specifies an N:N (many-to-many) relationship between `Teacher` and `Course of Study`, indicating that a teacher can teach multiple courses and a course can be taught by multiple teachers.
