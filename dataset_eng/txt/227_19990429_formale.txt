The data model defines the structure of a product catalog divided into multiple hierarchical levels. The `Supplier` entity, identified by a unique `ID` that acts as the primary key and described by a `Business Name`, can supply multiple `Brands`. The `Brand` entity is identified by a primary `ID` and a `Name`, and is associated with a single `Supplier`. A `Trademark` may comprise several `Categories` and may be associated with multiple `Items`. The `Category` entity, identified by its `Code` (primary key) and a `Name`, groups together one or more `Product Types`. In turn, the `ProductType` entity has a `Code` as primary key and a descriptive `Name`, and belongs to only one `Category`. Each `Product Type` can be divided into one or more `Models`. The `Model` entity is identified by a primary key composed of the `Variant` and `fk_tipo_tipo` attributes, thus establishing a mandatory link with the `ProductType` entity; it also supports a one-to-many hierarchical relationship on itself. Finally, the `Item` entity represents the concrete product unit and is identified by a primary key composed of `SKU` and `fk_model`, which links it to a single `Model`. The item also has a non-key attribute `Status`.
