The data model describes a hierarchy of information in the automotive sector, divided into five main entities. The `Home` entity represents the producer and is uniquely identified by its `Name` (PK), also containing the `Address` attribute. Each `House` can have multiple `Model` associated, establishing a one-to-many relationship. The `Model` entity is defined by a primary key composed of the `Name` of the model and the foreign key `FK_CASA`, which refers to the manufacturer; also includes the `Year` attribute. Each `Model` is associated with a single `Segment` via a many-to-one relationship. The `Segment` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has a `Description`. Further down the hierarchy, each `Model` can have multiple `Versions`, in a one-to-many relationship. The `Version` entity is identified by a composite primary key made up of its `Name` and the foreign key `FK_MODEL`, and contains the `Price`, `Ports` and `Speed` attributes. Finally, each `Version` is equipped with a unique `Engine`, defined in a many-to-one relationship. The `Engine` entity is characterized by a `Code` (PK) and the `Displacement`, `Cylinders` and `Power` attributes.
