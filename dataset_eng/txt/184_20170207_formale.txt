The data model describes the structure of an order management system. The `Customer` entity contains the customer records, identified by a `Customer Code` (PK) and described by `Name` and `Address`. The `Product` entity manages the product catalogue, where each item is identified by a `ProductCode` (PK) and has a `ProductName`. The `Courier` entity lists the shipping services, with `CodiceCorriere` as the primary key (PK) and `NomeCorriere` as the descriptive attribute. The central `Order` entity, identified by an `Order_ID` (PK) and containing the `Total Amount` attribute, is linked to a single `Customer` and a single `Courier` through one-to-many (1:N) relationships. Each order is composed of one or more detail lines, represented by the `OrderDetail` entity, which is identified by a `Row_ID` (PK) and contains information such as `Price` and `Insertion Date`. Each detail line is uniquely associated with an `Order` and a `Product` through two distinct one-to-many (1:N) relationships, thus establishing a direct connection between the product sold and the order to which it belongs.
