Welcome to the heart of our academic management system, a digital framework designed to map and organize the entire universe of knowledge offered by our institution. Imagine embarking on an educational path: your first choice is a "Course of Study", which represents your main area, such as a degree in Computer Engineering or a master's degree in Art History. Each "Course of Study" is unique and acts as a container for all the disciplines you will have to deal with.

Within this path, your study plan is made up of a series of specific teachings, which we call a "Course". The "Course" is the central element of our system, and is uniquely defined by the combination of two fundamental elements: the "Subject" it deals with and the "Study Course" to which it belongs. A "Subject" is an abstract discipline, such as "Mathematical Analysis" or "Greek Literature". The same "Subject" can be offered in different paths, but the "Course" of "Mathematical Analysis" for Engineering is a distinct entity from that for Physics. Therefore, each "Course of study" is divided into numerous "Courses", and each "Subject" can give rise to different "Courses" depending on the path in which it is inserted.

Guiding you on this learning journey is the "Professor". Every single "Course" is entrusted to the competence and passion of a single "Professor". However, a talented teacher can naturally be responsible for multiple different "Courses", bringing his or her expertise to various classes and disciplines. Finally, to support the study of each "Subject", the system catalogs a series of reference "Texts". Each "Subject" can have one or more recommended textbooks associated with it, providing students with the resources necessary to delve deeper into the topics and prepare themselves as best as possible. In this way, we create a clear and interconnected network that connects the study paths, the subjects taught, the specific courses, the teachers who lead them and the teaching materials that form their foundation.
