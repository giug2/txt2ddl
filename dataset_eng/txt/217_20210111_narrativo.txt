This digital system was designed to map the academic universe of an institution, creating a network of interconnected information that describes the organization, the educational offer and the teaching staff. At the heart of this structure we find the Departments, the pillars of the university, each identified by a unique code, a specific name and a physical location. Each Department contributes to defining the training offer, proposing one or more Degree Courses; the relationship is flexible, since a single Degree Course, like the interdisciplinary ones, can be managed by multiple Departments simultaneously.

The training path comes to life through individual Teachings. Each Degree Course, in turn identified by its own code and name, is divided into a series of specific subjects. Each Course is defined by a title and a subject code, and is strictly and uniquely linked to the Degree Course of which it is part, thus building a coherent and structured study plan.

But a university is not just made up of structures and courses, but of people. Professors are the fulcrum of academic life, each registered with a personal number, name and surname. Their link with the institution is dynamic: a professor can belong to multiple Departments, bringing his expertise to different areas, and, conversely, each Department is enriched by the presence of numerous teachers.

The system is further enriched with geographical data that provides a territorial context. The geographical organization is hierarchical: the territory is divided into Regions, each with its own code and name, which in turn contain numerous Cities. Each City, identified by a unique ISTAT code, belongs to one and only one Region. This geographical structure is intimately linked to the professors' personal data, allowing two distinct pieces of information to be traced: for each professor, the city of birth and the city of residence are recorded. In both cases, a professor is associated with only one city by birth and one by residence, while a single city can be the place of birth or residence for many different professors.
