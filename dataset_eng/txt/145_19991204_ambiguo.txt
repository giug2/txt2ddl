Basically, we have a bunch of lists here for keeping track of stuff in books. There is a list of writers, where for each one we have a code to avoid confusion, and then obviously the name and surname. Then there is the list of books, which also have an identification code and a title. The thing is, a writer may have made a lot of books, but sometimes a book happens to be written by multiple people, so there's a nice tangle of connections in there.

Each book, then, must fall into a genre, you know, like mystery or science fiction. We have a table with the genres, each with its own acronym and full name; a book only fits into one genre, but a genre obviously groups together a lot of books.

The most complicated part concerns the editions. A book may be printed several times, by different publishers or in different years. So, for each edition, we need to know which book it refers to and who published it; indeed, these two pieces of information together are precisely what identifies it. Plus, we note down the year it came out. The publishers, who are the ones who print, are in their own separate list with a code, name and the city where they are located. Sometimes, an edition is also part of a specific series, such as paperback ones. So we have another list for the series, each with its acronym and name, and each edition can be linked to one of these.
