Welcome to the beating heart of a system designed to orchestrate and celebrate the world of classical music. This digital platform was created to tell the story of every single note, from the moment it is conceived to the moment it resonates in a packed room. At the center of our narrative is the `Concert`, the main event, each time unique and identified by a code, with its own evocative `Title` and a `Description` that anticipates its emotions. Each concert fits harmoniously within an annual `Season`, a cycle of events that defines the cultural identity of an entire `Year`.

The magic of a concert comes to life in a specific place: each performance is hosted in a `Room`, characterized by its `Name` and its `Capacity`, a theater of dreams and sounds. Giving voice to the music is the `Orchestra`, a collective entity with its own distinctive `Name`, which interprets the scores for the public. Of course, a concert is not an isolated event; its success can be replicated, and for this reason the system keeps track of each `Replica`, associating each concert with the different dates on which it is performed again.

But what would a concert be without his music? The program for each evening is an ordered sequence of `Execution`. Each of these performances, defined by its `Position` in the playlist, represents the interpretation of a musical `Piece`. Each `Piece`, with its `Title` and unique code, is inextricably linked to the genius of the `Musician` who composed it. In this way, the system weaves a thread that connects the composer to his work and the work to its live performance.

Finally, individual talent: the `Artists`, identified by a `Serial number`, `Name` and `Surname`, are the real protagonists. Their relationship with the orchestra is twofold and fundamental. On the one hand, an artist can conduct several orchestras and, vice versa, an orchestra can be led by multiple conductors over time, in a flexible bond called `Conducts`. On the other hand, in a more intimate and specific relationship, each orchestra is uniquely associated with an artist who `Plays` a certain `Instrument`, perhaps representing a key figure such as the first violin or a reference soloist, creating an exclusive bond between the ensemble and the virtuoso.
