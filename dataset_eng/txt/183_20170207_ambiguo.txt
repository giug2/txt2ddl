So, we basically have a system that tracks a few things in healthcare. There are the patients, each with their own identification code, and then the usual information such as name and surname. Then, of course, there are the doctors; they too have a name, a surname and a code to recognize them, but their identification is also linked to the operational unit in which they work. These operational units are practically departments, such as cardiology or something like that, and each has its own name and code. A lot of doctors may be part of the same department, but a doctor is assigned to only one. Then there is the care part, which we call treatments. Each treatment has its own name, a start date and is directly and uniquely linked to a single patient; a patient, for his part, can undergo various treatments over time. Things get a little complicated when we look at how the various figures intersect: a doctor can follow quite a few different patients, and at the same time a patient can be seen by multiple doctors. Similarly, a doctor may be involved in many treatments, and sometimes a single treatment requires the involvement of multiple specialists. It's all a bit connected.
