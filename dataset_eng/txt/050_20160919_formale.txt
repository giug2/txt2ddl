The data model describes an academic management system based on five entities: PROFESSOR, COURSE, COURSE OF STUDY, SUBJECT and TEXT. The PROFESSOR entity is identified by a primary key (PK) `id_profESSORE` and contains the `personal data` attribute. The COURSE OF STUDY entity is defined by the PK `id_corso_di_studio` and the attribute `nome_corso_di_studio`. Similarly, the MATTER entity has `matter_id` as its PK and the `matter_name` attribute, while the TEXT entity is identified by the `text_id` PK and has a `title`. The COURSE entity acts as a central link and is characterized by a composite primary key made up of the attributes `channel`, `fk_corso_di_studio` and `fk_materia`, as well as a descriptive attribute `name_course`. The relationships between the entities are defined as follows: There is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between PROFESSOR and COURSE, indicating that a professor teaches multiple courses. Another 1:N relationship connects COURSE OF STUDY to COURSE, where each course belongs to a single course of study. Similarly, a 1:N relationship exists between SUBJECT and COURSE, associating each course with a specific subject via the foreign key (FK) `fk_materia`. Finally, a 1:N relationship is established between MATTER and TEXT, so a subject can have multiple reference texts.
