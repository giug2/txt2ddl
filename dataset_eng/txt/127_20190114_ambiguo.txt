So, basically we have some stuff related to universities. Each university has its own identification code to avoid confusion, its name and the year in which it was founded, more or less. These universities, of course, are located somewhere, that is, in a city. Each city is marked with its own code, has a name and we also know in which country it is located. The interesting thing is that a city can host a lot of different universities, but each university, by force of circumstances, is only in one city. Then there are the professors. For each teacher we know a code to identify him, his surname, his first name and when he was born. Clearly, each professor is tied to only one university, he cannot work everywhere at the same time, while a university has quite a few professors on its staff. Finally, there is the question of the qualifications of professors, such as their role or title. Each qualification has its own code and a name to clarify, and each professor has a specific one. Obviously, the same title can be assigned to a lot of different professors.
