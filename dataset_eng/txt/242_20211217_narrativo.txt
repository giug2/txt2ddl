This system was designed to map and manage the entire academic architecture of a university institution, offering a clear and interconnected vision of its educational offer and its internal organization. At the center of this structure is the Department, the main organizational and scientific unit. Each Department is uniquely identified by its own Code and has a Name that describes its area of ​​competence. It is the fulcrum around which academic life and research revolves.

Each Department is responsible for the planning and delivery of one or more training courses, known as Degree Courses. Each Degree Course is closely linked to its department, so much so that its identity is defined not only by its own specific Code, but also by the code of the department that manages it. This relationship ensures that a course only ever belongs to a single department. In addition to your name, which clarifies your study path, the number of available places is also specified for each course, a fundamental piece of data for managing enrolments.

To complete the academic picture, the system also catalogs the Scientific Sectors, which represent the different disciplines and areas of knowledge recognized at an academic level. Each sector is defined by a unique Code and an explanatory Name. The relationship between Departments and Scientific Sectors is particularly flexible and dynamic: a single Department, in fact, can extend its skills and research activities across multiple scientific sectors, reflecting the interdisciplinary nature of modern knowledge. At the same time, the same Scientific Sector can be relevant and studied in different departments, showing how disciplines can be approached from different perspectives within the same university.
