The data model describes a hierarchically structured academic information system. The `STATE` entity, uniquely identified by an `ABBREVIATION` and described by a `NAME`, has a one-to-many relationship with the `REGION` entity. Each `REGION`, in turn identified by a primary `ID` and equipped with a `NAME`, is associated with one or more `UNIVERSITIES`. The `UNIVERSITY` entity has a `UNIVERSITY_CODE` as primary key (PK) and a `NAME`. Each university is divided into one or more departments, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `DEPARTMENT` entity. The latter is identified by a composite primary key, made up of the `NAME` of the department and a foreign key `fk_university` which references the university to which it belongs; it also includes the `CLASSROOM_NUMBER` attribute. Each `DEPARTMENT` can provide multiple courses, as defined by the one-to-many relationship with the `DEGREE COURSE` entity, which is identified by a `COURSE_CODE` (PK) and described by `COURSE_NAME` and `INSTITUTION_YEAR`. Finally, the model defines the `TEACHER` entity, with `REGISTER` as the primary key. There is a one-to-many relationship between `TEACHER` and `DEGREE COURSE`, specifying that each degree course is associated with a single teacher, while a teacher can be responsible for multiple courses.
