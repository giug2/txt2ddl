The data model describes a structure for managing study trips. The `Airport` entity, identified by a unique `Code` and a `Name`, is associated with the `Departure City` entity, also defined by `Code` and `Name`, via a many-to-many (N:N) relationship. Each airport is also connected to multiple `Locations` through a one-to-many (1:N) relationship, where the `Location` entity is characterized by a primary `Code` and a `Name`. In turn, each `Location` can host multiple `Colleges` and offer different `Exams`, as defined by two distinct one-to-many relationships. The `College` entity is specified by `Passcode` (PK), `Name`, `Address`, `Telephone` and `Times`. The `Exam` entity is defined by `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Cost`. Each `College` can propose multiple `Stay` options (1:N relationship), where a stay is identified by a unique `Period` and has an associated `Price`. Finally, there is a many-to-many relationship between the `College` entity and the `Reduction Type` entity, which is defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`, indicating that a college can apply multiple reductions and a reduction can be valid for multiple colleges.
