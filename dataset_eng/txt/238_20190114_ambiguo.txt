So basically we have some music festival stuff. There is actually the festival itself, which we recognize by a unique code, and of which we know the name and year of the edition. Each festival, of course, is held in one place, which is nothing more than a city in a certain country; these places also have their own code to avoid confusion. In the same city, of course, you can organize a lot of different festivals. Then there are the artists, who are the heart of the matter. We know the stage name of each artist, the real one, from when he started playing and, like the others, he has his own personal code. The interesting thing is that each festival has its own nice lineup of artists, so an event has quite a few musicians connected to it. Finally, there's the type of music they make. Each musical genre has its own name and a number to identify it, and a lot of different artists can fall under the same genre, even if basically each artist is associated with a main musical category.
