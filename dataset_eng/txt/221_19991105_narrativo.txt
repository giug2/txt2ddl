This system was designed to orchestrate workflow within an organization, transforming complex goals into concrete, trackable actions. At the center of it all is the concept of **Project**, a large container that represents a specific initiative, such as the development of new software or the launch of a marketing campaign. Each activity carried out revolves around a well-defined project.

To ensure that a project can be managed effectively, it is broken down into smaller, more manageable work units, called **Tasks**. These tasks are not isolated, but always and only belong to a single, specific project, creating a clear work hierarchy. Furthermore, to bring order and standardization, each task must be classified according to a specific typology; in fact, there is a catalog of **TipoTask** that defines the nature of the activity, such as "analysis", "development" or "testing". In this way, a single task typology can be applied to countless different activities, but each single task is characterized by one and only one typology.

Of course, the work is done by people. The system manages a list of **Employees**, the real drivers of project progress. The structure is designed for maximum flexibility: an employee can be assigned to carry out multiple tasks, even on different projects, and at the same time, a single complex task can require the collaboration of multiple employees who combine their skills to complete it.

Finally, to support the execution of activities, the system integrates **Documents** management. A document can represent anything from a technical specification to a creative brief or a final report. The relationship is intelligently designed: it is possible for multiple tasks to refer to a single central document. For example, “write,” “review,” and “approve” tasks could all be linked to the same file, ensuring everyone is always working from the correct, up-to-date information.
