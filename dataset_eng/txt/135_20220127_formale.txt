The data schema defines four entities: DEPARTMENT, HEADQUARTERS, MANAGER and ASSISTANT. The DEPARTMENT and HEADQUARTERS entities are independent, identified respectively by the primary keys `department_id` and `head_office_id`. The MANAGER entity implements a many-to-many relationship, associating a manager with a specific department and location; its identity is defined by a composite primary key made up of `id_manager`, `fk_department` and `fk_headquarters`, where the last two attributes act as foreign keys to the DEPARTMENT and HEADQUARTERS tables. The ASSISTANT entity is linked to the MANAGER by a many-to-one relationship, indicating that multiple assistants can be associated with a single manager. The structure of the ASSISTANT entity highlights a strong dependency, since its primary key is also composite (`assistant_id`, `fk_manager`, `fk_sede`), establishing that an assistant is uniquely identified not only by its ID but also by the manager and the location to which it is assigned, with `fk_manager` and `fk_sede` referencing the composite key of the MANAGER entity.
