The data model defines the structure of an information system for university management. The `Department` entity is identified by a unique `Code` (PK) and described by a `Name`. The `Degree Course` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, and is associated with a single `Department` in a one-to-many relationship. The `Teaching` entity is characterized by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. The `Student` entity, identified by a `Number` (PK) and containing the `Surname` and `Name` attributes, is linked to both a single `Degree Course` and a single `Department` via two distinct one-to-many relationships. Finally, there is a many-to-many relationship between `Student` and `Teaching`, meaning that a student can attend multiple courses and a course can be followed by multiple students; this association is qualified by the `Vote` attribute, which describes the outcome.
