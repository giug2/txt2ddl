So, basically here we're talking about some stuff that revolves around cinema. There are films, each with its own identification code and title, and then there are directors, also with a code and name. The thing works is that a director may have made a lot of films, but a film is always linked to only one director, the main one let's say. These films are then shown at certain events, such as reviews or festivals, which have a title and a description to make it clear what they are about. A film can be presented at several different events, and one event, of course, can have quite a few films programmed. Each event is put on by a production company, which is identified by its name and also has a lead producer. It seems that each event is linked to a single production company, even if one company can organize several. Production companies, among other things, deal with actors; an actor, who has his own code, name, surname and perhaps a role in which he is more famous, can work for different production companies, and a production company obviously has a lot of actors under contract. Returning to events, these materialize in projections. A screening is basically when a film is shown, so it is defined by a precise date and time and the event to which it belongs. An event can have many projections, but a projection is only part of a specific event. Finally, these screenings take place in a cinema room, which has its own code, a name and a certain number of seats. There may be a lot of screenings in one theater, but a single screening is held in one and only one specific theater.
