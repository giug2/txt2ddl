The data model defines four main entities: MAIN_ARTIST, MUSIC_GENRE, SUPPORT_MUSICIAN, and RECORDING_STUDIO. Each entity is uniquely identified by a primary key (PK): pk_main_artist, pk_music_genre, pk_musician_support and pk_studio_recording. The relationships between these entities are structured as follows. There is a one-to-many relationship between LEAD_ARTIST and SUPPORT_MUSICIAN, where a lead artist can be tied to multiple support musicians, while each support musician is tied to only one lead artist. Both the MAIN_ARTIST entity and the SUPPORT_MUSICIAN entity have a many-to-one relationship with the MUSIC_GENRE entity, implying that multiple artists or musicians may belong to the same genre, but each is classified under a single genre. Similarly, both the MAIN_ARTIST and SUPPORT_MUSICIAN entities are linked to the RECORDING_STUDIO entity via a many-to-one relationship, meaning that multiple artists and musicians may use the same recording studio, but each is associated with a single studio.
