This system was designed to capture the essence and dynamics of the competitive world of skiing, tracking every descent, every finish line and the career of every champion. At the center of our narrative is the `Skier`, a unique athlete identified by a personal `Code`, as well as his `Name` and `Surname`. The story of every skier begins in a `City` of origin, also identified by a code and a name, which in turn is inextricably linked to a `Nation` to which they belong, thus creating a geographical and cultural bond for each athlete.

The stage where these athletes compete is the `Race`. Each competition is a unique event, defined by an identifying `Code` and held on a specific `Date`. A race does not happen in a vacuum: it takes place in a specific `Venue`, a ski resort with its own name and code, which is of course located in a specific `Country`. Furthermore, each race is characterized by a `Specialty`, such as slalom or downhill, also cataloged with a code and a name, which defines the rules and the nature of the challenge.

The crucial moment in which the history of the athlete and that of the competition intertwine is represented by `Participation`. This fundamental entity is the heart of the system, as it uniquely links a specific `Skier` to a specific `Race`. It does not simply record that an athlete competed, but documents the final result, i.e. the `Position` obtained. In this way, a single race is made up of many participations, one for each registered athlete, and at the same time a single skier accumulates numerous participations in different races over time. Each entry is also associated with the `Nation` for which the skier competed on that occasion, offering a complete and dynamic view of the world ski circuit and the athletes' careers.
