Imagine a system designed to orchestrate the beating heart of a university: the academic organization. This data structure is designed to precisely map who teaches what, where and with what support, ensuring fluid and transparent management of teaching activities. At the highest level we find the `DEPARTMENTS`, the organizational foundations of the university, where each department is a unique entity, unequivocally identified by its own code. The `COURSES` are born and live within these academic pillars. A course is not an isolated entity; its very identity is inextricably linked to the department to which it belongs, meaning that each course belongs to one and only one department, which can naturally offer a vast range of training paths. The academic landscape is populated by two key figures: the `PROFESSORS` and the `TUTORS`. Each professor, identified by his unique code, represents the main guide of teaching, while each tutor, also recognized by a unique identifier, plays a crucial role in supporting teaching and students. The true dynamism of the system emerges in the way these figures interact with the courses. This is not a simple one-to-one relationship, but a complex network of collaborations: a single course can be entrusted to a team made up of several professors and supported by several tutors. At the same time, a professor can extend his teaching to multiple courses, collaborating with a different group of tutors for each of them. Finally, a tutor can provide support through various courses, assisting different professors as needed. This intertwined relationship allows teaching assignments to be mapped with great flexibility, reflecting the collaborative and multifaceted reality of the academic world.
