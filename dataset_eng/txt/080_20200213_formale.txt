The data model defines a university hierarchical structure based on four main entities. The `Faculty` entity represents the top level and is identified by a `faculty_id` primary key. The `Department` entity is connected to it via an N:1 relationship, in which multiple departments can belong to a single faculty; this binding is implemented by the foreign key `fk_faculty`, which is also part of the composite primary key of `Department`, together with `department_id`. The `Department` entity acts as an aggregation point for the `Teacher` and `Course of Study` entities. Each of these entities establishes an N:1 relationship with the `Department`, indicating that each teacher and each course of study belongs to a single department. The primary key of the `Teacher` entity is composite, made up of `teacher_id` and `fk_department`. Similarly, the primary key of the `Course of Study` entity is a composite key made up of `study_course_id` and `fk_department`.
