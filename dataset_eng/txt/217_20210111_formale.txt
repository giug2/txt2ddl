The data model defines the structure of an information system for the management of academic, personal and geographical data. The `Department` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by the `Name` and `Location` attributes. The `CorsoDiLaurea` entity is identified by a `CodiceCorso` (PK) and a `Name`. The `Teaching` entity is characterized by a primary key composed of `CodiceMateria` and `fk_corso_laurea`, indicating an identifying dependency on the degree course, and has a `Title` attribute. The `Professor` entity is uniquely identified by the `Number` (PK) and includes the personal data attributes `Name` and `Surname`. The geographical component is represented by the entities `Region`, with key `CodiceRegione` (PK) and `Name`, and `City`, with key `CodiceISTAT` (PK) and `Name`. The relationships between the entities are defined as follows: each `Region` is associated with multiple `Cities` according to a one-to-many cardinality. A `Professor` is linked to a `City` through two distinct one-to-many relationships, specifying the place of birth and place of residence respectively. Each `Degree Course` is divided into several `Courses` (1:N). Finally, there are two many-to-many (N:N) relationships: one between `Department` and `Degree Course`, and another between `Department` and `Professor`.
