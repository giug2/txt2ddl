So, basically here we handle some stuff that concerns academic publications. There is the matter of the articles, each with its identification code, a title, perhaps a subtitle and obviously the entire text. These articles are written by authors, and it's flexible: an article can have multiple authors, and one author can write a lot of different articles. For each author we keep the name, surname, email and code to avoid confusion. These authors are usually linked to an institution, such as a university; each author reports to a single institution, but the same institution can have quite a few authors under it. We know the name, address and country of these institutions and they too have a unique code.

Going back to articles, when they are published they end up in a specific publication, which would be like an issue of a magazine. Each item only fits in one of these issues, but one issue can contain many items. When an article is placed there, we also note which page it starts from and where it ends. Each publication has its own number and date, and is identified by that number together with a reference to the volume to which it belongs. Finally, these publications are grouped into larger volumes, which have a title and are identified by the year. Thus, a year X volume may contain several numbered publications.
