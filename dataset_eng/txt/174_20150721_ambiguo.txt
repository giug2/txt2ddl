So basically we have a system that tracks some stuff about college students. First of all, there is the student registry, where for each one we have a unique code, the student number, and then the usual data such as name, surname and when they were born. Each student, during his career, enrolls one or more courses. This registration is a kind of practice in itself, with its own identification code and the reference academic year, and obviously it is always linked to a single student.

The various taxes to be paid are then linked to each registration, each with its own ID, an amount and the date on which it was issued. And, consequently, for the same registration we also keep track of the payments that arrive; each payment has its code, date and amount paid. Therefore, for a single annual registration, there may be different fee deadlines and different payments.

Clearly, an enrollment is always related to a specific degree course, which has its own code and name, such as "Management Engineering". A graduate program, over time, will have lots of enrollments from different students. Finally, these degree courses do not appear out of nowhere, but are part of a larger department, which also has a code and a name. Each department, of course, may offer several different degree programs.
