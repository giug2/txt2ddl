The data model is divided into five entities: PROFESSOR, COURSE OF STUDY, SUBJECT, COURSE and TEXT. The PROFESSOR entity, identified by the primary key `profESSOR_id`, has a one-to-many relationship with the MATTER entity. The MATERIA entity, with primary key `matter_id`, is in turn associated with a single professor. The COURSE OF STUDY entity, identified by `id_corso_di_studio` (PK), connects in a one-to-many relationship with the COURSE entity, indicating that a course of study includes multiple courses. The COURSE entity is an associative table defined by a primary key composed of two foreign keys: `fk_corso_di_studio`, which creates the relationship with COURSE OF STUDY, and `fk_materia`, which establishes a one-to-one relationship with the MATERIAL entity. Finally, the TEXT entity, identified by `text_id` (PK), is linked to COURSE by a one-to-many relationship, so each course can have multiple associated texts.
