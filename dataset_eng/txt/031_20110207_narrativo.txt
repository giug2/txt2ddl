This system was designed to be the beating heart of a commercial activity, managing the entire life cycle of products in an integrated and coherent way, from their supply to the final sale to the customer. At the center of everything we find our catalog of **Products**, each identified by a unique **Code** and characterized by a **Name**, a **Price** of sale and, fundamental for warehouse management, its **Available Quantity**. To keep the inventory tidy and easily navigable, each product belongs to a specific **Category**, which is defined by its own **Code** and a clear **Description**. Although many products can be part of the same category, a single product is always associated with one and only one category. Furthermore, for each item in the catalogue, the system tracks its origin, linking it to a single **Supplier**, identified by a **Code** and a **Name**. Of course, a supplier can supply the company with many different products. Interaction with customers is managed through the **Customer** entity, which collects essential information such as an identifying **Code**, **Name** and **Address** for shipments. When a customer decides to purchase, an **Order** is generated, a crucial document identified by a progressive **Number**, which records the **Date** of the operation and tracks its **Status** of progress. Each order can always be traced back to a single customer, who, over time, can place multiple orders. The real magic happens in the connection between orders and products: a single order can contain a list of several products, and, vice versa, the same product can be included in countless different orders, creating a dynamic network that represents the sales activity as a whole.
