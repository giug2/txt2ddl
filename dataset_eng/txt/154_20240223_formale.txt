The data model describes the structure for managing events and related participations. The `EVENT` entity represents a single event, uniquely identified by the `EVENT_DATE` (PK) and characterized by `START_TIME` and `ORGANIZER`. Each event can contain multiple speeches, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `SPEECH` entity, which describes a single presentation through the `speech_id`, `speech_title` and `key_speaker` attributes. An `INVENTION` can be associated with multiple sessions and a `SESSION` can include multiple interventions, defining a many-to-many relationship qualified by the `time_slot` attribute. The `SESSION` entity is identified by `session_id` (PK) and has a `session_name`. The registry entities are `SPEAKER`, with primary key `speaker_id` and `speaker_name`, and `COMPANY`, with primary key `company_id` and `company_name`. Actual participation is modeled by the `SPEAKER_PARTECIPATION` associative entity, which links a `SPEAKER`, a `SPEAKER`, and a `COMPANY` through three distinct one-to-many relationships. This entity has a composite primary key made up of `fk_company`, `fk_speaker` and `ROLE_EVENT`, uniquely specifying the role of a speaker from a given company for a given speech.
