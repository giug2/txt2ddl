So basically we're keeping track of a few things here. There are the professors, each with their own unique code and name, of course. Then we have the courses, also with their own number to recognize them and a name to understand what they are about. In addition, tutors also appear, who like the others have an identifier and a name. Finally, there are the departments, which are the largest structures, and they too have a code and a name to avoid confusion.

As for connections, it works more or less like this: each course belongs to only one specific department, so a department can have a lot of different courses under it. The most confusing part is that there is a kind of three-way relationship between professors, tutors and courses. It seems that loose combinations can be formed, where a professor, a tutor and a course are put together for some reason. This means that a professor can be associated with multiple courses and multiple tutors, and the same goes for courses and tutors. It's a very flexible system where everyone can be connected to everyone in various ways.
