So, basically we have some stuff floating around universities. There are universities, right? Each with its own name, a code to recognize it and the date it was born. Every university, of course, is located somewhere, in a city. The cities are simple, they have a name, a code and the country in which they are located. Clearly, in a city there can be multiple universities, but each university is in only one place. Then there are the professors, people with name, surname and their own identification code. Now, the interesting part is how professors are linked to universities. It seems that a professor is associated with a university, and many professors can be in the same university. But the real story is told by something called 'Afference'. This 'Afference' is like a register of works. For each professor, we keep track of when he started working in a certain place. To make sure you don't get confused, each line in this log is unique if you look at the professor and the start date together. Then there is also an end date, to say when the relationship ended. So, a professor can have a lot of these 'affections' over time, and the same goes for a university, which obviously has many such connections with various professors. And it's not over. Whenever a professor has an affiliation with a university, he also has a 'Qualification'. This is simply his title, like 'researcher' or 'ordinary', each with its own code and name. Therefore, this afference not only tells us where and when a professor worked, but also in what role. In short, it's a whole circle of professors who work in various universities, in certain cities, with roles that change over time, and we keep track of all this mess.
