Welcome to the beating heart of a system designed to orchestrate the magic of classical music, a world where every note, every musician and every performance has its precise place. Imagine being able to manage the entire universe of an orchestra, from the composition of a program to the opening night. At the center of it all is the `Concert`, a unique musical programme, identified by a code and enriched by a title and description that capture its essence. Each concert program is designed to be performed by a specific `Orchestra`, a glorious entity with a distinctive name and venue, and finds its home in a dedicated `Theatre`, also identified by a code and name.

A concert program, however, is not a single event; can be replicated over multiple evenings. For this reason, each `Concert` can give rise to multiple distinct `Events`, each set at a precise `Date` and `Time`, allowing the public to enjoy the same music at different times. The true soul of a concert lies in the songs performed. A `Concert` is in fact a sequence of musical `Pieces`, each with its own code and title. The relationship is flexible: a single piece can be performed in countless different concerts and, of course, a concert is made up of many pieces. To ensure flawless performance, the system even records the `Position` of each piece within the concert setlist.

Behind every `Piece` there is the genius of a `Composer`. Each composer, registered with a unique code, first and last name, may be the author of a large collection of pieces, but each piece in our world is attributed to a single, great master. But who gives life to these notes? The `Orchestra`, a collection of talented `Musicians`. Each musician, with his serial number, name and surname, is a member of one and only one orchestra. To precisely define each person's role, `Composition` comes into play. This is not a musical work, but the detailed mapping of the orchestra's personnel: each `Composition` record creates an indissoluble bond between a `Musician`, the `Instrument` that plays (in turn defined by a code, a name and a type, such as "strings" or "brass") and the orchestra of which they are part. In this way, the system not only organizes events and programs, but also celebrates the complex and wonderful synergy of people and instruments that transforms written music into an unforgettable experience.
