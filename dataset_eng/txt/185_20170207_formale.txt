The data model describes a system for managing musical information, divided into four main entities: Record Label, Artist, Producer and Album. The `RecordLabel` entity acts as a strong entity, uniquely identified by a primary key `record_label_id` and containing the `label_name` attribute. The entities `Artist` and `Producer` are connected to it through dependency relationships. The `Artist` entity is identified by a composite primary key, made up of `artist_id` and `fk_record_label`, and has an `art_name` attribute; the relationship with `EtichettaDiscografica` is of type N:1, indicating that multiple artists can belong to a single label. Similarly, the `Producer` entity is defined by a composite primary key (`producer_id`, `fk_record_label`) and a `name` attribute, also establishing an N:1 relationship with `RecordLabel`. The `Album` entity is in turn dependent on the `Artist` entity, with a primary key composed of `album_id` and `fk_artist` and an `album_title` attribute; this implements a 1:N relationship, where an artist can have multiple albums, but each album is associated with a single artist. Finally, the model predicts an N:N relationship between the `Producer` and `Album` entities, meaning that an album can be made by multiple producers and, vice versa, a producer can work on multiple albums.
