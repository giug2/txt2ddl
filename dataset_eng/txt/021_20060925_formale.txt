The data model defines the management of art exhibitions, itineraries and visits. The `Show` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Title`, `StartDate` and `EndDate`. The `Framework` entity has a unique `Code` (PK), `Author` and `Year`, while the `Room` entity is defined by `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Dimension`. The associative entity `Exhibition` connects these entities, indicating which painting is displayed in a given exhibition; its primary key is composed of the foreign keys `fk_mostra` and `fk_quadro`. There are one-to-many relationships between `Show` and `Exhibition`, between `Painting` and `Exhibition` and between `Room` and `Exhibition`. The `Route` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK), has a many-to-many relationship with `Exposure`; this relationship is characterized by an `Order Number` attribute to define the sequence of works in the itinerary. A `Visit Type`, identified by a `Code` (PK) and with `Name`, `Time` and `Duration` attributes, is associated with a single `Itinerary` (1:N relationship). Finally, the `Visit` entity represents a specific occurrence of a visit, identified by a primary key composed of `Date` and `fk_type_visit`, and includes the `Number of Participants`. Each `Visit` is linked to a unique `Visit Type` via a one-to-many relationship.
