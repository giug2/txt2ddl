So, in practice we have a kind of archive where there is a list of production companies, each with its own identification code, nothing special. The interesting thing is that each production company can have a lot of franchises under it, such as film sagas, and each franchise is inextricably linked to its parent company, so much so that to understand which franchise we are talking about you also have to know who produced it. Inside each of these franchises, then, we find a bit of everything: obviously there are the various films that are part of them, and here too a film does not exist without its reference franchise. Same story for the actors, who are somehow "filed" within a specific franchise, as if they were part of its team. So, to summarize, a production house churns out multiple franchises, and each franchise has its own stable of actors and its own slate of films. The matter gets a bit confusing because, as is normal, actors don't just make one film, so an actor can appear in a lot of different films and, vice versa, each film has its own cast of people. It's all a big mix.
