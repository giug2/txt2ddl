The data schema describes a system for managing customer orders. The `Customer` entity represents the customer registry and is identified by a primary key (PK) `Customer Code`; also includes the `Last Name` and `First Name` attributes. The `Order` entity stores the header information of an order, with `OrderID` as the primary key and `OrderDate` as the descriptive attribute. The `OrderLine` entity represents the detail of each order and is characterized by a composite primary key, formed by the `fk_order` and `RowNumber` attributes; the other attributes are `ProductDescription`, `DeliveryDate`, `Price` and `Supplier`. There is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between `Customer` and `Order`, meaning that a customer can place multiple orders, but each order is associated with a single customer. An additional one-to-many (1:N) relationship connects `Order` and `OrderLine`, establishing that an order is composed of one or more detail lines, and each line belongs to only one order. This second relationship is implemented explicitly via the foreign key (FK) `fk_order` in the `OrderLine` entity, which references the primary key of `Order`.
