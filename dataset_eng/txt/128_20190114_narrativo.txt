Imagine a system designed to map and tell the stories of academia, an ever-evolving universe of institutions, places and, above all, people. At the center of this system we find the great institutions of knowledge, the **Universities**, each identified by a unique code and characterized by its name and year of foundation, a reference to its historical legacy. Each university is inextricably linked to its territory; in fact, each university is based in one and only one **City**, while a single city, defined by its name, the country it belongs to and an identification code, can be the proud home of multiple university institutions.

The beating heart of this world are the **Professors**, each registered with their own name, surname and a personal code that uniquely distinguishes them. The system recognizes that each professor has a primary link with an institution, associating each of them with a specific reference university. However, the true richness of the system lies in its ability to trace the professional path of each academic over time. This is possible thanks to a central concept called **Afference**, which represents a specific chapter in a teacher's career.

Each affiliation record is a bridge that connects a single professor to a single university for a given period. This link is established by a start date and can be concluded with an end date, thus outlining the duration of an assignment. But an afference doesn't just say *who* worked and *where*; also specify *in which role*. In fact, each affiliation is associated with a precise **Qualification**, such as "Researcher" or "Full Professor", each defined by a name and a code. Thanks to this structure, a professor can accumulate numerous afferences throughout his life, showing his evolution through different qualifications and his passage between various universities. In the same way, a university collects the history of all the influences that have affected it, creating a living archive of its past and present teaching staff. In this way, the system is not a simple list, but a dynamic narrative of academic careers and the institutions that shaped them.
