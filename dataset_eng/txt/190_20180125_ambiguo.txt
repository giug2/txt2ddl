So basically here we have a system for keeping track of a lot of products. Each product has its identification code, the SKU, and obviously a name to understand what it is. These products are always tied to a brand, you know, a specific brand. A brand, which has its own code and name, can have a lot of stuff under it, many different products.

The brands, in turn, are not isolated; they usually belong to a larger industrial group, a sort of parent company. Therefore a group, identified by its code, can own several brands, but each brand belongs to only one group.

To organize everything, the products are also placed in subcategories. Each product fits into one and only one subcategory, and a subcategory can contain a bunch of items. These subcategories, which have a name and their own code, are not placed there at random, but derive from larger categories. In practice, a main category, also with a code and name, is divided into many subcategories. The important thing is that a subcategory is always and only under a main category; to identify it well, in fact, you have to look at both its code and that of the category to which it is linked.
