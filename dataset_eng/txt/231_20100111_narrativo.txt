Imagine a digital ecosystem designed to orchestrate the entire academic experience, from a single teaching concept to complex departmental management. This system is the beating heart of our training offer, guaranteeing clarity and structure to each study path. At the center of everything we find the Course, each identified by a unique Code, with a Title and a Description that illustrate its objectives. Each course is not an isolated entity, but lives within a well-defined context: it belongs to a specific Department, recognized by its Name, and takes place in a specific Academic Year, identified by a Code and the Reference Year. The teaching activities of a course come to life in a physical classroom, characterized by a Code, a Name and a number of available seats. The life of a course is articulated through a series of scheduled lessons, each set at a precise DateTime and unequivocally linked to its course. But the real backbone of a course is its structured program, an ordered sequence of contents that guides the student in his learning. This program is built by assembling different Teaching Modules, real building blocks of knowledge, each with its own Code and Title. The beauty of these modules is their reusability, being able to be included in different course programs. Behind each module there is the mind of an Author, identified by a Code and a Name, who oversaw its creation; although an author can create many modules, each individual module is the brainchild of a single creator. Naturally, it is the teachers who give life to this system, each registered with their number, name and surname. Their connection to the academic organization is twofold and fundamental. On the one hand, each teacher belongs to one or more Departments, creating a network of transversal skills. On the other hand, each Department has a clear guide: a single teacher presides over it, occupying a specific Chair and assuming responsibility for it, in an exclusive relationship that defines academic leadership.
