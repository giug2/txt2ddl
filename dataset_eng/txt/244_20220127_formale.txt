The data model describes the structure of a music database, divided into four main entities: ARTIST, GENRE, ALBUM and SONG. The ARTIST entity is uniquely identified by a primary key `artist_id`. Similarly, the GENRE entity is defined by a `genre_id` primary key. The ALBUM entity has a composite primary key, consisting of `album_id` and `fk_genre`. The latter acts as a foreign key, establishing a many-to-one (N:1) identifying relationship with the GENRE entity, implying that each album belongs to a single genre. The SONG entity is characterized by a composite primary key made up of `song_id` and `fk_album`. The `fk_album` attribute is a foreign key that establishes a many-to-one (N:1) identifying relationship with the ALBUM entity, uniquely associating each song with a single album. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship is defined between ALBUM and ARTIST, indicating that an album can be made by multiple artists and an artist can participate in multiple albums; such a relationship requires an associative entity for its physical implementation.
