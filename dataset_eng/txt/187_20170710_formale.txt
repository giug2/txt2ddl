The data model describes a management system for a film archive. The ARTIST entity, identified by an ARTIST_ID, contains the personal data attributes SURNAME, NAME and BIRTH_DATE. The FILM entity is uniquely defined by its ISAN code and has a TITLE. The CUSTOMER entity, identified by CUSTOMER_ID, is described by SURNAME, NAME and PHONE. Other supporting entities include PRODUCTION_STUDIO, identified by STUDIO_ID and NAME, and DISTRIBUTOR, identified by DISTRIBUTOR_ID and NAME. The PHYSICAL_MEDIA entity represents a physical copy of a film, identified by a COPY_CODE and characterized by an ENTRY_DATE. The relationships between entities are defined as follows: There is a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between ARTIST and FILM, indicating the participation of multiple artists in multiple films. Each artist is associated with a single PRODUCTION STUDIO in a many-to-one relationship (N:1). Similarly, each film is linked to a single DISTRIBUTOR (relationship N:1). A film can exist on multiple physical copies, establishing a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between FILM and PHYSICAL_MEDIA. Finally, a PHYSICAL_SUPPORT can be rented by only one CUSTOMER (relationship N:1); this rental relationship is qualified by the RENTAL_EXPIRY_DATE attribute.
