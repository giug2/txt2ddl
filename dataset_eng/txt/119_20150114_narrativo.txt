This system was designed to tell the story of our democracy, election after election, offering a clear and structured vision of political representation. It all starts from the geography of the vote: the national territory is divided into large areas, the Regions, each identified by a name and a unique code that guarantees their recognisability. In turn, each Region is made up of multiple electoral colleges, which represent the local arenas of political competition and are recognized by a specific number. This structure tells us that many constituencies always belong to one and only one region. The protagonists of this narrative are, on the one hand, the political parties, each with its own name and a code that distinguishes it, and on the other, the People, the citizens who run to represent the population, each registered with their own personal code. The heart of the system beats to the rhythm of elections, a fundamental event that we identify with the Year in which it takes place. It is at this precise moment that a Person can become an Elect, taking on the role of Deputy. Each Deputy is inextricably linked to their territory, being elected in a single and specific College. Naturally, many different deputies can come from the same constituency over the years. Likewise, each Deputy represents a Party, adhering to a single political acronym, while a party, obviously, can count on numerous deputies elected throughout the country. In this way, the system weaves a network of information that allows us to navigate the political composition of the parliament, connecting each elected representative to his party, his constituency of origin and, consequently, his region, year after year.
