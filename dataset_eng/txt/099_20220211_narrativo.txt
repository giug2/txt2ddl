This system was designed to orchestrate the entire visit experience within a cultural panorama, connecting museums, works of art and visitors through thematic itineraries and organized tours. At the center of this universe is the **Museum**, each institution is identified by a unique code and known to the public by its name. Each museum can offer its visitors numerous thematic **Itineraries**, each designed to guide exploration according to a specific thread, such as a historical period or an artistic movement. Each itinerary is defined by its own identifying code and belongs exclusively to a single museum, ensuring that each itinerary is unique in its context.

The heart of each **Itinerary** is the sequence of **Works** of art that compose it. A work, identified by its code, can be a point of interest in several different itineraries; in the same way, a single itinerary is naturally composed of multiple works. To ensure a fluid and coherent experience, the system stores the order number of each work within each specific itinerary, thus defining the exact progression of the visit.

To further enrich the offer, each itinerary can be experienced through one or more **Guided tours**. Each visit is an event in itself, characterized by a code, a descriptive name, a precise starting time and a pre-established duration. While a thematic itinerary can be explored independently, a guided tour represents its structured and accompanied version, inextricably linked to a single and specific itinerary.

Finally, the system manages the scheduling and availability of these experiences. Each **Guided Tour** is scheduled to take place on certain **Days** of the week. Flexibility is maximum: the same guided tour can be available on multiple days, and numerous different guided tours can be offered on a single day. For each visit and day combination, the system tracks the number of participants, allowing for efficient management of reservations and resources.
