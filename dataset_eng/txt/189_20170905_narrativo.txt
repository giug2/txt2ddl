Imagine a system designed to orchestrate the talent and efforts within an organization, a beating digital heart that knows exactly who is doing what and where. This database was created to efficiently and clearly manage the allocation of human resources on various work fronts. At the center of everything we find the concept of **EMPLOYEE**, each person is unique and is unequivocally identified by their **REGISTER NUMBER**. At the same time, the company lives and breathes through its **PROJECTS**, each of which is distinguished by a unique **PROJECT_CODE**.

To define "how" an employee contributes to a project, the **JOB** comes into play. Each job, such as "Senior Analyst" or "Junior Developer", is defined by its own **JOB_CODE** and enriched by a **DESCRIPTION** which clarifies tasks and responsibilities.

The real heart of the system, the element that connects all these pieces, is **ASSIGNMENT**. An assignment represents the concrete act of tying a specific **EMPLOYEE** to a specific **PROJECT**. This union is so fundamental that the combination of the employee number and the project code creates a unique assignment record. Of course, a single employee may be involved in multiple projects throughout his career, and therefore may have numerous assignments. Likewise, a complex project will require input from multiple people, resulting in many different assignments, one for each team member. Finally, each assignment is qualified by a specific task, explaining in what role that employee is contributing to that project. This means that the same job, for example "Project Manager", can be associated with countless different assignments throughout the company, linking various employees to various projects but always under the same umbrella of responsibility. In this way, the system offers a complete and dynamic view not only of the workload, but also of the skills used in each individual company initiative.
