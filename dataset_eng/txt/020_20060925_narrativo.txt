This system was designed to orchestrate the fascinating world of art exhibitions, elegantly managing every detail that transforms a collection of works into a memorable event. The beating heart of every cultural initiative is the **Exhibition**, identified by a unique code, which comes to life through an evocative title and takes place in a precise time frame, defined by a start and end date. Naturally, an exhibition would not exist without its true protagonists: the works. Each **Painting** is a carefully cataloged treasure, distinguished by its own identification code, the author who created it and the year of its creation. The physical space also plays a fundamental role: each **Room** of the museum, with its specific name and dimensions, is ready to welcome the masterpieces, also being identified by a unique code. The true fulcrum of the system, which magically links these elements, is **Exposure**. This entity acts as a precise register that creates an inseparable link between a work, an event and a place. In practice, it documents exactly which specific Painting is placed in a specific Room within a particular Exhibition. Thanks to this structure, a single Exhibition can flexibly host a large number of Paintings, and at the same time, a single Painting can travel over time and be presented in many different Exhibitions, just as a Room can be the setting for countless exhibitions over the years.
