Imagine managing a vibrant and dynamic universe dedicated to the magic of cinema, where every detail, from the creation of a film to its theatrical release, is orchestrated with precision. This system was designed precisely to bring this world to life. At the center of everything we find the **Film**, each film is a unique entity, identified by its specific `Film Code` and characterized by its unmistakable `Title`. Each film, however, is not born from nothing: it is the work of a single and visionary **Director**, who is in turn registered with a personal code and a name. A director may, over the course of his career, direct multiple films, but each film is inextricably linked to the vision of its individual creator.

The life of a film does not end with its creation; it must be celebrated and shown to the public. This is where **Events** come into play, which can be festivals, themed events or special previews. Each event has its own code, a title and a description that tells its essence. The link between films and events is very flexible: a single film can be screened as part of many different events, and an event, such as a large festival, can obviously include a large selection of films in its programme. Behind the organization of many of these events lies a **Production House**, identified by its name and led by a chief producer. Although a production company can sponsor and organize countless events, each event is sponsored by only one of them. Furthermore, production companies maintain collaborative relationships with a vast universe of **Actors**, each with their own identification code, name, surname and a main role that distinguishes them. This relationship is dynamic: a production company works with many actors and an actor can collaborate with several production companies over the course of his career.

The event then materializes in the magical moment of the **Screening**. Each event can have one or more projections, each uniquely defined by the combination of its date and time with the event to which it belongs. This screening does not take place in a vacuum, but in a specific **Cinema Room**, a physical place with its own code, a name and a defined capacity in terms of seats. Naturally, the same room can host countless screenings over time, but every single screening takes place in one and only one room, creating a unique and unrepeatable experience for spectators. In this way, the entire life cycle of a cinematographic work, from its artistic conception to the applause of the audience in a dark room, is traced and managed in a harmonious and complete way.
