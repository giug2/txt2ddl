So, basically, here we have some stuff that revolves around the world of cinema. There are directors, each with his own code, name and surname, and each director is linked to a city, which in turn is within a nation. Both cities and countries have their own name and code to avoid confusion. Then there are the cinemas, also with a name and a code, and each cinema is located in a specific country. Inside a cinema, obviously, there are a lot of screenings. Each screening is an event in itself, with its own code, a date, and belongs to a certain cinematographic genre, such as comedy, drama and so on. Things get interesting with nominations: Think of a nomination as a sheet that connects a director to a specific screening. In fact, to identify it you need the codes of both. On this sheet you also note whether they won a prize or not at the end. A director can accumulate a lot of nominations, and even a single screening can receive more than one. To top it off, each entry is somehow associated with a country, perhaps the country of the festival or something like that.
