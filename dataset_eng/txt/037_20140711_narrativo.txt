This system was designed to orchestrate and catalog the wonderful world of classical music, from the genius of the composer to the roar of applause in the hall. At the center of this universe we find the single musical **Piece**, each masterpiece identified by a unique code and known by its **Title**. Naturally, each piece has an origin, a creator: the **Musician**, also registered with a personal code and his own name, who may have composed countless pieces over the course of his career, while each single piece can be traced back to a single great master.

These compositions come to life within a **Concert**, a cultural event defined by a title and a description that illustrates the theme or program. A concert can be a rich mosaic of different pieces, and, at the same time, a famous piece can be performed in many different concerts over time. To allow the public to enjoy these performances, each concert program can be staged several times; every single performance is a **Replica**, a concrete event that takes place on a specific date and time, inextricably linked to the concert it is bringing to the stage.

Giving voice to the notes is the **Orchestra**, an artistic entity with a prestigious name and a year of foundation that marks its history. It is the orchestra that performs the concerts, and each orchestra can have a very vast repertoire, performing in numerous musical events. The beating heart of every orchestra is its members, the **Orchestrali**. Each of them is a professional identified by a serial number, a name and a surname. The link between a musician and an orchestra is formalized through a **Participation**, which records the membership of an orchestral member to a specific group. And since a musician is inseparable from his traveling companion, participation also specifies which musical **Instrument**, identified by its own code and name, that orchestral player plays within that specific orchestra, thus creating a complete and detailed picture of who, what and how contributes to the magic of each individual performance.
