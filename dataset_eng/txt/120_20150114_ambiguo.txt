So, in this system we have some stuff that revolves around the world of elections. There is something called 'College', which can be recognized by its 'Number'; think of it as a kind of voting precinct. These colleges, in turn, are part of a 'Region', which has its own 'Code' to avoid confusion and, obviously, a 'name'. The rule is simple: many constituencies can be within a single region, but a constituency cannot be divided between multiple regions. Then there are the 'Elections', identified by the 'Year' in which they are held, and for each we know more or less the number of 'voters' involved. The link between constituencies and elections is a bit of an entanglement: a single election involves a lot of constituencies, and at the same time a constituency participates in many elections over time. Finally, we have the 'People' and the 'Chosen', both with their own personal 'Code'. It is immediately clear that a 'Chosen One' is nothing more than a 'Person' who has won, a kind of subcategory, even if it is not explicitly said.
