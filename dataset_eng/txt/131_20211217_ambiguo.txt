So basically we have a bunch of stuff that we call "Missions". Each mission has its identification number, which is the most important thing to avoid confusion, and then a name to understand something. Under each mission, then, there are a lot of "Lines", which are kind of sub-objectives or lines of work. These lines also have their own number, but to know which mission they are part of, they are linked to the number of the main mission; in short, a mission can have many lines, but one line always and only belongs to that mission. For the lines, in addition to the name, we also write down the budget, that is, how much money you can spend on them. Finally, there is another cauldron of information, the "Recommendations", which are a kind of suggestions or directives, each with its own code and name. The peculiar thing is that missions and recommendations are very loosely connected: one mission can follow several recommendations and, vice versa, a single recommendation can be applied to a lot of different missions. It's a big mix, things cross over a lot.
