The data model is structured around four main entities: PROFESSOR, COURSE, TUTOR and DEPARTMENT. Each entity is uniquely identified by a primary key (PK) called `pk_professore`, `pk_corso`, `pk_tutor` and `pk_dipartimento` respectively. The interactions between these entities are defined by specific relationships. There is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between DEPARTMENT and COURSE, where a department may offer numerous courses, but each course belongs to a single department. The system also provides three many-to-many (N:N) relationships. The first links PROFESSOR and COURSE, indicating that a professor can teach multiple courses and a course can be taught by multiple professors. The second N:N relationship is established between PROFESSOR and TUTOR. Finally, a third N:N relationship exists between TUTOR and COURSE, allowing a tutor to be associated with multiple courses and a course to have multiple support tutors.
