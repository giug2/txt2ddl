The data model describes the management of ski competitions. The `Skier` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and contains the `Surname` and `First Name` attributes. The registry entities `City`, `Country`, `Specialty` and `Location` are all identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. The `Race` entity is defined by a primary key composed of `Code`, `fk_specialty` and `fk_venue`, and includes a `Date` attribute. The associative entity `Participation` is identified by a primary key composed of the foreign keys `fk_race` and `fk_sciatore`, and records the final `Position`. Relationships structure the domain: an N:1 relationship links each `Skier` to his or her `City` of origin, and each `City` is linked to a `Nation` via an N:1 relationship. Similarly, each competition `Venue` belongs to a `Country` with an N:1 relationship. The `Race` entity is linked via N:1 relationships to `Specialty` and `Venue`, as indicated by its composite key. The many-to-many relationship between `Skier` and `Race` is resolved by the entity `Participation`, which is in a 1:N relationship with both `Skier` and `Race`. Finally, an N:1 relationship links `Participation` to `Country`, associating each participation with a specific country.
