This system was designed to map and manage the entire academic organization of a complex educational institution, such as a university or large college. At the center of everything we find the **School**, which represents the fundamental macro-structure, such as a faculty or a polytechnic, uniquely identified by its own code and characterized by a specific name. Each School offers a variety of training courses, the **Courses of Study**. Each course, such as "Computer Engineering" or "Classical Literature", is strictly and uniquely linked to its school; in fact, its identity is defined by the combination of its own identification code and that of the School that provides it. This unbreakable bond ensures that each course is unambiguously placed in its correct academic context. At the same time, the organization is divided into **Departments**, specialized entities such as the "Department of Mathematics" or "Political Sciences", each distinguished by its own name and unique code. It is within this organizational fabric that the key figure of the **Professor** is inserted. Each teacher, registered with name and surname, is formally based in a single Department and belongs to a specific School. Your identity in the system is so precise that it is determined by a unique combination of your personal ID, School and Department. However, true academic collaboration and the flexibility of the system emerge in the relationship between teachers and training offerings: a Professor, despite belonging to a department, can lend his teaching in multiple Study Courses, even outside his narrowest scope, enriching the students' experience. At the same time, a single Study Program can make use of the skills of numerous professors from different departments, thus creating an interdisciplinary and dynamic learning environment.
