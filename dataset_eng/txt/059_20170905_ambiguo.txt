So basically we have a system for keeping track of orchestras. Each orchestra is identified by its own code, has a name and a date, like the date of foundation or something like that. Of course, each orchestra is located in a city, and a city can host several orchestras. Cities, in turn, are grouped by country, so a country contains a lot of cities, but a city only fits in one country. Then there are the musicians, each with their own identifier, name and surname, the usual things. It seems that every musician comes from only one conservatory, while a conservatory, obviously, trains a lot of musicians. The slightly trickier part is how we connect musicians to orchestras. There is a kind of intermediate entity, called 'Orchestra Composition', which basically photographs the lineup of an orchestra in a given year. So, an orchestra has several 'compositions' over time, and one of these compositions is made by a lot of musicians. At the same time, a musician can be part of different ensembles, perhaps in different years or with different orchestras. This 'composition' is uniquely defined by the year and orchestra to which it refers.
