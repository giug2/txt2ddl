This information system was designed to map and manage the entire ecosystem of a large retail network, providing a complete view ranging from the corporate structure down to the individual product on the shelf. At the top of this organization we find the main entity, the 'Chain', identified by a unique acronym and a commercial name. Each chain represents the brand under which a multitude of 'Shops' distributed across the territory operates. Each store is in turn a well-defined entity, with its own identification code, a name and a physical address, but its identity is always inextricably linked to the parent chain to which it belongs, establishing a clear hierarchical relationship in which a chain can have many stores, but each store belongs to only one chain.

The beating heart of the commercial activity are the 'Products', each cataloged with a unique code and a descriptive name. To trace the origin of each item, the system links it directly to its 'Producer', which is also registered with a code and name. This relationship tells us that while a single manufacturer may be the source of numerous different items, each marketed product comes from a single, specific manufacturer. The availability of these products in stores is managed flexibly: a store can offer its customers a wide range of different products, and, at the same time, a single successful product can be sold in many stores in the network, creating a dynamic relationship between what is sold and where.

It is the staff who bring each store to life, represented by the 'Employee' entity. Each person who works for the organization is registered with a personal code, name and surname. The system enriches each employee's profile with precise geographical information, thanks to the connection with a list of 'Cities'. In particular, for each employee two distinct but fundamental pieces of information are tracked: their city of 'Birth' and their current city of 'Residence', thus allowing us to have a clear picture of the origin and location of the workforce. Finally, the system models the working relationship between employees and stores in great detail. A store obviously employs more people, but it is also possible for an employee to work in multiple stores. What makes this management particularly effective is the ability to specify the 'Role' that an employee holds within a particular store. In this way, the system not only knows who works where, but also what specific job they perform in that context, such as "manager", "cashier" or "warehouse worker", for each specific job.
