Imagine following a student's journey through our university system, a journey that begins with their identity and branches out through their academic choices and administrative duties. At the center of this universe there is, of course, the **Student**, a unique profile for each individual, unequivocally identified by a code, the **Matriculation**. In addition to this identifier, we store your fundamental personal data: your **Surname**, your **Name** and your **Date of birth**. Each student's academic journey takes shape through one or more enrollments over time; for this reason, each individual student can be linked to multiple **Course Enrollment** records. Each of these registrations represents a chapter of your career, such as a specific year of studies, and is therefore an event in itself, tracked with a unique **Registration_ID** and associated with a specific **Academic Year**.

Enrollment, however, is not an abstract act: the student enrolls in something specific. The **Degree Course** comes into play here, the chosen course of study, defined by a **CourseName** and identified by a unique **CourseCode**. The same degree course, such as Computer Engineering or Classics, naturally welcomes the enrollments of numerous students, which is why each course is linked to multiple **Course Enrollment** records. In turn, the courses do not arise from nothing, but are offered by a larger organizational structure: the **Department**. Each department, identified by a **Department Code** and known by its **Department Name**, is the home of several degree programs, creating a link whereby a single department can offer and manage many different degree programs.

Returning to the student's path, each **Course Registration** also involves administrative obligations, in particular economic ones. For each annual registration, the system generates one or more **Fees**, each with its own **Fee_ID** which makes it unique, an **Amount** to be paid and a **Emission Date**. This allows you to manage, for example, the different installments of university fees for a single academic year. Finally, to close the administrative loop, the system keeps track of the payments made. Each **Payment** is registered with its own unique **Payment_ID**, reporting the **Amount** paid and the **Payment Date**. Since a student can pay the fees with multiple payments, each **Course Registration** can be associated with numerous payments, thus allowing the financial status of each student to be precisely monitored for each year of their university career.
