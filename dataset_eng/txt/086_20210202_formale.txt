The data model describes the structure for the management of healthcare swabs, patients and the related territorial administrative entities. The `Region` entity represents the primary administrative unit, uniquely identified by `RegionID` (PK) and characterized by a `Name`. Each region has multiple local health authorities associated with it, defining a one-to-many relationship. The `ASL` entity represents the local health authority and is identified by a primary key composed of `CodASL` and `fk_regione`; the `fk_region` attribute acts as a foreign key to the `Region` entity. Additional ASL attributes are `Name` and `Address`. Each ASL manages a set of patients, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `Patient` entity, which is identified by the `CF` (PK) and described by the `Name` attribute. Finally, the `Swab` entity records the details of each test performed and is identified by a `CT` (PK) code, also including `Data` and `Result`. Each patient can be associated with multiple swabs, while each swab refers to a single patient, setting up a one-to-many relationship between `Patient` and `Swab`.
