So, basically we keep track of some stuff: there are the artists, their works of art and the museums. For each artist, we have the usual things, name and surname, and then his own code to avoid confusion. Same thing for works of art: each one has its own title, a style, and it also has its own identifying number. For museums, however, all we need is the name, which is what distinguishes them, and the city where they are located. The interesting part is how they relate to each other. An artist may have created a lot of different works, but it is also possible that a single work is the result of the work of multiple artists, such as a collaboration. So the thing is quite flexible. Then, the works of art end up somewhere, right? Well, a museum can have an infinite number of works in its collection, but each work, obviously, is in only one museum. When a work is placed in a museum, we also note down a couple of additional pieces of information, such as which room it is in and the year in which the museum acquired it.
