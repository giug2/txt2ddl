So basically, we have some stuff to keep track of. There are the managers, each with their own code, and then the departments, also identified in some way. The thing is that a department can have quite a few managers in it, but a manager is only in that department, he cannot split himself. Then, each manager has a group of assistants under him, who are also registered with their own identifier. So, a boss has more than one assistant helping him, but an assistant only reports to that specific boss. As a result, even assistants end up being linked to a single department, that of their manager. Finally, there is the question of headquarters, the physical places where people work. It works a bit like departments: in one location you can find a lot of managers and a lot of assistants, but both managers and assistants are assigned to one and only one location.
