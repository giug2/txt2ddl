The data model describes the management of works of art, artists and museums. The `Artist` entity is defined by the `Last Name` and `First Name` attributes and is uniquely identified by an `Artist ID`, which serves as the primary key. The `WorkArte` entity represents a work of art, characterized by a `Title` and a `Style`, with `WorkID` as the primary key. The `Museum` entity is identified by its `Name`, which is the primary key, and includes a `City` attribute for location. There is a relationship with many-to-many (N:N) cardinality between `Artist` and `WorkArte`, indicating that an artist can create multiple works and a work can be the result of the collaboration of multiple artists. Furthermore, a one-to-many (1:N) relationship is defined between `Museo` and `OperaArte`, specifying that a museum can display many works, but each work is placed in a single museum. This relationship is qualified by the `Exhibition Hall` and `Acquisition Year` attributes, which describe the specific placement of each work within the museum it belongs to.
