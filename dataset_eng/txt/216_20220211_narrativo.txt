This system was designed to orchestrate the complex academic life of a university, offering a clear and structured vision of the teaching organisation, from its broadest subdivision down to the detail of the individual timetable. At the center of everything we find the Faculties, the large thematic areas of the university, each uniquely identified by a Code and described by a specific Name, such as "Engineering" or "Letters and Philosophy". Each Faculty acts as a container for the various study paths it offers.

In fact, within each Faculty, a multiplicity of Degree Courses comes to life. Each course is defined by its own identifying code, which together with the code of the faculty to which it belongs guarantees its uniqueness, and by a complete name that describes the path, for example "Computer Engineering". The link is clear: a single Faculty can host numerous Degree Courses, but each Degree Course belongs exclusively to a single Faculty, creating an orderly and intuitive hierarchy.

The educational path of each Degree Course is defined by the Subjects that compose it, each represented by a unique Code. The relationship between courses and subjects is very flexible: the same degree course obviously includes many subjects, but a single subject, such as "Mathematical Analysis I", can be fundamental for several different degree courses. To manage this complexity, when a subject is associated with a course, the Course Year in which it must be followed is also specified, thus organizing the study plan in a progressive manner.

The teaching of these subjects takes place through the Lessons. Each Lesson, identified by a Code, has a Title, a Start Time and a Duration, and belongs specifically to a single Degree Course. Finally, to build the weekly timetable, the system associates each lesson with the days of the week in which it takes place. A Day of the Week is simply defined by a Code and its Name, such as "Monday". Because a lesson can repeat over multiple days and many different lessons can occur on a single day, this relationship is extremely dynamic. Whenever a lesson is scheduled on a particular day, the designated Room Capacity is also recorded, which is crucial information to ensure there is room for all students.
