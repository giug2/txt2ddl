The data model defines six main entities: Chain, Store, Product, City, Employee, and Manufacturer. The Chain entity is identified by an Abbreviation (PK) and has a Name. Each Store is identified by a primary key composed of a Code and the foreign key fk_catena, establishing a one-to-many identifying relationship with Catena; it also has the Name and Address attributes. The Product entity, identified by a Code (PK) and with a Name attribute, is associated with a single Manufacturer through a many-to-one relationship; the Manufacturer is in turn defined by a Code (PK) and a Name. There is a many-to-many relationship between Store and Product to represent the assortment. The Employee entity, identified by a Code (PK) and characterized by Name and Surname, is connected to the Store by a many-to-many relationship that includes the descriptive attribute Role. Finally, the City entity, with Code (PK) and Name, establishes two distinct one-to-many relationships towards Employee to specify the city of birth and city of residence respectively.
