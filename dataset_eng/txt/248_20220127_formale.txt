The data model describes an academic system structured around four main entities: CAMPUS, DEPARTMENT, STUDENT and PROFESSOR. The CAMPUS entity is identified by a primary key (PK) `campus_id`. The DEPARTMENT entity is linked to the campus via a many-to-one (N:1) relationship and is identified by a composite primary key, made up of `department_id` and `fk_campus`, where the latter acts as a foreign key (FK) towards the CAMPUS entity. The STUDENT entity is identified by a composite primary key that includes `student_id` and `fk_department`; the `fk_department` attribute establishes a many-to-one (N:1) relationship with the DEPARTMENT entity, associating each student with a single department. Finally, the PROFESSOR entity, identified by its primary key `professor_id`, is linked to the DEPARTMENT entity through a many-to-many (N:N) relationship, indicating that a professor can belong to multiple departments and a department can include multiple professors.
