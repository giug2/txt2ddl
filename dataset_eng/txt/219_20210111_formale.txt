The data model describes the organizational structure of a university system. The `University` entity is uniquely identified by an `Acronym` (PK) and has a `FullName`. Each university aggregates one or more `Department` entities, which are identified by a primary key composed of a specific `Code` and the foreign key `fk_university`, establishing a one-to-many identifying relationship; each department is also described by a `Name` and a `Location`. The educational offer is represented by the entities `CorsoDiLaurea`, with primary key `Codice` and attribute `Name`, and `Facolta`, with primary key `Codice` and attribute `Name`. There is a many-to-one relationship between these, where multiple degree courses belong to a single faculty. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Department` and `Degree Course`, indicating that a department can provide multiple courses and a course can be managed by multiple departments. The teaching staff is modeled by the `Teacher` entity, identified by the `Number` (PK) and with `Name` and `Surname` attributes. The relationship between teachers and departments is many-to-many, allowing a teacher to belong to multiple departments and vice versa; this association is qualified by the `Framework` attribute. The `Municipality` registry entity, identified by `CodiceIstat` (PK) and `Name`, is linked to `Teacher` through two distinct one-to-many relationships to record both the municipality of `Birth` and that of `Residence` of each teacher.
