This system was designed to map and manage the intricate academic organization of a modern university, distributed across multiple locations. At the center of it all we find the CAMPUS entity, which represents the different physical locations of the institution, each with its own unique identity. Within these campuses, knowledge is organized into DEPARTMENTS, each dedicated to a specific area of ​​knowledge and uniquely identified. The beating heart of academic life is represented by the figure of the PROFESSOR, whose identity in the system is particularly rich and contextual: a professor is not defined only by his personal code, but by the unique combination of this code, the department to which he is affiliated and the campus in which he works. This structure reflects the dynamic nature of his role, allowing a single professor to collaborate and teach in multiple departments, and, vice versa, for each department to draw on the skills of numerous teachers, creating a dense network of professional interconnections. To support teaching and research activities, the figure of the TUTOR comes into play. Each tutor is directly and unequivocally associated with a single reference professor within a specific campus. This relationship clarifies that, while a professor may supervise and guide a group of multiple tutors, each tutor reports to and reports to only one mentor teacher, solidifying a clear bond of supervision and collaboration.
