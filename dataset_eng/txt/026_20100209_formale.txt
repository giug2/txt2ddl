The data model describes the management of customers and current accounts. The `Customer` entity is uniquely identified by the Tax Code (`C.F.`), which acts as the primary key (PK), and includes the `Surname`, `Name` and `BirthDate` attributes. The `Checking Account` entity is identified by its primary key `Number`. There is a many-to-many (N:N) cardinality relationship between the entities `Customer` and `Current Account`, meaning that a customer can hold multiple accounts and an account can be jointly held by multiple customers. This relationship has a specific attribute, `address`, which qualifies the link between a specific customer and a specific account.
