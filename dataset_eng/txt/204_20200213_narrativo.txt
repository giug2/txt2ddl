This system was designed to orchestrate the entire software development lifecycle, offering a clear, integrated view of every component, from people to code. At the heart of it all is the `Project`, the fundamental unit of our work, identified by a unique `ProjectID` and described by a `ProjectName` and a detailed `Description` that defines its objectives.

No project comes to life on its own; for this reason, each initiative is entrusted to a specific `Team`. Each team, recognizable by its `Team Name`, adopts a work `Methodology` that guides its operations. The strength of a team lies in its members: the `Team Members`. Each member is a real person, with a `First Name` and a `Last Name`, and a `MemberID` that identifies him or her in the system. The structure is clear: a team is made up of many members, but each member belongs to a single team, ensuring cohesion and accountability.

The practical work on a project is divided into logical units called `Module`. Each module, identified by a `ModuleID` and a `Name`, represents a functionality or component of the software. The true flexibility of the system emerges here: a single module can be so versatile that it can be reused in multiple projects, and, conversely, a project is typically composed of a multitude of modules. To manage this complexity, when a module is associated with a project, it is assigned a specific `Priority`, to guide the development phases. Behind each `Module` there is the talent of a `Developer`, a professional figure identified by `Developer_ID`, `Name` and `Surname`, who is responsible for its creation. A developer can put his signature on many different modules, becoming a point of reference for those specific parts of the code.

To fully understand the dynamics of a team, it is essential to know who has which skills. The system manages this aspect through the `Technology`, each with its own `TechnologyID`, a `Name` and a `Category` to which it belongs. The association between people, teams and skills occurs through the `Role Assignment`, a register that uniquely links a `Team Member` to a specific `Technology` within its `Team`, creating a clear map of technical responsibilities and specializations.

Finally, the development cycle is completed with the delivery of the product. Each `Project` has its own `Release` history, each marked by a `Release Date` and a `Version` number, which testify to its evolution over time. All the valuable source code generated during the process is securely held in a `Repository`, identified by a `Repository ID` and an access `URL`, ensuring that each project has its own digital, tidy and traceable home.
