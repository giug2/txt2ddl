The data schema describes a product and supply management system. The central entity is `Product`, identified by a composite primary key consisting of `SKU` and `fk_subcategory`. There is a one-to-many recursive relationship on the `Product` entity itself. Products are classified hierarchically: the `Category` entity, identified by `Code` and with a `Name` attribute, has a one-to-many relationship with the `Subcategory` entity. The latter is defined by a composite primary key (`Code`, `fk_category`) and a `Name` attribute, and is linked to `Category` via a many-to-one relationship. The template specifies a one-to-many relationship between `Product` and `Subcategory`. Each `Product` is associated with one or more lots through a one-to-many relationship with the `Lot` entity, which is identified by `LotCode` and has the `Quantity` and `ProductionData` attributes. The entity `Supplier`, with primary key `PartitaIVA` and attribute `RagioneSociale`, is linked to several entities: a many-to-one relationship links `Lot` to `Supplier`, indicating that multiple lots can come from a single supplier. Additionally, a one-to-many relationship connects `Category` to `Supplier`. Finally, the `Warehouse` entity, identified by `Code` and having an `Address`, is linked to `Supplier` via a many-to-one relationship, whereby multiple suppliers can be associated with a single warehouse.
