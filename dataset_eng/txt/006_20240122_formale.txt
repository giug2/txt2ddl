The data model describes the organizational structure of a business entity through four main entities: DEPARTMENT, MANAGER, ASSISTANT and HEADQUARTERS. Each entity is identified by a unique primary key, respectively pk_department, pk_manager, pk_assistant and pk_sede. The relationships between these entities define the operational structure. There is a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between MANAGER and DEPARTMENT, indicating that a manager can be associated with multiple departments and, vice versa, a department can have multiple managers. Similarly, an N:N relationship connects MANAGER and ASSISTANT, allowing a manager to have multiple assistants and an assistant to support multiple managers. The relationship between ASSISTANT and DEPARTMENT is also many-to-many, establishing that an assistant can be assigned to multiple departments and a department can make use of multiple assistants. Finally, a one-to-many (1:N) relationship connects LOCATION to DEPARTMENT, specifying that each department is located in one and only one location, while one location can host multiple departments.
