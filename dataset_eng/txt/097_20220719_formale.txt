The data model describes the structure for managing musical events and their orchestras. The `Piece` entity, identified by a `Code` (primary key) and a `Title`, is associated with a single `Composer` in a many-to-one relationship; the `Composer` is defined by `Code` (PK), `Last Name` and `First Name`. A piece can be performed in multiple concerts and, conversely, a concert can include multiple pieces, establishing a many-to-many relationship. The `Concert` is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has a `Title` and a `Description`. Each concert is associated with multiple `Events` (reruns), where an event is uniquely identified by its `Date` (PK) and has a `Time`. A concert takes place in a single `Theatre`, identified by `Code` (PK) and `Name`, and is performed by a single `Orchestra`, identified by its `Name` (PK) and `Venue`. The `Musician` entity is defined by `Serial Number` (PK), `Last Name` and `First Name`. The relationship between `Orchestra` and `Musician` is many-to-one, indicating that multiple orchestras can be associated with a single musician. The `Composition` entity, with `Code` as the primary key, models the internal structure of the orchestra, associating an orchestra and a musician with a specific `Instrument` in one-to-many and many-to-one relationships respectively. The `Tool` is described by `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Type`. Finally, `Composition` presents a one-to-many recursive relationship, indicating a possible internal hierarchical structure.
