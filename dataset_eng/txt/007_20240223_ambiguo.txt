Basically, we have a system that keeps track of some written stuff. There is a kind of collection, the REVIEW, which is identified by a precise DATE and of which we also know the TIME and who took care of it, the EDITOR. Each review of these, of course, contains a lot of ARTICLES.

An ARTICLE is defined by a title, an author and also has its own identification code, even if it doesn't seem to be the most important thing. The interesting thing is that the same article can end up in several different NEWSPAPERS, and a newspaper can publish many articles. When an article is published in a newspaper, we also note which page it is on. Each NEWSPAPER, in turn, has its own code and a name to recognize it.

Then there's the whole part about who writes. We have a list of AUTHORS, each with their name and a code, and a list of INSTITUTIONS, such as companies or universities, also with a code and a name. The meeting point is a kind of form, called "AUTHOR WITH RELATIONSHIP AND ROLE", which in practice links an author to an institution and specifies what ROLE he has in that context. This combination of author, institution and role is what makes it unique. A single author can have a lot of these connections, perhaps he works for multiple organizations with different roles, and obviously an organization has many people within it with various roles.

In the end, it all ties back to the ARTICLE: every piece of writing is linked to one or more of these "cards". This means that for an article we know exactly who worked on it, for which institution and in what specific role at that moment.
