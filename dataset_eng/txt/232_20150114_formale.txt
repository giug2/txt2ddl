The data model describes an academic management system structured around five main entities and their relationships. The entities defined are: `University`, identified by a `University Code` (PK) and a `name` attribute; `Department`, whose primary key is `DepartmentCode`; `CorsoDiLaurea`, defined by a `CodiceCorso` (PK) and a `name`; `Student`, uniquely identified by the `Number` (PK); and `Enrolment`, characterized by the `Academic Year` attribute which acts as the primary key. The interconnections are established by a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between `Department` and `University`, indicating that multiple departments belong to a single university. The concept of `Enrolled Student`, which acts as an associative entity, is at the center of two relationships: one of a many-to-one type (N:1) towards `Department`, associating each enrollment with a single department, and another, again many-to-one (N:1), towards `CorsoDiLaurea`, whereby each enrollment refers to a single degree course.
