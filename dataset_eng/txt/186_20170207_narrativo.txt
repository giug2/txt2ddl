This system was designed to orchestrate the beating heart of university academic life, modeling the fundamental interactions between students, teachers and their educational path. At the center of this world we find the Student, whose identity is not defined only by a unique matriculation number, but is intrinsically linked to the Degree Course in which he is enrolled; this strong connection ensures that each student is always included in his specific study path. Each Degree Course, in turn, represents a unique academic container, capable of accommodating numerous students, but each individual student belongs exclusively to a single course, following a well-defined path. On the other side of the desk is the Teacher, identified by his own unique code, a key figure and point of reference for the subjects within his competence. The essence of his role is manifested in the exams he manages: each exam is in fact inseparably associated with the teacher who presides over it, creating a bond such that a professor can be responsible for many exams, but each specific exam is headed by a single teacher. The crucial moment in which these paths meet is the relationship between Students and Exams. This is a dynamic and flexible interaction: over the course of his or her career, a student will take and pass numerous different exams, and, at the same time, each individual exam will be taken by a multitude of students. This network of relationships allows us to precisely map each individual's academic career, drawing a complete and coherent picture of the educational system.
