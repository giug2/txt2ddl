So, basically here we keep track of a few people, each person is identified by their tax code, which is the most important thing, and then oh well, we write down their surname, name and date of birth. Each of these people can have a lot of utilities in their name, like telephone contracts or things like that; however, each user is linked to one and only one person. These users have their own number to recognize them and are associated with a specific address. The organization is also geographical, let's say: each user belongs to a certain district, and obviously a lot of different users end up in a district. Each district then has its own prefix that identifies it and a name, and in turn is part of a larger province. A province, identified by an acronym and its name, can contain many districts, but a district is only in one province. Returning to utilities, bills are issued for each of them over time, so a user can have a lot of connected bills. Each bill has its own identification code, a date and the amount to be paid. Finally, payments need to be managed: a single bill could be paid multiple times, so it can be linked to different payments, but each single payment, which also has a code, a date and an amount, always refers only to a specific bill.
