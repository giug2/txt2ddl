The data model describes the structure of a database for the management of editorial publications, organized hierarchically. The `CollanaEditoriale` entity represents a series of publications, uniquely identified by its `CollanaTitle` (primary key) and characterized by a `Genre`. Each necklace can contain multiple volumes, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `Volume` entity. The latter is a weak entity whose primary key is composed of the foreign key `FK_COLLANA`, which refers to the series it belongs to, and the `NumeroVolume` attribute. In turn, each volume is composed of multiple articles, through a one-to-many relationship with the `Article` entity. `Article` is also a weak entity, identified by a primary key composed of the foreign key `FK_VOLUME` and the `ChapterNumber`, and has the `PagesNumber` attribute. The `Collaborator` entity lists the people involved in the process, identified by a `Collaborator_ID` (primary key) and described by the `Surname` and `Name` attributes. Finally, two distinct many-to-many relationships are defined between `Article` and `Contributor` to track specific roles: the `author` relationship associates one or more collaborators with each article as authors, while the `reviewer` relationship associates one or more collaborators with each article as reviewers.
