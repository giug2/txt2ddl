Imagine a digital universe created to explore and catalog the world's artistic heritage, a system that allows you to virtually travel from nations to the masterpieces they guard. At the broadest level, we find the Nations, each uniquely identified by an acronym and known by its name. Each nation is a mosaic of Cities, each with its own identification code and name, and each city belongs to only one nation. Cities are the beating heart of culture, and it is here that museums are located. A single city may be proud to host numerous museums, but each museum, in turn, has its headquarters in one and only one city, and is recognized by a unique code and name. Crossing the threshold of a museum, you enter its rooms: each museum is divided into several rooms, each with an identification code, a specific name and a defined size, but each room belongs unequivocally to a single museum. It is within these rooms that the magic comes to life, as each room can display numerous works of art. Each Work is a unique piece, registered with its own code, celebrated by its title and contextualized by the year of creation. Of course, a work would not exist without its creator. The system celebrates the Authors, each registered with a code and a name, specifying that an author may have created many works, but each work is the fruit of the genius of a single author. Furthermore, for each author the country of origin is recorded, creating an indissoluble bond between the artist and his land of origin. Finally, to fully understand the nature of a masterpiece, each work is classified according to a certain Type, such as "painting" or "sculpture". Each type, defined by a code and a name, can group together countless works, but each single work belongs to a single and specific category, thus completing a rich and interconnected map of the art world.
