So basically we have a system for keeping track of a few things about art. We start with the nations, each with its own acronym that identifies it and a full name. Inside every nation, there are a lot of cities, and obviously every city belongs to one and only one nation, there is no escape. Cities also have their own identification code and name. In these cities we then find museums; a city can host several, but each museum is fixed in a single city and has its own ID to avoid confusion, in addition to the name. Inside a museum there are rooms, and here the thing is a bit particular: to identify a room without doubt, its name is not enough, but you also need to know which museum it belongs to, it is a kind of pair of data that makes it unique. Then, for each room, we also know how big it is. Moving on to the creative part, we have the artists, each with their own ID, name and surname, and linked to a single nation of origin, even if a nation can have many artists. Each artist hopefully produces quite a few works of art, but each work can be traced back to a single creator. The works are registered with their own code, a title and the year of creation. Finally, they need to be exhibited: a work can be moved and therefore found in multiple rooms over time, and a room, of course, can contain a lot of different works.
