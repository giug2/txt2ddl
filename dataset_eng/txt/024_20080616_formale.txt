The data model describes a flight booking system. The `Passenger` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and contains personal data such as `Name`, `Surname` and `Telephone`. The `Company` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. Similarly, the entities `Aircraft` and `Airport` are identified by a `Code` (PK) and contain a `Name`; the airport also includes the `City`. The `Class` of service has a `Code` (PK) and a `Description`.

The concept of flight is divided into two entities. `Abstract Flight` represents the generic route, identified by a primary key composed of `Number` and `fk_company`, which acts as a foreign key to `Company`. Each `Abstract Flight` is associated, via N:1 relationships, with an `Aircraft`, a departure airport and an arrival airport (`Airport` entity). The `Specific Flight` entity represents a scheduled instance of a flight and has a primary key composed of `Date`, `Time` and `fk_AbstractFlight`, which establishes a 1:N relationship with `Abstract Flight`.

The booking process is managed by the following entities. `Booking` is a container uniquely identified by a `Code` (PK). Each reservation is made up of one or more `Booking Segments` (1:N relationship), identified by a primary key composed of `Number` and `fk_booking` (FK towards `Booking`). Each segment is linked to a `Specific Flight` and a `Class` via N:1 relationships. Finally, an N:N relationship connects `Passenger` and `Booking`, indicating that multiple passengers can be included in a single booking and a passenger can make multiple bookings.
