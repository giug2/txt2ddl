The data model describes the organizational and logistical structure of a university system. The central UNIVERSITY entity, identified by a primary CODE and a NAME, aggregates multiple FACULTY and DEPARTMENT entities. Both the FACULTY and the DEPARTMENT are defined by a CODE as primary key (PK) and a NAME, and each is linked to a single UNIVERSITY in an N:1 type relationship. Each DEPARTMENT can offer multiple ACADEMIC_COURSE entities; this course is identified by a composite primary key made up of COURSE_CODE and fk_department, establishing a 1:N relationship with the department it belongs to. Lessons take place in a CLASSROOM, identified by a CODE (PK) and a NAME, which belongs to a single FACULTY. The association between CLASSROOM and ACADEMIC_COURSE is 1:N and includes specific attributes such as Start_Time and End_Time to define the timetable. The weekly programming is managed by an N:N relationship between ACADEMIC_COURSE and LESSON_DAY, where the latter entity is a reference table with NUMBER (PK) and DESCRIPTION. The individual lessons held are tracked in the ACTUAL_LESSON entity, identified by a composite key of DATE and fk_course, and records ACTUAL_START and ACTUAL_END. The management of material resources includes the EQUIPMENT_TYPE entity, with CODE (PK) and NAME, which categorizes the tools. Each EQUIPMENT_TYPE can include multiple EQUIPMENT instances, each with a unique SERIAL number such as PK. An ACADEMIC_COURSE may require a specific EQUIPMENT_TYPE (relationship N:1), and multiple ACTUAL_LESSONS may be associated with the use of a single EQUIPMENT (relationship N:1).
