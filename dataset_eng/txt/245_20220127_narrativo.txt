This system was designed to map and manage the entire organization of a healthcare network, offering a clear and detailed view of how structures, departments and staff interact with each other. The fulcrum of our information universe is the HOSPITAL, every single structure is conceived as a unique entity, identified by an exclusive code and precisely localized through its address and the city it belongs to. Within each hospital structure, life and care are organized in specific DEPARTMENTS, which represent the fundamental operational units such as cardiology, paediatrics or surgery. Each department is inextricably linked to a single hospital, unable to exist except as part of a larger structure; in fact, to identify it with certainty, its specific code is not enough, but it is necessary to associate it with the hospital to which it belongs. Each department is also described by its name and the floor on which it is located, to facilitate physical identification.

The protagonists of this scenario are, of course, the health professionals: DOCTORS and NURSES. For each of them, the system records essential personal information, such as name and surname, associating it with a unique identification code which guarantees its unmistakability within the database. The real strength of this model lies in the way it intertwines the professional lives of staff with the physical structure of the departments. The system is designed to manage a complex network of collaborations: a single doctor can serve in multiple departments and collaborate with several nurses. Likewise, a nurse may be assigned to multiple departments and work alongside multiple doctors. Finally, each department is animated by a team made up of numerous doctors and nurses, whose specific assignments and interactions are precisely tracked. This overview allows you to understand not only who works where, but also how care teams are formed, ensuring efficient management of human resources across the entire healthcare organization.
