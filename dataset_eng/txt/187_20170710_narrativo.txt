This system was designed to completely and efficiently manage the catalog and operations of a video rental shop, putting the cinematographic work and its enjoyment by enthusiasts at the center of attention. The beating heart of our universe is the FILM, each work is uniquely identified by its international ISAN code and has a title that makes it recognizable to the public. Behind every great film there are talents, the ARTISTS, whose personal data such as name, surname and date of birth we keep to celebrate their contribution. The relationship between artists and films is dynamic and rich: a film may boast the participation of numerous artists, and a single artist may have contributed to creating multiple masterpieces over the course of his or her career. Often, these artists are linked to a specific PRODUCTION STUDIO, a creative house that can represent and launch the careers of many different talents. Once a film is ready for the general public, the DISTRIBUTOR, the entity responsible for its marketing, comes into play; a distributor may handle a large portfolio of films, but each individual film is entrusted to the care of a single distributor. To make a cinematographic work tangible and available for rental, each title is replicated on multiple physical copies, the PHYSICAL SUPPORTS. Each of these copies, whether a DVD or Blu-ray, is tracked with a unique code and we record its date of entry into our inventory. Of course, a film can exist in countless copies. The real protagonists of this experience are our CUSTOMERS, registered with their essential data such as name, surname and telephone number. The magical moment occurs when a CUSTOMER rents a PHYSICAL SUPPORT: this operation is recorded by associating the specific copy with the customer and, crucially, a RENTAL EXPIRY DATE is established to know when the film will have to return home, ready for a new viewer. A customer can rent several copies over time, but each individual copy, at a given time, is entrusted to only one customer, creating a unique bond between the work and its temporary user.
