This system was designed to map the intricate and dynamic network of relationships within an academic institution, offering a clear vision of how educational provision is structured and managed. At the center of everything we find the **DEPARTMENT**, the organizational pillar that defines the areas of knowledge. Each department, identified by a unique code, acts as the parent company for a multitude of **COURSES** of study. The relationship here is clear and hierarchical: each single course belongs exclusively to a single department, guaranteeing a clear academic attribution, while a department can naturally offer a wide and varied range of teachings.

These courses are animated by **PROFESSORS**, whose skills are the beating heart of teaching. The system reflects the flexible and collaborative nature of the modern academic world: a professor can lend his expertise to multiple courses, and, vice versa, a single course can be enriched by the joint teaching of multiple professors. This structure allows you to easily manage co-teaching and transversal teaching assignments. The fundamental figure of the **TUTOR** intervenes to support the teaching activity and the students. Again, the relationship is designed to be versatile: a course can benefit from the support of an entire team of tutors, and a single tutor can make his or her skills available to assist students in several courses. Finally, the system also models the link of mentorship and supervision, describing how professors and tutors interact with each other. This connection is also flexible, allowing one professor to supervise multiple tutors and one tutor to refer to multiple professors, creating a collaborative network that fosters growth and knowledge exchange within the institution.
