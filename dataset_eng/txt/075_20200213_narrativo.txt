This system was designed to map and organize the entire academic structure of an educational institution, creating a clear and interconnected picture of the world of knowledge it represents. At the top of this organization we find the School, the larger entity, as a faculty. Each School is unique, defined by its own identification code and a specific name that describes its general area of ​​expertise, such as "School of Engineering" or "School of Humanities". Each School is divided internally into one or more Departments. Each Department, despite having its own name and identification code, is inextricably linked to a single School, creating a clear hierarchy. This relationship tells us that while a School may house numerous specialized departments, each department answers to and belongs to a single parent School. Going into further detail, we discover that each Department is the cradle of different Study Courses. Similarly to the previous report, a Course of Study is defined by its name and a unique code, but is always based within a single Department. This means that a department can offer a wide range of training courses, but every single degree or specialization course has a precise departmental location. Finally, the system manages the fundamental human element of teaching: the Professors. The structure here becomes more dynamic to reflect academic reality. A Professor, identified by his own code and name, is not tied to just one course. On the contrary, the system is designed to manage a complex relationship in which each professor can teach multiple Courses of Study and, at the same time, each Course of Study can draw on the expertise of multiple professors. This multiple interconnection is the heart of the system, allowing you to precisely record which professor is assigned to which course, creating a network that links teachers and training paths in a flexible and complete way.
