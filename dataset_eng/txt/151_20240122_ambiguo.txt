So, in this system we have a few different things. First of all, there is a list of professors, each with their own identification code to avoid confusion. Then, obviously, there are the courses, and these too have their own number to recognize them. Furthermore, we have the figure of tutors, who are like assistants, and they also have a personal identifier. Finally, everything is organized into departments, and each department has its own code. As far as connections go, it's a bit of a mess. A professor can teach a lot of different courses, and on the other hand a single course can easily have more than one professor working on it. The same thing happens between professors and tutors: a professor can be connected to several tutors, and a tutor can lend a hand to multiple professors, there is no fixed link. And, as you can imagine, this also applies between tutors and courses: a tutor can supervise students of different subjects, and a subject can have more than one tutor giving support. The only slightly stricter rule concerns departments: each department, let's say, "owns" a group of courses, so a course only ever belongs to a single department. There are no courses that are halfway between two places.
