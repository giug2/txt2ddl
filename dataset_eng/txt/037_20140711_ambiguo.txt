Basically, we have a whole system here for keeping track of music stuff, like concerts and stuff like that. There is a list of musical **Pieces**, each with its own `Code` so as not to confuse them and a `Title`. Each piece is obviously connected to a **Musician**, who we imagine is the composer, also identified by a `Code` and his `Name`; the thing is that a musician may have written a lot of pieces, but a piece can be traced back to only one author. Then there are the **Concerts**, which are the actual events, with their own identifying `Code`, a `Title` for the program and a `Description` to help you understand something. This is flexible, because a concert can include many different pieces and, similarly, a single piece can end up in a lot of different concerts. Each concert is not a unique event, it can be repeated, and for this there is the **Replica**, which basically tells us that that given concert was held on a certain `Date` and at a certain `Time`; the date, together with the reference to the concert, is what makes it unique. Playing is the **Orchestra**, which can be recognized by its `Name` and of which we also know the `Foundation Year`. An orchestra can perform many concerts, but a concert is associated with only one orchestra. Inside the orchestra there are the **Orchestra Members**, the natural persons, each with their `Number`, `Name` and `Surname`. Of course, they play **Tools**, which are simply a list with a `Code` and a `Name`. To bring all these musicians together, there is a contraption called **Participation**, which is essentially a register that ties an orchestral player to a specific orchestra, and this combination is unique. The instrument is also connected to this same participation, so in simple terms it tells us that that guy, in that orchestra, plays that particular instrument.
