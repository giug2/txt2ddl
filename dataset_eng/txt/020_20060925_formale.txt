The data model describes the management of art exhibitions through four main entities. The `Show` entity represents a single exhibition, uniquely identified by a `Code` (PK) and characterized by `Title`, `DateIn` and `DateEnd`. The `Painting` entity catalogs works of art, each with a unique `Code` (PK), `Author` and `Year` of creation. The `Room` entity defines the exhibition spaces, identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Name` and `Size`. The relationship between these entities is managed via the `Exhibition` associative table, which links a specific painting to a specific exhibition. The primary key of `Exhibition` is composite, made up of the `fk_show` and `fk_framework` attributes, which act as foreign keys to the `Show` and `Framework` tables respectively, implementing a many-to-many relationship. Finally, a one-to-many relationship connects the `Room` entity to the `Exhibition` entity, indicating that each room can host multiple exhibited works.
