The data model defines the structure for managing musicians' participation in orchestral compositions. The `MUSICIAN` entity represents the records of the musicians, each uniquely identified by a `musician_id` (PK) attribute. The `ORCHESTRA COMPOSITION` entity identifies a specific orchestral formation in a given year, using the `YEAR` attribute as the primary key. The `ROLE` entity catalogs positions within the orchestra, defined by a `CODE` (PK) and a descriptive `NAME`. The association between these entities is managed through the `PARTICIPATION` link entity, whose primary key is composite, formed by the `fk_musician` and `fk_composizione_orchestra` attributes. This entity implements the one-to-many relationships from `MUSICIAN` and `ORCHESTRA COMPOSITION`, effectively establishing a many-to-many relationship between them. Finally, a one-to-many relationship connects the `ROLE` entity to `PARTICIPATION`, specifying the role that each musician played in a given orchestral composition.
