The data model defines a structure for managing electoral information, divided into five main entities. The `College` entity is identified by a `Number` attribute that acts as the primary key (PK). The `Region` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `name` attribute. The `Elected` entity is uniquely identified by the `Year` (PK) attribute. The `Party` entity has a `Code` as primary key (PK) and a `name`. Finally, the `Person` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK). The model establishes the following relationships: an N:1 cardinality relationship between `College` and `Region`, whereby multiple constituencies belong to a single region; an N:1 relationship between `Deputy` and `Constituency`, indicating that multiple deputies are elected in a single constituency; and an N:1 relationship between `Deputy` and `Party`, which associates multiple deputies with a single party affiliation.
