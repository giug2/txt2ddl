Welcome to the heart of our academic management system, a framework designed to map and organize our university's entire ecosystem. At the highest level, we find the **Campus**, the physical and logistical foundations of our institution, each uniquely identified. Each campus is a world unto itself, hosting within it a variety of academic **Departments**. A department, such as Physics or Literature, is closely linked to the campus on which it is located; in fact, it is uniquely defined not only by its name but also by the campus it belongs to, ensuring that there is no confusion between departments of the same name in different locations. Our **Students** live and study within this organizational framework. Each student is enrolled in a single, specific department, which represents his or her major course of study and academic home. While a department accommodates a large community of students, each individual student has a unique departmental affiliation. Finally, to complete the picture, there are the **Professors**, the pillar of our training offer. The relationship between professors and departments has been designed to be flexible and collaborative: a professor can in fact be affiliated with multiple departments, encouraging interdisciplinarity and the sharing of knowledge, and, naturally, each department makes use of the skills of numerous professors to cover its teaching and research offering.
