The data model defines the structure for managing information about orchestras, musicians, and their geographic and educational affiliations. The `NATION` entity, identified by the primary key `nation_id`, contains the name of the country and has a one-to-many relationship with the `CITY` entity. The latter, identified by `id_citta` and containing the name attribute, is therefore associated with a single nation but can host multiple orchestras. The `ORCHESTRA` entity has a unique identifier `orchestra_ID`, a name and a date; it is linked to a single `CITY`. To historicize the formation of an orchestra, the associative entity `ORCHESTRA COMPOSITION` uses a primary key composed of the `YEAR` attribute and a foreign key `fk_orchestra`, establishing that each orchestra can have multiple annual compositions. The `MUSICIAN` entity is defined by `musician_id` as the primary key and the first and last name attributes. Each musician is associated with a unique `CONSERVATORY`, identified by `id_conservatorio` and its name, establishing a one-to-many relationship between conservatories and musicians. Finally, there is a many-to-many relationship between `ORCHESTRA COMPOSITION` and `MUSICIAN`, indicating that an annual composition includes multiple musicians and that a musician can participate in several compositions over time.
