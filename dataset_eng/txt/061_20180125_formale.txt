The data model describes a conference management system. The `Country` entity is defined by a primary identifier `CN` and a `Name`. Each nation can contain multiple cities, as represented by the `City` entity, which is identified by the code `CC`, has a `Name` and is linked to a single nation. The `Area` entity is identified by a primary `Code` and a `Name`. The entity `Matter` is connected to it through a one-to-many relationship; each matter belongs to a single area. The `Matter` entity is characterized by a `Name` and a primary key composed of the `CM` and `fk_area` attributes, where `fk_area` refers to the area it belongs to. Finally, the `Convention` entity, identified by a `Code` and described by a `Title`, represents the central element. Each conference takes place in a single `City` and deals with a single `Matter`, thus establishing two one-to-many relationships, respectively, from the `City` and `Matter` entities towards `Conference`.
