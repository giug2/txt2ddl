The system describes an academic context centered on subjects, each identified by a unique code and characterized by a name. Each subject is associated with a department, establishing a direct link with the entity of the departments, which are in turn defined by an identification code and a name. The subjects are organized within degree courses, which have their own identification code, name and description. A subject can be part of multiple degree courses and, vice versa, each course is made up of several subjects. A degree course consists of specific exam sessions, each of which represents a single test on a certain date and at a specific time. Each degree course is managed by a specific chair and each exam session takes place in a designated room. The classrooms are defined by a code and a name, while the chairs are uniquely identified by their name and have a reference office and a responsible full professor. The chairs are made up of teachers, each registered with a unique serial number, a surname and a name. The belonging of a teacher to a chair and the role he holds within it is managed by the IncaricoDidattico relationship, which connects a teacher to a chair and the academic role he holds. Finally, academic roles are an entity in their own right, described by an identifier and name.
