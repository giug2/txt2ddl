This system was designed to map and manage the entire academic structure of a university institution, creating a clear and interconnected picture of the educational organization. At the top of this structure we find the Faculties, the large thematic areas that group together similar disciplines, each identified by its own unique code and a specific name, such as "Faculty of Engineering" or "Faculty of Letters". Each Faculty acts as a container for numerous Departments, creating a clear organizational hierarchy. A Department, in turn, represents a more specialized unit, characterized by its own name and an identification code that is unique only within the Faculty to which it belongs, linking it inextricably to it.

The beating heart of each Department is made up of two fundamental elements: the Study Courses and the Teachers. Each Department is responsible for the delivery of multiple Courses of Study, each with its own name and unique identifier for that department, ensuring that each course is clearly placed in its academic context. At the same time, each Department has a teaching staff. Each Teacher, defined by name and surname, has an identification code that uniquely distinguishes him within his specific Department, underlining his primary link with that unit. Finally, the system models the dynamic relationship between teaching and courses: a single Teacher can make his/her expertise available in multiple Study Courses, and, at the same time, a Study Course is often enriched by the contribution of several Teachers, reflecting the collaborative and multidisciplinary nature of the academic environment.
