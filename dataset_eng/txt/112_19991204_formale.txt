The data model defines the structure for managing a car fleet, divided into five main entities: Nation, Home, Model, Car and Person. The Nation entity is uniquely identified by the NationAcronym (PK) attribute and contains a Name. There is a one-to-many relationship between Nation and Home, establishing that each automaker belongs to only one nation. The House entity is identified by a Code (PK) and has a Name attribute. In turn, each House is associated with multiple models via a one-to-many relationship. The Model entity is defined by a primary key composed of the Name and fk_house attributes, where the latter acts as a foreign key (FK) towards the House entity; it also has the Category attribute. Each Model can correspond to multiple Car instances, according to a one-to-many relationship. The Car entity is identified by the License Plate (PK) and described by the Year attribute. Finally, the Person entity, identified by the Tax Code (PK) and characterized by Surname and Name, is linked to the Car entity by a one-to-many relationship, indicating that a person can own multiple cars, but each car is owned by only one person.
