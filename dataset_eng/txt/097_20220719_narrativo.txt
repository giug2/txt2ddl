Welcome to the beating heart of the world of classical music, a universe where every note, every instrument and every artist contributes to creating an unforgettable experience. This system was designed to capture the essence of this world, harmoniously managing all the information that links a composer to his work and an orchestra to its audience. At the center of everything we find the `Concert`, a unique artistic event, identified by a code and enriched by a title and a description that tell its soul. Each `Concert` comes to life in a specific `Theatre`, a magical place with its own name and code, which hosts the performance. Although a concert program is unique in its conception, it can be replicated over multiple evenings; for this reason, each individual performance is recorded as an `Event`, precisely defined by a start date and time.

The program of a concert is made up of a selection of musical `Pieces`, each with its own title and identification code. Of course, behind each piece there is the genius of a `Composer`, a key figure in our system, registered with name, surname and a personal code. The relationship is clear: a single composer can be the author of countless pieces, while a piece is the fruit of the mind of a single master. Giving voice to these magnificent scores is the 'Orchestra', a cohesive entity with its own name and location, responsible for the performance of a given concert.

But what makes an orchestra such? It is the set of its `Musicians`, talented artists identified by a number, name and surname. To define the internal structure and role of each artist, the concept of `Composition` of the orchestra comes into play. This does not represent a piece of music, but rather the specific role that a `Musician` plays within the band. Each `Composition` uniquely links a musician to a specific `Instrument`, which in turn is cataloged with a code, a name and a typology (such as "strings" or "brass"). This way, the system knows that a particular musician plays a particular instrument within that specific orchestra. The structure is also hierarchical: a `Composition` can be part of a larger one, allowing players to be organized into sections and subsections, such as the first violin section which is part of the larger string family. Thus, from the genius of the composer to the individual musician on stage, every element of the magic of a concert is traced, preserved and told.
