This system was conceived as a digital historical archive to preserve the memory and evolution of an orchestra over time. At the center of our narrative are the individual musicians, each identified by a unique code that accompanies them throughout their career within the institution. The life of the orchestra, however, is not static; its formation changes, enriches and transforms with the passing of the seasons. For this reason, the system crystallizes the structure of the ensemble in a specific "orchestra composition" for each single year, making the year itself the identifier of that particular formation.

Within this structure, each musician takes on a very specific role, whether that of first violin, percussionist or director. Each role is cataloged with a descriptive name and a unique identification code. The very heart of the system, which ties these stories together, is the concept of "participation". This entity represents the indissoluble bond between a musician and a specific annual composition of the orchestra. In fact, a single orchestra composition of a given year is composed of multiple participations, one for each musician present. Likewise, a musician, over the course of his career, can accumulate numerous participations, leaving his mark over several years. Each participation is associated with the role that the musician played in that specific season, thus allowing us to precisely reconstruct not only who played, but also which part he played in a given year. The uniqueness of an entry is guaranteed by the combination of musician and year, ensuring that each artist's history within the orchestra is recorded accurately and unambiguously.
