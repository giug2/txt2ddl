Imagine being able to travel through time, leafing through the pages of the history of a great orchestra to discover not only its performances, but the very soul that made it up year after year. This system was designed precisely to be a living chronicle of the orchestral world, a detailed archive that celebrates every single talent that contributes to the creation of music. The beating heart of this narrative is the entity "ORCHESTRA IN THE YEAR", which does not represent a generic orchestra, but a precise photograph of it in a specific historical moment, capturing its unique formation for a specific season.

But what would an orchestra be without its artists? For this reason, each "MUSICIAN" is registered as an individual, with his own history and path. However, a musician in an orchestra defines himself through the sound he produces, through his "INSTRUMENT". To capture this fundamental union, the system uses a special link, "MUSICIAN INSTRUMENT", which inseparably associates an artist with his violin, his oboe or his piano. This allows us to understand that a single musician can be a virtuoso of multiple instruments and that it is precisely this specific combination of talent and technique that is called upon to be part of the orchestral team of a given year. A magnificent network is thus created which shows how the same musician, perhaps with different instruments, may have been part of multiple orchestral formations during his career.

Excellence, however, has deep roots, made up of study and dedication. Our system honors this path by tracking every "DIPLOMA" obtained. This is not a generic title, but a qualification closely linked to a musician's mastery of a particular instrument, certifying for example his specialization in the cello or the transverse flute. Finally, to complete the picture of an artist's education, each diploma is linked to the prestigious "CONSERVATORY" that awarded it. In this way, it is possible to not only appreciate a musician's talent, but also recognize the institution that shaped his skill. By putting all these pieces together, you get not a simple database, but a symphony of information that tells the dynamic story of how a collection of individuals, with their instruments, their certified skills and their academic origins, come together year after year to bring the magic of an orchestra to life.
