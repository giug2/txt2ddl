The data model defines a structure for managing projects, resources, and people. The geographical location is hierarchically structured: the `Region` entity, identified by `CodiceRegione` (PK), is in a one-to-many relationship with `Citta` (PK `CodiceISTAT`), which in turn can contain multiple `SedeOperativa` (PK `CodiceSede`). The central entity `Project`, identified by `Project Code`, is associated with one or more operational offices. A project is divided into `Tasks` (PK `TaskCode`) through a many-to-many relationship that specifies an `Estimated Budget` for each association. Tasks require `Resource` (PK `ResourceCode`), and resources are in turn associated with `Risk` (PK `RiskCode`); both connections are modeled as many-to-many relationships. Staff is represented by the `TeamMember` entity, with primary key `Number`, and its assignment to projects is an N:N relationship that includes the `Allocation` attribute. The internal hierarchical structure of the team is implemented through the associative entity `HierarchicalRelationship`, which establishes a recursive relationship on `TeamMember`. Its primary key is composed of the foreign keys `fk_upper_member` and `fk_inferior_member`, both referencing a team member's serial number. Each hierarchical relationship is further qualified by a `Relationship Type` (PK `Relationship Type Code`), which describes its nature.
