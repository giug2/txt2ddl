Welcome to a digital archive designed to explore the rich and dynamic universe of orchestral music. This system was created to navigate through the history, geography and people who bring the symphonies to life, allowing you to discover the connections that bind musicians, orchestras and places into one great narrative. Our journey starts from geography, since every sound has its origin. The world is organized into Nations, each identified by a unique code and its own name. Within each nation numerous Cities flourish, also defined by an identifier and a name, and each city belongs unequivocally to a single nation, creating a clear map of our musical world.

It is in these cities that we find the beating heart of our exploration: the Orchestras. Each orchestra is a unique entity, registered with its own ID, a prestigious name and the date marking its foundation. Each of them has a headquarters, a city to which it belongs, and although a single city can be the cradle of multiple orchestral formations, each orchestra is inextricably linked to a single city of origin. But what would an orchestra be without its artists? The system celebrates every single Musician, registered with name, surname and a personal identification code. To fully understand their journey, we also keep track of their training, associating each musician with the Conservatory where they honed their talent. Each conservatory, in turn, is a distinct institution with its own name and code, and can boast of having trained countless talents throughout its history.

The real magic, however, lies in understanding how orchestras evolve over time. An orchestra is not a static entity; its members change, generations follow one another. To capture this fascinating evolution, the system introduces the concept of Orchestra Composition. This is nothing more than a historical photograph, a snapshot documenting the exact formation of a specific orchestra in a specific year. For each orchestra, therefore, we can have multiple "compositions", one for each year whose members we have registered. It is through this mechanism that we connect musicians to orchestras. The composition of an orchestra in a given year is made up of a set of musicians, and at the same time, the career of an individual musician can be traced through the various orchestral compositions of which he has been a part over the years, perhaps in different orchestras or in the same orchestra in different eras. In this way, the system not only catalogs data, but tells stories: the history of an orchestra through its musicians, the career of a musician through the orchestras he has honored with his talent, and the cultural map of the cities and nations that gave birth to these magnificent musical institutions.
