The data model defines the structure for managing customer records and related current accounts. There are two main entities: `Customer` and `Bank Account`. The `Customer` entity is uniquely identified by the `C.F.` attribute, which acts as the primary key (PK), and also includes the `Last Name`, `First Name`, `Date of Birth` and `Address` attributes. The `Current Account` entity is uniquely identified by its `Number` (PK). A relationship with one-to-many cardinality (1:N) is established between the two entities from the `Customer` to the `Current Account`, specifying that a single customer can be the holder of multiple current accounts, while each current account can be traced back to a single customer.
