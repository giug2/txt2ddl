The structure of the data model is divided into four main entities: PROFESSOR, COURSE, TUTOR and DEPARTMENT. The PROFESSOR entity is uniquely identified by `professor_id` (PK) and contains the `professor_name` attribute. The COURSE entity is defined by the `course_id` primary key and the `course_name` attribute. Similarly, the TUTOR entity is identified by `tutor_id` (PK) and has a `tutor_name` attribute, while the DEPARTMENT entity is identified by `department_id` (PK) and includes the `department_name` attribute. The model provides a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between the COURSE and DEPARTMENT entities, whereby each course belongs to a single department, while a department can include multiple courses. A ternary relationship with many-to-many-to-many (N:N:N) cardinality is also defined that associates the entities PROFESSOR, TUTOR and COURSE, indicating a complex link that allows a professor, a tutor and a course to be associated in multiple combinations.
