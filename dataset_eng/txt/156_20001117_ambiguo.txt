So, we basically have a system for keeping track of a lot of publications. There is something called an Editorial Series, which would be like a series of books, identified mainly by its title and with an indication of the genre. Each series can contain quite a few Volumes, and each volume is unique thanks to its number and the fact that it is linked to a specific series. Inside each volume, then, there are a lot of Articles, which are a bit like chapters; to find one you need to know what volume it belongs to and what chapter number it is. We are also interested in knowing more or less how many pages there are of these articles. Finally, there are the Collaborators, that is, the people, each with their own identification code, name and surname. It gets interesting when we see how these people link to the articles: for each article, there is a link to say who the authors are, and there can be more than one. Similarly, there is another type of link, always between article and collaborator, to know who acted as reviewer, and also in this case it can be different people. So, an article can have one group of authors and another group of reviewers.
