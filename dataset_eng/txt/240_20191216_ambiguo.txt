So, in this system there is some stuff that goes around the university world, it seems. First of all there are the Students, each with their Serial Number which is that number that identifies them and no two are the same, then oh well, name and surname. Each student, obviously, is linked to only one Degree Course, which in turn has its own code to be recognized and a name. A graduate program, of course, can have a lot of students enrolled.

These degree courses are not there randomly, but belong to a Department, which is a larger structure. A department, which also has its own identification code and name, can offer various degree courses. It also seems that there is a direct connection between the student and his home department, which ultimately is that of his course.

Then there are the subjects, the Teachings. These also have their own unique code and name. The messiest part is how they connect to students: basically, each student can take a lot of different teachings and, at the same time, each teaching is followed by a bunch of students. The important thing is that when a student takes an exam for a certain course, the grade he or she has obtained is recorded somewhere, which is therefore something that is exactly halfway between that specific student and that specific subject.
