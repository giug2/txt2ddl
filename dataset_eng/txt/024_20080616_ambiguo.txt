So basically there's a system that handles some stuff on flights. There are Passengers, each with their own identification code, name, surname and telephone number. These guys make Reservations, and this is where things get interesting: a reservation can group multiple people together, and the same person can have several reservations around. Each booking, which has its own code to avoid confusion, is actually made up of one or more pieces, the Booking Segments; think of the stages of a journey, each with its own progressive number and obviously closely linked to the main booking, so much so that it cannot exist on its own. Each of these segments refers to a specific Class, such as economy or business, which is a simple thing with a code and a description, and above all it links to a specific Flight, the one you really have to take. Here we need to distinguish, because there is the abstract flight, which is more of an idea, like the Rome-Milan route with a certain flight number, which is always the same and is managed by a very specific airline. Then there is the specific Flight, which is the actual departure of that abstract flight on a certain date and at a certain time. Thus, one flight idea can give rise to many specific flights over time. This abstract Flight, in addition to being of a Company, is also connected to a type of Aircraft and, obviously, to Airports, probably one for departure, one for arrival and perhaps another for stopover. Both the Companies, the Aircraft and the Airports have their codes and names to be recognised.
