This system was designed to offer a complete and interconnected vision of a network of commercial activities, carefully managing every aspect, from points of sale to products, up to the staff who animate their operations. At the center of our universe are the Stores, each uniquely identified by its own Code and described by a Name and a physical Address. The true wealth of each store lies in the products it offers. Each Product is a unique entity, defined not only by its Code but also by the indissoluble bond with its Manufacturer; in fact, the complete identification of an item requires the combination of its code and that of the Manufacturer who created it. Naturally, each Producer, in turn registered with a Code and a Name, can create an infinite variety of products, but each product comes from a single and specific creator.

To maintain order and facilitate navigation, each individual Product is carefully classified within a single Type, a product category defined by an Acronym and a descriptive Name. A Type can encompass numerous different products, but each product belongs to one and only one category. The flexibility of our model emerges in the way in which Stores relate to these categories: a single Store can be specialized in multiple Types of goods, and, similarly, a certain Type of product can be distributed and sold in many different stores in the network.

The beating heart of this organization are the Employees, each registered with a personal number, a Name and a Surname. The system recognizes the dynamism of modern work, allowing an Employee to provide service in multiple Stores and, vice versa, each Store to be animated by the work of many collaborators. Finally, to have a complete human picture, the system keeps track of the geographical information linked to each employee, referring to a list of Cities, each with its own Code and Name. In particular, two distinct but fundamental pieces of information are recorded for each Employee: the City of Birth, which is unique for each person, and the City in which they are currently resident, which is also unique. This allows us to understand not only where our collaborators work, but also their origins and their current geographical location.
