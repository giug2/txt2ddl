Basically, we have two main lists of things here. On one side there is a list of Projects, and each of these has its own identification code to avoid confusion. On the other side, we have a list of people, the Employees. For each employee we keep a bit of information aside: their identification number, which is their unique number, their last name and first name, the date they started working and what their general role in the company is. What's interesting is how these two things are connected. Any project can have a lot of employees in it, and, vice versa, one person can work on several different projects at the same time. When an employee is assigned to a project, we also note what specific role he/she holds for that job, which may be different from his/her usual job.
