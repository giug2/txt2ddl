So, in this system we have a few things that revolve around the organization of work. There are the offices, which are practically physical places, and each has its own code so as not to confuse them. Then there are the departments, the various areas or sectors, and they too have their own number to recognize them.

The interesting part concerns the managers, those who command. To identify a manager, a single piece of data is not enough, but you must put together his personal code, the department to which he is linked and also the location. It is the combination of these three pieces of information that makes it unique. This makes it clear that there is a good connection between managers and departments, a great mix where a boss can be connected to multiple sectors and, at the same time, a sector can have more than one manager.

Finally, there are the assistants, those who help the bosses. An assistant also has his own identification code, but to be sure who we are talking about, we must also know which manager he is connected to and in which office. The rule here is simpler: a manager may have a nice group of assistants working for him, but each assistant reports to one and only one manager.
