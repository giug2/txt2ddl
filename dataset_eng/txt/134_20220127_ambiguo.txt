So, in practice we have a few people who work, there are the managers, who are like the bosses, and then the assistants who give them a hand. Each of them has their own personal code so as not to confuse them, in addition to their name and surname. Then there are the physical places, the offices, which are the main buildings scattered around, and for each office we know where it is located, with address and city. Inside these locations there are offices, and an office is always necessarily inside a location, it cannot exist on its own. In fact, to find a specific office you need to know both its number and where it is located. Each office also has a name and is on a certain floor. The trickiest part is how they all mix: one manager may have to deal with multiple assistants, and they may work together in different offices. Likewise, an assistant may work for multiple managers and be located in various offices. And, as a result, in any office you can find a mix of managers and assistants collaborating. In short, it's all very flexible, anyone can work with anyone else, anywhere.
