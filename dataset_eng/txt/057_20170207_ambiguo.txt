So, basically here we have a system that manages a few people and jobs. There are clients, who are the ones who ask for things, and each is identified by their own personal code. Then there are the projects, and each project is closely linked to a client; in fact, to know what project we are talking about, you need not only its specific code but also that of the client who requested it. This means that a client can have a lot of projects, but a project belongs to only one of them. On the other side of the organization there are departments, such as offices or departments, each with its own identifying number. Employees work within these departments. An employee is defined both by his identification number and by the department he belongs to, it is an inseparable thing. Clearly, there can be quite a few employees in a department. This becomes intertwined when employees are put to work on projects: an employee may be involved in more than one project and, at the same time, a single project may need a lot of different people to complete. In short, it's a lot of comings and goings of people working on different things.
