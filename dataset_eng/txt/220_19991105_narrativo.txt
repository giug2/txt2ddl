This system was designed to manage a dynamic and complex commercial ecosystem, a true virtual market where different actors interact to bring products to customers. At the center of everything we find the concept of Product, each item is unique and clearly identified in our database. Of course, every product has an origin: a Supplier. The structure tells us that a single Supplier can be the source of many different products, allowing us to manage large inventories and relationships with various business partners.

The marketplace comes to life thanks to Sellers, unique entities that populate our platform. A Seller has the ability to create countless Offers, which represent the beating heart of the entire system. An Offer is not an entity in itself, but the crucial meeting point between several elements: it arises when a specific Seller decides to market a specific Product. This means that the same Product can be the subject of multiple Offers, perhaps created by different Sellers under different conditions, and similarly, a single Seller can have a vast portfolio of active Offers.

To organize and present this multitude of commercial proposals, the Catalog comes into play. Each Catalog functions as a thematic showcase or a seasonal collection, grouping numerous Offers within it. Each Offer, therefore, belongs to a single and specific Catalogue, thus creating coherent and easy-to-consult collections. The very nature of the Offer is defined by the unique combination of the product it promotes, the seller who offers it and the catalog in which it is included, ensuring that every single commercial proposal is perfectly traceable and distinct from all the others.
