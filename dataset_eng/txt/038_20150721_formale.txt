The data model describes a utility and billing management system. The central entity is **User**, identified by a unique `Number` and characterized by an `Address`. Each user is associated with one and only one **Person**, who is defined by his/her tax code (`CF` as primary key), `Surname`, `Name` and `Date of birth`; a person can be the owner of multiple users. Multiple **Bills** are connected to each user, each identified by a unique `Code` and containing `Date` and `Amount`. Similarly, each user can have multiple **Payment** records, identified by a `Code` and containing `Date` and `Amount`. Geographic location is managed through the **Province** and **District** entities. A `Province`, identified by its `Acronym` and `Name`, groups together several districts. A **District**, in turn, is identified by a `Prefix` and a `Name` and contains multiple users, establishing a hierarchical relationship that goes from the province to the user.
