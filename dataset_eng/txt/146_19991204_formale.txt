The data model describes a system for managing vehicles and related purchase transactions. The `Nation` entity, identified by `Nation Sign` (PK), is associated with the `House` entity, which represents a car manufacturer. Each `House`, identified by a `Code` (PK), belongs to only one `Nation`, establishing a one-to-many relationship. In turn, a `Made` produces multiple `Model` vehicles; the `Model` entity is identified by a primary key composed of its `Name` and the foreign key `fk_home`, which links it to the constructor. Each `Model` can exist in different `Version`s, each identified by a key composed of a `Code` and the foreign key `fk_model`, and characterized by a `displacement` attribute. The `Car` entity represents a single physical vehicle, uniquely identified by its `Registration Plate` (PK) and attributable to a specific `Version`. Transactions are handled through the `Purchase` entity, whose primary key of `Date` and `fk_auto` (a foreign key to `Car`) records the purchase of a specific vehicle on a specific date. Finally, the `Person` entity, identified by the `CodiceFiscale` (PK), is the purchaser, and can be associated with multiple `Purchase`s.
