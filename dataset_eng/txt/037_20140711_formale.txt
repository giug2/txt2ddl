The data model is structured for the management of musical events, orchestras and compositions. Primary entities include `Piece`, identified by a unique `Code` and with a `Title`, and `Musician`, identified by a `Code`. There is a one-to-many relationship between `Musician` and `Piece`, in which a musician can compose multiple pieces. The `Concert` entity, identified by a `Code`, has a many-to-many relationship with `Piece`, to define the performance program, and a one-to-many relationship with `Orchestra`, indicating that each concert is performed by a single orchestra. The `Orchestra` entity is identified by its `Name`. Each `Concert` can have multiple performances over time, modeled by the `Replica` entity, whose primary key is composed of the `Data` and the foreign key of the concert (`fk_concerto`). The orchestra staff is managed through the entities `Orchestral`, with primary key `Register`, and `Instrument`, with key `Code`. Their association with an orchestra is defined by the `Participation` entity, which establishes a relationship between an `Orchestral`, an `Orchestra` and the `Instrument` played in that context. The primary key of `Participation` is a key composed of the foreign keys of `Orchestral` and `Orchestra`.
