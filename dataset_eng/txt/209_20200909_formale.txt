The data model defines the structure of an academic information system. The `Teacher` entity is identified by a `Number` (PK) and contains the `Surname` and `Name` attributes. The `Department` entity is characterized by a unique `Code` (PK), `Name`, `Location` and `Contact`. The `Course of Study` is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has a `Name` and `Foundation Year`; each course is associated with a `Coordinator` in a one-to-many relationship. The `Coordinator` entity is defined by an `ID` (PK) and tracks the personal data and the period of the assignment. The `Teaching Provided` entity represents the instance of a teaching in a given `Academic Year` and is identified by a composite primary key that references the course code and the department code. There is a one-to-many relationship between `Course of Study` and `Teaching Provided`, and a similar relationship between `Department` and `Teaching Provided`. The relationship between `Teacher` and `Teaching Provided` is many-to-many. Financial management is modeled by the entities `Income Range` and `Exemption Type`, both identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Description`. The `Contribution Quota` entity defines the `Amount` due and acts as an associative table, with a primary key composed of the foreign keys towards `Teaching Provided` and `Income Range`, establishing a one-to-many relationship from these two entities towards the quota. Finally, a many-to-many relationship is defined between `Department` and `Exemption Type`.
