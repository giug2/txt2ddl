This system is designed to map and manage the entire academic organization of an educational institution, offering a clear and detailed view of how educational resources are structured and allocated. At the center of this structure we find the Departments, the large thematic areas that act as pillars of the training offer. Each Department is uniquely identified by its own code and has a name that defines its area of ​​competence.

Within each Department, the teaching offering flourishes through the Courses. Each course, characterized by a unique identification code and a specific title, is strictly linked to a single Department. This relationship guarantees clear ownership and orderly management: while a Department can provide numerous courses, each individual course belongs to a single departmental structure, thus outlining a precise and easily navigable hierarchy.

The beating heart of teaching is represented by people: Professors and Tutors. Each professor and each tutor is registered in the system with their own identification code and respective name. The real strength of the system lies in the way it orchestrates the collaboration between these figures within the courses. It doesn't just say who teaches what, but describes the specific work team for each individual course. The structure allows you to combine professors, tutors and courses in a flexible and multiple way. This means that a given course can be taught by multiple professors, each perhaps assisted by one or more specific tutors. Likewise, a professor can teach several courses, collaborating with different teams of tutors depending on the needs of each course. This complex network of relationships captures the real dynamics of academic life, precisely tracking who collaborates with whom and in which educational context, thus providing a complete and detailed picture of academic responsibilities and synergies.
