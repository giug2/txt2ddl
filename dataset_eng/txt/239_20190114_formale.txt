The data model describes the management of teams, players and their contractual relationships. The `Team` entity is identified by a unique `Code` and includes attributes for the `Name` and `Foundation Year`. Each team is associated with a single `City`, defined by `Code`, `Name` and `Country`, through a many-to-one relationship. The `Player` entity is represented by a `Serial Number` which acts as the primary key and contains the `Surname` and `First Name` fields. There is a direct many-to-one relationship between `Player` and `Team`, whereby a player belongs to only one team, which can have many players. The `Contract` entity formalizes agreements and is identified by a composite primary key made up of `StartDate` and the player foreign key (`fk_player`). This entity includes the `EndDate` attribute and serves as the hub for one-to-many relationships with the `Team`, `Player`, and `Role` entities (identified by `Code` and `Name`). Therefore, a contract refers to a single player, a single team and a single role, while each team, player and role can be associated with multiple contracts.
