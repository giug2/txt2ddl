The data model describes the structure of an academic management system, structured around five main entities. The central entity is `Course`, which represents a single instance of a course. It is identified by a composite primary key made up of the attributes `Canale`, `fk_materiaa` and `fk_corso_di_studi`. The `Subject` entity, identified by `id_materia`, and the `Course of study` entity, identified by `id_corso_di_studio`, are connected to the `Course` entity via one-to-many relationships; each course belongs uniquely to a subject and a course of study. The `Professor` entity, with primary key `professor_id`, is associated with `Course` through a many-to-many relationship, indicating that a professor can teach multiple courses and a course can be taught by multiple professors. Finally, the `Text` entity, identified by `text_id`, has a many-to-one relationship with `Course`, specifying that multiple courses can adopt the same reference text.
