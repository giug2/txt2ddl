Imagine a system designed to precisely track every stage of a product's life cycle within a complex logistics network. At the heart of this world are our warehouses, each identified by an unmistakable code and located at a specific address. These warehouses are not simple buildings, but nerve centers of activity, populated by our staff. In fact, each employee is registered in the system with a unique serial number and his or her name, and is assigned to work in a specific warehouse. Although a single warehouse can be the operational base for many employees, each staff member reports to a single location, thus ensuring a clear organization of human resources across the territory.

The main activity revolves around product management. Each item we deal with is cataloged with its own unique SKU code and a descriptive name. To ensure maximum traceability, the products are not managed individually, but grouped into production batches. Each batch is identified by a specific code and carries with it the fundamental information of its production date. This structure lets us know that a given product type may have been manufactured in numerous different batches over time, but each individual batch contains exclusively items of a single product type.

The system creates a common thread that links people, places and goods. In fact, each production batch is directly linked to the employee who managed it. This means that, while an employee may be responsible for the movement of various batches during his working day, for each individual batch it is always possible to trace the sole operator who handled it. In this way, a clear and complete chain of responsibility is created: from the product to the batch of which it is part, from the employee who managed that batch to the warehouse where the entire operation took place.
