The data model describes an academic management system. The `Student` entity is identified by a primary key `student_id` and contains the `full_name` attribute. Similarly, the `Teacher` entity is defined by a `teacher_id` primary key and a `full_name` attribute. The `Classroom` entity represents physical classrooms and is identified only by `classroom_id`. The central entity `Course` is characterized by a composite primary key made up of `course_id` and `fk_teacher`. This last field also acts as a foreign key, establishing a many-to-one (N:1) relationship with the `Teacher` entity, whereby each course is associated with a single teacher, while a teacher can teach multiple courses. There is a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between `Course` and `Student`, which allows a student to enroll in multiple courses and a course to have multiple students enrolled. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship connects the `Course` and `Classroom` entities, indicating that a course can be taught in multiple classrooms and a classroom can host multiple courses.
