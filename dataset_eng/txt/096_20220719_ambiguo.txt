So, imagine that there is a big box called Faculty, which just has a name and its own code to recognize it. Inside this box there are other smaller ones, the Departments; each of these is necessarily within a single faculty and has its own name and number. To find out which faculty a department belongs to, there is a direct reference. Then there are the people, the teachers, with name, surname and their personal code. Each teacher is linked to a specific department, he cannot belong to anyone. Indeed, to identify him without making mistakes, sometimes you have to put his code together with that of the department. Same story for the Study Courses: each course, with its name and code, belongs to a specific department. It gets a little more complicated when we look at who teaches what. One teacher can easily lecture in a lot of different courses, and obviously a course of study needs quite a few teachers for all subjects. So there is a big mix: a professor is connected to many courses and a course is connected to many professors.
