So basically here we have a few things put together. There is a sort of list with all the exhibitions, each with its own unique code to avoid confusion, a title and then the dates of when it starts and ends. Then, elsewhere, we keep track of the paintings, also with an identification code, and we note who the author is and more or less what year it is.

The central point is a kind of register, let's call it "Exposition", which serves to connect everything. Inside this thing we put together the code of an exhibition with the code of a painting. This allows us to say that a certain exhibition has a lot of different paintings in it and, at the same time, that a single painting may have been exhibited in multiple exhibitions over time. It's kind of a bridge between the two things.

Finally, there are the rooms. Each room has its own code, a name and maybe how big it is. The interesting thing is that the register that connects the exhibitions to the paintings is also linked to the rooms. In practice, for each painting that ends up in an exhibition, we also say precisely which room it is in. So, each room can contain quite a few of these painting-exhibition combinations.
