So basically all the event stuff is handled here. There is the main event, which is practically identified by its date, and for which we note down who organizes it and what time it starts. Inside each event, then, there are a lot of interventions, such as various speeches or presentations, each with its title and a main speaker who is somewhat of the star, as well as some code to keep track of it.

These interventions are organized into sessions, which are like thematic blocks with their own name. The somewhat confusing thing is that a single intervention could be inserted into several different sessions, and obviously each session has quite a few interventions within itself; for each match, we keep aside the exact slot time.

Then, of course, we have the people speaking, the speakers, each registered with their own name and a code. And there are also companies, with their name and an ID to recognize them. It all ties together with a kind of shareholding register. This system tells us that a certain speaker, who comes from a specific company, has a very specific role in a given speech. So, a company can send a lot of people to speak, and a speaker can be involved in multiple speeches, perhaps with different roles. In short, this is how we know who does what, on whose behalf and during which speech.
