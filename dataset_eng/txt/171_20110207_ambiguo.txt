So basically, we have a system that keeps track of a few things, like books. Each book has its own unique code, such as the barcode, then obviously the title, how many copies we have around and the year it was released. These books are all grouped by genre, you know, like mystery, science fiction, etc. So a genre, which has its own identifying number and name, can contain a lot of books, but each book is only in one genre. Likewise, there are those who publish them, the publishers. They also have a code and a name, and each publisher has printed quite a few books, but a book comes out from only one publisher.

Then there's the whole lending part. Every time a book comes out, a kind of loan record is created, with its code, the date and the current status, such as whether it is still out or returned. The interesting thing is that a book can be loaned a lot of times, and a single loan transaction could even involve multiple books at the same time, it's something that intersects.

Finally, there are the people, the users. Everyone has their card, which is their main identifier, and then we recorded their name and where they live. Clearly, each loan is linked to a specific person. A user can make a lot of loans over time, but each individual loan record is associated with only one user, the one who took out the book.
