The data model describes the organizational structure of musical events. The main entity is **Tour**, identified by a `TourCode` (PK) and characterized by a `TourName`. Each tour is made up of one or more **Concerts**, establishing a one-to-many relationship. A **Concert** is identified by a primary key composed of `ConcertCode` and `fk_tour`, where the latter acts as a foreign key towards the Tour entity; the entity also has a `Data` attribute. Each concert takes place in a single **City**, defining a many-to-one relationship. The **City** entity is identified by `CityCode` (PK) and `CityName`. Multiple **Performances** can take place within a concert (one-to-many relationship), each identified by a `PerformanceCode` (PK) and described by `SetDescription` and `StartTime`. Artist participation is modeled through a many-to-many relationship between the **Performance** entity and the **ArtistParticipation** entity. The latter, identified by `CodicePartecipazione` (PK), is in turn connected in a many-to-one relationship to both an **Artist** and a **Band**. The **Artist** entity is defined by `ArtistCode` (PK) and `ArtName`, while the **Band** entity is defined by `BandCode` (PK) and `BandName`. Finally, each band is associated with a city of origin, via a many-to-one relationship with the **City** entity.
