The data model describes the structure for managing artistic events. The ARTIST entity lists the artists and is uniquely identified by the primary key `artist_id`, also containing the `artist_data` attribute. The TOUR entity represents a tour, identified by `tour_id` (PK) and described by `tour_name`. The LOCATION entity manages performance locations, with `location_id` as the primary key and `location_name` as the descriptive attribute. The central entity EVENT represents a single performance and is identified by a composite primary key made up of the `replica`, `fk_tour` and `fk_location` attributes. The latter two act as foreign keys, establishing many-to-one relationships towards TOUR and LOCATION respectively. A one-to-many relationship is established between ARTIST and EVENT, indicating that an artist can hold multiple events. Finally, the PLAYLIST entity, identified by `playlist_id`, defines a list of songs (`song_title`) and is linked to LOCATION by a one-to-many relationship.
