So, basically we have some kind of system for tracking football leagues. There are tournaments, each with its own code to recognize it and a name, like "Serie A". Each tournament, obviously, has a lot of teams participating, and for each team in that tournament we note its position in the standings and the points it has accumulated. Teams are identified simply by their name.

Then there are the matches, which are the heart of everything. A match is defined by a set of things: the team playing at home, the team playing away and the day it was played. Only these three pieces of information together make us understand which meeting we are talking about. Furthermore, we also know in which stadium the match took place. Clearly, a team plays a lot of games, and for every game they participate in, we keep track of how many goals they score.

Finally, we have the players. Each player belongs to only one team and we identify him uniquely by his jersey number combined with the team he plays for; therefore, the number 10 of one team is different from the number 10 of another. We also know their name and date of birth. The interesting thing is that a player can play in a lot of different games and, for each specific game he took part in, we note down the role he played on the pitch that day.
