The data model describes the structure of a product catalog. The `Manufacturer` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `name`. Each manufacturer is associated with one or more brands through a one-to-many relationship with the `Brand` entity, which is in turn defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `name`. Product classification is managed via the `Category` and `Subcategory` entities. The `Category` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `name`. Each category can contain multiple subcategories, as defined by the one-to-many relationship with the `Subcategory` entity. The latter is identified by a composite primary key made up of `Code` and `fk_category`, where `fk_category` is a foreign key that references the `Category` to which it belongs. The central entity, `Product`, is uniquely identified by a `SKU` (PK) and has a `Name`. Each product is linked to one and only one `Brand` and one and only one `Subcategory` via two distinct one-to-many relationships.
