The data model defines the structure for managing software development projects. The main entity is `Project`, uniquely identified by `ProjectID` and described by `ProjectName` and `Description`. Each project is associated with a single `Team`, a `Repository` and can have multiple `Releases`. A `Team`, identified by its `TeamName` (PK) and characterized by a `Methodology`, is composed of multiple `TeamMember` entities. Each `Team Member` is identified by `MemberID` (PK) and defined by `Last Name` and `First Name`. The `Repository` entity has a `RepositoryID` (PK) and a `URL`, while a `Release` is defined by `ReleaseDate` (PK) and `Version`. Projects are divided into `Module`, identified by `ModuleID` (PK) and a `Name`. The relationship between `Module` and `Project` is many-to-many, with a `Priority` attribute that qualifies the association. Each `Module` is assigned to a single `Developer`, an entity defined by `Developer_ID` (PK), `Last Name` and `First Name`. Technical skills are managed through the associative entity `AssegnazioneRoolo`, identified by `IDAssegnazione`. This entity connects a `TeamMember` to a specific `Technology` within a given `Team`, establishing many-to-one relationships to each of these three entities. The `Technology` entity is identified by `TechnologyID` (PK) and classified by `Name` and `Category`.
