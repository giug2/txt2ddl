So basically, we have some stuff about people. Each guy is uniquely identified by his tax code, and then oh well, we keep his name and surname aside. These people can own cars, indeed, one person can have quite a few, while each car, which we recognize by its license plate, belongs only to one person. We also know the year of each car. Then, every car is of a certain type, a model, right? Like a Panda or a Golf. These models have a name and we stick them in some category, like 'utility' or 'sports'. Of course, there may be a lot of cars that are the exact same model. And where do these models come from? From car manufacturers, of course. We have a list of manufacturers, each with its own identification code, name and country. Each car company can churn out a lot of different models, and to make sure you don't confuse a model with another that perhaps has the same name but is from another brand, the model is identified both by its name and by a direct link to the company that produces it.
