The data model describes the management of bank accounts and associated transactions. The `Current Account` entity represents a single account, uniquely identified by the `Number` attribute which acts as the primary key (PK); also includes information about `Owner`, `Address`, `Starting Balance` and `Ending Balance`. The `Movement` entity models a single operation, identified by a `Code` (PK) and characterized by `Date`, `Detail` and `Amount`. The `Movement Type` entity acts as a registry to classify operations, defined by an `Id` (PK) and a `Description`. There is a one-to-many relationship between `Current Account` and `Movement`, indicating that each account can be associated with multiple movements, but each movement belongs to only one account. Similarly, a one-to-many relationship links `Movement Type` and `Movement`, establishing that each motion is classified according to a single type, which can characterize multiple motions.
