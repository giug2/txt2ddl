The data model describes a commercial offer management system structured around five main entities: Seller, Offer, Catalog, Product and Supplier. The `Seller` entity is identified by a primary key `seller_id`, the `Catalog` entity by `catalog_id`, the `Product` entity by `product_id`, and the `Vendor` entity by `vendor_id`. The `Offer` entity serves as the core of the model, representing the association between the other entities and has a composite primary key made up of the `offer_id`, `fk_product`, `fk_seller` and `fk_catalog` attributes. The defined relationships are as follows: a one-to-many (1:N) relationship exists between `Product` and `Offer`, as well as between `Seller` and `Offer`, indicating that each product and each seller can be associated with multiple offers. There is also a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between `Offering` and `Catalogue`, where multiple offers can be combined into a single catalogue. Finally, a many-to-one (N:1) relationship links `Product` to `Supplier`, establishing that multiple products can be supplied by a single supplier.
