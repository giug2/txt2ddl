So basically here we have a few people and things mixing together. First of all there are the Customers, each with their own personal code to recognize them, most obviously their name and surname. Then there are the Projects, which are always linked to a specific customer; in fact, each project has its own identification number but also carries with it the code of the customer to which it belongs, just to avoid confusion. We also know what a project's name is and when it started. On the other side there is the internal structure, with the Departments, such as the administration or the technical office, each with its own code and name. Employees work within these departments. Each employee has his own name, surname and an identification code, but he is also closely linked to the department in which he is located, so much so that to identify him you need both his code and that of the department. The beauty comes when you put it all together: an employee can be assigned to a lot of different projects, and at the same time a project can have a team of multiple people working on it. Similarly, an employee may deal with quite a few customers, and a single customer may interact with several company employees. In short, it's all a mix of people, clients and jobs to be done.
