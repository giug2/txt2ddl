This system was designed to orchestrate and catalog the universe of a publishing house, precisely tracing the path of each publication, from its conception within a series to the contribution of each individual person involved. At the heart of our publishing world lies the "Editorial Series", the identity that groups similar works under a single flag. Each series is unequivocally defined by its "Collection Title" and is further characterized by a specific "Genre", which orients its audience and content.

A series is not an abstract entity, but comes to life through its "Volumes". Each volume is a fundamental piece of the series to which it belongs, and the bond between the two is indissoluble. A volume is uniquely identified by a combination of its progressive "NumeroVolume" and, above all, by a direct reference to the series to which it is part. This guarantees that two volumes with the same number cannot exist within the same series and that each volume always has a well-defined editorial "home".

Going into even more detail, each volume is in turn composed of a series of "Articles" or chapters. Each article is a separate unit of content, identified by its position, i.e. the "Chapter Number", within the specific volume that hosts it. Naturally, for each article we also keep track of its extension, recording the "Number of Pages" that compose it.

Finally, the real creative engine of this system are the "Collaborators", the people who give shape to the contents with their ingenuity and expertise. Each collaborator is registered in our system with his personal identifier, his "Name" and his "Surname". The real richness of the model lies in how we manage their relationship with the items. An article, in fact, is not the result of the work of a single person, but of a team. For this reason, each article can be linked to multiple collaborators, and the role held is specified for each of them. One collaborator can be the "author" of an article, contributing directly to its writing, while another can act as a "reviewer", ensuring its quality and accuracy. This allows us to have a complete and multifaceted vision of the creative process, recognizing that a single article can have multiple authors and a dedicated team of reviewers, and that the same person can cover different roles in different contexts.
