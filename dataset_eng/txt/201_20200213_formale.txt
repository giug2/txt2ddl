The data model defines a hierarchical structure for automotive information. The root entity is `Automotive Home`, identified by a primary key `auto_home_id` and described by a `name` attribute. The `Brand` entity is connected to this through a one-to-many relationship; each `Automobile Manufacturer` can own multiple brands. The `Brand` entity is defined by a composite primary key that includes its identifier `brand_id` and the foreign key `fk_casa_automobilistica`, as well as a `name` attribute. In turn, each `Brand` is associated with one or more models via the `Model` entity. This entity is identified by a primary key composed of `model_id` and the foreign key `fk_brand`, and has a `name` attribute. Finally, the `Powertrain` entity details the powertrain options for each model. Its primary key is composed of `engine_id` and the foreign key `fk_model`, establishing that each `Model` can have multiple engines associated with it. The `Motorization` entity also includes a `name` attribute for its description.
