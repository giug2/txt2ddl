The data model describes a library management system structured around five fundamental entities. The `Book` entity is uniquely identified by the `ISBN` (PK) attribute and contains descriptive information such as `Title`, `Available Copies` and `Publication Year`. The `Genre` and `Publisher` entities act as classification tables, identified by `GenreID` (PK) and `PublisherID` (PK) respectively, and contain the `GenreName` and `PublisherName` attributes. Each `Book` is associated with a single `Genre` and a single `Publisher` via many-to-one (N:1) relationships. The `User` entity represents the members of the system, identified by the `Card` number (PK) and characterized by `FullName` and `Address`. Transactions are managed by the `Loan` entity, which has `LoanID` as the primary key (PK) and the `LoanData` and `Status` attributes. Each `Loan` is linked to a unique `User` through a many-to-one relationship (N:1). Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship exists between `Book` and `Loan`, indicating that a book can be the subject of multiple loans and a loan can include multiple books, which implies the existence of an association table for their correlation.
