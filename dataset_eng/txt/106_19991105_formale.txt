The data model describes an academic system structured around five fundamental entities: Professor, Subject, Course of Study, Text and Course. The entities Professor, Subject, Course of Study and Text are identified respectively by the primary keys (PK) id_professore, id_materia, id_corso_di_studio and id_testo. The central entity, Course, is identified by a composite primary key made up of the attributes id_corso, fk_materia and fk_corsodistudio. The Course Key structure intrinsically establishes a many-to-one (N:1) relationship to the Course of Study entity and a one-to-many (1:N) relationship from Subject to Course, meaning that each course belongs to a single course of study and a single subject, while a subject can be delivered in multiple courses. The model also defines a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between Professor and Course, whereby a professor can teach multiple courses and a course can be taught by multiple professors. Finally, there is a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between Course and Text, indicating that each course adopts a single text, which can be used by multiple courses.
