The data model defines the structure for managing vehicles and their classifications. The `MARKET SEGMENT` entity categorizes models using a unique `CODE` (PK) and a `DESCRIPTION`. Each `MODEL`, identified by a `CODE` (PK) and described by `LENGTH` and `NUMBER OF SEATS`, belongs to a single `MARKET SEGMENT`, establishing a one-to-many relationship. The `POWER TYPE` entity is defined by a `CODE` (PK) and a `DESCRIPTION`. Each `VERSION` of a model is identified by a `CODE` (PK) and specified by `NAME`, `CILIDE` and `PRICE`; it is uniquely associated with both a `MODEL` and a `POWER TYPE`, configuring two distinct one-to-many relationships. Finally, the `CAR` entity represents the physical instance of the vehicle, identified by the `CHASSIS` (PK) and equipped with the `COLOR` and `DATE` attributes. Each car corresponds to one and only one `VERSION`, in a one-to-many relationship.
