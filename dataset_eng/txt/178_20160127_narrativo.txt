Imagine exploring the beating heart of a university, a system designed to map and understand the entire academic ecosystem in a clear and intuitive way. At the highest level of this organization we find the Departments, the large academic families that define the main areas of knowledge. Each department is a unique entity, identified by its own specific code and known by its distinctive name, such as "Department of Humanities" or "Department of Engineering". The mission of a department is to bring study paths to life; in fact, each individual department can offer and manage numerous degree courses, each dedicated to a specific discipline. However, each degree course, such as "Computer Science" or "Philosophy", belongs exclusively to only one department, thus ensuring clear academic and organizational responsibility. Going down the level, each Degree Course is in turn a well-defined entity, with its own code that distinguishes it from all the others and a name that describes its field of study. It is within these courses that university life takes shape, as each educational path welcomes its own community of students. Finally, we arrive at the protagonist of this narrative: the Student. Each person who embarks on their academic journey is unambiguously identified by a personal identification number. For each student, the system records essential information such as name, residential address and the year of the course they are attending. In this framework, the path of each individual student is closely linked to a single degree course, ensuring that their career is always well defined and traceable. In this way, a logical and coherent map is created that connects each student to his specific degree course, which in turn is managed by a reference department, offering a complete and organized vision of the entire institution.
