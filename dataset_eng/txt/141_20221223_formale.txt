The data model defines five main entities: INSTITUTE, CITY, COUNTRY, RESEARCHER and QUALIFICATION. The INSTITUTE entity is identified by a unique CODE (PK) and contains the NAME and YEAR FOUNDATION attributes. The CITY, COUNTRY and QUALIFICATION entities are described by a CODE (PK) and a NAME. The RESEARCHER entity is identified by a CODE (PK) and has the attributes SURNAME, NAME and DATE OF BIRTH. The relationships structure the model as follows: a many-to-one (N:1) relationship connects each INSTITUTE to a single CITY, and another N:1 relationship connects each CITY to a COUNTRY. Staff affiliation is managed by a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between INSTITUTE and RESEARCHER, establishing that an institute employs multiple researchers, each of whom belongs to a single institute. Finally, a many-to-one relationship (N:1) associates each RESEARCHER with a specific QUALIFICATION.
