The data model defines the structure for managing colleges and associated services. The central entity is `College`, identified by a unique `Code` and characterized by `Name`, `Address` and `Telephone`. Each `College` is located in a specific `Location`, which is in turn defined by a `Code` and a `Name`; a single location can host multiple colleges. Each `Location` is associated with one or more `Airports`, each identified by its own `Code` and `Name`. There is a many-to-many relationship between the `Airport` entity and the `Departure City` entity (identified by `Code` and `Name`), indicating that an airport can serve multiple cities and vice versa. A `College` offers different types of `Stay`, each described by a `Period` which acts as a primary key, a `Price` and a number of `Hours`. Additionally, a college provides multiple `Exams`, where each exam is defined by a unique `Code`, `Name`, `Cost` and is associated with a single college. Finally, the model includes the `Reduction Type` entity (with `Code` and `Name`), linked to `Colleges` via a many-to-many relationship, allowing a college to apply multiple reductions and a reduction to be valid for multiple colleges.
