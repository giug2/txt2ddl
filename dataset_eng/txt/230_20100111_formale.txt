The data model specification defines a system for managing film screenings and events. The `Film` entity is identified by a `FilmCode` (PK) and has a `Title`. The `Director` entity is defined by a `Director Code` (PK) and a `Name`. Each film is associated with a single director, while a director can direct multiple films (N:1 relationship). The `Actor` entity is identified by `ActorCode` (PK) and includes the `FirstName`, `LastName` and `MainRole` attributes. The `ProductionHome` entity is identified by its `Name` (PK) and has a `ManagerProducer`. There is a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between `Production House` and `Actor`, indicating that a production house works with multiple actors and an actor can collaborate with multiple production houses. The system also manages the `Event` entity, identified by `EventCode` (PK) and characterized by `Title` and `Description`. An event is organized by a single `Production House`, which can organize multiple events (relationship N:1). A many-to-many (N:N) relationship exists between `Movie` and `Event`, allowing a movie to be featured in multiple events and an event to include multiple movies. Each event can have multiple projections (1:N relationship); the `Projection` entity is a weak entity identified by a composite primary key made up of `DateTime` and `fk_event`, which acts as a foreign key to `Event`. Finally, the `SalaCinema` entity is defined by `CodiceSala` (PK), `Name` and `PosiASedere`. Each screening takes place in a specific `SalaCinema`, and a room can host multiple screenings (relationship N:1).
