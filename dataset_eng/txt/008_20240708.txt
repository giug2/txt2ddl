The system describes a musical universe centered on songs, each identified by a unique code and characterized by a title 
Each piece associated with an author establishing a direct link with the entity of the composers who are in turn defined by an identification code, a surname and a name 
The musical pieces are organized within concerts which have their own identification code, title and description 
The specific structure of each concert is detailed in the Programming report which associates a piece with a specific concert by specifying its position in the execution order via an integer value. A concert takes the form of specific events, each of which represents a single performance on a certain date and at a specific time
Each event is also characterized by the orchestra that performs it and the theater that hosts it. Theaters are defined by a code and a name while orchestras are uniquely identified by their name and have a headquarters and a director. 
The orchestras are made up of musicians, each registered with a unique serial number, surname and name
The membership of a musician to an orchestra and the instrument he plays within it is managed by the Composition relationship which connects a musician to an orchestra and the instrument he uses 
Finally, musical instruments are an entity in their own right described by an identifying code, a name and a type
