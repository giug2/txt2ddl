The data model defines the structure for managing music competitions within festivals. The main entities are `Festival`, `Band`, `Musician` and `Performance`. The `Festival` entity is identified by a unique `Code` (PK) and has a `Name`. The `Band` entity is identified by its `Name` (PK). Band participation at festivals is modeled by a one-to-many relationship, which tracks performances through the `RANKING_POSITION` and `TOTAL_SCORE` attributes. Each `Band` is composed of multiple musicians, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `Musician` entity. The latter is identified by a composite primary key that includes `Member_ID` and a foreign key `FK_BAND`, uniquely linking it to its band; additional attributes are `Name` and `Date_of_Birth`. The `Performance` entity represents a competitive event between two bands on a specific date. Its primary key is composite, made up of `FK_BAND_MAIN`, `FK_BAND_SFIDANTE` and `Data`, and includes the `Stage` attribute. A band can participate in multiple performances, and for each participation the `VOTES_RECEIVED` are recorded. Finally, a many-to-many relationship connects `Performance` and `Musician`, indicating that a musician can take part in multiple performances and a performance involves multiple musicians; the `ROLE_ON_STAGE` attribute qualifies this relationship, specifying the function of each musician during the event.
