So, basically we have a few lists of things, all connected to each other in a kind of hierarchy. On top of everything there are the Faculties, each with its own identification code, nothing strange. Then, going down a step, we find the Departments; the particular thing is that to identify a specific department its code is not enough, but also that of the faculty to which it belongs. In practice, each department is necessarily linked to a single faculty, but a faculty can obviously contain a lot of different departments. The same logic applies to Study Courses: each course is defined by its own number and the number of the department that hosts it, therefore a course cannot exist on its own, it is necessarily within a department. And, as before, a department can have many courses. Finally, there are the teachers, and here too history repeats itself: to know who we are talking about, we need both their personal code and the code of the course to which it is connected in some way. This means that a lot of teachers can be associated with a single course of study, creating a kind of close bond between them.
