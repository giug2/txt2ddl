This system is designed to create a complete and organized catalog of conferences, offering a clear view of both their geographical location and their thematic scope. The beating heart of this structure is the Conference, every single event is uniquely identified by its own Code and described by a Title that expresses its central topic. A conference, by its nature, needs a place and a theme to be defined.

As regards the geographical dimension, each conference takes place in one and only one city, but the same city can have the honor of hosting numerous events over time. Cities are in turn contextualized within a nation. Each city is identified by a code which, combined with the code of the country it belongs to, makes it unique on a global level. This structure ensures that each city belongs to only one nation, while a nation, of course, can include multiple urban centers.

On the thematic side, the soul of a conference is the subject matter. Each event focuses on a single, specific topic, thus ensuring a clear focus. On the other hand, a single subject can be so relevant that it becomes the central theme of several conferences. To offer an even more structured classification, subjects are grouped into broader categories. Each subject, defined by its own code and name, belongs to one and only one general thematic area, which can contain numerous specific subjects within it. In this way, the system not only archives individual events, but weaves them into a network of knowledge, creating a rich and navigable map that connects places of knowledge with the disciplines that animate them.
