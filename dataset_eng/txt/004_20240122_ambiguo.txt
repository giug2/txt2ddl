So, in this system we have a few things going around. There are managers, departments, assistants and offices, and each of these has its own code to avoid confusion. The thing is, the connections are quite flexible. A manager, for example, can be responsible for multiple departments and, vice versa, a department can report to a lot of different managers. The same story goes for managers and their assistants: a boss can have several helpers, and an assistant can easily lend a hand to more than one manager. And, as you might imagine, assistants are also tied to departments in this somewhat loose way, so an assistant can work for several departments and a department has a nice group of assistants in it. The only slightly more rigid restriction concerns the headquarters: a headquarters can contain a lot of departments, but every single department belongs to one and only one headquarters, it cannot be in two places at the same time.
