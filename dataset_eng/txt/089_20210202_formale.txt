The data model describes a health tracking system based on four main entities: Region, Local Health Authority, Patient and Swab. The `Region` entity is uniquely identified by a `RegID` (PK) and has a `Name` attribute. Each `Region` can contain multiple ASLs, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `ASL` entity. The latter is identified by a `CodASL` (PK) and described by the `Name` and `Address` attributes. Continuing hierarchically, each `ASL` is associated with multiple patients in a one-to-many relationship. The `Patient` entity is identified by its Tax Code (`CF`), which serves as the primary key (PK), and includes the `Name` attribute. Finally, each patient may undergo multiple tests, as described by the one-to-many relationship with the `Swab` entity. The `Swab` entity is identified by a primary key composed of the `CT` and `fk_patientID` attributes, where the latter acts as a foreign key to link the swab to the specific patient. The non-key attributes of `Swab` are `Data` and `Result`.
