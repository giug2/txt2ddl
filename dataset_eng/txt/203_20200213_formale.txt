The data model describes the management of resources and projects in a business context. The `Company` entity is identified by a `company_id` primary key and has a `company_name`. The other main entities are connected to it. The `Team` entity, identified by `id_team`, represents work groups. The `Project` entity is defined by a composite primary key (`project_id`, `fk_company`), establishing an N:1 relationship with `Company`, so each project uniquely belongs to a single company. The `Developer` entity is identified by a composite primary key made up of `developer_id`, `fk_company` and `fk_team`, and contains the `name` and `surname` registry attributes. This structure defines an N:1 relationship with both the `Company` entity and the `Team` entity, binding each developer to a specific company and a single team. Finally, there is a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between `Developer` and `Project`, indicating that a developer can be assigned to multiple projects and a project can involve multiple developers.
