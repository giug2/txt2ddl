The data model defines an academic system composed of four main entities: PROFESSOR, DEPARTMENT, STUDENT and CAMPUS. Each entity is identified by a unique primary key: `pk_professore` for PROFESSOR, `pk_departimento` for DEPARTMENT, `pk_studente` for STUDENT and `id_campus` for CAMPUS. The interactions between the entities are modeled through the following relationships: a many-to-one relationship connects professors to their home department, and a similar relationship links students to their home department. There is a one-to-many relationship between CAMPUS and DEPARTMENT, specifying that a campus can host multiple departments, but a department belongs to only one campus. Finally, a many-to-many relationship between PROFESSOR and STUDENT describes the teaching interaction, indicating that a professor can follow multiple students and a student can be followed by multiple professors, the implementation of which will require an intermediate association table.
