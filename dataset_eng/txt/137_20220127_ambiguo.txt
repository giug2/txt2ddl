So, basically here we have a system that keeps track of a few things, like who works where. There are offices, physical places, and each has its own code to avoid confusion. Inside each office, then, there are various departments or work groups. The particular thing is that a department is defined not only by its name or code, but also by the location in which it is located; in short, a department belongs only to one location. Then there are the assistants, those who work in the departments. They too are tied to a specific post: an assistant is in only one department, although obviously there can be several assistants in one department. Finally, there are the managers, the bosses. Here the matter becomes more intertwined: a manager can very well follow more than one department, and at the same time a department can have more than one boss to report to. It's a kind of shared management, where responsibilities overlap a little.
