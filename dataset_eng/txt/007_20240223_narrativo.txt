This system was designed to create a dynamic and detailed archive of press reviews, transforming a simple collection of news into a network of interconnected information. At the center of everything we find the REVIEW, the starting point of our information journey. Each review is a unique entity, unequivocally defined by the DATE on which it was created, and enriched by details such as the TIME of compilation and the name of the EDITOR who expertly assembled it. Each of these reviews acts as a container for a multitude of ARTICLES, and each single article belongs exclusively to a specific review.

Each ARTICLE is cataloged with its own identifier, a title that summarizes its content and the name of its main author. But the story of an article does not end here. To understand where it was published, the system correlates it with one or more NEWSPAPERS. A newspaper is identified by a unique CODE and its name. The relationship between articles and newspapers is very flexible: an article can be published in multiple newspapers and, of course, a newspaper publishes countless articles. To keep track of this complexity, the system even records the specific Pages where the article appeared in each newspaper, providing a precise reference to its physical or digital location.

The true heart of the system, however, lies in the profound understanding of who is behind each written piece. We do not limit ourselves to knowing the AUTHOR, identified by his CODE and NAME, but we also explore his professional context. For this reason, we introduce the concept of ORGANIZATION, i.e. the institution or organization for which an author works, also identified by a CODE and a NAME. The magic happens in the connecting entity "AUTHOR WITH RELATIONSHIP AND ROLE", which unites an author to an institution specifying his ROLE in that precise context (for example, "researcher", "journalist", "consultant"). This unique combination of author, institution and role is what is attributed to the writing of an article. In this way, a single author can have multiple affiliations and roles over time or simultaneously, and an institution can have numerous affiliated authors. Each article, therefore, is the product of the work of one or more of these well-defined professional figures, allowing us to understand not only who wrote a piece, but also in what capacity and on behalf of which institution they did it, thus creating a rich and detailed map of the media landscape.
