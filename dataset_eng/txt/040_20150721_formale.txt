The data model defines five main entities: Professor, Course, Study Program, Subject and Text. The Professor entity is identified by a `professor_id` attribute which acts as the primary key. Similarly, the entities Course of Study, Subject and Text are identified respectively by the primary keys `id_corso_di_studio`, `id_materia` and `id_testo`. The Course entity has a more complex structure, with a composite primary key made up of the `fk_corso_di_studio`, `fk_materia` and `Canale` attributes, which uniquely identify it. The relationships between entities are one-to-many. Each Professor can be associated with multiple Course instances. A Course of Study comprises a set of Courses, and a Subject may be taught in several Courses. Finally, each Subject can have one or more Reference Texts associated with it. The `fk_corso_di_studio` and `fk_materia` keys in the Course entity also act as foreign keys, implementing relationships with the Course of Study and Subject entities.
