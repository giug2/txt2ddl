So, basically we have some stuff that involves research institutes or something like that. Each institute has its own identification code, a name and the year in which it was founded. These places aren't up in the air, eh; each institute is located in a specific city, and obviously there can be multiple institutes in a city. In turn, the cities, which are registered with a code and a name, are part of a nation, also with its own code and name. So, you know, a lot of cities can be in the same country, but a city is just there. Then there are the people, the researchers, with their data: a personal code, surname, name and when they were born. The interesting thing is that each researcher is tied to only one institute, the guy who works there, while an institute can have a lot of researchers under it. Finally, these researchers have a kind of label, a qualification, which is the same for many of them but each individual researcher has only one. Think “senior researcher” or “apprentice,” things like that. In practice it is a system for keeping track of who works where, in which city and country the institute is located, and what role each person has.
