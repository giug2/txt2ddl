Imagine a digital academic universe, designed to map and organize an institution's entire educational journey. At the highest level of this structure we find the **Course of Study**, which represents the large container of a degree course, such as "Computer Engineering" or "Classical Literature". Each Course of Study is a world unto itself, a defined path made up of fundamental stages. These stages are the **Subjects**, or rather the abstract disciplines that constitute the knowledge of that path, such as "Mathematical Analysis" or "Latin Literature". A single Matter, however, can come to life in different ways and at different times; for this reason, each Subject can be delivered through numerous specific **Courses**. The Course is the beating heart of the system: it is the concrete manifestation of a Subject in a given context, teaching that takes place in a classroom or online. Every single Course belongs unequivocally to a single Study Course, contributing to building the training plan.

Guiding the students on this learning journey are the **Professors**. The relationship between them and the Courses is dynamic and flexible: a professor can dedicate himself to teaching multiple Courses, and, at the same time, a single Course can benefit from the expertise of multiple teachers, perhaps for different modules or in co-teaching. Finally, each learning path is based on solid study materials. For this reason, each specific Course is associated with a reference **Text**. It is important to note that, although a Course is based on a main textbook, the same Text can be a fundamental pillar for many other Courses, becoming a transversal reference point for different disciplines or training paths. In this way, the system interweaves degree paths, subjects, practical lessons, teachers and teaching materials into a clear and navigable network of knowledge.
