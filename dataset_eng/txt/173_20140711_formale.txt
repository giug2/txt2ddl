The data model defines the structure for managing software projects. The central entity is `Project`, identified by `Project_ID` (PK) and characterized by `Project_Name` and `Description`. Each project is divided into multiple `Sprints`, whose primary key is composed of `Date_End` and `fk_project`, establishing a one-to-many relationship with `Project`; each sprint also includes a `Release_Version`. Projects use a set of software `Modules`, identified by `Module_ID` (PK) and `Name`, through a many-to-many relationship. Each `Module` is developed by a single `Developer`, identified by `Developer_ID` (PK), who may be responsible for multiple modules. Operational management is entrusted to a `Team`, uniquely identified by `Team_Name` (PK), which can manage multiple projects in a one-to-many relationship. The teams are made up of `Team Members`, identified by the `Serial Number` (PK) and with `Name` and `Surname` attributes. A member's membership in a team is defined in the associative entity `Assignment`, whose primary key is composed of the foreign keys `fk_team_member` and `fk_team`. Finally, the `Technology` entity, with key `Technology_ID` (PK), has a one-to-many relationship with `Assignment`, indicating that a technology can be associated with multiple personnel assignments.
