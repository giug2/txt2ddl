The system manages a catalog of books, where each book is identified by a unique ISBN code and described by a title, the number of copies available and its year of publication.
Each book belongs to a specific literary genre and is associated with a specific publisher.
Genres are in turn defined by an ID and genre name, while each publisher is registered with its own ID and name.
There are constraints that guarantee data consistency, ensuring that the genre and publisher assigned to a book actually exist in the respective records.
The system also tracks library users, each registered with a membership number, full name and address.
A user can make loans, each characterized by an identifying ID, a date and a status (e.g. 'active', 'repaid').
Again, a constraint ensures that each loan is linked to a validly registered user.
A loan detail structure is used to detail the contents of a loan.
Each detail relates a loan to a single book, specifying which volume was taken.
Special constraints guarantee that both the loan and the book to which each detail refers are present and valid within the system.
