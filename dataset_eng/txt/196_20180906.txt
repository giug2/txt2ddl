The system described manages the patients' personal information, storing for each patient the tax code, surname, name and date of birth.
Each patient can have one or more hospitalizations over time, which are uniquely identified by a Hospitalization ID and are associated with a specific department.
There is a direct link between a hospitalization and the patient, since each hospitalization must refer to a valid tax code present in the patient registry.
The organizational component is structured on several levels: each hospitalization takes place in a hospital, identified by its unique code.
The hospitals are in turn characterized by the name of the structure and the local health authority they belong to.
This ASL is linked by its code to an entity that describes the Local Health Authorities, each defined by its own code and official name.
For each hospitalization, one or more invoices are issued, each identified by a unique invoice number and containing the date of issue and the amount.
Each invoice is unambiguously associated with a single hospitalization, thus ensuring that each charge is correctly linked to the hospitalization to which it refers.
Finally, the system tracks payments made to settle invoices.
Each payment is recorded with its own transaction ID, date and amount paid, and is strictly linked to a specific existing invoice, ensuring the integrity and traceability of financial transactions.
