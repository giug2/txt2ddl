The data model describes a system for managing film screenings and award nominations. The `Director` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and contains the `Surname` and `Name` attributes; each director is associated with a single `City`. In turn, the `City` entity, defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`, belongs to a single `Nation`, identified by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. The `Cinema` entity, with `Code` (PK) and `Name` attributes, is located in a single `Country`. The `Projection` entity is identified by a primary key composed of `Code`, `fk_genre` and `fk_cinema`, and has a `Data` attribute; each screening is associated with a single `Film Genre` (identified by `Code` and `Name`) and takes place in a single `Cinema`. The `Nomination` associative entity links a director to a specific screening and has the `Award Earned` attribute. Its primary key is composed of the foreign keys `fk_projection` and `fk_director`, establishing that a nomination refers to only one director and one screening, while a director and a screening can have multiple nominations. Finally, each `Nomination` is associated with a single `Country`.
