So, in practice we have a kind of customer registry, where the most important thing to find them is their tax code. Then, obviously, we also note down their name, surname, when they were born and the address where they usually live, in short, their main home. On the other hand there is the whole matter of current accounts, and each of these has its own specific number that makes it unique. The point is that these two things intersect: a customer can be linked to a lot of different accounts, and at the same time a single account can be in the name of multiple people, such as a family or partners. When this link is created between a person and an account, sometimes we also write down a particular address, perhaps different from the customer's main address, which is used specifically for communications linked to that specific account.
