The data model describes the organizational structure of an academic institution, defining the main entities and their interconnections. The fundamental entities are DEPARTMENT and CAMPUS, each identified by a unique primary key, `department_id` and `campus_id` respectively. The PROFESSOR entity is defined by a composite primary key made up of `professor_id`, `fk_department` and `fk_campus`. This structure means that a professor record represents a unique association of an individual with a specific Department and Campus, to which it is linked via corresponding foreign keys. At a conceptual level, an N:N cardinality relationship between Professor and Department is specified. Finally, the PhD STUDENT entity is identified by a composite primary key that includes `phd student_id`, `fk_professor`, and `fk_campus`, indicating that a PhD student is uniquely defined in the context of a supervisor and a campus. There is an N:1 cardinality relationship between PhD Student and Professor, establishing that multiple PhD students can be supervised by a single professor.
