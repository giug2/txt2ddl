So, basically we have a few lists to keep track of our stuff. There is a list of warehouses, each with its identification code, the name of the location, and more or less the year in which it was opened. Obviously, each warehouse is not floating in thin air, it is linked to a specific municipality, and multiple warehouses can be in the same place. This municipality has its ISTAT code that identifies it and its name, in short, the usual stuff. In turn, each municipality is part of one and only one province, which we recognize by its acronym and full name. Inside the warehouses, then, there is all the goods, the products. It works like this: a warehouse can contain a lot of different products, but every single product, with its SKU code, is registered as if it were in that specific warehouse and that's it. For each product we know the name, brand and when it was released on the market. Finally, to avoid confusion, each product is placed in a specific category, such as "electronics" or "household", which has its own number and name. Clearly, multiple products can end up under the same category.
