The data model describes four main entities: Customer, Item, Product and Brand. The Customer entity is uniquely identified by a Customer Code (PK) and contains the Surname and Name attributes. The Item entity represents a specific physical instance, identified by a Series Number (PK) and characterized by a Production Year. The Brand entity is defined by a Brand Code (PK), a Name and the Country of Origin. The Product entity is identified by a composite primary key made up of the ProductName and fk_brand attributes, and includes the Type attribute. The relationships between the entities are as follows: A one-to-many relationship exists between Brand and Product, where the fk_brand attribute in Product acts as a foreign key to Brand. An additional one-to-many relationship connects a Product to many Items, indicating that each Item is an instance of a single Product. Finally, a one-to-many relationship between Customer and Item is defined, whereby each customer can be associated with multiple items.
