This system was designed to map and manage the complex network of collaborations and assignments within a large, structured organization. At the center of everything we find physical entities and the people who populate them. The organization is divided into different offices, each identified by a unique code, which represent our main locations, such as buildings or city branches. Within each of these locations, working life takes place in the offices. It is important to note that an office does not exist in the abstract; it is unequivocally defined not only by its identification number, but also by the specific location in which it is located, creating an indissoluble link between the physical space and its macro-area to which it belongs.

The people who animate these spaces are divided into two key roles: Managers and Assistants, each registered in the system with their own personal identifier. The real strength of this model, however, lies not in the simple list of places and people, but in the way it describes their dynamic interactions. The heart of the system is a complex relationship that binds together a Manager, an Assistant and an Office. This is not a simple assignment, but a flexible mapping of operational collaborations: a single Manager can in fact collaborate with multiple Assistants, and these collaborations can take place in different offices. Similarly, an Assistant can support multiple Managers, and a single Office can be the scene of the activities of multiple manager-assistant pairs. In this way, the system does not simply say who works where, but tracks the specific synergy that is created between a manager, his support and the physical place where this collaboration comes to life.
