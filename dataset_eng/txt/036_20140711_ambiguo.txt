So, in this system we have some stuff that goes around a university, more or less. There is the matter of the Departments, which we recognize by their name and then we also write down the address somewhere. A lot of professors are connected to each department, but each professor works for only one department, it cannot be split. We identify professors with a unique code and obviously they also have a name. Then there are the Courses, which are the subjects, in short. These also have a code to avoid confusion and a title that makes it clear what we are talking about. The slightly more complex part is the Editions: in practice, a course can be held several times, perhaps in different years, and each of these is an edition. Therefore, a course has many editions, but one edition is always and only of that particular course. Each edition has its specific code and the year in which it takes place. And who holds it? Well, a professor. A single professor can be responsible for quite a few different editions, but each edition is entrusted to a single teacher, to be clear.
