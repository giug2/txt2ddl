Basically, we keep track of a few things in here, mainly the people who use the system and the orders they place. There is the entire user registry part, where for each one the fundamental thing is the email, which is a bit like their unique code to recognize them. Then, of course, there's the usual information like first name, last name, when he registered and the address where we send him the stuff. On the other hand there is the whole question of orders, and each of these has its own identification code to avoid confusion. The logic is that one person, one user, can place a lot of orders over time, while every single order is only ever linked to that specific person who placed it.
