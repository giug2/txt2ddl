So, basically we have a few things that revolve around theaters and shows. There is a list of authors, with the usual data such as name, surname and dates of birth and death. Each author, of course, may have written a lot of plays, each with its own title and the year in which it was created. However, a work is always linked to a single author, let's not get confused.

Then there are the actual theaters, with their name, address and telephone number. A theater can stage quite a few shows, which here we call "plays on the bill". Each of these has a period, with a start and an end date, and always refers to a specific play and is held in only one theatre.

Speaking of money, the matter is a bit of a crossroads of things. There is a concept of "Price", which is connected to a specific theater. But the final price also depends on other variables. There is a "Show Category", which could be like the first show, the afternoon show, things like that, and there is also the "Seat Type", that is, if you are in the stalls or in the gallery. Essentially, a price is a result of where you sit, what type of show it is, and what theater you're in. Finally, there are the reductions, the discounts. A theater can offer different types of discounts, and the same type of discount, such as a student discount, can be applied in multiple theaters, so there is a lot of possibilities.
