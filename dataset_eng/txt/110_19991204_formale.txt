The data model defines the structure for managing personal data, vehicles and car manufacturers. The `Person` entity is uniquely identified by the `Fiscal Code` (PK) and includes the `Surname` and `First Name` attributes. The `House` entity represents a manufacturer, identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Name` and `country`. The `Model` entity is identified by a primary key composed of its `Name` and the foreign key `fk_casa`, which refers to the `Home` it belongs to; it also has the `Category` attribute. The `Car` entity represents a single vehicle, identified by the `Plate` (PK) and characterized by the `Year` of registration. The relationships defined are one-to-many: a `House` can produce multiple `Model`, a `Model` can be associated with multiple `Cars` and a `Person` can own multiple `Cars`. Each car can be traced back to a single model and a single owner.
