The data model is structured around three main entities: Mission, Line and Recommendation. The `Mission` entity is uniquely identified by a `Number` attribute, which acts as the primary key (PK), and has a descriptive `Name` attribute. The `Line` entity represents a specific component of a mission and is linked to it by a one-to-many (1:N) identifying relationship; its primary key is in fact composite, formed by the `Number` attribute (specific to the line) and the `fk_mission` attribute, which acts as a foreign key (FK) referencing the `Mission` entity. Additional attributes of `Line` are `Name` and `Budget`. The third entity, `Recommendation`, is an independent entity identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by a `Name`. Finally, a many-to-many (N:N) relationship is defined between the `Mission` and `Recommendation` entities, meaning that each mission can be associated with multiple recommendations and each recommendation can be relevant to multiple missions, the implementation of which will require an association table.
