Welcome to the beating heart of the cinematic world, a system designed to capture every nuance of film creation and distribution, from the first draft of a screenplay to the final applause in the theater. At the center of this universe is the **Film**, a work of art identified by a unique **Code** and described by its **Title** and its **Plot**. Each film is the result of the vision of a specific **Production House**, recognized by its **Name** and with its own **Headquarters** and a **Director** of reference; although a production company can create countless films, each film is born under the aegis of only one of them. The narrative of a film is constructed through a sequence of **Scenes**, each with its own **Code** and **Title**. It is essential to know that a film is made up of many scenes and the same scene could theoretically be part of multiple films, and for this reason the system keeps track of the precise **ORDER** of each scene within each film, ensuring the coherence of the story. Behind each scene there is the pen of a **Screenwriter**, identified by a **Code** and a **Name**, who can write multiple scenes. A film would not be complete without its performers: each **Actor**, registered with a unique **Number**, a **Name** and a **Surname**, brings the characters to life. The link between an actor and a production is managed through **Participation**, a key concept identified by a **Code**. This participation establishes that an actor is involved in a project of a specific production company and defines the **Role** he plays, in turn described by a **Code** and a **Name**. An actor can have many participations in his career, but each participation ties him to a specific role for that production. Finally, the finished work reaches its audience through **Screening**, a unique event defined by its **Date** and **Time**. A single film can be shown countless times, and each screening takes place in a specific **Cinema**, which is in turn identified by a **Code** and a **Name**. A cinema can host many screenings, but each individual screening takes place in a single room, thus closing the circle that brings the magic of cinema from the creator to the viewer.
