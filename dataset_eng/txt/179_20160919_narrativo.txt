Imagine a system designed to carefully and precisely catalog the entire universe of a book collection, starting from the abstract idea of ​​the work up to the physical object you hold in your hands. The beating heart of this structure is the BOOK entity, which represents the literary work in its essence, identified by a unique BOOK_ID code and described by its ORIGINAL_TITLE, the AUTHOR who created it and the PUBLICATION_YEAR. In order to organize this vast world of stories, each BOOK is associated with a single LITERARY_GENRE, in turn defined by a GENRE_ID and its GENRE_NAME, and is published by a specific PUBLISHING_HOUSE, identified by a PUBLISHER_ID and its name. Of course, a single publishing house or literary genre can encompass countless different books. But a book is not just an idea; takes shape in the real world through his publications. This is why each literary work, or BOOK, can come to life through numerous different EDITIONS over time. Each EDITION is an entity in itself, marked by its unique ISBN code worldwide, and has its own TITLE (which could be a translation), a precise NUMBER_PAGES and a PRICE. Each edition is characterized by a specific physical FORMAT, such as "hardcover" or "paperback", described by a FORMAT_ID and a DESCRIPTION, and while one format type can be used for countless different editions, a single edition only ever has one format. Finally, the journey ends with the tangible object. A single edition, such as the 2010 paperback edition of a famous novel, can exist in multiple physical instances in the warehouse or on the shelf. Each of these is a BOOK_COPY, individually tracked with its COPY_ID, and of which we know the CONSERVATION_STATUS and the PURCHASE_DATE, inextricably linked to the specific edition from which it comes. In this way, the system captures the entire knowledge chain, from the author's inspiration to the specific copy ready to be read.
