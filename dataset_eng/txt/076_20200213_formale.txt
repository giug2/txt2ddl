The data model describes a hierarchically organized academic structure. At the highest level is the `School` entity, identified only by its `school_id` (PK). Each `School` is associated with one or more `Departments` in a one-to-many relationship; the `Department` entity is identified by a composite primary key made up of `department_id` and the foreign key `fk_school`. In turn, each `Department` manages multiple `Professor` and `Course of Study` entities. The `Professor` entity is defined by a composite primary key (`professor_id`, `fk_department`), which uniquely ties it to a single department. Similarly, the `Course of Study` entity is identified by a composite primary key (`id_corso_di_studio`, `fk_dipartimento`), establishing its affiliation to a single department. A many-to-many relationship is also defined between `Professor` and `Course of Study`, indicating that a professor can teach multiple courses and a course can be taught by multiple professors.
