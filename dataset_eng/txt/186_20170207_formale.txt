The data model defines an academic management system structured around four main entities: Teacher, Student, Exam and Degree Course. The Teacher entity is identified by a primary key `id_docente`, while the CorsoDiLaurea entity is uniquely defined by its primary key `id_corso_laurea`. The Student entity has a composite primary key, consisting of the attributes `matricola_studente` and `fk_corso_laurea`, indicating that a student's identifier is unique only within his/her specific degree course. This structure implements a many-to-one (N:1) cardinality relationship between Student and Degree Course. Similarly, the Exam entity is identified by a primary key composed of `exam_id` and `fk_teacher`, establishing a many-to-one (N:1) relationship with the Teacher entity, where each exam is associated with a single teacher. Finally, the model predicts a many-to-many (N:N) cardinality relationship between the Student and Exam entities, which implies that a student can take multiple exams and an exam can be taken by multiple students.
