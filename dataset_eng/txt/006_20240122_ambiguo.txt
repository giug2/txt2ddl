Basically, we have a system that manages the organization of a company. There are the locations, which are the physical places, the offices, and each has its own unique code to avoid confusion. Inside one of these offices there can be a lot of departments, but each department is necessarily in one and only one office, it cannot be scattered around. Then there are the people who work there: we have managers, bosses, each with their own identifier. This is a bit fluid, because a manager can be responsible for multiple departments, and at the same time, a department can have more than one boss. Helping out are the assistants, who also have their own code. Here too the situation is quite intertwined: a manager can have several assistants who support him and an assistant can easily work for multiple managers. Finally, assistants are also tied to departments; in fact, an assistant can be assigned to various departments and, obviously, a department needs quite a few assistants. In short, it's all a great connection between people and offices.
