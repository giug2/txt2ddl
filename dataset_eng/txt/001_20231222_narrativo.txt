Welcome to the beating heart of a system designed to orchestrate the complex and fascinating world of air travel, transforming the logistics of the skies into a coherent and traceable story. Our narrative begins from the geographical foundations of the world: every journey has an origin and a destination, rooted in specific locations. The system maps the globe starting from the NATIONS, each identified by a unique code and name. Within each nation, we find a mosaic of CITIES, each of which can host one or more AIRPORTS, the real hubs of this global network. In this scenario the various airlines operate, also linked to a nation they belong to and recognized through an identification code. Each company manages a series of FLIGHTS, which are not simple trips, but represent the structured air routes that connect the world; a flight is unambiguously identified not only by its number, but by the combination of that number with the company operating it, thus distinguishing the flight of one company from that of another with the same number. Of course, a flight cannot exist without an aircraft: the system precisely distinguishes between the AIRCRAFT TYPE, which describes the generic model as an "Airbus A320", and the specific AIRCRAFT, i.e. the individual physical aircraft with its unique registration code. Each aircraft type can correspond to numerous individual aircraft, and each flight route has a default aircraft type associated with it. The true intelligence of the system emerges in the management of time planning. We start from the DAY entity, which simply catalogs the days of the week. The connection between the route and its frequency occurs via the FLIGHT ON THE DAY, a crucial entity that establishes on which day of the week a given flight is scheduled to operate, also specifying the departure and arrival airports for that day. Finally, from the weekly planning we move on to the real event with the ACTUAL FLIGHT. This entity captures the concrete instance of a flight on a specific DATE, recording the precise actual departure and arrival times and, crucially, associating the specific AIRCRAFT that physically traveled that route with that journey, thus transforming an abstract plan into a concrete and monitorable event from take-off to landing.
