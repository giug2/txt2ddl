The data model defines a hierarchical structure for a product catalog, starting with the `Category` entity, identified by a `CategoryID` primary key (PK) and containing the `Name` attribute. Each `Category` is associated with one or more instances of the `Subcategory` entity according to a one-to-many (1:N) relationship. The `Subcategory` entity is characterized by a composite primary key, made up of `CodSottocategoria` and `fk_categoria`, where the latter acts as a foreign key (FK) towards `Category`. The additional attributes of `Subcategory` are `Name` and `Description`. In turn, each `Subcategory` can contain multiple products, establishing a one-to-many (1:N) relationship with the `Product` entity. The `Product` entity is uniquely identified by its `SKU` PK and has a `Name` attribute. Finally, each `Product` can be associated with multiple reviews through a one-to-many (1:N) relationship with the `Review` entity. The `Review` entity is defined by the primary key `ReviewID` and the `Date` and `Rating` attributes.
