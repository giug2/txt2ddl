So, basically we have a few things that revolve around a university. There are faculties, you know, macro-areas such as Engineering or Literature, and each has its own code to be recognised. Then there are the departments, which are another subdivision, and they also have their own identification number. Courses of study, such as a three-year degree in something, are closely linked to a faculty; in fact, to identify them in a unique way, their code is not enough, but it is also necessary to know which faculty they belong to. In short, a faculty has quite a few courses within it, but a course is only in one faculty. Finally, there are the teachers, the profs. They are a little more complex: each teacher is connected to both a department and a faculty. This means that a lot of professors can be part of the same department and faculty. To be sure who we are talking about, a professor's identifier is a mix of his personal code, that of his faculty and that of his department, all three together.
