This system was designed to orchestrate and archive in detailed detail the world of academic and professional conferences. At the top of the structure we find the "Series", a conceptual container that groups together events related over time, such as a cycle of annual conferences; each series is uniquely identified by a code and has a distinctive title. Each series can give rise to numerous "Congresses", which represent the individual editions of the event. A conference is uniquely defined by its own code and by the code of the series to which it belongs, and is characterized by a specific date on which it takes place. Each conference takes place in a specific "City", and naturally, the same city can host many different conferences over time. The internal organization of a conference is punctuated by "Sessions": each conference is divided into one or more sessions, each with its own identification code, a thematic title and a start time. The beating heart of these events are people and their skills. The system therefore manages a registry of "People", each registered with a code and a name, and at the same time a catalog of "Institutions", such as universities or companies, also identified by a code, a name and associated with the "City" in which they are based. The connection between the participants and the congress activities occurs through a crucial entity called "Person with Affiliation". This does not simply represent a person, but the instance of a person participating on behalf of a specific institution. Each "Person with Affiliation" record, with its own code, creates a unique bridge between a "Person" and an "Institution". It is precisely through this bond that active participation is modeled: a single session can see the intervention of multiple people, each with their own affiliation, and, in the same way, the same person affiliated with an institution can participate or present in different sessions, creating a rich and flexible network of interconnections that faithfully reflects the dynamics of a conference.
