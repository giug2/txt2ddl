The data model defines an academic management system. The central entity is the `Course`, uniquely identified by `Course_ID`. Each course is associated with a single `Level` (identified by `Cod_Level`), belongs to a single `Department` (identified by `Cod_Dept`) and is taught by a specific `Teacher` (identified by `ID_Teacher`). The `Teacher` entity has a `Name` attribute and is associated with a unique `Role`, which is defined by `Role_Code` and `Description`. The entities `Level`, `Department` and `Classroom` are master data, each with a primary identifier code and a `Description` attribute. A `Course` is divided into one or more `Lessons`, where each lesson is identified by a composite primary key that includes `Num_Lesson` and the foreign key of the course it belongs to (`fk_course`), establishing a dependency relationship. Each `Lesson` takes place in a specific `Room`, identified by `Cod_Aula`.
