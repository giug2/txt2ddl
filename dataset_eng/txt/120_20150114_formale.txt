The data model describes a structure for managing electoral information, divided into five main entities: Constituency, Region, Elected, Election and Person. The `College` entity is uniquely identified by a `Number` attribute that acts as the primary key (PK). The `Region` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `name` attribute. The `Election` entity is characterized by a `Year` as the primary key and a `voters` attribute. Finally, the `Elect` and `Person` entities are modeled separately, each identified by a single `Code` attribute as the primary key. The defined relationships establish that each `College` belongs to only one `Region`, configuring an N:1 cardinality relationship. There is also a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between `Constituency` and `Election`, meaning that a constituency participates in multiple elections and an election takes place across multiple constituencies. The entities `Elected` and `Person` do not have explicit relationships with the other entities of the schema.
