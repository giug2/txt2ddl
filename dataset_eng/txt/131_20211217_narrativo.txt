This system has been meticulously designed to orchestrate and monitor complex strategic initiatives, offering a clear and structured view of the objectives and resources deployed. The beating heart of the model is the **Mission**, which represents a major strategic objective, a fundamental pillar of a broader action plan. To ensure flawless and unambiguous management, each Mission is identified by a unique number and accompanied by a name that clearly describes its purpose and scope of intervention. However, a Mission does not remain an abstract concept; comes to life and materializes through one or more operational **Lines**. Each Line can be imagined as a specific project, an area of ​​intervention or a chapter of expenditure that directly contributes to the success of the mother Mission to which it is inextricably linked. Each Line is in turn defined by its own identification number within its Mission, by a descriptive name and, crucially, by a dedicated budget that establishes its financial resources. The **Recommendations** intervene to guide and influence the entire strategic system, which can be considered as guiding principles, quality standards or transversal directives to be respected. Each Recommendation is identified by a unique code and a clear name. The true strength of the system emerges from the flexibility with which these elements interconnect: a single Mission may in fact have to adhere to multiple different Recommendations, while, at the same time, the same Recommendation, such as a principle of environmental sustainability, can be applied to numerous Missions, guaranteeing coherence and synergy to the entire strategy.
