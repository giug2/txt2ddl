So basically we handle two main things here. On one side there is a sort of list of all the current accounts, and each one has its own specific number that identifies it, you know, so as not to cause confusion. On the other side we have the people, the customers. We keep aside the usual things for each person: name, surname, when they were born and above all the tax code, which is the one that cannot be the same for anyone else and we need to be sure of who we are dealing with. The slightly peculiar thing is how they connect to each other. A customer can have more than one account, obviously, but even a single account can be registered in the name of multiple people, such as a family account. And there is another detail: when we put together a person and an account, we also note down an address, which is not necessarily that of the customer in general, but is precisely linked to that specific combination between that person and that account.
