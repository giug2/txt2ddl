This system was designed to map and manage the complex network of relationships within a modern business organization, focusing on the hierarchical, operational and support structure. The architecture starts from the physical foundations, represented by the different SITE in which the company has a presence. Each HEADQUARTERS acts as a container for the various operational units, the DEPARTMENTS. The relationship here is very clear and defined: each DEPARTMENT is located in one and only one LOCATION, thus providing an immediate vision of the geographical distribution of the activities, while a single LOCATION can naturally host a large number of different departments.

Within this structure, we find the key figures who animate it. MANAGERS are responsible for strategic and operational guidance. The model is designed to be extremely flexible, recognizing that leadership is not always a one-to-one relationship. In fact, a single MANAGER can have responsibility for multiple DEPARTMENTS, allowing transversal supervision of related areas. At the same time, a particularly large or strategic DEPARTMENT can be entrusted to the joint leadership of several MANAGERS, creating a co-management system.

Supporting the entire organizational machine are the ASSISTANTS, whose role is modeled with the same flexibility. An ASSISTANT is not necessarily tied to just one person or department, but can act as a shared resource to maximize efficiency. This means that an ASSISTANT can provide support to multiple MANAGERS at the same time, and, vice versa, a MANAGER can make use of the collaboration of a team of multiple ASSISTANTS. This logic also extends to the relationship with the operational units: an ASSISTANT can serve for several DEPARTMENTS and, in the same way, a DEPARTMENT can count on the help of multiple assistants to manage the administrative workload. This creates a dynamic ecosystem in which people and functions are fluidly interconnected, reflecting the reality of a collaborative and cross-functional work environment.
