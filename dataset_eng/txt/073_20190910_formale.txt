The data model defines the structure for managing care practices. The `Assisted` entity represents the customer, uniquely identified by a `Code` attribute which acts as a primary key and completed by the `Surname` and `Name` personal data attributes. There is a one-to-many relationship between the `Client` entity and the `Practice` entity, meaning that each client can own multiple cases, while each case belongs to a single client. The `Practice` entity, which represents the file, is identified by an `ID` as the primary key and has a `Data` attribute. In turn, each `Practice` can contain one or more performances, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `Performance` entity. The latter describes a single service entry and is characterized by a composite primary key, formed by the `fk_practice` and `Row` attributes, where `fk_practice` acts as a foreign key towards the `Practice` entity. The descriptive attributes of the `Service` include `Description`, `Date`, `Amount` and `Subject`.
