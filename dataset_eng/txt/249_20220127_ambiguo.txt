So, in this system there's some stuff that goes around the university, more or less. We have professors, who need a lot of information put together to be uniquely identified: their personal code is not enough, but they also need to know which department and which campus they are connected to at that moment. Then there are the departments, much simpler, each has its own code and so on. Same thing for the campuses, each location has its own number and you don't think about it anymore. Finally, there are the tutors, and they too are a bit complicated: each tutor has his own ID, but this alone is not enough, because he is strictly linked to a specific professor and a specific campus, as if it were a fixed pairing. As regards connections, the situation is this: a professor can easily be part of different departments, and at the same time a department obviously has a nice group of professors within it, it's a fairly free and cross-relationship. Instead, the link between tutors and professors is more hierarchical: a professor can have a lot of tutors under him, but each individual tutor refers to a single professor, he cannot be the assistant of multiple people at the same time.
