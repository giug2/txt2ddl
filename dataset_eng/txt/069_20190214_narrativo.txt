This system was designed to precisely orchestrate and track the entire logistics process, putting the concept of `Shipping` at the center of everything. Each shipment represents a unique journey, identified by its specific `ID`, which connects a sender to a destination. The heart of our organization is the `Sender`, a person or company identified by a unique `CM` code and a `Name`. To better understand our customer base, each sender belongs to only one `Category` (such as "Business" or "Individual"), defined by a `CCM` code and a description, although a category can obviously group together numerous senders. A single sender has the ability to initiate countless shipments over time, but each shipment always originates from a single, well-defined sender.

Each expedition is characterized by fundamental attributes that define its nature and destination. First of all, each shipment is associated with a specific `Type` (for example "Standard", "Express", "Insured"), identified by a `CT` code and a description, thus allowing you to manage different service methods; one type of service can of course be applied to a large number of shipments. Furthermore, each shipment is directed to one and only one `Destination`, also cataloged with a `CDes` code and description, although a single destination can be the arrival point for multiple shipments from different senders.

A shipment is not an abstract entity, but a container of physical objects. For this reason, each shipment is made up of one or more `Packages`. Each package is identified by a progressive number `NP` which, combined with the identifier of the shipment to which it belongs (`fk_spedizione`), makes it absolutely unique within the system. This unbreakable bond guarantees that a package always belongs to one shipment only. Finally, to better manage storage and transport, each individual package is classified according to a `Size` (such as "Small", "Medium", "Bulky"), defined by a `CDim` code and a description. While multiple packages may share the same size, each package is assigned one and only one size category. In this way, the system offers a complete and detailed view, from the profile of the customer who ships to the physical characteristics of each individual package that makes up the delivery.
