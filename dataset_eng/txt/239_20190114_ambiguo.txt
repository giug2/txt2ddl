So, basically we have a system that talks about teams and players. Each team has its own identification code, a name and the year it was born, things like that. Of course, each team has its headquarters in a city, and it may happen that multiple teams come from the same city, but a team is only in one place. The cities, in turn, are registered with their own code, the name and the nation to which they belong.

Then there are the players, each with a sort of unique serial number to recognize him, in addition to his name and surname. What's interesting is how they tie into teams. It seems like a player belongs to only one team, and a team obviously has a lot of players.

But the real fulcrum of everything is the contracts. A player can have a series of contracts over time, but each contract is tied only to him. These contracts are identified by the date they start and by the player himself, so you can't go wrong. They also have an end date, of course. Furthermore, a contract is always associated with one and only one team, so a team ends up with a bunch of contracts for its players. And not only that, the contract also specifies the role that the player must play. There is a list of possible roles, each with its own codicil and description, and each contract chooses one. Basically, a contract says which player plays for which team, from when to when, and doing what.
