Let's imagine a digital ecosystem that maps the academic life of a university, starting from its largest organizational structure: the Department. Each Department, uniquely identified by an acronym and known by its full name, represents the beating heart of a specific area of ​​knowledge. Two fundamental paths branch out from each Department: the training one and the human one. On the educational side, a single Department gives life to numerous Degree Courses, each distinguished by a unique Code and a Name that describes the course of study. At the same time, each Department is the home of a teaching staff, where each Teacher is registered with a personal number and their Name and Surname.

The student's journey continues within a Degree Course, which is divided into a series of specific teachings. Each Teaching is clearly defined by its own Code and Name, representing a single subject of study. The teaching of each Course comes to life through a sequence of Lessons, each of which is a training event in its own right, identified by a Code, a Title that summarizes the topic and a precise Duration.

To support learning, each lesson can be enriched with multiple resources. This is how the Teaching Material comes into play: each file, be it a slide, a document or an exercise, is cataloged with a unique Code, its original File Name and the Upload Date. This material does not exist in a vacuum; it is closely linked to the academic context. In fact, each piece of Teaching Material is uploaded by a specific Teacher, who can therefore be the author of numerous different files. Furthermore, to ensure correct management and use, each Teaching Material is associated with a specific Format, identified by an ID and described by its Extension (such as 'pdf' or 'pptx'), allowing the system to know that a certain type of file can be used for many different materials. In this way, a complete and interconnected information network is created that extends from the departmental macro-structure down to the single support file for a lesson.
