The data model defines an academic information system structured on six main entities. The `REGION` entity is identified by the `ABSCRIPTION` as the primary key and has a `NAME`. The `CITY` entity is defined by a unique `ID` (PK) and a `NAME`. The `UNIVERSITY` entity is characterized by an `ID` (PK) and a `NAME`. The `DEPARTMENT` entity has a composite primary key, consisting of `NAME` and `fk_university`, and an `OFFICE_NUMBER` attribute. The `COURSE` entity is identified by the `CODE` (PK) and includes the `COURSE_NAME` and `ACADEMIC_YEAR` attributes. Finally, the `PROFESSOR` entity is defined by `SURNAME` (PK), `NAME` and `SURNAME`. The relationships between the entities are as follows: a one-to-many relationship connects `REGION` to `CITY` and `CITY` to `UNIVERSITY`. Similarly, a one-to-many relationship exists between `UNIVERSITY` and `DEPARTMENT`, indicating that a university includes multiple departments. A one-to-many relationship between `REGION` and `PROFESSOR` is also defined. The relationship between `PROFESSOR` and `COURSE` is one-to-many, where a professor can teach multiple courses. Finally, a many-to-many relationship exists between `DEPARTMENT` and `COURSE`, allowing a course to be associated with multiple departments and a department to offer multiple courses.
