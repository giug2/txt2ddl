Welcome to the beating heart of our academic organization, a system designed to map and manage the entire universe of university teaching. At the center of this world we find the concept of **Course**, which represents the single and specific provision of teaching in a given context. Each Course is a unique entity, defined by a precise combination of elements: it is inextricably linked to a specific **Subject**, which constitutes its disciplinary content, is entrusted to the guidance of a single **Professor** and is part of the training path of a specific **Course of Study**.

Let's imagine the path of a student: he enrolls in a **Course of Study**, such as "Computer Engineering" or "Classical Literature". This path is made up of a multitude of **Courses** that the student must attend. Each **Professor** of our university can be responsible for holding multiple different **Courses**, bringing his experience to various classrooms and contexts, but each single **Course** is taught by a single teacher, guaranteeing clear teaching responsibility.

The same **Subject**, for example "Mathematical Analysis I", can be divided into several distinct **Courses**, perhaps offered in different academic years or at different Study Courses. This allows us to precisely track every single training activity. To support the study of each **Subject**, the system provides for association with a reference **Text**. It is interesting to note how a single **Text**, perhaps a fundamental manual, can be adopted as a reference book for multiple similar **Subjects**, becoming a pillar of knowledge that cuts across multiple disciplines, while each **Subject** relies on a main text to guide learning. In this way, the entire educational ecosystem, from the major degree courses down to the single recommended book, is interconnected in a clear and coherent network of relationships, designed to best orchestrate the training offer.
