So basically we have a few lists of things here. There is a list of professors, each with their own identification code to avoid confusion. Then there are the subjects, which also have their own number, and the study courses, such as degrees, which also have their own code. There is also a collection of textbooks, each with its own identifier. The real fulcrum of everything is something called 'Course', which is not just the subject, but is more the specific event, the actual teaching. In fact, to define one of these courses, you have to put together a lot of stuff: its code, but also the code of the subject being taught, that of the professor who teaches it and even that of the course of study to which it belongs. It's a bit of a mess of pieces. The connections are quite intuitive: a professor can teach quite a few different courses, and a single subject can be taught in many courses. Likewise, a bunch of these specific courses end up belonging to a single course of study. The slightly peculiar part concerns the books: it seems that a lot of different subjects can be associated with a single textbook, as if several disciplines used the same manual.
