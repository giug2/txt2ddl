The data model describes the management of musicians, instruments, diplomas and orchestras. The main entities are `MUSICIAN`, identified by the primary key `musician_id`, and `INSTRUMENT`, identified by `instrument_id`. The relationship between these is managed through the associative entity `MUSICIAN INSTRUMENT`, whose composite primary key is formed by the attributes `fk_musician` and `fk_instrument`. This configuration establishes a one-to-many relationship from both `MUSICIAN` and `INSTRUMENT` to the link entity, thus allowing a musician to be associated with multiple instruments. The system also models academic qualifications through the entities `CONSERVATORY`, with primary key `id_conservatorio`, and `DIPLOMA`, with key `id_diploma`. There is a one-to-many relationship between `CONSERVATORY` and `DIPLOMA`, where a conservatory can award multiple diplomas. Each diploma is in turn linked to a specific musician-instrument combination, establishing a one-to-many relationship from the entity `MUSICIAN INSTRUMENT` to `DIPLOMA`. Finally, the `ORCHESTRA IN YEAR` entity, identified by `id_orchestra_anno` and containing the `year` attribute, represents the formation of an orchestra in a given period. This entity is placed in a many-to-many relationship with `INSTRUMENT MUSICIAN`, indicating that an orchestra in a given year is composed of multiple musicians and that a musician can be part of multiple orchestras.
