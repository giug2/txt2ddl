Welcome to the beating heart of our system, a digital universe designed to orchestrate and celebrate the world of classical music. Here, every note, every performance and every artist finds its place in a coherent and interconnected narrative. At the center of everything is the single musical `Piece`, a work of art identified by a unique code and its title, inextricably linked to the genius of the `Composer` who created it. Of course, a composer can give the world multiple masterpieces, but each piece comes from a single creative mind.

These pieces come to life within a `Concert`, which is not a single performance, but an artistic program, a curated selection of works with their own title and a description that illustrates their theme. A concert can be a mosaic of several pieces, and in the same way, a famous piece can be reinterpreted in countless different concert programs over time. Each concert program is entrusted to the interpretation of a specific `Orchestra`, a prestigious entity defined by its name, its location and its director. Although an orchestra may have many concerts in its repertoire, each program is designed to be performed by that single ensemble.

The orchestra itself is a living organism, composed of talented `Musicians`. Our system keeps track of this `Composition`, associating each musician, with his personal serial number, to the orchestra he is part of and specifying the `Instrument` he plays. This allows us to know that a musician belongs to a particular orchestra and that, within it, many colleagues may play the same type of instrument, such as first violins or cellos.

Finally, the magic happens when a concert program turns into a tangible 'Event' for the audience. A single concert can be replicated over multiple evenings, and each of these performances is a unique event, defined by a precise date and time. Each event takes place in a physical place, a specific 'Theatre', which with its name and unique code becomes the setting for countless musical evenings. In this way, our system not only catalogs the music and its creators, but tells the story of every single performance, from the conception of the work to the final applause of the audience in the room.
