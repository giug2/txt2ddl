So, in practice there is a kind of main strand of events, let's call it a Series, which has its own code and a title to be recognized. Each series, then, is made up of a lot of specific conferences. Each conference is identified by a set of things: which series it belongs to, a specific date and the year in which it is held. Furthermore, each conference takes place in one and only one city, although obviously a city can host quite a few different conferences over time. Within each conference the day is broken up into various Sessions, each with its code, a title and a time to find out when it starts. Now comes the part about the people. There are People, with their own code and name, but things get complicated with an intermediate entity, the Person with affiliation, which is basically a link. In fact, a natural person can be linked to more than one of these "affiliations". Each of these "persons with affiliation" is linked to only one institution, such as the university or the company he works for at that moment. It is precisely these figures with affiliation who participate in the sessions: a session can have a lot of participants and, at the same time, the same person with his affiliation can be present in multiple sessions. To close the loop, institutions are based somewhere, so in one city you can find a lot of different institutions.
