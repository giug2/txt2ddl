This system was designed to capture the beating heart of musical life, managing every detail that transforms a simple score into an unforgettable evening of concert. At the center of this universe we find the concept of **Concert**, a unique artistic event, identified by its own code and described by a title and a presentation that illustrate its theme. However, the magic of a concert does not end in a single evening; for this reason, each concert program can be staged several times through **Reruns**. Each replica represents a specific performance of that concert on a specific date, and it is precisely the combination between the date and the concert to which it refers that makes it an unrepeatable event. Furthermore, each replica comes to life in a physical place, a **Concert Hall**, characterized by an identification code, a name and a maximum capacity of spectators, and the same hall can obviously host countless repeats of different concerts over time.

Giving voice to the music is the **Orchestra**, an artistic entity defined by its unique name and led by a conductor. Each concert program is performed by a specific orchestra, although the same orchestra may have many different concerts in its repertoire. An orchestra is a group of talents, and in fact it is made up of numerous musicians, the **Orchestrali**. Each orchestra member is a natural person, registered with a serial number, name, surname and the instrument he plays. The relationship between orchestral players and orchestras is flexible: an orchestra is made up of many musicians and, at the same time, a single orchestral player could collaborate with several orchestras over the course of his career.

Finally, we get to the essence of it all: the music itself. The program of each concert consists of a selection of musical **Pieces**. A piece, identified by a code and a title, can be performed in several concerts, just as a single concert can include multiple pieces. Behind every piece there is the creative genius of the **Musician** who composed it. Each piece of music can be traced back to a single musician, who is in turn identified by a code and his name, but a great musician, over the course of his life, can naturally compose a vast repertoire of pieces. In this way, the system intertwines the history of music, its creators and its performers with the living chronicle of the events that bring it to the public.
