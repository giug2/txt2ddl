The data schema defines a model for academic management based on six main entities and their interrelationships. The `Department` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, has a one-to-many relationship with the `Teacher` entity, indicating that each teacher belongs to a single department. The `Teacher` is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has a `Name`. The `Sector` entity, defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, has a one-to-many relationship with `Discipline`, so each discipline belongs to a single sector. The model also specifies a one-to-many relationship between `Teacher` and `Sector`, where a teacher can be associated with multiple sectors. The `Discipline` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, is divided into one or more `Subjects`. The `Matter` entity is characterized by a composite primary key made up of the `Suffix` and `fk_discipline` attributes; the latter also acts as a foreign key towards `Disciplina`, establishing a unique membership link. A one-to-many recursive relationship on the `Matter` entity itself is also defined. The `Course` entity is identified by a composite primary key that includes `CS` and `fk_subject`, and has a non-key attribute `Semester`. The presence of `fk_matter` establishes a relationship where a subject can have multiple associated courses. Finally, a one-to-many relationship links `Teacher` to `Course`, implying that each course is taught by a single teacher, who may own multiple courses.
