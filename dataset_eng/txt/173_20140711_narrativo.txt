This system was designed to orchestrate the dynamic world of software development, offering a clear and structured view of every component, from people to final products. At the center of this universe lies the `Project`, the container of every creative effort. Each project is unique, identified by a `Project_ID`, and described by a `Project_Name` and a detailed `Description` that defines its objectives. To ensure agile and measurable progress, each project is divided into a series of sequential `Sprints`. A sprint represents a work cycle with a precise `End_Date` and culminates in the release of a specific `Release_Version` of the software, always belonging only to a single reference project.

Driving the success of these projects is the `Team`, a cohesive group of professionals identified by a unique `Team_Name` and its `Creation_Date`. A team may be responsible for managing multiple projects at the same time, but each project is placed under the care of a single team to ensure clarity and accountability. The composition of a team is defined by the people who are part of it. Each `Team Member` is registered in the system with a unique `Serial number`, `Name` and `Surname`. The link between a person and a workgroup is formalized through an entity called `Assignment`, which creates a bridge between a `TeamMember` and a `Team`, allowing you to track who works where.

The beating heart of development is the code, organized into functional `Modules`. Each module, with its `Module_ID` and `Name`, represents a distinct software component. The authorship of each module is clearly defined: a `Developer`, in turn identified by a `Developer_ID` and `Name`, is responsible for creating many modules, but each individual module is the result of the work of a single developer. These modules are like reusable building blocks: a project can be composed of many different modules, and at the same time, a single module can be integrated into multiple projects, promoting efficiency and maintainability.

Finally, to complete the picture, the system tracks technological skills. Each `Technology`, from the programming language to the framework, is cataloged with a `Technology_ID` and a `Technology_Name`. This data becomes fundamental in the `Assignment` entity, which is not limited to connecting a person to a team, but can also specify the main technology used in that context, thus offering a detailed map of the skills used within the organization.
