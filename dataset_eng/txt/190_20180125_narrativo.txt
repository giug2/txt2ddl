This system was designed to tell the complete story of each product in a catalogue, organizing information in a hierarchical and intuitive way. At the center of this universe lies the single **Product**, unequivocally identified by its SKU code and described by a specific name. However, a product is never an isolated entity; its identity is enriched by a network of connections that define its origin and classification.

To understand where a product comes from, we connect it to its **Brand**, a unique entity defined by its own code and name. Each product belongs to one and only one brand, but a single brand can obviously boast a whole range of different products. The origin story goes even deeper: each brand, in turn, is part of a larger **Industrial Group**. This means that an industrial group, such as a large holding company, can own and manage numerous brands, each of which belongs to a single group.

Parallel to its origin, each product has a specific place within the catalogue, defined by a clear classification structure. At the basis of this organization we find the **Category**, a broad and general grouping identified by a code, such as "Electronics" or "Clothing". To offer a greater level of detail, each category is divided into multiple specific **Subcategories**. A subcategory is defined not only by its code, but also by its inextricable link with its parent category, ensuring that, for example, the "Smartphone" subcategory only exists within the "Electronics" category. Finally, each individual product finds its precise location by being assigned to one and only one subcategory, thus completing a rich and interconnected information framework that allows you to navigate from the general to the particular and to understand the commercial relationships that link each item.
