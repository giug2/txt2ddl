So, we basically handle a few things here. There is a list of people, customers, each with their own identification code to avoid confusion, and obviously name and surname. The important thing is that each customer can have a lot of stuff, different items in short. These items are the actual objects, the physical ones, and to distinguish them from each other we use a serial number that is unique for each one, and we also note the year in which they were made. Then, there is a kind of product catalogue, which is not the single object but the model, like "SuperVision TV". A product is defined by its name and, crucially, the brand that makes it; in fact, without knowing the brand, the name alone is not enough to identify it. Furthermore, each product has its own typology. Finally, we have the list of brands, each with its code, name and country of origin. Clearly, a brand can churn out a lot of different products, and each product model can exist in many examples, i.e. articles. In summary, a brand makes products, there are many specific items of these products, and these items end up in the hands of our customers.
