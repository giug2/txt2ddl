Welcome to the beating heart of a musical universe, a system designed to catalog and make sense of the infinite connections that bind music to its creators. At the center of everything we find the Record Label, identified by a unique code, which represents the production company of an artist; of each label we know the name, registered office and country of origin. Numerous talents can flourish under the wing of a single label, but each Artist is contractually linked to only one record company. The artist, in turn, is recognized through his own identifier and characterized by his surname, the stage name that makes him famous and a contact email for official communications. The fruit of the creativity of these artists is the Song, a unique musical work with its own title, the complete text and an indication of any collaborations or featuring. The magic of music often lies in collaboration, which is why a song can be the result of the work of multiple artists, and, in the same way, an artist can interpret countless songs over the course of his career. The songs do not live isolated, but are collected and organized within an Album. Each album, which has a release date, is part of a larger Series, identified by a name and the year it began. This link is so close that an album is uniquely defined not only by its catalog number, but also by the series to which it belongs. When a song is included in an album, it acquires a precise context: it is assigned a specific track number and its exact duration is recorded, thus defining its place within the musical narrative of the record. In this way, the system weaves a complete web of information, allowing you to seamlessly navigate from the grand plan of a series of albums down to the single lyric sung by an artist under the aegis of his record label.
