So basically here is a system that keeps track of books. Think of a book as the original idea, with its author, the title of when it first came out, and the year. Each book, then, belongs to a certain literary genre, such as detective stories or fantasy, and is published by a specific publishing house. Clearly, a publisher can publish a lot of different books, and a genre groups together quite a few, but each book is tied to only one publisher and only one genre.

Of each book, then, there are various editions. One is the hardcover edition, another the paperback edition, and so on. Each edition has its own things: a title that is perhaps a little different, the number of pages, the price and that ISBN code that identifies it. Each edition, obviously, is linked to a single "conceptual" book. These editions are also classified by format, such as "paperback" or "hardback", where each format can be used for an infinite number of different editions, but an edition has only one type of format.

Finally, for each specific edition, there are actual physical copies. Each individual copy has its own unique code to recognize it, a note on how it is placed, such as whether it is new or damaged, and the date it was purchased. Therefore, there may be many copies of a certain edition of a book, but each physical copy is inextricably linked to that single edition and to nothing else.
