So, basically we're talking about football here. We have nations, each with its own name and code to recognize it. Inside every nation, obviously, there are a few cities, and each city necessarily belongs to a single nation. Then, in a city there may be several football teams, but each team has its headquarters in one place only. We know the name of these teams, the date of foundation and they also have a unique identifier.

Each team, year after year, has its seasonal roster, which is practically the list of players for that specific championship. This is closely linked both to the season and to the team itself. And this is where the players come into play, each registered with their name, surname and their own personal code. It works like this: in a squad of a certain season there are a lot of players, and the same player may have been part of many different squads over time, perhaps changing teams or playing for many years. Finally, there is where these players come from: we have youth clubs, which are a bit like football schools. Several players can come from one of these clubs, but apparently each player is linked to only one youth club of origin.
