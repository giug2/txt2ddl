So, basically we have a system for keeping track of some stuff, starting with places. There is the idea of ​​a country, which is identified by an acronym and has its own name. Each country, of course, has lots of cities in it, and each city is only in one country. We recognize the cities with their own code and they also have a name. Then, around these cities, there are our warehouses. Each warehouse is located in a specific city, but a city can have more than one. Even the warehouses have their own identification code so as not to confuse them and a name to understand something. Inside each warehouse, goods are placed on shelves; a shelf is identified by its code but also by the warehouse in which it is located, so you cannot have two shelves with the same code in the same place. Every warehouse has quite a few shelves, and each one has its own capacity to know how much stuff will fit. And what do we put on the shelves? The products. Each product has its own unique code, a name and the year it was made. The rule is that a product fits on only one shelf, but on one shelf you can find several products. Where does this stuff come from? From suppliers. A supplier can give us a lot of different products, but only one supplier gives us each product. We manage these suppliers with a code, their company name and VAT number. And, to close the loop, each supplier is based in a specific country, even though there can be many suppliers in one country.
