The data model defines the structure of a management system for catering and logistics. The fundamental entities include: **Logistics Center**, uniquely identified by a `Code` and characterized by a `Name`; **City**, with `Code` (PK) and `Name`; **Restaurant**, described by `Code` (PK), `Name`, `Address` and `Telephone`; **Promotion Type**, with `Code` (PK) and `Name`; **Supplier**, also identified by `Code` (PK) and `Name`; **Plate**, with `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Price`; and finally **Special Menu**, defined by `Validity Period` (PK), `Price` and `Number of Courses`. The interconnections between these entities are structured as follows: a `City` can have multiple Logistics Centers and multiple Restaurants, establishing two distinct one-to-many relationships. Each `Restaurant` is located in a single `City` and can offer multiple Special Dishes and Menus, through further one-to-many relationships. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Restaurant` and `Promotion Type`, meaning that a restaurant can adopt various promotions and the same promotion can be applied by multiple restaurants. Similarly, a many-to-many relationship connects `Logistics Center` and `Supplier`, allowing each center to use multiple suppliers and each supplier to serve multiple fulfillment centers.
