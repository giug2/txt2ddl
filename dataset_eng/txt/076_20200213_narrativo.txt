Our system is designed to map the complex structure of an academic organization, starting from its highest level down to individual teaching interactions. At the top of this universe we find the School, the main entity that acts as a container for the various areas of knowledge. Each School, identified by a unique code, is the beating heart of different disciplines and branches into multiple Departments. A Department represents a specific thematic area, such as "Engineering" or "Literature", and always belongs only to one School; its identity is in fact defined not only by its own code, but also by the association with the school of reference. Within each Department, academic life develops along two fundamental axes: the teaching staff and the educational offering. On the one hand, we have the Professors, each uniquely linked to their own Department. This link is so close that a professor is uniquely identified in the system only through the combination of his personal identifier and that of his department. On the other hand, each Department designs and manages a series of study courses, such as three-year or master's degrees. Similar to professors, a Study Course belongs exclusively to a Department and its identity is determined by the pair of its code and that of the department that provides it. Finally, the system models the dynamic relationship between who teaches and what is taught: a Professor can lend his teaching in multiple Study Courses, and, vice versa, a single Study Course makes use of the skills and teachings of numerous professors to complete its study plan.
