The data model defines a hierarchical structure for cataloging commercial products. The `IndustrialGroup` entity, identified by a `GroupCode` (PK) and a `GroupName`, represents the highest level of business aggregation. Each `IndustryGroup` can own multiple brands, establishing a one-to-many relationship with the `Brand` entity. The `Brand` entity, identified by a `BrandCode` (PK) and a `BrandName`, is therefore associated with a single group. The product classification is managed by the `Category` and `Subcategory` entities. A `Category` is defined by a `CategoryCode` (PK) and a `CategoryName`. Each `Category` can contain multiple subcategories, in a one-to-many relationship with the `Subcategory` entity, which is identified by a primary key composed of `SubcategoryCode` and `fk_category`, where `fk_category` is the foreign key that links it to the `Category` it belongs to. Finally, the `Product` entity represents the specific item, identified by a `SKU` (PK) and a `ProductName`. Each product is uniquely associated with a `Brand` and a `Subcategory`, participating in two distinct one-to-many relationships.
