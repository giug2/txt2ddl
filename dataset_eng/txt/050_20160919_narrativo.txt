This system was designed to map an institution's academic universe, orchestrating the complex interactions between who teaches, what is taught, and to whom. At the center of this universe we find the main training path, the COURSE OF STUDY. Each path, whether it is a degree in Computer Engineering or in Classics, is defined by a unique identifier and a clear name, representing the main path that students choose to take. Within each course of study, knowledge is organized into distinct SUBJECTS, which constitute the pillars of knowledge. Each SUBJECT, such as "Mathematical Analysis" or "Roman History", has its own identification code and a specific name. To support the learning of each subject, the system associates one or more reference TEXTS with it; each book is cataloged with its own identifier and title, creating an essential bibliography for study.

However, a subject only comes to life when it is offered as a specific COURSE. A COURSE is not an abstract entity; it is a concrete lesson, a precise teaching experience that takes place in a specific context. To identify it without any ambiguity, the system considers a unique combination of three elements: the SUBJECT it deals with, the COURSE OF STUDY to which it belongs and a specific "channel", which could represent a section or a group of students. This structure guarantees that, even if the subject "Mathematical Analysis" is present in multiple study paths, every single instance of that course is perfectly distinguishable. Guiding the students through these paths of knowledge is the PROFESSOR, a key figure in the system, identified by a unique code and his personal data. The relationship is clear: every single COURSE is entrusted to the guidance of a single PROFESSOR, who is the teaching manager. In turn, a professor can put his experience at the service of numerous courses, thus weaving the dense network of the university's educational offerings.
