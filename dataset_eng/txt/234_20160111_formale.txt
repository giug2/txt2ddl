The data model describes the management of literary works and their physical copies. The `GENRE` entity, identified by `GENRE_ID` and with a `GENRE_NAME`, has a one-to-many relationship with the `LITERARY_WORK` entity. The latter, identified by `WORK_ID`, contains attributes such as `AVERAGE_NUMBER_PAGES` and `NUMBER_VOLUMES`. Each work can have multiple `EDITIONS` (1:N relationship). The `EDITION` entity is uniquely identified by an `ISBN` and described by `EDITION_TITLE`, `FORMAT` and `COVER_PRICE`. Each edition is also associated with a specific `LANGUAGE` (identified by `ISO_CODE` and `LANGUAGE_NAME`) in a one-to-many relationship. Finally, each `EDITION` can have a variable number of physical instances represented by the `COPY_WAREHOUSE` entity (1:N relationship), which is identified by `COPY_ID` and tracks the `STORAGE_STATUS` and `ACQUISITION_DATE` of each specimen.
