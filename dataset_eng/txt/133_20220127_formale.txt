The data model describes the company organizational structure through four main entities: HEADQUARTERS, OFFICE, ASSISTANT and MANAGER. The LOCATION entity represents physical locations and is identified by a `location_id` primary key. The OFFICE entity is identified by a composite primary key (`office_id`, `fk_location`), establishing a many-to-one relationship with LOCATION, where each office belongs to a single location. Similarly, the ASSISTANT entity is defined by a composite primary key (`assistant_id`, `fk_office`), which implements a many-to-one relationship with OFFICE, associating each assistant with a single office. Finally, the MANAGER entity, identified by the primary key `manager_id`, is linked to the OFFICE entity by a many-to-many (N:N) relationship, indicating that a manager can manage multiple offices and an office can have multiple managers.
