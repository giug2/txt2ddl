The data model defines the structure for cataloging works of art and their geographical and museum location. The main entities are NATION, CITY, MUSEUM, HALL, ARTIST and WORK OF ART. The NATION entity is identified by an ABBREVIATION (PK) and has a NAME. The CITY entity is identified by an ID (PK) and a NAME, and each city belongs to only one country. The MUSEUM entity, identified by an ID (PK) and a NAME, is located in a single city, which can host multiple museums. Each museum is composed of one or more ROOM entities, identified by a primary key composed of NAME and fk_museo; each room is also described by a DIMENSION. The ARTIST entity is identified by an ID (PK) and characterized by NAME and SURNAME; each artist is associated with a single nation of origin, which can have multiple artists. The WORK OF ART entity, defined by a unique CODE (PK), TITLE and YEAR, is created by a single artist, who can be the author of multiple works. Finally, each work of art is exhibited in a single room, which can contain multiple works. All relationships are one-to-many.
