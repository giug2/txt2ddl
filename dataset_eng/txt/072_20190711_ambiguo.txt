So basically here we have a system that keeps track of some music related stuff. There is a list of musicians, each with their own identification code, and then there is a list of all possible instruments, also with their own number. To know who plays what, there is a kind of register that acts as a bridge, bringing together a musician with an instrument; this is because obviously one person can play multiple things and an instrument, like a violin, can be played by a lot of people.

Then the orchestra comes into play, which is not fixed but is seen year by year, like a photograph of the formation in a certain period. This entity, the orchestra in a given year, is connected to that register of musicians and instruments. In practice, an annual orchestra lineup consists of a group of people playing their instruments, and at the same time, a musician with his instrument could be part of several orchestra lineups over the years.

Finally, there is the whole part related to training. We have conservatories, each individually identified, who issue diplomas. A conservatory may churn out a lot of degrees, but each degree comes from only one institution. The interesting thing is that these degrees are not directly linked to the musician and that's it, but to that specific combination of musician and instrument. This suggests that a diploma is specific to a person's skill on a certain instrument, and it also seems that more than one can be had for the same combination.
