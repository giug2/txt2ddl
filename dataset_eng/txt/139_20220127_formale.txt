The data model describes an organizational structure based on four entities: `OFFICE`, `HEADQUARTERS`, `MANAGER` and `ASSISTANT`. The ` OFFICE ` and ` LOCATION ` entities are defined by single primary keys, `office_id` and `office_id`, respectively. The `MANAGER` entity is characterized by a composite primary key that includes `manager_id`, `fk_office` and `fk_headquarters`, establishing an identifying dependency on the `OFFICE` and `HEADQUARTERS` entities. The relationship between `Manager` and `Office` is many-to-many (N:N) type. The `ASSISTANT` entity also has a composite primary key, made up of `assistant_id`, `fk_manager` and `fk_sede`. There is a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between `ASSISTANT` and `MANAGER`, indicating that a manager can have multiple assistants. In this relationship, the `fk_manager` and `fk_sede` attributes in the `ASSISTANT` entity act as a composite foreign key referencing the `MANAGER` entity.
