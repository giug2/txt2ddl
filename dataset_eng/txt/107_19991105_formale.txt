The data model defines four main entities: Actor, Film, Director and Cinema. The `Actor` entity is identified by a primary key `actor_id` and contains the `full_name` attribute. Similarly, the `Director` entity is structured with a `director_id` primary key and a `full_name` attribute. The `Cinema` entity is defined solely by its primary key `cinema_id`. The `Film` entity is identified by a composite primary key consisting of `film_id` and `fk_director`. This last attribute also acts as a foreign key, implementing a many-to-one (N:1) relationship with the `Director` entity, where each film is associated with a single director. The model also includes two many-to-many (N:N) relationships: one between `Movie` and `Actor`, meaning that a movie can have multiple actors and an actor can star in multiple movies, and another between `Movie` and `Cinema`, meaning that a movie can be shown in multiple theaters and a theater can show multiple movies.
