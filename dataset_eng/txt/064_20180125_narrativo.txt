This system was designed to map and organize the complex and interconnected universe of academic and professional conferences, creating a knowledge network that connects events, people and places. At the heart of everything we find the "Series", a concept that represents a cycle of recurring events, such as an annual conference, identified by its own unique "Code" and described by an evocative "Title". Each single series can give rise, over time, to numerous specific "Conferences". Each conference is a unique event, defined not only by the series to which it belongs, but also by its exact "Date" and the "year" in which it takes place; this combination of elements guarantees its unique identification. Naturally, each conference has a physical location, and for this reason it is always associated with one and only one "City", which in turn can host various events over time.

The internal organization of a conference is divided into various "Sessions". Each session, characterized by an identifying "Code", a specific "Title" and a start "Time", represents a single thematic block of the event. A conference is made up of multiple sessions, but each session belongs unequivocally to a single conference. The real protagonists of these sessions are the people. The system manages the basic personal information of each "Person", recording their "Name" and a unique "Code". At the same time, it lists the "Institutions" to which they belong, such as universities or research centres, also identified by a "Code" and a "Name", and geographically located in a specific "City", which can be home to many different institutions.

The crucial hub of the system is the “Person with Affiliation” entity. This does not simply represent an individual, but rather the link between a person and an institution in a given context. A single person can in fact have multiple affiliations throughout their career, and this model allows them to be recorded all separately. Likewise, a single institution will be the affiliate body for numerous people. It is precisely this "Person with affiliation" who actively participates in the events: the system establishes a dynamic relationship in which each session can host multiple participants (speakers, moderators, etc.), and the same person, through his affiliation, can intervene in different sessions. In this way, the system does not limit itself to cataloging events, but draws a detailed map of the collaborations, intellectual exchanges and professional paths of experts in the scientific and cultural panorama.
