The data model describes a project management system, structured around nine main entities: Feature, Customer, Project, Sprint, Team, Platform, Developer, Technology and Assignment. The `Customer` entity is identified by `Customer_ID` and has the `Last Name` and `First Name` attributes. The `Project` entity is identified by `Project_ID` and contains `Name` and `Description`. The `Feature` entity, with primary key `ID_Feature`, includes a `Name`. The `Sprint` entity is uniquely identified by `End_Date` and has a `Duration` attribute. The `Team` is identified by its `Name` and associated with an `Office`. The `Developer` is identified by the `Serial Number` and includes `Last Name` and `First Name`. The `Platform` and `Technology` entities are identified by `Platform_ID` and `Technology_ID` respectively and contain a `Name`; Technology also has a `Category`. Finally, the `Assignment` entity is identified by `Assignment_ID`. Relationships are defined as follows: each `Feature` is requested by a single `Customer` in a many-to-one relationship. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Feature` and `Project`. A `Project` is composed of many `Sprints` (one-to-many relationship) and is associated with a single `Platform` and a single `Team` (many-to-one relationships). There is a many-to-one relationship between `Team` and `Developer`. Both `Team` and `Developer` have a one-to-many relationship to `Assignment`, indicating that an assignment is tied to a single team and a single developer. Many assignments can refer to a single `Technology` (many-to-one relationship). A one-to-many hierarchical relationship is also defined on the `Assignment` entity itself, to model dependencies or sub-tasks.
