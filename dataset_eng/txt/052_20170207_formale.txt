The data model defines four main entities: Customer, Employee, Project, and Department. Each entity is identified by a unique primary key (`customer_id`, `employee_id`, `project_id`, `department_id`) and has a descriptive name attribute. The interconnections between entities are established by three relationships. There is a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between Employee and Department, whereby each employee is associated with a single department, while a department can include multiple employees. A second many-to-one (N:1) relationship links the Employee entity to the Project entity, indicating that each employee is assigned to only one project, which can have multiple employees within it. Finally, a one-to-many (1:N) relationship links Customer and Project, establishing that a customer can be associated with multiple projects, but each project refers to a single customer.
