The data model specification describes four main entities: `Author`, `Novel`, `Character`, and `Publisher`. Each entity is identified by a unique primary key (PK): `author_id` for `Author`, `character_id` for `Character`, and `publisher_id` for `Publisher`. The `Novel` entity is characterized by a composite primary key, made up of `novel_id` and `fk_author`, which establishes a many-to-one (N:1) identifying relationship with the `Author` entity; this implies that a novel is uniquely associated with a single author, while an author may have written multiple novels. There is also a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between `Novel` and `Publisher`, indicating that each novel is published by a single publisher. Finally, the model defines a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between `Novel` and `Character`, whereby a novel can contain multiple characters and a character can appear in multiple novels.
