This system was designed to manage the fascinating universe of books, following the path of each volume from its editorial origin to the hands of the reader. At the center of everything we find the Publishing House, identified by a unique Publisher Code and known by its Name. Each publishing house is intrinsically linked to a specific publication language, in turn defined by a Language Code and a Name; although one language may be used by many publishers, each publishing house is associated with only one primary language. Publishing houses organize their publications in series, thematic series uniquely recognizable by the combination of their series name and the code of the publisher who publishes them, thus guaranteeing that each series belongs to a single publisher, who can edit multiple series. To help readers orient themselves, each series is associated with a literary genre, such as detective stories or science fiction, identified by a genre code and its name, knowing that a single genre can naturally include countless different series.

The individual editions of the books come to life within each series. Each Edition is a distinct entity, traced with its own Edition Code and inextricably linked to the series it belongs to, and of which we also know the Number of Pages. From the concept of Edition we move on to the physical object: the Physical Copy. For each published edition, there may exist numerous tangible copies, each recorded in our inventory with a unique Inventory Code and indication of its Year of Print. In this way, a single edition can be represented by a large number of copies, but each physical copy corresponds exactly to a specific edition. Finally, the beating heart of this world is the User. Each reader is registered with a personal User Code, together with his Surname and Name. The most important interaction occurs when a User borrows a Physical Copy; the system manages this complex dynamic by allowing a user to borrow many different books over time and, similarly, allowing a single physical copy to pass through the hands of many readers over its lifetime.
