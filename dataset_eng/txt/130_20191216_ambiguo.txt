So basically we have a system that tracks a few things about skiers. Each skier has his own unique code, plus first and last name, and is at home in a city. Each city, in turn, is part of a nation, so there is this geographical connection. Then, obviously, there's the whole racing part. Each race is an event in itself, with its own code, a date, and takes place in a specific location, which in turn is located in a country. The races are not all the same, each one has a certain specialty, like slalom or something like that. The heart of it all is participation: you keep track of when a skier does a certain race. This thing, participation, connects a skier to a race and also tells us what position he placed. Basically, a skier can do a lot of races and a race has a lot of participants. It also seems that each participation is associated with a nation, probably to know which flag the skier was racing for on that occasion.
