This system was designed to catalog the world of the automobile in a complete and detailed way, starting from the broader concept of the brand up to the single physical vehicle leaving the factory. The heart of our universe is the BRAND, each manufacturer is defined by a unique code and its commercial name. Each brand creates and markets different MODELS of vehicles; a model, such as a "Punto" or a "Golf", is identified by a specific code and has distinctive characteristics such as name, length and number of seats. Of course, each model belongs to one and only one brand. To better position the vehicles on the market, each MODEL is associated with a precise MARKET SEGMENT, which describes its category (for example, "utility car" or "SUV"), thus allowing it to be classified based on its target customer base.

To offer a wide choice to consumers, a single MODEL can be offered in multiple VERSIONS. Each version represents a specific setup, with its own identification code, a commercial name, a specific engine capacity and a list price. Each version is inextricably linked to the model from which it derives. A fundamental aspect of each VERSION is its POWER TYPE; whether petrol, diesel or electric, each type is cataloged with a code and description. A specific version is designed for a single type of power supply, although the same type of power supply can be used for many different versions.

Finally, we come to the final, tangible product: the CAR. Every single car produced is a unique entity, unequivocally identified by its CHASSIS number. Added to this are specific details such as its COLOR and the date of production or registration. The crucial link is that each physical car corresponds exactly to one and only one VERSION, thus inheriting all the characteristics defined upstream. In this way, starting from a single chassis, we can trace the entire genealogy of the vehicle: its version, the basic model, the type of fuel, the market segment and, finally, the brand that built it.
