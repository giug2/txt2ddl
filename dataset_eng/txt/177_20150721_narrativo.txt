At the center of our management system is the Event, the beating heart of every initiative, whether it is a large conference, a specialist seminar or an interactive workshop. Each Event is unique and serves as the main container for all related activities. The true richness of an Event is manifested through its numerous Sessions, which represent the individual meetings, presentations or debates scheduled. A Session is unequivocally defined by a combination of three elements: the Event to which it belongs, the physical Room in which it takes place and the specific Topic that is covered. This guarantees that there cannot be two identical meetings in the same place and on the same theme within the same event.

Each individual Session is closely linked to a single Topic, ensuring that each intervention has a clear and precise thematic focus. However, the flexibility of the system allows a Topic of particular interest to be explored in depth across multiple Sessions, perhaps from different points of view or in different contexts. The Speakers, the true protagonists of knowledge sharing, bring these sessions to life. The relationship between them and the sessions is dynamic and complex: a highly experienced speaker could be invited to speak in multiple sessions during the same event, while a single session, such as a round table or discussion panel, can be enriched by the presence of multiple speakers. Finally, to support learning and dissemination, each Session may be associated with Supporting Material, such as presentations, documents or handouts. While each session is linked to a single package of materials, the same set of resources may be so foundational or versatile that it can be reused across multiple sessions, creating a coherent and interconnected information ecosystem for organizers and attendees.
