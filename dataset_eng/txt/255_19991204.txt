The scheme outlines an information system for a library or bookstore in which there are languages, each identified by a Language Code and described by a Name.
The Publishing Houses are connected to these, defined by an identifying Publisher Code, a Name and the Language they belong to, establishing a referential integrity bond with the Languages ​​entity.
The Genre entity is introduced, identified by a Genre Code and a Name. Consequently, the Collana entity, while maintaining its identification key composed of CasaEditrice and NomeCollana, now establishes a referential integrity constraint with the new Genre entity.
The most significant change is the insertion of the Edition entity, which is interposed between the series and the individual physical copies of the books.
Each Edition, identified by the pair of Collana and CodiceEdizione attributes, has a NumeroPagine attribute and refers to the Collana entity.
The CopiaFisica entity, uniquely identified by the CodiceInventario and characterized by the AnnoStampa, is no longer linked directly to a series but to a specific Edition via a referential constraint.
Finally, the loan or possession relationship is made explicit through an associative entity that connects a Physical Copy to a User, establishing referential integrity constraints with the respective entities.
The latter, the User entity, collects the personal information of individuals, identified by their User Code and described by Surname and Name.
