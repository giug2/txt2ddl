So, we have a few different things going on in this system. There are the professors, each recognizable by their own personal code. Then there are the departments, which also have their own identification number that distinguishes them. There are also courses, but to understand which one it is, its code is not enough, you also need to know which department it belongs to, it's a somewhat related thing. Finally, there is the figure of the tutor, who also has his own ID so as not to confuse him. Things connect in strange ways: a course is necessarily under one department, but obviously a department can have lots of different courses. The real tangle, however, is another: it seems that professors, tutors and courses are all mixed together in a sort of large network, where a professor can be associated with multiple courses and multiple tutors, a course can have various professors and tutors, and a tutor can follow different situations. In short, it is a beautiful complicated plot where everyone can be connected to everyone else in almost any possible combination.
