So, in practice we have companies, each with its own identification code to recognize it. Inside a company there are a few divisions, which are like large departments. Each division has its own code, but to find it you must also know which company it belongs to, they are closely linked. Obviously, a company can have a lot of divisions, but a division only fits into a single company. Then, going down the level, each division is broken up into various work teams. Teams also have their own ID, but they too are closely linked to their division, you can't think of them alone. A division may contain quite a few teams, but a specific team belongs to one and only one division. Finally, there are the people, the employees. Each employee has his own employee number, let's say, but he is always assigned to a team, so he too is tied to that team's code. There can be multiple people in a team, but each person works in only one team at a time. It's a kind of hierarchy, everything nestled inside each other starting from the company.
