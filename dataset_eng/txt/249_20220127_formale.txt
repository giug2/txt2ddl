The data model defines four entities: DEPARTMENT, CAMPUS, PROFESSOR and TUTOR. The DEPARTMENT entity is uniquely identified by a primary key `department_id`, while the CAMPUS entity is identified by the primary key `campus_id`. The PROFESSOR entity is defined by a composite primary key made up of the `professor_id`, `fk_department` and `fk_campus` attributes. This structure implements a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between professors and departments, where `fk_department` is a foreign key that references the DEPARTMENT entity and `fk_campus` references the CAMPUS entity. A professor, identified by his `professor_id`, can therefore be associated with multiple departments. Finally, the TUTOR entity has a composite primary key made up of `tutor_id`, `fk_professore`, and `fk_campus`. There is a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between TUTOR and PROFESSOR, establishing that multiple tutors can be associated with a single professor; this link is created via a foreign key in TUTOR which references the PROFESSOR entity.
