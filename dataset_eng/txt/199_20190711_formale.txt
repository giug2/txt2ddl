The data model describes a system for managing flight operations, pilots and their ratings. The main entities defined are FLIGHT_OF_DAY, uniquely identified by `id_flight_day` and characterized by a `date_flight` attribute; PILOT, with primary key `pilot_id`; AIRCRAFT_MODEL, with primary key `airplane_model_id`; FLIGHT_LICENSE, identified by `license_id`; and FLIGHT_SCHOOL, with key `flight_school_id`. The PILOT_AIRCRAFT_ENABLED associative entity implements the relationship between pilots and aircraft models, using a composite primary key formed by the `fk_pilot` and `fk_airplane_model` attributes. This structure establishes a one-to-many relationship from the PILOT entity and a one-to-many relationship from the AIRCRAFT_MODEL entity to AIRCRAFT_RATE PILOT, allowing a pilot to have multiple ratings and a model to be associated with multiple rated pilots. The FLIGHT_SCHOOL entity is linked to FLIGHT_LICENSE by a one-to-many relationship, indicating that a school issues multiple licenses. In turn, a single rating (PILOT_AIRCRAFT_RATING) is associated with multiple flight licenses in a one-to-many relationship. Finally, there is a many-to-many relationship between the FLIGHT_OF_DAY entity and the PILOT_AIRCRAFT_ENABILITY entity, to correlate scheduled flights with specific pilot-aircraft skills.
