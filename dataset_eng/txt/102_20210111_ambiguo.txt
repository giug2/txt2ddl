So, we basically have a system that handles some supermarket stuff. Each supermarket is identified by a code, then has a name and an address, normal things. Inside these shops, the products are organized into categories, such as "fresh" or "canned", and each category has its own acronym and full name. The thing is that a supermarket can deal with a lot of different categories, and the same category, obviously, can be found in a lot of supermarkets. Then there are the actual products; each product is strictly linked to a single category, it cannot belong to more than one. To identify a product in a unique way, its code is not enough, you also need to know the acronym of its category, and then oh well, it has its commercial name. Moving on to the people, there are the employees, each with their serial number, name and surname. It seems that an employee can work in multiple places, and a supermarket clearly has many employees. There is also the whole geographical part: we have the provinces, each with a code and a name, and within each province there are various cities, also with a code and name. Each city is in a single province. The interesting thing is that for each employee we know two things about the cities: which one they were born in and which one they live in. So, a city may be the birthplace for a lot of employees, but a person is born in only one place; the same goes for residence, an employee lives in only one city.
