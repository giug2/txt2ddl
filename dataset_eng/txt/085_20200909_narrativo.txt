This system was designed to orchestrate and manage the vibrant world of theater, capturing every step of the creative and organizational process, from the author's idea to the audience's applause. At the center of each representation is the vision of an `Author`, a unique figure registered in our archive with an identifying `Code`, his `name`, `surname` and the fundamental dates of his life, the `date of birth` and the `date of death`. Each author can create countless masterpieces, but each 'Theatrical Work' is born from the pen of a single creator. Each of these works is in turn a distinct entity, characterized by its own `Code`, a memorable `Title` and the `Year` of its first draft.

An opera, no matter how magnificent, needs a stage to come to life. Here the `Theatre` comes into play, a physical and cultural place identified by a `Code`, a `Name`, an `Address` to reach it and a `Telephone` to contact it. The magical moment in which an opera meets its audience is sanctioned by the creation of an 'Opera on the bill'. This fundamental entity represents the union between a specific `Theatrical Work` and a specific `Theatre` for a specific `Period` of time. Naturally, a single theater can host a rich program with many different works, and the same work can be performed in different theaters around the world.

Giving voice and body to the characters are the `Actors`, each registered with their own personal `Code`, `name` and `surname`. Their talent is directly linked to the single staging: an actor can be part of the cast of several works on the bill, and each performance is animated by a company made up of several actors, creating a dense network of artistic collaborations.

The spectator experience begins with the purchase of a `Ticket`, which is not a simple piece of paper but a passport for a special evening. Each ticket is inextricably linked to a specific `Work on the bill` and to a well-defined `Type of place`, which could be the stalls or the gallery, each with its own `Code` and a `Description`. The `Price` of the ticket varies based on this unique combination of show and location. Finally, to make art more accessible, each `Theatre` has the possibility of offering discounts and concessions. These opportunities are categorized as `Reduction Type`, each with a `Code` and a `Description` (for example "students" or "seniors"). A theater can offer different types of discounts and, at the same time, a certain discount category can be available at multiple theatres, thus promoting culture and participation.
