This system was designed to map and organize the entire academic universe of an institution, creating a network of information that connects teachers, areas of study and courses offered. At the highest level, the organization is divided into Departments, each identified by a unique code and name. Each Department is the academic home for many Professors, each of whom is registered with his own identification code and name. At the same time, knowledge is structured into broad disciplinary sectors, such as "Engineering" or "Humanities", which are also defined by a code and a name. Each Sector, in turn, groups together several more specific Disciplines; a Discipline, such as "Computer Science", is therefore uniquely defined not only by its code but also by the Sector to which it belongs. This creates a clear hierarchy of knowledge. Each academic sector also boasts the expertise of many specialized professors.

Going into the detail of the training offer, each Discipline takes the form of a series of study subjects. A Subject is uniquely identified by the combination of a suffix (for example "Advanced" or "Laboratory") and the reference Discipline, allowing you to create variations of the same subject. It is interesting to note how one Subject can be a prerequisite for many others, thus creating a structured and sequential learning path for students. Finally, to be delivered, a subject is included in the study plan as a specific course. A single subject may be offered in many different courses, perhaps in different years or semesters. Each Course is an entity in itself, with its own code, and is associated with a specific course of study and semester. Fundamentally, each individual Course is taught by a single Professor, who, of course, may be responsible for teaching numerous courses during his or her career.
