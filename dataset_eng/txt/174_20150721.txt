The system describes a university reality in which departments are managed, each uniquely identified by a code and characterized by a name. In turn, the departments offer different degree courses; each course is defined by a code, a name and the code of the department it belongs to, thus establishing a direct link with the entity of the departments. At the same time, the system registers the students, recording for each one the student number which acts as a unique identifier, the surname, the name and the date of birth. The core of the model is represented by course enrollments. Each registration is identified by a unique ID and also has the reference academic year. Each registration is associated with a student, whose student number must correspond to a student registered in the system, and must necessarily belong to one of the registered degree courses, thus linking the registration to a specific study path. Finally, the system tracks the economic activities linked to each registration. Taxes are managed, each with its own identifying ID, issue date and amount; each fee is unambiguously associated with a specific membership. Payments are recorded in the same way, each identified by a unique ID and characterized by a date and amount. Again, each payment is directly linked to a specific enrollment, ensuring the referential integrity of the data.
