Imagine having to build the digital heart of a university, a system that represents its entire academic structure in a clear and coherent way. At the center of this organization we find two fundamental pillars: the Faculties and the Departments. Each Faculty is a unique entity, identified by its own code, and represents a large area of ​​knowledge, such as Literature or Engineering. At the same time, each Department, also identified by a unique code, constitutes a more specialized research and teaching centre. The Courses of Study, i.e. the training courses offered to students, fit within this framework. The system establishes an indissoluble bond: each Course of Study, despite having its own identifier, must necessarily belong to one and only one Faculty. This relationship is so strong that the identity of a course is defined not only by its code but also by its Faculty, ensuring that there is no ambiguity about its academic location. Multiple courses can naturally flourish under the aegis of the same faculty, creating a rich and varied educational offering. Finally, we come to the people who animate this world: the Teachers. Their identity in the system is defined by a triple belonging. A teacher is not just an individual with his own personal code, but is inextricably linked to both a specific Faculty and a specific Department. This means that the system knows with certainty which faculty a professor teaches for and which department he belongs to for his research activities. Although a faculty or department can accommodate numerous teachers, each individual teacher has a single faculty and a single department of reference, creating a clear and complete picture of his role within the university.
