Imagine a digital ecosystem designed to orchestrate the academic life of a university institution, where every element, from people to spaces, finds its precise location. At the top of this organization we find the Department, the beating heart of research and teaching, uniquely identified by its Name and led by a Director. Each Department is a world unto itself, but is closely connected to the teaching staff. In fact, the system manages the teachers, each registered with a personal number, name and surname, and tracks their belonging to the various departments. The relationship is flexible: a teacher can collaborate with multiple departments and, for each department in which he works, the subject of his competence is specified, creating a clear picture of the academic specializations.

Under the aegis of a Department, degree courses are created and developed. Each course belongs to a single reference Department and is defined by a unique pair of Code and Name, enriched by a detailed Description that illustrates the training objectives. The structure of a Degree Course is divided into individual Teaching Modules, the fundamental building blocks of knowledge. A Teaching Module, identified by a Code and a Title, can be a fundamental teaching shared between multiple study paths, and, vice versa, each Degree Course is made up of numerous modules. This flexibility allows us to create interconnected and multidisciplinary training paths. Behind each Teaching Module there is the mind of an Author, identified by his own Code and Name, who is responsible for creating the contents. Although an author may have developed several modules, each individual module can be traced back to a single creator.

University life is not just theory, but also logistics. For this reason, the system manages the classrooms, each with its own code, a name for easy identification and an indication of its seating capacity. Each Degree Course has a main reference room, but the same room can host the lessons of different courses during the day. Finally, to complete the academic cycle, the system organizes the Exam Sessions. Each session is inextricably linked to a single Degree Course and is set on a specific date. A course will naturally have multiple exam sessions throughout the year, allowing students to measure their learning at different times.
