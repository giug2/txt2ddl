So basically we have a little bit of conference related stuff here. Every congress, or conference, has its own code to recognize it and a title, so we understand what it is about. These events, obviously, are held somewhere, and in fact each conference is linked to a specific city. Clearly a lot of conferences can be held in a city, but a single conference takes place in only one place. The cities, in turn, have an acronym, like a code, and their name, and are part of a nation. Each nation, which also has its own acronym and name, groups together quite a few cities, but a city is only in one nation, it cannot be in two places at the same time. Then, returning to the conferences, each of these deals with a certain subject. Let's say that a subject can be the topic of many different conferences, but a conference focuses on a single subject. The interesting thing is that these subjects are themselves within larger areas. Each area, with its code and name, contains several subjects, but one subject belongs only to one thematic area. In fact, to identify a subject, its code is not enough, you must also know which area it is connected to, it is a kind of fixed pair.
