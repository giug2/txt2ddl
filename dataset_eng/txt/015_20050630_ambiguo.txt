So, basically there is this system for managing hotels. Each hotel is in a certain hamlet, you know, a small town or a specific area. Obviously in a hamlet there can be a lot of hotels, but a hotel is just there and that's it. We keep a bit of stuff about each hotel: the name, where it is located, the category such as how many stars it has, how many beds there are in total, and the usual contacts such as telephone and fax. Then, for hotels, there is the whole issue of rooms. A hotel can have rooms of various types, such as single, double, suite, and so on. And the same type of room, like the 'standard double', can be found in a lot of different hotels, it's not like it's exclusive to one. Similarly, each hotel can offer different treatments, such as full board, half board, room only... and these options are not unique to a hotel, but are more or less standard and you can find them almost everywhere. Now comes the fun part, the prices, that is, the tariffs. Each rate, which is practically a number, a specific price, is linked to only one hotel. So a hotel has its own price list, so to speak. But it's not that simple. A rate also depends on the season – such as high season, low season, etc. – and each season has its own rates. And again, a fee is tied to a specific service. So, to know how much something costs, you have to put together the hotel, the season you are in and the service you want. It's all a combination of things to get the final price.
