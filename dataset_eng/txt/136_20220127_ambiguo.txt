So basically we're keeping track of a few things here. There are managers, each with their own personal code and name, in short, the bosses. Then we have the various departments, also with an identification number and a name to understand what they do. Obviously there are also assistants, who are the people who lend a hand, and we have an ID and a name for them too. Finally, we manage the offices, that is, the physical places where people work, each with its own code and address. It works more or less like this: each department is necessarily located in one and only one location, but it is clear that the same location can host a lot of different departments. The most confusing part is how people are linked: there is a kind of big three-way connection between managers, assistants and departments. This means that a manager can deal with multiple assistants in various departments, an assistant can lend a hand to several managers, perhaps scattered around the company, and consequently a department can have a nice mix of managers and assistants working together in some way.
