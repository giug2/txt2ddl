So basically we have a system for tracking music. There are the artists, each one has their own unique code so as not to cause confusion. Then there are the musical genres, which also have their own identifying number. Things get interesting with albums: each album is tied to only one specific genre, but obviously a genre can contain an infinite number of albums. To identify an album uniquely, however, its ID is not enough, you also need to know which genre it belongs to, so these two pieces of information always go together. Same story for songs: a lot of songs fit into an album, but a song only fits into that one. And even for songs, to find one you need to have both its ID and that of the album it's linked to. Finally, the connection between artists and albums is a bit of a mess, in the sense that an artist can churn out a lot of albums, but at the same time an album can be the fruit of the work of multiple artists, think of collaborations or compilations.
