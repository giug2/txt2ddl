The data model defines the structure for managing a logistics system. The `Warehouse` entity is identified by a `Warehouse Code` (PK) and contains an `address` attribute. Each warehouse has multiple shelves associated with it, as defined by the `Shelf` entity, whose primary key is `ShelfCode`. The relationship between `Warehouse` and `Shelf` is one-to-many, establishing that a shelf belongs to only one warehouse. The system tracks counting operations through the `Inventario` entity, identified by `InventoryData` (PK) and containing the `counted_articles` attribute. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Shelf` and `Inventory`, indicating that an inventory operation can involve multiple shelves and a single shelf can be subject to multiple inventories. The model also includes the `Product` entity, uniquely identified by `SKU` (PK), and the `Operator` entity, identified by the `Serial number` (PK), which, in the specified scheme, do not have direct relationships with the other entities.
