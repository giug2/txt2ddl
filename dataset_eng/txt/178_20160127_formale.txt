The data model describes an academic structure based on three main entities: Department, Degree Course and Student. The Department entity is uniquely identified by a Department Code (PK) and has a Department Name. The Degree Course entity, identified by a CourseCode (PK), includes a CourseName. Finally, the Student entity is defined by a Student Number (PK), StudentName, Address and CourseYear. The relationships between these entities are hierarchical: there is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between Department and Degree Course, indicating that each department can offer multiple degree courses, but each degree course belongs to a single department. Similarly, a one-to-many (1:N) relationship links Degree Course and Student, so each degree course can have many students enrolled, while each student is enrolled in only one degree course.
