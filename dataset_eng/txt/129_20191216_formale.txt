The data model describes four main entities: `Skier`, `Specialty`, `City` and `Country`. The `Skier` entity is identified by a unique `Code` (PK) and includes the `Surname` and `First Name` attributes. The entities `Specialty`, `City` and `Country` are also identified by a respective `Code` (PK) and described by a `Name`. The relationships between entities are defined as follows: A one-to-many relationship links `Nation` to `City`, establishing that each city belongs to only one nation. Similarly, one-to-many relationships exist between `Country` and `Skier` and between `City` and `Skier`, indicating that each skier is associated with a single country and a single city. Finally, a many-to-many relationship exists between `Skier` and `Specialty`, meaning that a skier can excel in multiple disciplines and each discipline can be practiced by multiple skiers. This association is further specified by the `Wins` attribute, which quantifies a skier's successes in a given specialty.
