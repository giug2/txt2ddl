The data model describes a system for managing musical events, structured around seven main entities. The **Piece** entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has a `Title`. The **Musician** entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, is linked to `Piece` by a one-to-many relationship, so a musician can compose multiple pieces. The **Concert** entity is identified by a primary key composed of `Code` and `Title` and contains a `Description`. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Piece` and `Concert`, indicating that a concert can include multiple pieces and a piece can be performed in multiple concerts. Each concert can have multiple performances, modeled by the **Replica** entity, whose primary key is composed of `Data` and `fk_concerto`, which represents a foreign key to the `Concert` entity. The **Hall** entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Name` and `Capacity`, has a one-to-many relationship with `Concert`, since a hall can host multiple concerts. Similarly, the **Orchestra** entity, identified by `Name` (PK) and characterized by a `Conductor`, has a one-to-many relationship with `Concert`, as an orchestra performs multiple concerts. Finally, the **Orchestral** entity is identified by a `Serial Number` (PK) and has a `Name` and `Surname`. The relationship between `Orchestra` and `Orchestrale` is many-to-many and includes the descriptive attribute `Instrument`, specifying which instrument an orchestral player plays in a given orchestra.
