The data model describes a system for restaurant management, divided into geographical, registry and product entities. The geographical structure is hierarchical: the `Province` entity, identified by the `Acronym` (PK), is in a one-to-many relationship with the `Municipality` entity (PK: `Code`). Each `Municipality`, in turn, is associated with multiple records in the `Address` entity (PK: `Code`). The main entity `Restaurant` (PK: `Code`) is linked to one or more `Address` via a one-to-many relationship, to represent the different locations. The composition of the menu is defined by a many-to-many relationship between `Restaurant` and `Dish` (PK: `Code`), which includes the `Cost` attribute to specify the sales price. Each `Dish` is composed of multiple `Ingredients` (PK: `Code`), according to a many-to-many relationship. In turn, the ingredients are associated with possible `Intolerances/Allergies` (PK: `Code`) via a further many-to-many relationship. The system also manages the `Person` entity (PK: `CF`), which participates in a many-to-many relationship with `Restaurant`, enriched by the `Quote` attribute to model participations. Finally, kinship relationships between people are managed through a recursive relationship on the `Person` entity, implemented through the `Kinship` associative entity, whose primary key is composed of the foreign keys `fk_person1` and `fk_person2`. Each kin relationship is qualified by a `Kinship Type` (PK: `TypeCod`), linked to `Kinship` by a one-to-many relationship.
