This system was designed to map the organizational architecture of an enterprise, starting from its broader structure down to the individual operational components. At the top of the hierarchy we find the fundamental entity, the Company, uniquely identified by its own code. Each company, in turn, is organized into one or more Divisions, which represent its different branches or departments. A division cannot exist independently, but is always closely linked to a specific parent company; in fact, to identify it unequivocally, its identification code is not enough, but it is necessary to associate it with the company to which it belongs. The operational life of the organization pulsates within these divisions. First of all we find the Employees, the people who contribute to the success of the company with their work. Each employee is assigned to one and only one division, and their identification is unique within that specific context. In parallel with the staff, each division manages and carries out various Projects. Similar to employees, each project is born and developed under the responsibility of a single division, being inseparably linked to it for its identification and management. In this way, the model describes a clear hierarchical structure where a company contains many divisions, and each division, in turn, can have numerous employees within it and manage multiple projects.
