Basically, we have a system here for keeping track of some music biz stuff. At the center of everything are the record labels, each with its own name and a code to recognize it. Then, linked to these labels, there are the artists; for each artist we have his stage name and an identification code, but the strange thing is that to find it you need not only his code but also that of the label he is with, as if they were one and the same. An artist, obviously, can make a lot of albums, and each album has its own title and number, but here too, to be sure which album we are talking about, you need to know which artist it belongs to. At the same time, there are also producers, who have a name and their own ID, and they too are always connected to a specific record label. The most flexible relationship seems to be that between producers and albums: a producer can work on multiple different records and, vice versa, a single album can have the work of multiple producers behind it, a true team effort.
