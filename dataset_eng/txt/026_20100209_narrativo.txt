This system was designed to flexibly and completely manage information relating to a banking institution's customers and their current accounts. At the center of our world are Customers, unique people identified unequivocally by their Tax Code. For each customer, in addition to this fundamental data, we store essential personal information such as name, surname and date of birth, which allow us to have a complete and personal profile. In parallel, we manage the fundamental entity of the Current Account, each individual account being distinguished from all others through a unique identification number. The real strength of the system lies in the way in which these two realities, the customer and the account, are connected to each other. We understand that a customer may have multiple current accounts, perhaps one personal and one joint, and, similarly, a single current account may be held in the name of multiple customers, such as in the case of a family or business account. To reflect this complexity, we have established a very flexible relationship that allows each customer to be associated with any number of accounts and each account to be linked to multiple customers. Furthermore, to ensure maximum precision in the management of communications, each time a customer is associated with an account, an address is specified. This address is not linked to the customer itself, but precisely to that specific relationship with that account, allowing us, for example, to send correspondence from a specific account to a domicile other than the customer's residence, thus offering a highly personalized service.
