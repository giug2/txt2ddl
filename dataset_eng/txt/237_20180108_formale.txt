The data model defines the structure of a university information system, divided into academic and registry entities. The organizational structure is represented hierarchically starting from the `Faculty` entity, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. Each faculty is associated with one or more departments, described by the entity `Department` with attributes `Code` (PK) and `Name`. In turn, each department offers one or more degree courses. The `CorsoDiLaurea` entity is defined by a unique `Code` (PK), `Name`, `TipoLaurea` and `AnnoAttivazione`, and maintains a membership relationship with a single department. The main entity of the domain is the `Student`, uniquely identified by the `Number` (PK) and characterized by `Surname`, `Name`, `Average Grades` and `CFUAcquired`. Each student is enrolled in a single degree course. Finally, the `StudentStatus` entity, composed of `Code` (PK) and `Description`, classifies the career condition of each student, establishing a one-to-many relationship with it.
