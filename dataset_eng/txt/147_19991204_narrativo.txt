This system was designed to map in detail the fascinating world of cars, starting from their geographical origin up to the final owner. The journey begins with the single `Nation`, identified by a unique `Nation Initials`, which represents the cradle of car manufacturers. Each manufacturing `House`, in turn, is recognized by a unique `Code` and a `Name`, and is inextricably linked to a single `Nation` of origin, although a nation can host numerous manufacturing companies. The beating heart of the production of each `House` is represented by its `Models`. A `Model` is uniquely defined not only by its `Name`, but also by the `Manufacturing Company` to which it belongs, ensuring that each model is unambiguously associated with its creator. Each `House` can give rise to numerous `Models`, but each `Model` belongs unambiguously to only one `House`.

To organize this wide range of vehicles, each `Model` is classified within a specific `Category`, such as "SUV" or "utility car", identified by a `Category Code`. A category can group together many different models, but each model fits into only one category. Going into even more detail, each `Model` is divided into different `Versions`, each with its own `Version Code` and technical specifications such as `Displacement`. A `Version` is always the child of a single `Model`, but a model can offer a wide choice of versions to satisfy different tastes and needs.

From the world of design we then move on to the reality of the road with the `Car` entity, which represents the single physical vehicle. Each `Car` is unmistakably identified by its `Plate` and has a `Year` of production. Each of these cars is the concrete exemplification of one and only one `Version`, which means that countless identical cars can be produced from a single version. Finally, the system manages people and property. Each `Person` is registered with their own unique `Tax Code`, `Surname` and `First Name`. The link between people and cars is flexible to reflect reality: a `Person` can own multiple cars and, at the same time, a `Car` can be owned by multiple people, thus also managing cases of joint ownership.
