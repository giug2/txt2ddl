Imagine a vast and interconnected musical universe, a place where every note, every melody and every artist has its place. This system was designed precisely to navigate this world, to catalog and understand the complex relationships that bind music to its creators and its history. At the center of everything is the `Song`, the single musical creation, identified by a unique code and known by its `Title`. To give order to this immense library, each song is associated with a single `Musical Genre`, which in turn, with its specific name, can embrace countless compositions, guiding the listener through soundscapes ranging from rock to jazz, from classical to electronic music. But what would a song be without its `Artist`? Each musician is registered with their name, surname and a personal code. The relationship between artists and songs is dynamic and fluid: an artist can create an infinite number of songs over the course of his career and, at the same time, a single song can be the magical result of a collaboration between multiple artists. The journey of a song into the real world is made possible by the `EtichettaDiscografica`, the record companies which, identified by a code, a name and a location, produce and distribute the music. Often, songs are collected in an `Album`, a collection of works presented to the public with a specific name. The crucial moment in which all these elements meet is `Publication`. This fundamental entity represents not just a song, but the specific event of its release. Each publication inextricably links a `Song` to a `Record Label` in a specific `Year`. This allows us to trace the history of a song: a song can have multiple publications, perhaps coming out first as a single and then being included in an `Album` that collects several publications. Likewise, each label maintains a vast catalog of releases, and each album is, in effect, a curated collection of these individual releases, creating a detailed and fascinating map of the history of recorded music.
