The data model describes the structure for managing veterinary information, divided into four main entities: `Owner`, `Animal`, `Care Plan` and `Clinical Area`. The `Owner` entity is identified by a primary key `owner_id` and contains the `owner_name` attribute. The `Animal` entity is identified by the primary key `animal_id` and has an `animal_name`. The `Care Plan` entity is defined by the primary key `care_plan_id` and a `care_plan_name`. Finally, the `Clinical Area` entity is identified by `clinical_area_id` (PK) and includes a `clinical_area_name`. The relationships between entities are as follows: There is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between `Owner` and `Care Plan`, indicating that an owner can be associated with multiple care plans. There is also a many-to-one (N:1) relationship between `Animal` and `Care Plan`, so multiple animals can follow the same plan. Similarly, a many-to-one (N:1) relationship links `Animal` and `Clinical Area`, associating multiple animals with a single clinical area. Consequently, the `Animal` entity will contain foreign keys to `Care Plan` and `Clinical Area`, while the `Care Plan` entity will contain a foreign key to `Owner`.
