So, in practice we have a kind of general container for events that we call Series, each with its own title, and a lot of Congresses end up inside. Each conference is linked to only one of these series, and to identify it its code is not enough, but you must also know which series it belongs to. Obviously, each conference is held on a certain date and in a specific city, and there can be several different conferences in the same city. Inside a congress, then, there are the various Sessions, each with its own title and a specific time. People participate in these sessions, but it's a bit complex: we have a kind of connection, called Person with Affiliation, which unites people with their institutions. In practice, a session can have a lot of people affiliated, and the same person can participate in multiple sessions. This connection points on one side to the actual Person, with his name, and on the other to the Institution from which he comes, also with a name. To finish the tour, each Institution is located in a specific City. In short, everything is held together by codes everywhere to avoid confusion.
