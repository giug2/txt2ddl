The data model describes the structure of a university teaching management system. The central entity is the `Course`, identified by a `Code` (PK) and characterized by a `Title` and a `Description`. Each `Course` is associated with a single `Department`, a single `Room` and a single `Academic Year`. The `Department` entity is identified by its `Name` (PK), the `Classroom` entity by its `Code` (PK) and includes `Name` and `Places`, while the `Academic Year` entity is defined by `Code` (PK) and `Year`. The structure of a `Course` is defined by the associative entity `Program`, whose composite primary key (`Order`, `fk_corso`) establishes the ordered sequence of the `Didactic Modules` that compose it. A `Learning Module`, identified by its `Code` (PK) and `Title`, is in turn attributable to a single `Author`, defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. Learning activities are modeled by the `Lesson` entity, whose composite primary key (`DateTime`, `fk_course`) uniquely links each lesson to a specific course at a specific time. The `Teacher` entity is identified by the `Serial Number` (PK) and has the `Name` and `Surname` attributes. Two distinct relationships exist between `Teacher` and `Department`: a many-to-many relationship, represented by the `Affers` attribute, which models a teacher's membership in one or more departments, and a one-to-one relationship, with the `Chair` and `Chair` attributes, to designate the teacher who manages a single department.
