This system is designed to clearly and intuitively map the entire organizational architecture of a company, outlining the hierarchical and functional structure from the highest to the most operational level. At the center of everything we find the Headquarters, the physical pillars of the organization; each location is a unique entity that represents a distinct operations center, such as a branch or main office. Working life within each location is then divided into specific departments. A department is not an abstract entity, but is always and inextricably linked to a specific location to which it belongs; in fact, while a single location can be a vibrant ecosystem that hosts numerous departments, each department belongs exclusively to one and only one location. Populating these departments are the Assistants, the beating heart of daily activities. Each assistant is assigned to a single department, creating a direct and clear link of belonging and responsibility. A department, therefore, can count on a team made up of many assistants, but each assistant dedicates their skills to only one departmental area. Finally, the figure of the Manager intervenes to supervise and guide the operations. Management is conceived in a flexible and dynamic way: a single department can benefit from the guidance of multiple managers, perhaps with different skills, to cover all aspects of the work. At the same time, a talented manager is not confined to a single team, but can extend his or her responsibility across different departments, ensuring consistency and a transversal strategic vision. This structure allows for collaborative, multi-level leadership, capable of adapting to complex business needs.
