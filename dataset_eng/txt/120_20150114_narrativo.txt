This system has been meticulously designed to map and analyze the geography and chronology of the democratic process, offering a clear and structured view of how elections take place across the country. At the center of our structure we find the geographical organization of the vote: the country is divided into Regions, each uniquely identified by a Code and known by its name. In turn, each Region is divided into multiple electoral colleges, each distinguished by its own identification number. This relationship establishes that a constituency can belong to one and only one region, while a region, of course, includes numerous constituencies. Parallel to this spatial dimension, the system manages the temporal dimension through the Election entity. Each electoral consultation is recorded with the indication of the year in which it was held and keeps track of the total number of eligible voters, allowing us to build a complete history of voting appointments. However, the true heart of the model lies in the interconnection between these two worlds: a single College participates in several Elections over time, and, vice versa, a single Election takes place simultaneously in a vast set of Colleges. This flexible relationship is crucial for analyzing vote evolution and turnout in a granular way. Finally, the system keeps track of the people who animate this process: each individual involved is registered as a Person via a unique code, and particular attention is paid to those who obtain a seat, specifically cataloged as Elected and identified by their own code.
