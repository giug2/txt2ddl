So basically we have some stuff to deal with. There are the big chains, you know, the main brands, each with its own unique acronym and full name. Then, of course, each chain has a lot of stores underneath it scattered around. Each store has its own code, but to identify it without making mistakes you also need to know which chain it belongs to, otherwise there will be confusion. And then, oh well, each shop has a name and is at a certain address. Inside these shops, goods and products are sold. Each product has its own code to recognize it and a name. The thing works is that a shop can have a lot of different products for sale, and you can find the same product in quite a few shops, it's not an exclusive. Speaking of products, every item is made by someone, by a manufacturer. A manufacturer, who has his own code and his own name, can churn out many different items, but a single product always and only comes out of a single factory, from a single producer. Then there are the people who work there, the employees. Everyone has their own identification code, name and surname, as usual. People can work at more than one store, and a store, of course, has more than one employee. When we associate a person with a store, we also note what role they have in there, such as whether they are a cashier, a warehouse worker or something like that. As for employees, we also keep track of some demographic information. We have a list of cities, each with its own code, and we are interested in knowing in which city an employee was born and, separately, in which city he currently resides. So, basically, a person is tied to a city of birth and a city of residence, and a city can be the place of birth or residence for a lot of people.
