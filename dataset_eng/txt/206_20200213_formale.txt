The data model describes four entities: Division, Office, Project, and Employee. The `Division` entity is identified by a `division_id` primary key, while the `Office` entity is defined by a `office_id` primary key. The `Project` entity has an N:1 relationship with `Division`, indicating that each project belongs to only one division, which can manage multiple ones. The identification of a project occurs through a primary key composed of `project_id` and the foreign key `fk_division`, which establishes the reference to `Division`. Finally, the `Employee` entity is linked via N:1 relationships to both `Division` and `Office`, meaning that each employee is assigned to a single division and a single office. The primary key of the `Employee` is composite and made up of `employee_id`, the foreign key `fk_division` and the foreign key `fk_office`, guaranteeing the uniqueness of the employee within the defined organizational context.
