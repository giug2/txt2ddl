The data model is structured around four main entities: MANAGER, DEPARTMENT, ASSISTANT and HEADQUARTERS. The MANAGER entity is uniquely identified by `manager_id` (PK) and contains the `manager_name` attribute. The DEPARTMENT entity is defined by its primary key `department_id` (PK) and the `department_name` attribute. The ASSISTANT entity is characterized by the primary key `assistant_id` (PK) and the `assistant_name` attribute. Finally, the LOCATION entity is identified by `location_id` (PK) and described by the `location_address` attribute. The relationships between these entities are defined as follows: there is a many-to-one (N:1) cardinality relationship between DEPARTMENT and HEADQUARTERS, whereby each department is associated with only one location, while one location can host multiple departments. There is also a many-to-many-to-many (N:N:N) ternary relationship that links the MANAGER, ASSISTANT and DEPARTMENT entities, modeling the joint assignment of managers and assistants to specific departments.
