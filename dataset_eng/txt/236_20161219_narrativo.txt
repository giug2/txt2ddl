This system was designed to map and navigate the complex world of higher education, offering a complete vision that starts from geographical location up to the heart of the training offer. Our exploratory journey begins on a large territorial scale, with the STATE entity, where each nation is uniquely identified by an acronym and its full name. Each state acts as a container for a set of REGIONS, each of which is in turn defined by an identification code and a name, and belongs exclusively to a single state, thus creating a clear geographical hierarchy.

It is within these regions that academic institutions, UNIVERSITIES, arise and thrive. Each university is a unique entity, recognized by its own code and official name, and is firmly rooted in a single and specific region, inextricably linking knowledge to its territory. Each university represents a complex ecosystem, internally organized into various Departments. A DEPARTMENT is uniquely defined not only by its name, but also by the university to which it belongs, ensuring that there can be no confusion between departments of the same name from different universities. For each department we also know a fundamental resource such as the number of classrooms available to it.

The beating heart of each department is its educational offering, represented by the DEGREE COURSES. Although a department can create numerous study paths, each single degree course, identified by a unique code, a specific name and the year in which it was established, is managed and belongs to a single reference department. Finally, we arrive at the central figures of teaching: the TEACHERS. Each teacher is identified by a personal number and plays a crucial role. According to this structure, a single teacher can be the manager or coordinator of multiple degree courses, guiding different training paths, while each degree course is headed by a single reference teacher, who represents its main academic figure. In this way, the system weaves together geography, institutional organization, and people to create a detailed and navigable picture of the academic landscape.
