The data model describes a management system for warehouses, products and suppliers. The `COUNTRY` entity, identified by the `ABSCRIPTION` primary key, has a one-to-many relationship with the `CITY` and `SUPPLIER` entities, indicating that a country can have multiple cities and multiple suppliers. Each `CITY`, identified by an `ID` (PK), belongs to a single country and can host multiple warehouses. The `WAREHOUSE` entity, identified by an `ID` (PK), itself contains multiple shelves. The `SHELF` entity is characterized by a composite primary key, formed by `SHELF_CODE` and `fk_warehouse`, thus establishing a mandatory and unique association with a `WAREHOUSE`. Each shelf can contain multiple products. The `PRODUCT` entity, uniquely identified by its `SKU` (PK) and described by `PRODUCT_NAME` and `PRODUCTION_YEAR`, is supplied by a single `SUPPLIER`. Finally, the `SUPPLIER` entity, identified by an `ID` (PK) and with attributes such as `COMPANY_NAME` and `VAT_INFORMATION`, is associated with a single `COUNTRY`.
