This system was designed to map a university's academic ecosystem, capturing the fundamental connections between its structures and the people who bring them to life. At the highest level we find the CAMPUS, the physical headquarters that acts as a container for intellectual and educational activities. A single CAMPUS can host numerous DEPARTMENTS, each dedicated to a specific area of ​​knowledge, but each DEPARTMENT has its precise headquarters within a single CAMPUS, thus creating a well-defined physical and organizational link. PROFESSORS operate within each DEPARTMENT; the structure provides that a DEPARTMENT is made up of many teachers, but each PROFESSOR formally refers to and belongs to only one DEPARTMENT, which represents his academic home. At the same time, the beating heart of each DEPARTMENT is represented by the STUDENT community. Similarly to teachers, a DEPARTMENT welcomes a large number of STUDENTS, but each STUDENT is registered and linked to a single reference DEPARTMENT for their study path. Finally, the model captures the central teaching relationship between teachers and learners: it is a dynamic and multifaceted interaction in which a PROFESSOR can teach many different STUDENTS and, at the same time, a STUDENT, over the course of his career, has the opportunity to learn from numerous PROFESSORS, thus enriching his own education.
