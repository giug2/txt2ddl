The data model defines the structure for managing museums, artists and works of art. The main entities include NATION, identified by an ABBREVIATION and a NAME; CITY, with a unique ID and a NAME; and MUSEUM, also with an ID and NAME. The ARTIST entity is defined by an ID, NAME and SURNAME, while the WORK OF ART entity is identified by a CODE and has the attributes TITLE and YEAR. The weak entity SALA is characterized by a composite primary key made up of its NAME and the foreign key fk_museo, which refers to the museum it belongs to, and includes a SIZE attribute. The relationships establish that a NATION can contain multiple CITIES and one ARTIST, each belonging to a single nation. A CITY can host multiple MUSEUMS, but a museum is located in only one city. A MUSEUM is made up of multiple ROOMS, with each room belonging to a single museum in an identifying relationship. An ARTIST can be the author of multiple WORKS OF ART, but each work has only one artist. Finally, there is a many-to-many relationship between ROOM and WORK OF ART, which allows a room to exhibit multiple works and a work to be placed in multiple rooms.
