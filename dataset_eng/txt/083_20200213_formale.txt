The data model describes the management of musical events and the composition of orchestras. The `Piece` entity, identified by a unique `Code` (PK) and a `Title`, is associated with a single `Composer` via a many-to-one relationship; the `Composer` is defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. The pieces are performed within a `Concert`, identified by `Code` (PK), `Title` and `Description`. The relationship between `Piece` and `Concert` is many-to-many, enriched by the `POSITION` attribute to specify the order of execution of a piece in the program. Each `Concert` is performed by a single `Orchestra` in a many-to-one relationship; the `Orchestra` entity is identified by the `Name` (PK) and characterized by `Venue` and `Conductor`. A single `Concert` can be replicated in multiple instances, modeled by the `Event` entity through a one-to-many relationship. An `Event` is uniquely identified by its `Date` (PK) and specifies a `Time`. Each `Event` takes place in a single `Theatre`, establishing a many-to-one relationship with the `Theatre` entity, which is defined by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. The internal structure of an orchestra is described by the associative entity `Composition`, identified by its own `Code` (PK). This entity links a `Musician`, an `Orchestra` and an `Instrument`, specifying that a `Musician` (identified by `Number` as PK, `Last Name` and `First Name`) plays a given `Instrument` (identified by `Code` as PK and `Name`) as part of a specific `Orchestra`.
