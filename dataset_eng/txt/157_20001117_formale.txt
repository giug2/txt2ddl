The data model describes the structure of an academic system, divided into five main entities: Department, Degree Course, Teaching, Disciplinary Area and Teacher.

The Department entity is uniquely identified by the Name attribute, which acts as a primary key (PK), and has a Headquarters attribute. The CorsoDiLaurea entity is defined by a composite primary key, made up of the name of the course and a foreign key that refers to the department it belongs to (FK_DEPARTMENT), and includes the AnnoDiAttivazione attribute. The Teaching entity is identified by a primary key composed of its name and a foreign key that refers to the degree course (FK_CORSO_DI_LAUREA); its attributes include Training Credits, Lesson Hours and Course Year. The Disciplinary Area entity is identified by the SSD Code (PK) and contains a Description. Finally, the Teacher entity is identified by the Registration Number (PK) and has the attributes Band, Years of Experience and Office.

The relationships between the entities are defined as follows: there is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between the Department and the Degree Course, indicating that each department manages multiple degree courses, but each course belongs to a single department. Similarly, a one-to-many relationship (1:N) links Degree Course and Course, so each degree course is made up of multiple courses, each belonging to a single course. Furthermore, a many-to-one relationship (N:1) is established between Degree Course and Disciplinary Area, where multiple degree courses can belong to the same disciplinary area. Finally, a many-to-one relationship (N:1) connects Teaching and Teacher, specifying that a teacher can own multiple teachings, while each teaching is assigned to a single teacher.
