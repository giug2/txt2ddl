The data model describes the structure of a music catalog, divided into five main entities. The `EtichettaDiscografica` entity represents the record companies and is identified by a `Label_ID` (primary key), with descriptive attributes such as `Name`, `Registered Office` and `Country`. The `Artist` entity, identified by `Artist_ID` (primary key), lists musical performers via `Surname`, `ArtName` and `ContactEmail`. Each `Artist` is associated with a single `Record Label` through a many-to-one relationship. The `Song` entity models individual tracks, each defined by a `Song_ID` (primary key), `Title`, `Featuring` and `Text`. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Song` and `Artist`, indicating that a song can be performed by multiple artists and an artist can perform multiple songs. The `Series` entity defines a collection of publications, uniquely identified by the `StartYear` attribute (primary key) and described by `SerieName`. The `Album` entity connects to it via a many-to-one relationship. An `Album` is identified by a primary key composed of `CatalogNumber` and `fk_series`, where `fk_series` acts as a foreign key to the `Series` entity. Finally, a many-to-one relationship links `Song` to `Album`, establishing that a song belongs to only one album. This association is qualified by the `TrackNumber` and `Duration` attributes, which respectively specify the position and length of the song within the album.
