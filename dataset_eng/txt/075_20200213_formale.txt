The data model defines an academic hierarchical structure based on four entities. At the top is the `School` entity, identified only by the primary key `school_id` and described by the `name` attribute. The `Department` entity is connected to it via a one-to-many relationship, where each department belongs to a single school; its primary key is composite, made up of `department_id` and the foreign key `fk_school`, and has the `name` attribute. Following the hierarchy, the `Course of Study` entity has a one-to-many relationship with `Department`, with a composite primary key combining `study_course_id` and the `fk_department` foreign key, in addition to the `name` attribute. Finally, a many-to-many relationship exists between `Professor` and `Course of Study`, implemented through the `Professor` entity. The latter is characterized by a composite primary key made up of `professor_id` and `fk_corso`, which acts as a foreign key towards `Course of Study`, and includes the `name` attribute for the teacher.
