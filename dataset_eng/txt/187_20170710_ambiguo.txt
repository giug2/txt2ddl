So, basically here we keep track of a few things related to the world of cinema. There are artists, you know, actors and people like that, of whom we have names, surnames and dates of birth, each with their own identification code. It seems like a lot of these artists work for a certain production studio, but obviously a studio has several artists under contract. Then, of course, an artist may have made a lot of films, and in one film there are quite a few different artists.

Speaking of films, each one has its own title and a unique code, like an ISAN, to avoid confusion. Each film is linked to only one distributor, the one responsible for shipping it, even if one distributor clearly manages many films. Of each film, then, we have several physical copies, such as DVDs, and each single copy has its own code to recognize it and we know when it entered the system. Clearly, a physical copy refers to only one film.

Finally, there are the customers, registered with their first name, last name and a telephone number, and they also have their own ID. These people can borrow physical copies of films. A customer can rent multiple things, but a specific copy at a given time can only be in the hands of one person. And when someone takes something, we write down somewhere the date they have to return it.
