So, basically there's a whole system for keeping track of music tours. Each tour has its own code to avoid confusion and, obviously, a name. A tour is made up of a lot of different concerts, and each concert is tied to one and only one specific tour; it is identified by its own code but also by that of the tour to which it belongs, so everything is connected, and it has a date. Of course, every concert takes place in a certain city, and a city can host quite a few different concerts.

Inside each concert, then, there are various performances, which would be individual sets or performances. Each one has its own starting time and a sort of description of what they play, and a code to recognize it. Things get interesting with who plays: during a performance multiple artists or bands can perform, and the same artist can do multiple performances around. To manage this intersection, there is a kind of register, a "participation", which holds the performance together with those who play it. This participation is connected on the one hand to the artist, who has his stage name and an identification code, and on the other to the band, which also has a name and a code. This way you know if it was a single person or an entire group playing. Oh, and apparently bands are also linked to a city, probably the one of origin, and several bands can emerge from one city.
