The data model defines the structure of a hospital information system, divided into four main entities: HOSPITAL, WARD, DOCTOR and NURSE. The HOSPITAL entity is uniquely identified by a `hospital_id` and contains personal data such as `address` and `city`. The DEPARTMENT entity is in a one-to-many identification relationship with HOSPITAL; its primary key is composite, made up of `department_id` and `fk_hospital`, establishing that a department is uniquely defined only within a given hospital. The DOCTOR and NURSE entities represent medical personnel and are identified by `doctor_id` and `nurse_id` respectively. There is a many-to-many ternary relationship (N:N:N) that links the entities DOCTOR, NURSE and DEPARTMENT. This relationship shapes staff assignments to departments, implying that a doctor can collaborate with multiple nurses in different departments, and vice versa, requiring an association table for its implementation.
