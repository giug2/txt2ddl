This system was designed to catalog the entire automotive universe in a hierarchical and detailed way, starting from large corporations up to the beating heart of each vehicle: the engine. At the top of this structure we find the large **Car Manufacturers**, the parent entities that own and manage the various brands. Each car manufacturer, uniquely identified, is like a large family that welcomes different **Brands** under its roof. Each brand, with its name and distinctive identity, belongs exclusively to a single automotive manufacturer, creating a direct and clear link between the brand we know and the industrial group that controls it.

Going down a further level, each Brand creates a range of **Models** of vehicles. A Model, which we recognize by its specific name such as "Panda" or "Golf", is always the result of the work of a single Brand. This structure allows us to precisely navigate from the manufacturing company to the brand and, finally, to the specific vehicle that populates our roads.

Finally, we arrive at the most technical and versatile component: **Motorization**. The system recognizes that the relationship between a Model and its engines is flexible and complex. On the one hand, the same model can be offered to the public with different engine options - think of a petrol, diesel or hybrid version of the same vehicle. On the other hand, a specific engine, perhaps a particularly efficient and appreciated engine, can be shared and installed on several different models, even within the same brand or group. In this way, our database not only organizes the genealogy of each car, but also maps the technological synergies that link different vehicles through their fundamental mechanical components.
