So, basically we have a list of companies, each with its own code and name, rather simple. Then there are the teams, also with a number to recognize them and a name. Things get a little more complicated with projects: each project has its own name and code, but this code only makes sense if you know which company it belongs to, because it is closely linked to it. Therefore, a single company can have a lot of different projects in its belly. And then come the developers, who are the most complicated part. To uniquely identify one of them, a single code is not enough, but you have to put together his personal ID, the company he works for and also the team he was included in. Of course, many developers can work for the same company and be part of the same team. Finally, to understand who does what, take into account that a developer can be put to work on several different projects, and at the same time a project is carried out by quite a few people, so there is a lot of mixing.
