So basically we have a few things to deal with. First of all there is the stuff we sell, the products, each with its own identification code, a name, a price and obviously we keep track of how many pieces we have available. These products are not placed randomly, but are grouped into categories, each with its own description and code. A product only ever belongs to one category, but a category can contain a lot of different products. Then there is where this goods comes from: each product is supplied to us by a single supplier, who we recognize through a code and his name. Of course, a supplier can give us a lot of different items. When people buy, we record the orders. Each order has its own number, date and status to understand what stage it is at. These orders are always placed by a specific customer; We know his name, where he lives and we have given him a code to avoid confusion. A customer can place as many orders as he wants, but an order always belongs to one customer only. The slightly more intertwined part is that within one order there can be many different products, and the same product can obviously be sold in lots of different orders.
