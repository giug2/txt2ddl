The data model describes the structure of an academic information system. The `Department` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and includes the `Name` and `Location` attributes. The `Faculty` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. The `CorsoDiLaurea` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has a `Name`. The `Teaching` entity is characterized by a primary key composed of `Code` and `fk_faculty`, where the latter acts as a foreign key towards the `Faculty` entity; the entity also includes the `Name` attribute. The `Teacher` entity is uniquely identified by a `Number` (PK) and includes the `Name` and `Surname` attributes. Finally, the `City` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. The relationships between the entities are defined as follows: there are two many-to-many relationships, one between `Department` and `CorsoDiLaurea` and the other between `Department` and `Teacher`. Each `Degree Course` is associated with multiple `Courses` in a one-to-many relationship, as is each `Faculty`. The `Teacher` entity is linked to the `City` entity via two distinct many-to-one relationships: one for the city of birth, with the `Birth` attribute, and one for the city of residence, with the `Resident` attribute.
