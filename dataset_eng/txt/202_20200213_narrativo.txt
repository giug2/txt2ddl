Imagine being able to navigate the intricate universe of the great cinematic sagas, exploring how they are born and develop, who creates them and who populates them. This system was designed precisely to map these narrative galaxies. At the top of everything we find the `Production House`, the creative entity, identified by its unique code. Each production company can create numerous `Franchises`, which represent large narrative worlds such as superhero sagas or space adventures. Each franchise, in turn, is identified by its own code, but is always unequivocally linked to a single parent production company, creating an indissoluble bond between the creator and his creation.

Within each `Franchise`, the narrative comes to life through two fundamental elements: the `Film` and the `Actor`. A single franchise can expand across a multitude of films, each with its own unique identifier within that specific franchise. Likewise, each franchise has its own cast of reference actors, and the system is designed so that an actor is uniquely identified precisely in the context of that particular franchise to which he is associated. The beating heart of this universe, however, lies in the way actors and films intertwine. The structure reveals to us that an actor can participate in numerous films within his franchise, and, naturally, each film is animated by a cast made up of multiple actors, creating a dense and fascinating network of collaborations that defines the very identity of the saga.
