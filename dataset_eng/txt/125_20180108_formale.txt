The data model defines the structure for managing sports clubs, players and their geographical location. The `Country` entity is identified by a `Code` attribute, which acts as the primary key, and a `Name`. Each country is associated with multiple cities via a one-to-many relationship with the `City` entity, which is in turn defined by a `Code` (primary key) and a `Name`. The `Society` entity represents a club, uniquely identified by a `Code` (primary key) and described by the `Name`, `Color` and `Foundation` attributes. Each company is based in only one city, establishing a one-to-many relationship between `City` and `Company`. The `Role` entity, characterized by a `Code` (primary key) and a `Name`, classifies the playing positions. Finally, the `Player` entity represents the athlete, identified by a `Code` (primary key) and specified by `Last Name`, `First Name`, `Height` and `Weight`. Each player belongs to a single club and plays a single role, thus establishing two distinct one-to-many relationships, from the `Club` and `Role` entities to the `Player` entity respectively.
