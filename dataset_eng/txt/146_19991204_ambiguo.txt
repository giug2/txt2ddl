In practice, here we are talking about people and machines. There are people, uniquely identified by their tax code, and then oh well, they also have a name and surname. These people can make purchases; each purchase is its own thing, defined by the date on which it occurs and by a reference to a car, and these two pieces of information together make it unique. What's a little strange is that a single purchase seems to be linked to a lot of different cars, while each specific car, the one with its license plate and year, is linked to just one purchase.

Going to look at the cars, every physical car is a certain version. There is another somewhat peculiar connection here: it seems that a single car can be the source for many different versions, but one version, with its engine capacity and code, derives from only one car. However, each version is clearly linked to a specific model. A model, which has its own name and a category such as SUV or small car, can have many versions. In turn, a model is produced by a car manufacturer, and obviously a company, with its code and name, can churn out many models. Finally, each manufacturer belongs to a nation, identified by an acronym, and in a nation there can be several car manufacturers.
