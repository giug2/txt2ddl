So, the thing here is that we have a few people and places. There are locations, you know, like branches around the city, and each location has offices inside. Then there are the people. We have the managers, who are the bosses, and for each of them it is not enough to know who they are, but we also need to know which office and which headquarters they are connected to at that moment, it's all a single block of information. A boss can be connected to multiple different offices, and similarly an office can have more than one manager hanging out there, so there's a lot of comings and goings. Finally, there are the assistants, those who lend a hand. The interesting thing is that each assistant is assigned to only one specific manager, but not to the manager in general, just to that manager in that specific location. So a boss can easily have a nice group of assistants under him.
