The data model describes a management system for supermarkets. The `Supermarket` entity is identified by a unique `Code` and has the `Name` and `Address` attributes. The `Category` entity is defined by an `Acronym` which acts as the primary key and a `Name`. The `Product` entity has a primary key composed of `Code` and `fk_category`, and includes the `Name` attribute; the `fk_category` establishes a link with the `Category` entity. The `Employee` entity is identified by the `Serial Number` and contains the `Name` and `Surname` attributes. The geographical entities `City` and `Province` are both identified by a unique `Code` and described by a `Name`. The defined relationships are as follows: a many-to-many (N:N) relationship exists between `Supermarket` and `Category`, and another of identical cardinality between `Supermarket` and `Employee`. The relationship between `Category` and `Product` is one-to-many (1:N), where each product belongs to only one category. There is a one-to-many (1:N) relationship between `Province` and `City`, whereby each city belongs to a single province. Finally, two distinct one-to-many (1:N) relationships between `City` and `Employee` are modeled: one for `Resident` and one for `Birth`, indicating that each employee has only one city of residence and one city of birth, while a city can be associated with multiple employees for both occurrences.
