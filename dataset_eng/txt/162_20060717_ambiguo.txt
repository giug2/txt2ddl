So, basically we have a system that revolves around restaurants. Each restaurant has its own basic information, such as a code to identify it, the name, where it is located and a telephone number. These restaurants, of course, are located in some city; a city may have a lot of restaurants, but a restaurant is only in one city. Then, each restaurant has its own menu, with a list of dishes. Each dish is a thing in itself, with its own code, name and price, and belongs only to that particular restaurant. In addition to individual dishes, a restaurant can also offer special menus, such as fixed price ones. These menus are defined by when they are valid, have their own price and tell you how many courses there are. These menus are also linked to a single restaurant. To attract people, restaurants can use various types of promotions, such as discounts or special offers. The interesting thing is that a restaurant can have multiple active promotions and the same promotion can be used by many different restaurants. Behind the scenes, there is all the logistics. There are large logistics centers, each with its own code and name, which are used for supplies. These centers are located in a specific city, and a city can have more than one. These logistics hubs interface with suppliers, who are the ones who bring the goods. The relationship here is a bit of an entanglement: a logistics center can be served by many different suppliers, and one supplier can easily deliver to multiple logistics centers.
