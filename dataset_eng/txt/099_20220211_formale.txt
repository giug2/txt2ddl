The data model describes the management of museums and related activities. The central entity is the `Museum`, identified by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`. Each museum can propose multiple `Itineraries` in a one-to-many relationship. The `Itinerary` entity is identified by a primary key composed of an `Acronym` and the foreign key `fk_idmuseo`, establishing an identifying link with the museum it belongs to; it also has a `Name` attribute. An itinerary is composed of multiple `Works` and, vice versa, a `Work` (identified by a PK `Code`) can be included in multiple itineraries, defining a many-to-many relationship. This association is characterized by the `N_order` attribute to specify the sequence of works within a path. Each itinerary can be associated with multiple `Guided Tours` (1:N relationship). The `Guided Tour` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Name`, `Time` and `Duration`. Finally, a many-to-many relationship links guided tours to days of the week: a `Guided Tour` can take place on multiple days, and a `Day` (identified by PK `Code` and `Name`) can accommodate multiple visits. The instance of this relationship is qualified by the `Num_participanti` attribute, which defines the number of registrants for a specific visit on a given day.
