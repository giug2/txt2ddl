The data model describes a system for managing maintenance interventions on vehicles. The `Vehicle` entity registers the vehicles and is uniquely identified by the `Plate` (PK); also includes the attributes `Owner`, `Owner Address`, `Starting Mileage` and `Current Mileage`. The `Intervention` entity tracks each individual operation, identified by the `Intervention Code` (PK), and contains `Intervention Date`, `Notes` and `Cost`. Complementary, the `Intervention Type` entity acts as a dictionary for operation categories, with `Type Code` as the primary key (PK) and a `Description` field. The entities are linked by two one-to-many (1:N) relationships: the first links a `Vehicle` to multiple `Intervention` records, establishing that each intervention is performed on a single vehicle; the second links an `Intervention Type` to many `Intervention` records, specifying that each intervention is of only one type. Both relationships imply the presence of foreign keys in the `Intervention` entity to reference `Vehicle` and `Intervention Type` respectively.
