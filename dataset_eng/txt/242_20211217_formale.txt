The data model describes the academic organizational structure, divided into three main entities: Department, Degree Course and Scientific Sector. The Department entity is uniquely identified by a Code (PK) and has a Name attribute. The Degree Course entity represents a course of study and is characterized by a Name and a Number of Places; its identification is guaranteed by a composite primary key, made up of its own Code and the foreign key fk_department, which establishes an identification dependency on the Department entity. The Scientific Sector entity is defined by a Code (PK) and a Name. The relationships between these entities are as follows: there is a one-to-many relationship between Department and Degree Course, so a department can provide multiple degree courses, while each degree course belongs to only one department. Furthermore, there is a many-to-many relationship between Department and Scientific Sector, indicating that a department can cover several scientific sectors and, vice versa, a sector can be the responsibility of multiple departments.
