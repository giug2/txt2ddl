The data model describes an academic system structured around four main entities: Teacher, Course, Student and Department. The `Teacher` entity is uniquely identified by a `teacher_id`, the `Student` entity by a `student_id` and the `Department` entity by a `department_id`. The `Course` entity has a composite primary key, formed by the `course_id` and `fk_teacher` attributes, establishing a one-to-many (1:N) identifying relationship with the `Teacher` entity; consequently, each course is uniquely associated with a single teacher, while a teacher can teach multiple courses. There is a many-to-many (N:N) relationship between the `Course` and `Student` entities, which allows a student to enroll in multiple courses and a course to be taken by multiple students. Finally, a one-to-many (1:N) relationship is defined between `Department` and `Course`, in which each course belongs to a single department, which can offer multiple courses.
