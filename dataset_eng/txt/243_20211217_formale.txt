The data model defines a university system based on six main entities. The `Faculty` entity is identified by a `Code` (PK) and has a `Name`. The `CorsoDiLaurea` entity is identified by a composite primary key made up of an `ID` and a foreign key `fk_facolta`, and includes the `Name` and `AnnualCost` attributes. The entities `DirettivaMinisteriale`, `BorsaDiStudio`, `Teaching` and `Department` are all identified by a unique `Code` (PK) and contain the attributes `Title`, `Name`, `Name` and `Name` respectively. The relationships between these entities are as follows: a one-to-many relationship links `Faculty` to `Degree Course`, indicating that each faculty offers multiple degree courses, but each course belongs to only one faculty. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Faculty` and `Ministerial Directive`. Another many-to-many relationship connects `CorsoDiLaurea` and `BorsaDiStudio`, which has a `Contribution` attribute to specify the amount. Each `Degree Course` is composed of multiple `Teachings` through a one-to-many relationship. Finally, the interactions between `Department` and `Teaching` are modeled by two distinct relationships: a one-to-many relationship establishes that a department is responsible for multiple teachings, specifying a `Responsible`, and a many-to-many relationship manages collaborations, defining `Collaborators` for each teaching-department pair.
