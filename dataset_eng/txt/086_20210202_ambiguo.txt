Basically, we have a system that tracks a few things about healthcare. There are Regions, each with its own code and name. Each region, then, has a certain number of ASLs under it. Local health authorities are identified by a code, but to be sure which one we are talking about you must always also specify the region they belong to, so their identifier is a sort of combination of these two codes. Obviously, of each ASL we know the name and where it is located, the address. Each ASL, then, includes quite a few people, the Patients. Each person is registered with his tax code, which is unique, and his name, and belongs to only one ASL. These people may take several swabs over time; every single swab is registered with its own unique identification code, the CT, and for each one we know the date on which it was taken and, clearly, the result. Naturally, each swab is linked to one and only patient, it's not like it can belong to multiple people.
