So, basically here we have a few things that revolve around the university. There is the `Professor`, who is simply a person with his own code. Then there are the books, called `Text`, also identified by a number. The highlight is the `Course`, which is a bit of a particular thing: to identify it you need to know its `Channel`, the `Subject` to which it is connected and the `Study Course` of which it is part. It's a combination of things that makes it unique.

Subjects, like "Mathematical Analysis", can have a lot of different courses associated with them, and the same goes for courses of study, like "Computer Engineering", which obviously group together quite a few courses. Each course, however, belongs to a single subject and a single course of study, there can be no confusion.

As for who teaches, the situation is quite free: a professor can hold several lessons in different courses, and at the same time a single course can have more than one professor taking care of it, perhaps they divide the program. Finally, the texts: each course seems to refer to only one specific book, but the same book can be used as a reference text for a lot of different courses, it is not an exclusive thing.
