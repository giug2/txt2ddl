The data model describes a cataloging system for works of art, structured around a geographic and physical hierarchy. The `Country` entity, identified by a `Acronym` as the primary key (PK) and a `Name`, has a one-to-many relationship with the `City` entity. Each `City`, defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name`, belongs to a single nation and can host multiple museums. As a result, there is a one-to-many relationship between `City` and the `Museum` entity, identified by `Code` (PK) and `Name`. Each museum is in turn composed of multiple rooms, establishing a 1:N relationship with the `Room` entity, which has the `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Size` attributes. The central entity of the model is `Work`, identified by a `Code` (PK) and characterized by `Title` and `Year`. Each work is placed in one and only one room. Furthermore, each work is associated with a single `Author` and belongs to a single `Type`. The `Author` entity, identified by `Code` (PK) and `Name`, is linked to a single `Country` of origin. Finally, the `Type` entity is defined by a `Code` (PK) and a `Name` for the classification of works.
