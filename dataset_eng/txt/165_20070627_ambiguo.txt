So basically we're keeping track of some stuff from the music world here. There are record companies, labels in short, each with its own code to recognize it, the name, where they have the office and in which country they are located. Then obviously there are the artists, who also have a unique identifier, their real surname, the stage name by which we all know them and an email to write to them. Clearly, an artist is signed to only one label, but a record company can have quite a few people under it. Moving on to the actual music, we have the songs, the pieces. Each song has its own code, a title, and we note if there is any featuring, that is, if someone else sings. Oh, and the whole text is there too. The interesting thing is that a song can be sung by multiple artists together, and one artist, as we know, can make a lot of songs. These songs then end up on the albums. An album is a bit particular, we identify it with a catalog number but also with a reference to a kind of series or series to which it belongs, and then we know when it was released. Obviously, an album contains many songs, but a certain song only fits in one album. When we put a song on a record, we also note what track number it is and how long it is in minutes and seconds. Finally, there are these 'series', which are kind of collections of albums. Each series has a name and we recognize it by the year it started. A bit like before, many albums can be part of the same series, but each individual album belongs to only one of these.
