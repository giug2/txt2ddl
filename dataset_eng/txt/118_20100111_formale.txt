The data model describes the management of concert seasons. The central entity is the Concert, identified by a unique Code (PK) and described by a Title and a Description. Each Concert is associated with a single Season (identified by Code and Year), takes place in a specific Hall (identified by Code, Name and Capacity) and is performed by a specific Orchestra (identified by Name). A Concert can have multiple Replicas, each defined by a key composed of the Date and the foreign key of the reference concert (fk_concerto). The playlist of a Concert is defined through the associative entity Execution, which links a Concert to a Musical Piece. The Execution entity is identified by a primary key composed of the Position of the piece and the foreign key of the concert (fk_concerto), establishing the order of execution. The Piece entity, identified by a Code (PK) and a Title, is in turn composed of a single Musician, identified by a Code and a Name. The Artist entity, defined by Serial Number (PK), Name and Surname, has two distinct relationships with the Orchestra: a one-to-one relationship, qualified by the Play and Instrument attributes, to represent the musicians who are stable members of the orchestra, and a many-to-many relationship, with the Conduct attribute, to model direction by one or more orchestra conductors.
