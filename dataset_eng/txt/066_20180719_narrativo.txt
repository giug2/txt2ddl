This system was conceived as a vast digital atlas of the world's artistic heritage, a journey that connects nations, cities, museums and works of art into a single, great narrative. Our world is organized starting from Nations, each identified by a unique acronym and its full name. Each nation is a mosaic of countless Cities, and the system ensures that each city, with its specific identifier and name, belongs to one and only one nation, creating a clear geographical map. The hearts of culture beat within these cities: the museums. A single city can be proud to host numerous museums, each registered with its own ID and name, but each museum is inextricably linked to a single city to which it belongs. Virtually entering a museum, we discover that it is divided into different rooms. Each room is uniquely defined not only by its name but also by the museum that contains it, allowing us to distinguish homonymous rooms in different institutions; we also know the size of each room. It is within these rooms that the most precious treasure is revealed: the works of art. A room can display multiple masterpieces, but each single work, identified by a unique code and described by title and year of creation, is placed in one and only one room. Naturally, every work of art is the fruit of the genius of an Artist. The system lists artists with their name, surname and a unique identifier, establishing that an artist can have created countless works, while each work can be traced back to its single creator. Finally, to complete the cultural and historical picture, each artist is associated with his country of origin, showing how a nation can be the cradle of many talents and allowing us to trace artistic currents across borders and eras.
