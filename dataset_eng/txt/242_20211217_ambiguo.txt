So, in practice we have Departments, each with its own identification code to avoid confusion and a name. Then, linked to these, there are the Degree Courses. Each course has its own code, but to understand exactly which one we are talking about you must also look at which department it is connected to; let's say that the code-department combination makes it unique. Obviously, each course has a name and you also know how many places are available. The rule is that a department can have a lot of courses under it, but a single course belongs only to one department. Finally, there are the Scientific Sectors, also with a code and a name to help us understand something. Here things are a little more fluid: a department can easily range over different scientific sectors and, vice versa, a sector can be the responsibility of several departments, there is quite a lot of comings and goings between them.
