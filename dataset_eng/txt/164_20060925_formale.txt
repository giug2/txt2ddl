The data schema defines a system for managing music festivals. The central entity is `Festival`, identified by a `Code` (PK) and characterized by `Name`, `StartDate` and `EndDate`. Each festival hosts one or more `Exhibitions`, establishing a one-to-many relationship. The `Exhibition` entity acts as an associative table with a primary key of `fk_festival` and `fk_artist`, uniquely linking an `Artist` to a `Festival`. Each `Artist`, identified by a `Code` (PK) and described by `Musical Genre` and `Training Year`, can hold multiple performances, but each performance is performed by only one artist. Similarly, each `Performance` takes place on a single `Stage`, defined by its `Code` (PK), `Name` and `Capacity`, while a stage can host multiple performances. There is a many-to-many relationship between `Thematic Path` (identified by a `PK Code`) and `Exhibition`, which indicates how performances can be grouped into thematic paths; this relationship is enriched by the `StopOrder` attribute to specify the sequence of a performance within a route. Each `Thematic Route` can be associated with multiple `Guided Tours`, each identified by a `Code` (PK) and detailed by `Name`, `Departure Time` and `Duration`. Finally, each `GuidatoTour` can have several concrete instances, represented by the `GruppoTour` entity, whose primary key is composed of `Date` and `fk_tour_guidato`, and which tracks the `Number of Enrollees` for that specific date.
