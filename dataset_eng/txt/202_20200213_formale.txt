The data model describes a system for managing film productions. The `ProductionCasa` entity is identified by a primary key `production_casa_id`. Each production company can own multiple `Franchise` entities, establishing a one-to-many relationship. A `Franchise` is uniquely identified by a composite primary key, made up of `id_franchise` and the foreign key `fk_casa_formazione` which indicates its membership. In turn, each `Franchise` can contain multiple `Films`. The `Film` entity is defined by a composite primary key composed of `id_film` and `fk_franchise`, uniquely tying each film to its reference franchise. Similarly, a `Franchise` is associated with multiple `Actor`. The `Actor` entity is identified by a composite primary key made up of `actor_id` and `fk_franchise`, binding each actor to a single franchise. Finally, there is a many-to-many relationship between the `Actor` and `Film` entities, meaning that an actor can star in multiple films and a film can have a cast of multiple actors.
