This system was designed to manage a catalog of books in a detailed and organized way, precisely distinguishing the essence of a literary work from its multiple physical manifestations. At the center of everything lies the fundamental entity, the LITERARY_WORK, which represents the abstract concept of a book, uniquely identified by a WORK_ID. General characteristics of each work are known such as the average number of pages and the number of volumes of which it is composed. To facilitate classification and research, each literary work belongs to a single and specific GENRE, in turn defined by a name and an identifying code, knowing that a single genre, such as the historical novel or science fiction, can include countless different works.

A literary work, however, can come to life in multiple forms. For this purpose, the EDITION entity exists, which represents a specific publication of a work. Each edition is unambiguously identified by its ISBN code and has its own attributes such as that publication's specific title, format (for example, paperback or hardback), and cover price. Of course, a single literary work can have many different editions over time. Furthermore, each edition is written in a precise LANGUAGE, cataloged using a standard ISO code and its full name, allowing the system to manage a multilingual catalog where the same language can be associated with infinite editions.

Finally, to manage the physical inventory, the system introduces the WAREHOUSE COPY. This entity represents the single physical book on the shelves. Each copy is linked to one and only one edition through its ISBN, but there can be numerous copies of the same edition. Each of these copies is tracked with its own unique identifier, and for each one the conservation status and date on which it was acquired is recorded, thus offering a complete and granular view of the resources available in the warehouse.
